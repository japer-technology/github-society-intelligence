# Anti-Patterns

A list of patterns that damage or destroy a Society of Repo.

---

## The monarch agent

One agent controls everything.

**Correction:** Decompose into constitutional agencies with critics and censors.

---

## The prompt swamp

Hundreds of prompts exist without constitutions, metrics, or lineage.

**Correction:** Replace important prompts with governed agencies.

---

## Memory hoarding

Every record is kept equally active.

**Correction:** Use memory temperature, supersession, and retirement rules.

---

## Cloud leakage

Sensitive context leaves the system for convenience.

**Correction:** Enforce cloud-egress policy and censor review.

---

## No settlement

Agencies act directly without a visible decision record.

**Correction:** No non-trivial action without settlement.

---

## No pricing discipline

External calls occur without budgets or cost tracking.

**Correction:** Record budgets and activate cost-critic.

---

## No retirement

Zombie agencies accumulate indefinitely.

**Correction:** Quarterly evaluation, differentiation, merge, and retirement paths.

---

## Fake autonomy

The system claims authority in domains reserved for humans.

**Correction:** Keep approval-gate and constitutional anchors strict.

---

## The over-delegated chain

Agency A calls B calls C calls D with no intact authority trace.

**Correction:** Enforce delegation-depth limits and preserve originating authority.

---

## Over-confidence without evidence

A proposal sounds certain but cannot show evidence, method, or observability limits.

**Correction:** Require full provenance fields and evidence-critic review.

---

## Over-coupled ecology

Workers, critics, censors, experiments, and memory writers all share state freely.

**Why it harms the society:** Independence disappears. Critic and censor drift follows worker incentives. Experimental changes leak into production memory.

**Correction:** Maintain an insulation map, protected memory scopes, and branch-isolated or shadow experiments.

---

## Double-purpose deadlock

One agency is forced to serve two goals that should diverge into specialist successors.

**Why it harms the society:** The agency oscillates, satisfies neither role well, and becomes unmeasurable.

**Correction:** Differentiate overloaded agencies, compare successors side by side, and promote the specialist that wins.

---

## Uncontrolled shared-state drift

Durable records are copied or edited across files without typed link semantics, supersession rules, or stewardship.

**Why it harms the society:** Frames, procedures, semantic facts, and decisions disagree silently.

**Correction:** Use the representation protocol, relational links, and steward review.

---

## Flat-society overload

Every settlement sees every raw proposal directly.

**Why it harms the society:** Attention collapses under scale, and executive briefings become dumps instead of judgments.

**Correction:** Use summary tiers, assembly agencies, and justified escalation to raw context.

---

## Summary

| Anti-pattern | Core failure | Primary correction |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Monarch agent | Single point of failure and opacity | Decompose into constitutional agencies |
| Prompt swamp | No governance or metrics | Versioned constitutions |
| Memory hoarding | Signal buried in noise | Temperature, supersession, retirement |
| Cloud leakage | Sensitive data leaves without governance | Cloud-egress censor |
| No settlement | No audit trail | Mandatory settlement records |
| No pricing discipline | Uncontrolled cost | Budget fields and cost-critic |
| No retirement | Zombie agencies | Quarterly review and retirement |
| Fake autonomy | Real harm from unilateral AI action | Human approval anchors |
| Over-delegated chain | Authority diffusion | Delegation-depth censor |
| Over-confidence without evidence | False certainty | Provenance and evidence review |
| Over-coupled ecology | Loss of independence | Insulation map |
| Double-purpose deadlock | Chronic role conflict | Differentiation protocol |
| Uncontrolled shared-state drift | Silent contradiction | Representation and relational memory |
| Flat-society overload | Attention collapse | Hierarchy and summaries |
