# Self-Ideals

Self-ideals are durable norms the society uses to regulate itself from within.

Critics and censors remain external controls. Self-ideals shape what the society aims to become.

> **Minsky:** "For the long-term stability of many other mental agencies
> depends on a certain sluggishness of our images of what we ought to be
> like."

This registry is *separate from* the [self-models](self-models.md)
registry: self-models describe what the society currently *thinks it
is*; self-ideals describe what it aims to *be*. Conflating the two is
the failure that the **Self-Ideal Stability Principle (P9)** is meant
to prevent — a society that updates its values to match its behaviour
no longer has values.

Self-ideals also carry an architectural load that no other governance
artifact does. Under deliberate divergence **D9**
([`../../THE-SOCIETY-OF-MIND/12-crosswalk-to-society-of-repo.md`](../../THE-SOCIETY-OF-MIND/12-crosswalk-to-society-of-repo.md)),
no individual agency carries a `goal` field. The only first-class
place "wanting" lives in SOR is here, at the society level. Per-agency
purposive reasoning is therefore expressible only by citing an ideal
from this file inside a settlement.

---

## Active ideals

| Ideal | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| **Evidence before confidence** | Strong claims require visible grounding |
| **Reversible change before irreversible change** | Prefer the path that keeps rollback available |
| **Local-first where risk is comparable** | Use local tools and models unless a governed reason exists not to |
| **Dignity over convenience** | Human and stakeholder dignity outrank speed or automation novelty |
| **Restraint under uncertainty** | Unknowns should narrow action, not justify bolder claims |

---

## How ideals are used

High-impact proposals must record:
- which ideal is most relevant
- whether the proposal advances or strains that ideal
- what tradeoff is being accepted

A proposal that cites no ideal where one plainly applies is itself an
audit signal: the proposing agency is either reasoning outside the
society's declared values, or treating the ideals as decoration.

---

## Stability discipline (P9)

The five active ideals above are protected by the **deep-tier** of
the [constitutional stability gradient](constitution.md). They may not
be added to, removed, or reworded on the same day a change is
proposed; the constitution requires a multi-cycle review with explicit
owner acknowledgement.

This sluggishness is the feature. A small wrong ideal held steady is
cheaper than a flexible right one that drifts.

---

## Drift review

If repeated settlements conflict with the same ideal, the ecology
monitor and governance review must open a drift review.

Drift review asks:
- is the ideal unrealistic?
- is the society failing to follow it?
- should policy, staffing, tooling, or metrics change?

Drift review does **not** ask: *should we soften the ideal so the
recent behaviour fits?* That move is forbidden by P9 and by the
deep-tier amendment standard.
