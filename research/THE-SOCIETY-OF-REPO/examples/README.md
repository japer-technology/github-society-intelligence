# SOR — Society of Repo Examples

This folder contains worked examples of complete Society of Repo configurations for four common use contexts.

Each example shows how to apply the SOR architecture — agencies, critics, censors, memory, K-lines, settlements, and governance — to a specific real-world situation.

## How to read these examples

Each example follows the same structure:

```text
1. Identity          — who this SOR is
2. Purpose           — what it exists to do
3. Scope             — what is in and out of scope
4. Agencies          — the worker bees (what they do, what they watch)
5. Critics           — challenge repos that guard quality
6. Censors           — hard-limit repos that enforce safety
7. Memory            — how the SOR remembers
8. K-lines           — activation patterns the SOR develops over time
9. Sample stimuli    — examples of what wakes the society
10. Sample settlement — how a decision forms and is recorded
11. Non-negotiable limits — what this SOR will never do without human approval
```

These examples are starting points.

A real SOR for your context will need its own constitution, authority registry, and approval gate.

See [../THE-SOCIETY-OF-REPO/](../../THE-SOCIETY-OF-REPO/) for the full specification.
