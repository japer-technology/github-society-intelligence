## What the image shows

Across the middle of the figure run two parallel structures. On the left, a fan of inputs converges into a small bowtie shape labelled *frame-agent*, with the inputs marked *RECOGNIZERS*. On the right, the same bowtie sends *pronomes* outward into a fan of *terminals*, marked *MEMORIZERS*; further to the right, another fan of *RECOGNIZERS* attaches to those terminals.

## What it illustrates

A recogniser is, in a sense, the opposite of a K-line — it fires *when* a particular state is present, rather than restoring that state. The figure shows the symmetry: every frame is bracketed by recognisers that turn it on and by memorizers that record what fills its terminals; each filler in turn has its own recognisers. This is the bidirectional plumbing that lets the same frame both be recognised and be reused as memory.
