# Figure 24-5 — Implementation analysis (SOR / FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION)

**Source figure:** [24-5.md](24-5.md) — *A picture-frame built from
direction-nemes.*
**Analysis target:** [`FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/)
(plan-of-record for `.forgejo-society/` and
`.forgejo/workflows/forgejo-society.yaml`).

This file inventories the ideas carried by Figure 24-5 and checks each
one against the implementation plan. The aim is to mark, plainly,
what the plan already absorbs and what it does not yet name.

---

## The ideas Figure 24-5 actually carries

1. **The Trans-frame architecture transfers.** The picture-frame is
   the Trans-frame of [24-2.md](24-2.md) with pronomes replaced by
   direction-nemes; the wiring is unchanged.
2. **A recogniser activates the frame.** The frame-agent is labelled
   *RECOGNIZER* — what turns the frame on is a unit that detects the
   relevant kind of scene.
3. **A trunk plus a row of terminals.** One frame-agent feeds many
   terminals; each terminal is one AND-gate.
4. **Terminals are addressed by direction-neme.** *NW*, *N*, *NE*,
   …, *SE* enter the row from the right; each lights exactly one
   terminal.
5. **Each terminal carries a K-line.** What was seen when the eyes
   last looked that way is stored as a reusable activation, not as
   a value.
6. **Recall is replay around the frame.** Re-arousing the frame
   plays each K-line back through its direction — *the simulus of
   looking around inside the remembered place*.
7. **Substitution is the design move.** Re-using one architecture
   by swapping the neme family is presented as a method, not as a
   one-off.

## What the implementation already absorbs

### 1 — Frames named by what they fit

Frames in
[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md)
carry a `matches:` block (`any_signals`, `any_labels`, `any_paths`,
`any_phrases`). The detector that *turns the frame on* is part of
the frame definition, which matches the figure's choice to label
the frame-agent as a recogniser.

### 2 — K-lines as reusable activation

K-line semantics in
[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md)
are explicitly "not session replay; reusable activation". The
figure's filler model — K-lines, not values — is in the plan's
vocabulary, even if not in its per-slot wiring.

### 3 — Substitution as a design move at the frame layer

Frames in the catalogue (`question`, `bug`, `feature`,
`code-change`, `security-sensitive`, `self-modification`, `novel`)
share a schema and differ only in `matches:`, `slots:`, and
defaults. The implementation already treats *new frame = same
schema, different content* as the normal way to extend the
catalogue.

## What the implementation does not yet take into account

### A — No "substitute the neme family" move

Figure 24-5 is built by taking [24-2.md](24-2.md) and swapping
pronomes for direction-nemes. The plan has no neme-family
parameter that a frame can choose. A frame's `matches:` references
fixed activator surfaces (signals, labels, paths, phrases); there
is no field that says *this frame is parametrised over the
direction-neme family*.

### B — No per-terminal K-line binding

As in [24.2-sor.md](24.2-sor.md): K-lines in the plan are bound to
a class and an `activation_snapshot.frame`, not to a particular
slot of a particular frame. The figure's *the K-line attached to
the NW terminal of this picture-frame* has no direct counterpart.

### C — No "replay around the frame" operation

The plan reactivates a K-line by boosting agency weights and
preloading files. It has no operation that *iterates the
terminals of a frame and restores each one's K-line in turn*,
which is what the figure's "looking around inside the remembered
place" requires.

### D — Recognisers are not separate from frames

In the figure, *RECOGNIZER* is a labelled role of the frame-agent
and a separate kind of unit appears in [24-6.md](24-6.md) and
[24-7.md](24-7.md). In the plan, recognition is the frame's own
`matches:` block; there is no recogniser manifest, no recogniser
catalogue, and no recogniser/memorizer split.

### E — Picture-frames as a frame kind are absent

The first-ship frame catalogue does not include any frame whose
job is to remember a scene or layout. Even granting gap A, no
frame in the plan today plays the role the picture-frame plays in
chapter 24.

## Implication for the plan (no changes proposed here)

Figure 24-5 is mostly a *re-use* figure: it claims one architecture
covers many cases when the neme family is allowed to change. The
plan currently extends its coverage by adding *frames*, not by
re-parametrising one frame over a different neme family. The
distinction matters only if the project ever wants the
picture-frame's specific property — *cheap re-walkable layout
memory* — which the plan does not currently target.

Any move to introduce neme-family parameters, recogniser units,
per-terminal K-line binding, or a replay-around-frame operation
would touch
[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md),
[`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md),
and
[`09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md),
and so falls under the "stop and ask" rules in
[`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md) §12 and
[`CLAUDE.md`](../../../../../CLAUDE.md).
