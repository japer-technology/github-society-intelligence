# Figure 24-6 — Implementation analysis (SOR / FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION)

**Source figure:** [24-6.md](24-6.md) — *A frame between memorizers
and recognizers.*
**Analysis target:** [`FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/)
(plan-of-record for `.forgejo-society/` and
`.forgejo/workflows/forgejo-society.yaml`).

This file inventories the ideas carried by Figure 24-6 and checks each
one against the implementation plan. The aim is to mark, plainly,
what the plan already absorbs and what it does not yet name.

---

## The ideas Figure 24-6 actually carries

1. **A frame has two kinds of neighbours, not one.** Recognisers on
   one side, memorizers on the other.
2. **A recogniser is the opposite of a K-line.** It fires *when* a
   state is present; a K-line *restores* a state.
3. **Recognisers turn the frame on.** They are the input side of
   the bowtie — the route by which the world wakes the frame.
4. **Memorizers record what fills the terminals.** They are the
   output side — the route by which the frame writes back to
   memory.
5. **Each filler has its own recognisers.** Recognisers are not
   only attached to whole frames; they live further down too, on
   slot contents.
6. **The plumbing is symmetric and bidirectional.** The same frame
   is both *recognisable* and *re-usable as memory*, and the
   diagram shows both directions at once.
7. **Recogniser and memorizer are separate kinds of unit.** They
   are drawn as distinct fans, not as facets of one component.

## What the implementation already absorbs

### 1 — Reading and writing are separated phases

The runtime pipeline in
[`03-runtime-pipeline.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/03-runtime-pipeline.md)
runs `perceive` (reading the stimulus and waking nemes) well before
`remember` (committing to memory). The *recogniser side* and the
*memorizer side* are temporally separated even if they are not
named as such.

### 2 — A dedicated writer agency

The `archivist` in
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md)
and
[`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md)
is the only unit permitted to promote material from `state/` to
`memory/`. *Memorizer* as a role — "the agent on the write side of
the frame" — is present at the run level as `agency.integration.archivist`.

### 3 — Frame matching as recognition

The `matches:` block on each frame is a recogniser in everything but
name: a structured detector that fires when the stimulus carries
the right signals, labels, paths, or phrases.

## What the implementation does not yet take into account

### A — No recogniser as a manifest kind

The plan has agencies, critics, and censors. It does not have
*recogniser* as a kind. Recognition is folded into the frame
schema (in `matches:`) and into agency activation (in polynemes),
not represented as units that can be excited, inhibited, listed in
a catalogue, or wrapped in a policy.

### B — No symmetry per frame

The figure draws the *same frame* with both recognisers on the
left and memorizers on the right. The plan's frames carry no
explicit write-side neighbours; an `archivist` is associated with
the whole settlement, not with a particular frame's slots. The
*bowtie around one frame* is not modelled.

### C — No per-filler recognisers

Figure 24-6's right-most fan ("each filler in turn has its own
recognisers") implies recognisers at the slot-content level. The
plan has no per-slot recogniser; recognition stops at the frame
boundary.

### D — Recognisers and writers are not symmetrical

Recognition in the plan is implicit in the matchers; writing is
done by a named agency (`archivist`). The two are different kinds
of object, not two faces of one architecture. The figure's claim
that *they are the same shape pointing in opposite directions* has
no operational counterpart.

### E — No "re-arouse this frame as memory" path

The figure makes the bidirectional move explicit: the frame can be
walked as memory just as it can be matched as input. The plan can
re-activate a K-line and preload its useful context, but it does
not re-walk a frame's terminals; this gap mirrors the one in
[24.5-sor.md](24.5-sor.md).

## Implication for the plan (no changes proposed here)

Figure 24-6 separates two roles — recognising and memorizing — and
argues that they bracket every frame symmetrically. The plan
absorbs the *temporal* separation (perceive then remember) and
names the memorizer side (`archivist`); it does not absorb the
*structural* separation (recogniser as a kind, recogniser per
filler, symmetric pairing per frame).

Any move to add a `recognizer` manifest kind, per-frame
write-side bindings, or per-slot recognisers would change the
manifest schema in
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md),
the frame schema in
[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md),
the memory layout in
[`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md),
and the identity scopes in
`THE-SOCIETY-OF-REPO/02-protocols/01-identity.md`, and so falls
under the "stop and ask" rules in
[`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md) §12 and
[`CLAUDE.md`](../../../../../CLAUDE.md).
