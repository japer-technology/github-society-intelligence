# Figure 24-3 — Implementation analysis (SOR / FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION)

**Source figure:** [24-3.md](24-3.md) — *The nine direction-nemes
around a scene.*
**Analysis target:** [`FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/)
(plan-of-record for `.forgejo-society/` and
`.forgejo/workflows/forgejo-society.yaml`).

This file inventories the ideas carried by Figure 24-3 and checks each
one against the implementation plan. The aim is to mark, plainly,
what the plan already absorbs and what it does not yet name.

---

## The ideas Figure 24-3 actually carries

1. **A small fixed vocabulary covers a large space.** Nine cells
   suffice to describe many different scenes.
2. **Direction is a kind of neme.** The cells are activators, not
   coordinates — *UP-LEFT* is a signal that can wake whatever the
   brain has attached to that region.
3. **The vocabulary is reused across unrelated scenes.** The same
   nine cells parse a tube end-on and a perspective view of a small
   structure.
4. **Coverage by partition.** The cells partition the scene; every
   part is somewhere, and only one place at a time.
5. **A familiar scheme survives unfamiliarity.** Even an unusual
   scene (the inside of a tube) is still readable in terms of *top*,
   *bottom*, *sides*.
6. **Direction-nemes are the same kind of thing as other nemes.**
   They plug into frames the same way as pronomes do in
   [24-2.md](24-2.md), which is what [24-5.md](24-5.md) will exploit.

## What the implementation already absorbs

### 1 — Small fixed vocabularies of activators

[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md)
ships `path-polynemes.yml`, `label-polynemes.yml`, and
`phrase-polynemes.yml` as deliberately small catalogues. The
discipline of *a few well-named activators cover many situations*
is honoured at the polyneme layer.

### 2 — Activators that wake other agencies

A polyneme entry carries `excite:` and `inhibit:` maps that wake
agencies on match. This is the same operational shape as a
direction-neme: a name that, when active, biases what becomes
active next.

### 3 — Reuse across unrelated stimuli

Path-polynemes such as `workflow-file`, `secret-file`, and
`policy-file` are reused across many different issues and PRs. The
*same scheme survives unfamiliarity* property is honoured for the
domains the plan covers (paths, labels, phrases).

---

## What the implementation does not yet take into account

### A — No spatial neme family

The plan has *path*, *label*, and *phrase* polynemes. It has no
*direction* or *region* polynemes, and no spatial activator of any
kind. The repo is treated as a graph of files, not as a place with
zones. Nothing prevents adding such a family, but nothing in the
catalogues today implies one.

### B — No notion of partition

Direction-nemes partition a scene: each part lives in exactly one
cell. Polynemes overlap freely — a single file path can match
`workflow-file` and `security-sensitive` at once, and both will
contribute weight. The plan does not currently carry the *exactly
one* discipline the figure relies on.

### C — Coverage is not enforced

The figure relies on the nine cells being exhaustive: every part of
the scene falls into some cell. The plan has no equivalent
guarantee for any polyneme family. There is no check that every
file in the repo matches at least one path-polyneme, and no
catalogue is described as *covering* its input space.

### D — No analogue of "looking at a region"

A direction-neme can be aroused on its own ("look up-left") so that
whatever is attached there becomes available. The plan's polynemes
fire as a side effect of matching the stimulus; nothing in
[`03-runtime-pipeline.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/03-runtime-pipeline.md)
or
[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md)
lets an agency directly fire a polyneme symbol to *probe* what is
attached to it.

### E — No second-pass abstraction over nemes

The figure prepares ground that [24-4.md](24-4.md) and
[24-5.md](24-5.md) reuse: the same nine cells reappear as wall zones
and as picture-frame terminals. The plan does not currently
identify any of its polyneme families as a reusable substrate for
later frame design; each polyneme catalogue is local to its own
matching surface (path, label, phrase).

## Implication for the plan (no changes proposed here)

Figure 24-3 introduces a kind of activator the plan does not yet
carry: one that partitions a fixed space and is reused as the
substrate for later frames. The plan's polynemes are close in
*role* but unlike in *shape* — they overlap, they do not cover
their input space, and they cannot be probed directly.

Any move to add a direction- or region-neme family, a partition
discipline, or a *probe a neme* operation would extend the activator
schema in
[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md)
and the perception step in
[`03-runtime-pipeline.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/03-runtime-pipeline.md),
and so falls under the "stop and ask" rules in
[`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md) §12 and
[`CLAUDE.md`](../../../../../CLAUDE.md).
