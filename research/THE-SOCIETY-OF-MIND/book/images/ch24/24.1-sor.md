# Figure 24-1 — Implementation analysis (SOR / FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION)

**Source figure:** [24-1.md](24-1.md) — *A frame terminal as an
AND-agent.*
**Analysis target:** [`FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/)
(plan-of-record for `.forgejo-society/` and
`.forgejo/workflows/forgejo-society.yaml`).

This file inventories the ideas carried by Figure 24-1 and checks each
one against the implementation plan. The aim is to mark, plainly,
what the plan already absorbs and what it does not yet name.

---

## The ideas Figure 24-1 actually carries

1. **A frame terminal is a unit, not a field.** It is drawn as its
   own AND-agent with a name, an input wiring, and an output, rather
   than as a passive slot on a record.
2. **Two distinct activations must coincide.** One input carries the
   activation of the surrounding frame; the other carries the
   activation of a particular pronome.
3. **AND, not weighted sum.** The terminal fires only when *both*
   signals are present; partial presence does not partly fire it.
4. **The pronome is a separately addressable thing.** *Origin*,
   *Destination*, *Vehicle* are not just labels on a slot — they are
   signals that other agencies can excite directly.
5. **The K-line attached to the terminal is gated by role.** The
   stored content is aroused only in the right role at the right
   time, never just because the frame is active.
6. **Atomicity.** This is named as *the* atomic operation of
   frame-based memory; everything in the chapter stacks on top of it.

## What the implementation already absorbs

### 1 — Frames as first-class objects

[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md)
defines frames with a schema, a first-ship catalogue
(`question`, `bug`, `feature`, `code-change`, `security-sensitive`,
`self-modification`, `novel`), and named slots. The *frame is active*
side of Figure 24-1's AND is therefore representable.

### 2 — Slots that name what must be filled

The `code-change` frame's required slots — `user_goal`,
`relevant_files`, `proposed_patch`, `tests`, `risks`,
`final_user_response` — are the plan's analogue of pronomes
(*Origin*, *Vehicle*, etc.): named places that a particular signal is
expected to land in.

### 3 — K-lines gated by situation, not by raw presence

K-line reactivation in
[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md)
scores `restore_when` against the current cue (terms, labels, paths,
symbols) before any K-line boost is applied. Some role-sensitivity is
therefore present: a K-line is restored only when the situation
matches, not whenever its symbols appear.

## What the implementation does not yet take into account

### A — No terminal as a named unit

A slot in the plan is a key in the frame's `slots:` map; it has no
manifest, no id, no `activates_on`. There is nothing in
`.forgejo-society/` corresponding to *the terminal for Destination in
the code-change frame*. The figure's central move — making the
terminal itself a unit you can wire to — is absent.

### B — Polynemes excite, they do not AND-gate

`polynemes` in
[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md)
carry `excite:` and `inhibit:` weights. A frame plus a polyneme adds
to an agency's activation; it does not produce a strict conjunction
that fires only when both are present. The AND in *AND-agent* has no
direct representation.

### C — No pronome layer

The plan has signals, labels, paths, and phrases as activators. It
does not name *role-bearing* activators distinct from slot keys. A
pronome is the kind of thing that can be aroused independently of the
frame ("activate the *Destination* pronome") and then conjoined with
whichever frame happens to be active. The plan has no field of that
shape.

### D — K-lines are bound to a class, not to a (frame, slot) pair

K-lines live at `memory/klines/<class>/<date>-<stimulus_id>.yml` and
carry an `activation_snapshot.frame` field. There is no concept of
*this K-line is the content of the Destination terminal of the
code-change frame*. Restoration is over the whole frame, not gated
per terminal.

### E — The atomicity claim is not modelled

Figure 24-1 is presented as the atom of frame-based memory. The plan
has many atoms (a frame match, a polyneme excite, a K-line restore, a
critic verdict) but it does not declare any one of them as the
ground-level operation that the others are built from. The
architectural simplification the figure proposes — *everything in
frame memory reduces to AND-agents on (frame, role) pairs* — is not
claimed anywhere in the implementation documents.

## Implication for the plan (no changes proposed here)

Figure 24-1 names a primitive — the (frame, pronome) AND-agent
holding a K-line — that the implementation plan does not currently
carry. The plan's frames-plus-polynemes-plus-K-lines apparatus
covers the same problem space at a coarser grain, with weighted
excitation in place of conjunction and class-level K-line storage in
place of per-terminal storage.

Any move to close the gap — a `terminal:` schema, a `pronome:`
activator kind, AND-gated activation, or per-terminal K-line
binding — would touch the manifest and activation primitives in
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md)
and
[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md),
and so falls under the "stop and ask" rules in
[`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md) §12 and
[`CLAUDE.md`](../../../../../CLAUDE.md).
