# Figure 24-7 — Implementation analysis (SOR / FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION)

**Source figure:** [24-7.md](24-7.md) — *Agencies sandwiched between
memorizers and recognizers.*
**Analysis target:** [`FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/)
(plan-of-record for `.forgejo-society/` and
`.forgejo/workflows/forgejo-society.yaml`).

This file inventories the ideas carried by Figure 24-7 and checks each
one against the implementation plan. The aim is to mark, plainly,
what the plan already absorbs and what it does not yet name.

---

## The ideas Figure 24-7 actually carries

1. **The recogniser/memorizer pattern generalises from frames to
   agencies.** The same sandwich applies one level up.
2. **An agency reads its lower neighbours through recognisers.**
   What enters the agency from below is a recognised pattern, not
   raw signal.
3. **An agency writes back through memorizers.** What leaves the
   agency toward its lower neighbours is a memorisation, not a
   command.
4. **Frames, K-lines, and intergents live in the middle.** The
   internal machinery of the agency is what the recogniser/memorizer
   layer brackets.
5. **There are lower-level agencies underneath.** Hierarchy is
   assumed; this is the standard floor plan that the rest of the
   book leans on.
6. **Outputs go up at the corners.** Small triangular outputs send
   signals to higher levels; the agency is part of a stack, not a
   leaf.
7. **The same plan is reusable.** The figure is presented as *the*
   standard floor plan — every agency in later chapters can be
   read as an instance of it.

## What the implementation already absorbs

### 1 — Agencies grouped into families, families into a society

Family folders (`agencies/perception/`, `agencies/code/`,
`agencies/integration/`, etc.) in
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md)
already imply a hierarchy: lower work happens inside families,
coarser orchestration happens at the workflow level. The "there
are lower-level agencies underneath" assumption is honoured at the
family/member granularity.

### 2 — Reading and writing at the agency boundary

Each agency manifest in
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md)
declares its inputs (signals it activates on) and its outputs
(handoffs and state writes via the schemas in
[`09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md)).
Both sides of the sandwich exist as concepts at the agency
boundary, even if not under those names.

### 3 — Internal machinery distinct from boundary

Frames, polynemes, and K-lines in
[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md)
do correspond, roughly, to the *frames, K-lines, intergents* in
the middle of Figure 24-7. The plan does keep the middle of the
sandwich distinct from the wiring at its edges.

### 4 — Outputs flow upward

Settlement artefacts and handoffs in
[`03-runtime-pipeline.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/03-runtime-pipeline.md)
and
[`09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md)
flow from individual agencies up to the run-level settlement,
which is exactly the "outputs at the corners go up" arrangement of
the figure.

## What the implementation does not yet take into account

### A — No recogniser/memorizer layer per agency

Each agency in the plan has a prompt body and an authority line; it
does not have a *recogniser layer* and a *memorizer layer* as
declared sub-structures. There is no `recognizers:` or
`memorizers:` block on a manifest, no per-agency catalogue of
either kind, and no enforcement that all reads pass through one
and all writes pass through the other.

### B — Reads are not all routed through recognisers

Agencies in the plan can read state files, repo files, and the
stimulus directly (subject to authority). The figure's discipline
that everything entering the agency *has been recognised first* is
not enforced by schema; it is followed loosely by the perception
pipeline and not at all by downstream phases.

### C — Writes are concentrated in one place, not distributed per agency

The plan routes all `memory/` writes through `archivist`. The
figure's symmetric story would have every agency carry its own
memorizer layer. These are different shapes: a single writing
agency at the run level versus a writer per agency. The plan's
shape is intentional (authority, auditability) but is not the
figure's shape.

### D — No "intergents" or similar mid-layer kind

The figure's middle band names "frames, K-lines, intergents". The
plan has frames and K-lines; it has no intergent or equivalent
mid-layer construct, and no schema entry for one.

### E — The hierarchy is shallow

As in [1.2-sor.md](../ch1/1.2-sor.md) gap B: the plan supports one
level of nesting (family → member). Figure 24-7's "lower-level
agencies" implies an indefinite stack. The plan cannot today
represent an agency that *contains* a lower-level agency that in
turn contains further agencies.

### F — Standard floor plan is not declared

Figure 24-7 presents itself as *the standard floor plan*. The
plan does not declare any single internal shape that all agencies
must instantiate. Some agencies are perceivers, some are critics,
some are integrators; their internal shapes differ. There is no
document saying "every agency has these three layers".

## Implication for the plan (no changes proposed here)

Figure 24-7 is the chapter's most general claim: *every agency in
the book is an instance of this sandwich*. The implementation plan
honours the *outer* facts the figure asserts (agencies have
boundaries, reads flow up, writes flow up, hierarchy exists) but
does not adopt the *internal* shape (every agency wears a
recogniser layer and a memorizer layer over a frame/K-line/intergent
middle).

Any move toward that internal shape — a standard per-agency layer
schema, per-agency recogniser and memorizer catalogues, or an
intergent kind — would change the manifest schema in
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md),
the frame and memory machinery in
[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md)
and
[`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md),
and the identity scopes in
`THE-SOCIETY-OF-REPO/02-protocols/01-identity.md`, and so falls
under the "stop and ask" rules in
[`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md) §12 and
[`CLAUDE.md`](../../../../../CLAUDE.md).
