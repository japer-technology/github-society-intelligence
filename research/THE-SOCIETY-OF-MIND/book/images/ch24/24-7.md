## What the image shows

A wide diagram with a central band split into two labelled boxes: *MEMORIZERS* on the left and *RECOGNIZERS* on the right, with arrows running both ways between them. Two cloud-shaped *LOWER-LEVEL AGENCIES* hang below, connected by upward arrows; small triangular outputs at the corners send signals up toward higher levels.

## What it illustrates

The figure generalises [24-6.md](24-6.md) from a single frame to an entire agency. The same sandwich applies: a layer of recognisers reads what is happening in the lower-level agencies, a layer of memorizers writes back to them, and the agents in between (frames, K-lines, intergents) connect the two. This is the standard floor plan that the rest of the book assumes whenever it draws an agency.
