# Figure 25-5 — Implementation analysis (SOR / FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION)

**Source figure:** [25-5.md](25-5.md) — *Compass-driven frame-array of a
cube.*
**Analysis target:** [`FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/)
(plan-of-record for `.forgejo-society/` and
`.forgejo/workflows/forgejo-society.yaml`).

This file inventories the ideas carried by Figure 25-5 and checks each
one against the implementation plan. The aim is to mark, plainly, what
the plan already absorbs and what it does not yet name.

---

## The ideas Figure 25-5 actually carries

1. **A frame-array can be indexed by a structured signal.** Here the
   index is the eight-point compass; the array is a 3×3 grid.
2. **The same signal that *moves* also *selects*.** A single
   direction-neme both displaces the viewer and switches the active
   frame.
3. **Cancellation is the source of constancy.** The shift in image
   and the shift in selected frame cancel, so the world appears
   stationary.
4. **Motion and perception are coupled at the signal layer.** They
   are not separate subsystems coordinated after the fact.
5. **The array has a geometry.** Adjacency in the grid is meaningful:
   *east* swaps to the neighbour on one side, not to an arbitrary
   sibling.
6. **A central rest position exists.** The 3×3 has a centre; movement
   is described as offset from rest.
7. **The trick is small.** No new agency is needed — just a wiring
   convention between the signal that moves and the signal that
   selects.
8. **Stability is a *consequence*, not a goal.** Nothing in the
   architecture is named *stability*; it falls out of cancellation.

## What the implementation already absorbs

### 1 — Signals carry the work of activation

[`09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md)
defines the signal layer; signals are the way one unit triggers
another. Coupling at the signal layer is the right substrate for
the figure's claim.

### 2 — Polynemes as shared-signal activators

Polynemes in
[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md)
are conceptually identical to the *direction-neme* the figure
describes: a single symbol that activates many units at once. The
direction-neme idea has a name in the plan.

### 3 — Phases as a coarse spatial geometry

The phase chain in
[`03-runtime-pipeline.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/03-runtime-pipeline.md)
is an ordered geometry — one phase has neighbours, you advance by
fixed steps, and you cannot "teleport" past phases. This is the
nearest plan-level analogue to "moving north swaps to the upper
neighbour."

## What the implementation does not yet take into account

### A — No frame-array, so nothing to index

The figure's grid presupposes a frame-array (Figure 25-4). The plan
has no `frame-array` kind (see gap A in
[25.4-sor.md](25.4-sor.md)), so there is nothing for a
direction-neme to point into. The "select a member of an array by
signal" operation has no target.

### B — Signals do not double as selectors

In the plan, a signal triggers an agency, a critic, or a phase
transition. It does not also *select a frame member of a group*. The
figure's central trick — *one signal both moves and selects* — has
no schema slot. There is no `signal.selects_member_of:` field.

### C — No grid geometry on frames

Frames in the plan are a flat list with no neighbour relations. No
schema in
[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md)
expresses "frame *X* is north-of frame *Y*." Adjacency, the figure's
prerequisite, is absent.

### D — No rest-position frame

The 3×3 has a centre — a default view. The plan has no notion of a
"default member" of a frame group, no `default_frame:` on an array,
and no behaviour for returning to rest after disturbance.

### E — Cancellation is not a recognised pattern

No critic, censor, or workflow phase in
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md)
detects, requires, or rewards "the change in input is exactly
cancelled by the change in interpretation." The figure's mechanism
of constancy is not a named pattern in the plan.

### F — Motion is not a first-class stimulus kind

The plan's stimuli are events (issue, PR, message); they do not
carry a "motion vector" — a structured description of *how the world
moved since the last run*. Without such a vector, there is no
direction-neme to fire.

### G — No coupling registry between signals

Even if motion existed as a kind, there is no registry that wires
"motion-signal *east*" to "frame-array-selector *swap-west*". The
figure's cancellation requires *paired wiring*; the plan has no file
where such pairs are declared.

### H — Stability is a goal in the plan, not a consequence

The plan pursues stability through critics, censors, settlement
gates, and budgets — explicit, top-down mechanisms. The figure's
*emergent* stability (it just falls out of paired signals) is the
opposite shape. The plan does not have a place to record "this
stability was not enforced, it emerged from cancellation."

## Implication for the plan (no changes proposed here)

Figure 25-5 is the figure that turns the frame-array of Figure 25-4
into a *mechanism*. It says: pair motion-signals with
selector-signals, and constancy comes for free. The implementation
plan has neither the array, nor the pairing, nor the motion
stimulus, nor a recognised cancellation pattern. The plan currently
achieves stability the hard way — by gating and budgeting.

Any move to close these gaps — a `frame-array` kind, a
`signal.selects_member_of:` field, a `motion` stimulus shape, a
direction-neme catalogue under `polynemes/`, a paired-signal
registry, or a `critic.cancellation` — would touch the frame and
polyneme machinery in
[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md),
the handoff and signal schemas in
[`09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md),
the agency catalogues in
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md),
the runtime pipeline in
[`03-runtime-pipeline.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/03-runtime-pipeline.md),
and the identity scopes in
`THE-SOCIETY-OF-REPO/02-protocols/01-identity.md`, and so falls
under the "stop and ask" rules in
[`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md) §12 and
[`CLAUDE.md`](../../../../../CLAUDE.md).
