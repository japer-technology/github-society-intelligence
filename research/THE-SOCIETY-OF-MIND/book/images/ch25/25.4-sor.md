# Figure 25-4 — Implementation analysis (SOR / FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION)

**Source figure:** [25-4.md](25-4.md) — *A frame-array sharing common
terminals.*
**Analysis target:** [`FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/)
(plan-of-record for `.forgejo-society/` and
`.forgejo/workflows/forgejo-society.yaml`).

This file inventories the ideas carried by Figure 25-4 and checks each
one against the implementation plan. The aim is to mark, plainly, what
the plan already absorbs and what it does not yet name.

---

## The ideas Figure 25-4 actually carries

1. **A frame-array is a family of frames.** Multiple frames are
   grouped as a unit, not addressed individually.
2. **The frames in an array share one set of terminals.** The slots
   are common; the contents persist as the array switches.
3. **Switching frames is the unit of perception change.** The
   operation is not "edit a frame" but "swap which sibling is active."
4. **The shared terminals carry the *world*.** What was learned in
   view 1 stays bound to the slot when view 2 takes over.
5. **Object constancy is a side-effect of shared terminals.** The cube
   feels like one thing because the terminals do not get wiped on
   switch.
6. **The array is itself addressable.** It is the array, not its
   members, that is excited by a higher-level signal.
7. **Members are siblings, not children.** None of the three views
   dominates; they take turns according to the polyneme of motion.

## What the implementation already absorbs

### 1 — Frames as a kind

[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md)
defines `frame` as a first-class manifest kind with a schema
(`schemas/frame.schema.json`). Frames have ids, slots, and required
fields — the right substrate for grouping.

### 2 — Slots persist across instances

When a `bug.frame` instance is filled (`reproduction`, `expected`,
`actual`, `final_user_response`), those slot *values* are written to
`memory/` and survive the run. The mechanism for "the slot keeps its
content" exists, even if not across sibling frames.

### 3 — Polynemes can drive switching

Polynemes in
[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md)
are symbol-like activators that wake many partial meanings. The
machinery for "a single signal triggers a coordinated switch across
many units" is conceptually present.

### 4 — Memory layer for terminal persistence

`memory/` in
[`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md)
is the persistence tier that *could* hold the shared-terminal store.
It is not used that way today, but the substrate is the right shape.

## What the implementation does not yet take into account

### A — No frame-array kind

There is no manifest kind for *a family of frames that share
terminals*. Frames exist as siblings in a flat catalogue (`bug`,
`question`, `feature`, …), and they do not share slot definitions.
The plan cannot today declare:

```yaml
kind: frame-array
name: <array-id>
shared_terminals: [...]
members: [<frame-id>, <frame-id>, ...]
```

This is the central absence the figure points at.

### B — Frames do not share slot identity

Two frames in the current catalogue may both have a `reproduction:`
slot, but those are *different* slots that happen to share a name.
The figure requires the *same* terminal, with the *same* binding,
visible to whichever sibling is active. Schema does not enforce or
even express this.

### C — Switching is not "swap the active member of an array"

`perceive → activate` selects a frame. There is no second mode where
the active frame is swapped for a sibling of the same array while
its terminals retain their content. The closest analogue is starting
a new run, which by construction discards in-run state.

### D — No mid-run reframe

Figure 25-4's switch happens as the viewer moves — *within* the act
of perceiving. The plan's pipeline phases run once each per run
([`03-runtime-pipeline.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/03-runtime-pipeline.md)).
Reframing mid-run is not a supported transition.

### E — No "world dossier" survives reframe

In the figure, what makes constancy possible is that the terminals
*carry the world*: face *E* stays known across views. The plan has
no per-object world dossier (see gap A in
[25.3-sor.md](25.3-sor.md)); without it, even if frame-arrays
existed, they would have no shared content to share.

### F — Higher-level addressing of an array

The figure implies a polyneme that excites *the array*, not its
members. The plan can excite a frame by id; it cannot excite a
group of frames atomically. There is no `array-id` to point at.

### G — Constancy as a critic

Figure 25-4 is, at the algorithmic level, *constancy machinery*. No
critic in
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md)
asks "is the percept changing because the object changed, or only
because the viewpoint changed?" That question is the operational
form of the figure, and it is not posed.

## Implication for the plan (no changes proposed here)

Figure 25-4 is the figure the chapter is built around. The
implementation plan has frames, slots, polynemes, K-lines, and
memory — the *parts* — but does not assemble them into a
frame-array: a named, addressable group whose members share
terminals and take turns.

Any move to close these gaps — a `frame-array` manifest kind,
shared-terminal references between frames, a mid-run reframe
transition, an object dossier in `memory/`, or a `critic.constancy`
— would touch the frame machinery in
[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md),
the state schema in
[`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md),
the runtime pipeline in
[`03-runtime-pipeline.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/03-runtime-pipeline.md),
the agency catalogues in
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md),
and the identity scopes in
`THE-SOCIETY-OF-REPO/02-protocols/01-identity.md`, and so falls
under the "stop and ask" rules in
[`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md) §12 and
[`CLAUDE.md`](../../../../../CLAUDE.md).
