# Figure 25-15 — Implementation analysis (SOR / FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION)

**Source figure:** [25-15.md](25-15.md) — *Epigraph from Allen Newell.*
**Analysis target:** [`FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/)
(plan-of-record for `.forgejo-society/` and
`.forgejo/workflows/forgejo-society.yaml`).

This file inventories the ideas carried by Figure 25-15 and checks
each one against the implementation plan. The aim is to mark, plainly,
what the plan already absorbs and what it does not yet name.

Figure 25-15 is an epigraph. Its content is methodological, not
mechanistic, and the analysis is correspondingly short and honest
about the thin operational surface.

---

## The ideas Figure 25-15 actually carries

1. **Questions arise from a point of view.** The frame chosen
   determines which questions are askable at all.
2. **A viewpoint structures what is problematical.** What counts as a
   problem is downstream of how the situation is framed.
3. **A viewpoint structures what counts as progress.** Answers and
   acceptable-results are framework-relative.
4. **The view does not determine reality.** It selects what is taken
   from reality and how it is organised.
5. **Reality has the final say, eventually.** A view can shape
   enquiry, but the world accepts or rejects it over time.
6. **A view's value is partly *organisational*.** The frame idea did
   its work less by being verifiable than by being at the right level
   of detail for the work.
7. **Level-of-detail is itself a methodological choice.** Choosing
   the right granularity is part of choosing the view.

## What the implementation already absorbs

### 1 — Frames decide which agencies activate

[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md)
makes the chosen frame gate which units run. This is the
operational form of "the view structures what is problematical" —
the frame chooses which questions even get asked downstream.

### 2 — Settlements record what counted as progress

The settlement schema in
[`03-runtime-pipeline.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/03-runtime-pipeline.md)
and the workspace conventions in
[`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md)
encode what counts as a successful run *under the bound frame*.
That is Newell's "what constitutes an answer (or progress)" made
concrete.

### 3 — The plan declares its level-of-detail explicitly

[`00-overview.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/00-overview.md)
and the SOR-to-implementation mapping in
[`11-mapping-sor-to-implementation.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/11-mapping-sor-to-implementation.md)
declare that the plan operates at the granularity of *manifests +
workflows*, not at the granularity of *neurons* or of *prose*. The
level-band is chosen and visible.

### 4 — Reality-rejection is honoured in the compliance posture

`WARNING.md` and the four-document compliance set treat
self-hosted Forgejo on owned hardware as the runtime, and refuse
shortcuts that would bypass that constraint. This is Newell's
"reality gets its own chance to accept or reject" applied at the
infrastructure layer: the plan does not let a convenient view
override what the deployment actually requires.

## What the implementation does not yet take into account

### A — Frame-relativity of *progress* is not surfaced

A run produces a settlement; the settlement does not say "this is
progress *under the `bug` frame*; it would not count as progress
under the `feature` frame." Newell's relativity is real but
unstated in the artefact. There is no `settlement.under_frame:`
field whose presence reminds readers that *progress is conditional*.

### B — No view-comparison phase

The plan binds one frame per run and does not later ask "what would
this run have looked like under a sibling frame?" Newell's
methodological move — *the view shapes the answers* — has no test
inside a run. No `critic.frame-relativity` exists.

### C — The view's *organisational* contribution is uncredited

K-lines and frames are credited for what they *contained*, not for
*how they organised the work*. The plan has no separate record of
"this frame was valuable because it grouped the work usefully, even
where individual claims turned out to be wrong." Differentiation
signal in
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md)
reads from outcomes, not from organisational utility.

### D — No long-arc "reality accepted/rejected" ledger

Newell's "in the long run reality gets its own chance" implies a
ledger of *past views* and *what reality eventually did to them*.
The plan has `memory/` and a decisions archive at
`THE-SOCIETY-OF-REPO/06-memory/decisions/`, but no schema that
links a *view* (a frame or a frame family) to a *long-run
acceptance/rejection signal*. The verdict on a view is not
recorded as such.

### E — Level-of-detail is chosen once, not as a critique target

The plan picks its granularity in
[`00-overview.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/00-overview.md)
and does not re-examine that choice during runs. No critic asks
"is the current frame's level-band too coarse or too fine for this
stimulus?" Newell's last point — choice of granularity *is* part
of the view — has no operational expression.

### F — Epigraph as methodological note has no schema

As with [25.14-sor.md](25.14-sor.md), an epigraph is a
documentation device, not a runtime kind. The plan does not need a
schema for it; recording this absence is enough to prevent later
confusion.

## Implication for the plan (no changes proposed here)

Figure 25-15 is a reminder that the framework matters as a framework
— that *how* a run organises a problem is itself part of the answer.
The implementation plan honours the active-view consequences (frames
gate, settlements record progress) but does not honour the
*relativity* (no view-comparison, no organisational credit, no
long-arc ledger of view-acceptance).

Any move to close these gaps — a `settlement.under_frame:` field, a
`critic.frame-relativity`, a `view-ledger` under `memory/`, or a
per-run level-of-detail check — would touch the runtime pipeline in
[`03-runtime-pipeline.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/03-runtime-pipeline.md),
the agency catalogues in
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md),
the frame and K-line machinery in
[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md),
the state and memory machinery in
[`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md),
and the identity scopes in
`THE-SOCIETY-OF-REPO/02-protocols/01-identity.md`, and so falls
under the "stop and ask" rules in
[`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md) §12 and
[`CLAUDE.md`](../../../../../CLAUDE.md).
