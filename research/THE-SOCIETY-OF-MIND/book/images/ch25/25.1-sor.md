# Figure 25-1 — Implementation analysis (SOR / FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION)

**Source figure:** [25-1.md](25-1.md) — *Candlestick/faces and Necker
cube.*
**Analysis target:** [`FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/)
(plan-of-record for `.forgejo-society/` and
`.forgejo/workflows/forgejo-society.yaml`).

This file inventories the ideas carried by Figure 25-1 and checks each
one against the implementation plan. The aim is to mark, plainly, what
the plan already absorbs and what it does not yet name.

---

## The ideas Figure 25-1 actually carries

1. **The same input admits more than one legitimate reading.** The
   silhouette is *both* a candlestick *and* two faces; the wireframe is
   *both* viewed-from-above *and* viewed-from-below.
2. **Perception locks in one reading at a time.** The alternatives are
   real, but the agency holding the percept holds exactly one.
3. **Switching, not blending.** Movement between readings is a
   reassignment, not a superposition.
4. **Locking-in is per level.** At each level of analysis, every part
   is assigned to exactly one whole; the constraint is structural, not
   stylistic.
5. **A frame wins the assignment.** The choice of reading is the
   choice of which frame currently owns the features.
6. **Stability is the dominant feel.** Even though the figure flips,
   each held interpretation feels firm. Ambivalence is rare; commitment
   is the default.
7. **Ambiguity is the diagnostic, not the norm.** These figures are
   interesting precisely because most real scenes do not flip — when
   they do, the lock-in machinery is exposed.

## What the implementation already absorbs

### 1 — Frames as named, mutually-exclusive interpretations

[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md)
ships a small first-ship frame catalogue (`question`, `bug`, `feature`,
`code-change`, `security-sensitive`, `self-modification`, `novel`).
These are precisely the kind of *competing readings* Figure 25-1 is
about: a stimulus is a bug-report *or* a question, not both at the
same step.

### 2 — A single frame is selected per run

The `perceive → activate` phases in
[`03-runtime-pipeline.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/03-runtime-pipeline.md)
end with a chosen frame attached to the workspace. The pipeline does
not carry two live frames forward in parallel. This matches the
figure's lock-in.

### 3 — Reassignment, not blending

When a later phase decides the chosen frame was wrong (for example,
the `novel.frame` analogy pass overrides an early guess), the
workspace switches frames; it does not average them. The mechanism in
[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md)
is replacement, which is the Necker-cube discipline.

### 4 — Authority of the chosen frame

Once a frame is bound, it constrains which agencies activate, which
critics matter, and which censors fire. That is the operational form
of "the winning frame owns the features."

## What the implementation does not yet take into account

### A — No record of the unchosen reading

When `perceive` picks `bug` over `question`, nothing in
[`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md)
preserves the alternative as a *live candidate*. The figure's central
point — that the other reading remains structurally available and can
take over at any moment — has no schema slot. The plan keeps the
winner and discards the runner-up.

### B — No flip operator

There is no phase, agency, or critic whose job is "reconsider whether
the other frame was right." The figure's flip is voluntary and cheap;
the plan's frame change requires a new run or a `self-modification`
escalation. A `critic.frame-flip` that watches for evidence the
runner-up frame now fits better is not in the catalogue.

### C — Lock-in is not enforced as an invariant

The plan *behaves as if* one frame is active at a time, but no
schema forbids two `frame:` bindings on the workspace state. Figure
25-1's "exactly one whole per part, at every level" would, if taken
seriously, be a validator: `state.frame` is a single value, and any
attempt to write two is a failure. That validator is implicit, not
explicit.

### D — No "level" axis on frames

Figure 25-1 is careful that lock-in happens *at each level*. The
plan's frames live at one level only — the run-level interpretation
of the stimulus. There is no `frame.level:` field, no per-agency
local frame, and no notion that a perceiver and an integrator might
hold different-level frames simultaneously without contradiction.

### E — Ambiguity is not first-class evidence

In the figure, the *fact* that a stimulus flips is informative — it
tells you the input is genuinely under-determined. The plan treats
low-confidence frame selection as a `novel.frame` fallback rather
than as a recorded property of the stimulus. There is no
`stimulus.ambiguity_score`, and `archivist` does not store "this
stimulus admitted N readings with confidences X, Y, Z."

### F — No catalogue of known bistable stimuli

Vase/faces and the Necker cube are *named* ambiguous figures with
known alternatives. The plan has no analogous catalogue of stimuli
known to flip — for example, a list of phrasings that have
historically resolved to two different frames in past runs — even
though `memory/` could in principle hold one.

## Implication for the plan (no changes proposed here)

Figure 25-1 makes a narrow but exacting claim: a perceiver commits to
one reading at each level, even when several are licit. The
implementation plan honours that commitment at the run level. What it
does not yet do is keep the unchosen reading alive, mark the stimulus
as bistable, or allow a later phase to flip the frame on evidence.

Any move to close these gaps — a `state.frame_candidates` list, a
`critic.frame-flip`, a `frame.level:` field, an
`stimulus.ambiguity_score`, or a `memory/bistable/` catalogue — would
touch the frame machinery in
[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md),
the state schema in
[`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md),
and the handoff schemas in
[`09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md),
and so falls under the "stop and ask" rules in
[`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md) §12 and
[`CLAUDE.md`](../../../../../CLAUDE.md).
