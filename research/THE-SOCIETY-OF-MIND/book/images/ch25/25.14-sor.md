# Figure 25-14 — Implementation analysis (SOR / FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION)

**Source figure:** [25-14.md](25-14.md) — *Epigraph from William
Hogarth.*
**Analysis target:** [`FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/)
(plan-of-record for `.forgejo-society/` and
`.forgejo/workflows/forgejo-society.yaml`).

This file inventories the ideas carried by Figure 25-14 and checks
each one against the implementation plan. The aim is to mark, plainly,
what the plan already absorbs and what it does not yet name.

Figure 25-14 is an epigraph, not a diagram. It carries claims, not
mechanisms; the analysis is correspondingly short.

---

## The ideas Figure 25-14 actually carries

1. **The fronting face monopolises attention.** What is in view tends
   to crowd out what is not.
2. **The unseen sides are typically *unthought of*.** Absence of
   visible cue is treated as absence of feature.
3. **A small motion is enough to *confound* the first idea.** Without
   prior preparation, the next view erases the previous one.
4. **The remedy is connection between the two ideas.** Constancy is
   not an automatic gift; it requires that the views be linked.
5. **The connecting is a learned skill.** Hogarth's lament is that
   most observers have not done the work.
6. **Imagination of unseen sides is the missing competence.** The
   skill is to hold what is not on the retina.

---

## What the implementation already absorbs

### 1 — A run attends to a single stimulus at a time

`perceive` in
[`03-runtime-pipeline.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/03-runtime-pipeline.md)
binds the run to the stimulus that triggered it. The plan does
naturally exhibit the *fronting* behaviour the epigraph describes:
what triggers the run is what gets considered.

### 2 — Memory tier as the substrate for connection

`memory/` in
[`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md)
is where past runs leave their traces, and K-lines in
[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md)
are the explicit record of which units worked together previously.
The substrate for *connection between views* exists.

### 3 — `kline-retriever` as a learned-connection unit

The presence of an agency dedicated to retrieving past assemblies
([`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md))
is the operational analogue of Hogarth's "connexion of the two
ideas" — recall, on entry, of what was learned in a related earlier
situation.

---

## What the implementation does not yet take into account

### A — No imagination of unseen sides

The epigraph's central skill is imagining the back of the object.
The plan has no agency in the perception family whose job is
"propose what is plausibly true about the stimulus *that is not
literally stated in it*." `patch-imaginer` is a code-side imaginer,
not a perception-side one. A `agency.perception.unseen-sides`
analogue is absent.

### B — Absence of cue is treated as absence of feature

If the stimulus does not mention `.forgejo/workflows/**`, no
perceiver wonders whether it nonetheless *implicates* that path.
The plan does not infer hidden involvement; it reads what is in
front of it. This is the epigraph's "left unthought of at that
time" diagnosed in the implementation.

### C — Confounding-by-small-motion is unguarded

The plan has no critic that detects "this run's perception is
unstable under a small rewording of the stimulus." A
`critic.perceptual-fragility` that probes whether the chosen frame
survives minor paraphrase does not exist.

### D — The "connecting" skill is not credited

K-lines exist; their *use* in perception is funnelled through
`kline-retriever`. There is no record that this retrieval *was the
connection* that made the current percept coherent with past ones.
The skill is exercised but not labelled, so it cannot be improved
deliberately.

### E — No training tier for the skill

Hogarth treats the skill as learned. The plan has the
`self-modification` frame for editing agencies, but no routine
training loop that says "perceivers that failed to connect a new
view to a known view get adjusted." Differentiation exists
([`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md)),
but it is not driven by perceptual-connection metrics.

### F — Epigraph-as-such has no representation

The plan has no notion of *quoted authority* — text from outside the
project taken as a position to honour. This is not a defect; it is
the epigraph form being a documentation device, not a runtime kind.
Worth recording so the absence is not later mistaken for an
oversight.

## Implication for the plan (no changes proposed here)

Figure 25-14 is a complaint, not an architecture. Its operational
content is thin by design: it names a skill (imagining unseen sides,
connecting views) that the plan has only in partial form. The plan
has the substrate (memory, K-lines, `kline-retriever`,
`self-modification`) but not the dedicated perceiver, the
fragility-probing critic, or the training loop.

Any move to close these gaps — an `agency.perception.unseen-sides`,
a `critic.perceptual-fragility`, a perception-side imaginer, or a
training tier that uses connection-failures as differentiation
signal — would touch the agency catalogues in
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md),
the frame and K-line machinery in
[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md),
the state and memory machinery in
[`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md),
the runtime pipeline in
[`03-runtime-pipeline.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/03-runtime-pipeline.md),
and the identity scopes in
`THE-SOCIETY-OF-REPO/02-protocols/01-identity.md`, and so falls
under the "stop and ask" rules in
[`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md) §12 and
[`CLAUDE.md`](../../../../../CLAUDE.md).
