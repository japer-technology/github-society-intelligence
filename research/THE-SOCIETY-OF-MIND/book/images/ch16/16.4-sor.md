# Figure 16-4 — Implementation analysis (SOR / FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION)

**Source figure:** [16-4.md](16-4.md) — *Proto-specialists sharing
common organs.*
**Analysis target:** [`FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/)
(plan-of-record for `.forgejo-society/` and
`.forgejo/workflows/forgejo-society.yaml`).

This file inventories the ideas Figure 16-4 carries and checks each
one against the implementation plan.

---

## The ideas Figure 16-4 actually carries

1. **One body, many drivers.** Many proto-specialists connect to one
   shared set of eyes, hands, and feet.
2. **A bus, not a switch.** The figure draws lines from each
   specialist converging on shared organs, implying a shared channel
   rather than a hard-wired selector.
3. **Sharing is the economy.** The motivation is cost: duplicated
   organs are expensive; shared organs are cheap.
4. **Sharing creates contention.** Many drivers + one steering wheel
   = an arbitration problem. The figure names it without solving it.
5. **Turn-taking is implied.** Because only one act-output makes
   sense at a time, specialists must take turns on the shared
   effectors.
6. **Sensors are also shared.** Perception is a single intake the
   specialists subscribe to, not many private feeds.
7. **Motivational identity persists across organ-sharing.** Even
   though *Thirst* now shares eyes with *Hunger*, it is still
   recognisably *Thirst*. Identity survives resource pooling.

## What the implementation already absorbs

### One runner, one repo, one tree

[`00-overview.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/00-overview.md)
and [`01-target-layout.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/01-target-layout.md)
describe a single workflow, a single runner, and a single
`.forgejo-society/` tree shared by all agencies. The "one body"
premise of Figure 16-4 is the plan's starting point.

### Shared perception bus

The polyneme system in [`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md)
gives a single stream of stimulus signals (paths, labels, phrases)
that any agency may subscribe to via `excite:`. This is the shared
*eyes* of Figure 16-4.

### Shared effectors

Writing happens through one mechanism: `act` produces commits on
`society/<stimulus_id>/candidate-<n>` branches
([`02-workflow-design.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/02-workflow-design.md),
[`11-mapping-sor-to-implementation.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/11-mapping-sor-to-implementation.md)).
Every agency that needs to "do" something uses the same effector
path. This is the shared *hands*.

### Contention is acknowledged at the run level

[`02-workflow-design.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/02-workflow-design.md)
sets a `concurrency:` group per stimulus, and
[`00-overview.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/00-overview.md)
states "one concurrency boundary per stimulus key." Two runs for
the same stimulus do not fight over the shared body — they queue.

## What the implementation does not yet take into account

### A — Contention *within* a single run

Figure 16-4's contention problem is "many specialists, same organ,
same moment." The plan's concurrency control resolves contention
*across runs* (different stimuli) but not *within a run*. Inside one
run, several agencies can all write proposals via the assembly
family, but there is no explicit arbitration step that says "Hunger
beat Thirst at this clock tick" — there is only `settle` choosing
among complete settlements, which is a later stage. The
moment-to-moment "who gets the hand right now" question is absent.

### B — No notion of an effector budget

The figure presupposes that the body's effectors are finite — only
one mouth, one pair of hands. The plan budgets *each agency*
(`budget.max_tool_calls`, `budget.max_wall_clock_s`) but does not
budget the shared organs themselves. There is no
"effector.commit.budget_per_run" or "effector.network.budget_per_run"
at the organ level that all agencies must draw from.

### C — Perception bus has no priority

Many polynemes can fire from the same stimulus, and many agencies
may be excited at once. There is no "the most strongly excited
specialist sees first" mechanism in
[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md);
weights influence selection but do not gate access to the perception
bus itself.

### D — Sensors are not modelled as organs

In the figure, *eyes* is a thing — a named shared resource with its
own line in the diagram. In the plan, perception is a *process*
(the `perceive` phase), not a resource. There is no
`organs/perception.yml` or `organs/effectors.yml` registry that
agencies declare a dependency on. Consequently, the schema cannot
express "this agency needs eyes," only "this agency is excited by
these signals."

### E — Identity-under-sharing is implicit

The figure's seventh idea — *Thirst* remains *Thirst* even after it
gives up its private hands — has no documented analogue. The plan
assumes agency identity is stable but does not say *what makes it
stable when everything around it is shared*. The identity protocol
([`THE-SOCIETY-OF-REPO/02-protocols/01-identity.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-INTRODUCTION/THE-SOCIETY-OF-REPO/02-protocols/01-identity.md))
gives ids but not motivational continuity.

## Implication for the plan (no changes proposed here)

Figure 16-4 names the price of efficiency: arbitration. The plan
pays that price across runs (via concurrency groups) but has not yet
said how it pays it within a single run. Adding a within-run
arbitration step, an organ registry with per-organ budgets, or a
priority field on the perception bus would touch the manifest schema
in [`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md),
the runtime pipeline in [`03-runtime-pipeline.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/03-runtime-pipeline.md),
and the activation protocol in [`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md),
and so falls under the "stop and ask" rules in
[`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md) §12 and
[`CLAUDE.md`](../../../../../CLAUDE.md).
