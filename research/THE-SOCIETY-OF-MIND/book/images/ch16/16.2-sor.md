# Figure 16-2 — Implementation analysis (SOR / FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION)

**Source figure:** [16-2.md](16-2.md) — *An animal as an assembly of
proto-specialists.*
**Analysis target:** [`FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/)
(plan-of-record for `.forgejo-society/` and
`.forgejo/workflows/forgejo-society.yaml`).

This file inventories the ideas Figure 16-2 carries and checks each
one against the implementation plan.

---

## The ideas Figure 16-2 actually carries

1. **Needs as the unit of decomposition.** The animal is partitioned
   along *Thirst*, *Hunger*, *Warmth*, *Safety*, *Sleep*,
   *Companionship* — not along organs or skills.
2. **One proto-specialist per need.** Each box is a closed mini-mind
   that owns the entire chain from drive to action.
3. **No shared organs (yet).** Each specialist has its own sensors and
   effectors drawn beside it; nothing is reused.
4. **Side-by-side, not stacked.** The diagram is flat: no hierarchy,
   no parent, no coordinator. Specialists exist in parallel.
5. **The naïve baseline.** The figure is offered as a *first* design,
   the one whose costs (duplication of heads, hands, feet) motivate
   the next two figures.
6. **Drives are first-class.** A *need* is not a label on a task; it
   is the identity of a whole agent.
7. **No goal arbitration.** Because needs do not yet share organs,
   the figure does not have to say how conflicts are resolved.

## What the implementation already absorbs

### Specialists exist and are narrow

[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md)
ships small, pointed agencies (`patch-imaginer`, `diff-skeptic`,
`kline-retriever`, `conscious-presenter`). The "one box per
function" shape of Figure 16-2 is honoured by the catalogue.

### Parallel, side-by-side activation

`02-workflow-design.md` and `06-frames-polynemes-klines.md` describe
multiple agencies activating from the same stimulus, in parallel,
without any of them being a parent to the others. The flatness of
Figure 16-2 is the plan's default shape too.

## What the implementation does not yet take into account

### A — Decomposition is by *family*, not by *need*

The plan's agency families are *perception*, *memory*, *code*,
*safety*, *identity*, *integration*, *assembly*, *meta-admin* — they
are organised by **function**, not by **drive**. Figure 16-2's
organising axis (one agent per need) has no representative in
`agencies/`. There is no `agency.drive.*` scope, no manifest field
`serves_drive:`, and no run-level concept of "which need does this
stimulus answer."

### B — No drive registry

The repo has no enumerated set of long-running needs the society
*is* trying to satisfy. `APPEND_SYSTEM.md` is described as
"character law / drives / boundaries" in
[`01-target-layout.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/01-target-layout.md)
and [`04-folder-spec.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/04-folder-spec.md),
but no schema names the individual drives, gives them ids, or lets a
critic ask "is this stimulus a Hunger or a Safety?"

### C — The naïve duplication cost is not stated

Figure 16-2 exists to *be rejected*. The plan presents only the
already-shared design (one runner, one `.forgejo-society/`, one
memory tree) and never spells out the alternative it implicitly
rules out — fully duplicated, per-drive stacks. The reasoning that
makes shared organs a choice rather than an assumption is missing
from the documentation set.

### D — Drives have no activation pathway

Polynemes activate agencies by *path*, *label*, *phrase*, and
*frame*. There is no `drive-polynemes.yml`. A stimulus cannot say
"this excites the Safety drive, which then excites whatever
specialists serve Safety," because neither side of that wiring
exists.

### E — No drive-level memory partition

[`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md)
partitions memory by class and by stimulus, not by drive. There is
no place to record "what Safety has learned" as distinct from "what
Code-quality has learned."

## Implication for the plan (no changes proposed here)

Figure 16-2 names a design axis the implementation plan does not
currently expose: *drive*. Any move to expose it — a `drives/`
registry, a `drive-polynemes.yml`, a `serves_drive:` manifest field,
or a per-drive memory partition — would extend the identifier scopes
in `THE-SOCIETY-OF-REPO/02-protocols/01-identity.md`, the manifest
schema in [`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md),
and the memory layout in [`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md),
and so falls under the "stop and ask" rules in
[`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md) §12 and
[`CLAUDE.md`](../../../../../CLAUDE.md).
