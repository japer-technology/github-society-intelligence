# Figure 16-13 — Implementation analysis (SOR / FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION)

**Source figure:** [16-13.md](16-13.md) — *The adult emotion
landscape.*
**Analysis target:** [`FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/)
(plan-of-record for `.forgejo-society/` and
`.forgejo/workflows/forgejo-society.yaml`).

This file inventories the ideas Figure 16-13 carries and checks each
one against the implementation plan.

---

## The ideas Figure 16-13 actually carries

1. **The original cross-exclusion set decays with development.** The
   tidy infant cluster of [16-12.md](16-12.md) becomes a scattered
   field.
2. **Labels no longer map cleanly to processes.** *Anger*, *Fear*,
   *Love*, *Jealousy*, *Greed*, *Curiosity*, *Boredom* overlap and
   blur.
3. **States combine and overwrite each other.** Development
   rearranges the original proto-specialists.
4. **Social pressure is one of the rewriting forces.** Many adult
   "emotions" are shaped by how they must be performed for others.
5. **Folk vocabulary lags behind mechanism.** Common-sense
   psychology cannot even agree which emotions exist.
6. **No central registry.** The adult landscape has no canonical
   list — that is part of its definition.
7. **Partial similarity replaces clean exclusion.** Faint lines, not
   inhibition arrows.
8. **Identity over time is the accumulated rewriting.** The adult
   is what the infant became under repeated overwriting.

## What the implementation already absorbs

### `self-modification` as a first-class frame

[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md)
defines the `self-modification` frame, and
`agency.meta-admin.differentiation-broker` (cited in
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md))
exists precisely so the agency set can be revised over time. Figure
16-13's "the original set decays and is rewritten" idea has a
mechanism, even if it is applied to agencies rather than emotions.

### Stable ids across rewrites

The identity protocol (`THE-SOCIETY-OF-REPO/02-protocols/01-identity.md`,
ids of form `{scope}.{kind}.{name}`) lets an agency persist under
the same id across body rewrites, supporting idea 8 — continuity of
identity across structural change — at the agency scale.

### History as the witness

Git history records every rewrite of every agency, frame, and
policy. The "accumulated rewriting" of idea 8 is, for the
implementation, literally `git log`.

### Multiple overlapping frames

`code-change`, `security-sensitive`, `self-modification`, `novel`
can co-fire on a stimulus. Frames already exhibit the *overlap*
idea 7 names — not as inhibition but as multiple partial matches.

## What the implementation does not yet take into account

### A — No long-arc developmental record

Figure 16-13 is a snapshot of *a process that has run for years*.
The plan records each settlement and each commit, but has no
higher-order artefact summarising "how the agency catalogue has
drifted over the lifetime of this society." There is no
`memory/development/<period>.md` or similar long-arc record.

### B — No model of overwriting

`agency.meta-admin.differentiation-broker` can *split* and
*introduce* agencies, but the plan does not document the inverse:
states that have been *overwritten*, *merged*, or *suppressed*.
Figure 16-13's "the infant set has been overwritten" needs an
overwrite log; nothing of the kind exists.

### C — Social shaping is not modelled

Idea 4 — that performance for others reshapes internal states — has
no operational analogue. Federation between societies
([`13-inter-repo-communication.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/13-inter-repo-communication.md))
exists, but there is no mechanism by which *being observed* changes
an agency's prompt or weights. The plan's agencies are not
self-modified by audience.

### D — Vocabulary drift is not tracked

Figure 16-13 implies the labels themselves drift. The plan's labels
(in `label-polynemes.yml`) are stable strings. There is no record
of "this label used to mean X and now means Y." The glossary in
`GLOSSARY.md` is a current snapshot, not a history.

### E — Partial-similarity wiring is absent

Frames overlap, but they do not declare *similarity to each other*.
There is no `similar_to: [<frame-id>: <weight>]` field on frames or
agencies. Figure 16-13's faint connecting lines have no analogue.

### F — No design acknowledgement that the catalogue is provisional

The plan presents the first-ship catalogues
([`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md),
[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md))
as starting points but does not write down — as Figure 16-13 would
demand — that the *adult* form of the society will look nothing
like the infant form, and that this is normal rather than failure.

### G — No emotion model at all

Most directly: the plan has no representation of emotion. The
figure's whole subject — that adult emotion is a *historical
residue* — cannot be evaluated against an implementation that does
not yet model emotion in the first place
(cf. [16.12-sor.md](16.12-sor.md) gap A).

## Implication for the plan (no changes proposed here)

Figure 16-13 is the chapter's final move: even the tidy mechanisms
it has just introduced get rewritten by life. The implementation
plan supports the *capacity* for that rewriting — frames,
differentiation broker, git history — but does not yet support its
*record*: long-arc development logs, overwrite traces, vocabulary
drift, partial-similarity wiring, and a stated expectation that the
first-ship catalogues will not survive contact with use. The plan
also has no emotion model against which to evaluate the figure
directly. Any of these would touch the manifest schema in
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md),
the activation protocol in [`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md),
the memory layout in [`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md),
the mapping in [`11-mapping-sor-to-implementation.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/11-mapping-sor-to-implementation.md),
and likely the protocols under
`THE-SOCIETY-OF-REPO/02-protocols/`, and so falls under the "stop
and ask" rules in [`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md) §12 and
[`CLAUDE.md`](../../../../../CLAUDE.md).
