# Figure 3-3 — Implementation analysis (SOR / FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION)

**Source figure:** [3-3.md](3-3.md) — *Ambiguous cube.*
**Analysis target:** [`FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/)
(plan-of-record for `.forgejo-society/` and
`.forgejo/workflows/forgejo-society.yaml`).

This file inventories the ideas carried by Figure 3-3 and checks each
one against the implementation plan. The aim is to mark, plainly, what
the plan already absorbs and what it does not yet name.

---

## The ideas Figure 3-3 actually carries

Pulled from the figure caption and Minsky's surrounding text in §3.4
*Heterarchies*:

1. **One stimulus admits multiple equally good interpretations.** The
   same retinal image can be read as cube-plus-added-cube or as
   cube-minus-removed-cube.
2. **Both readings are coherent in isolation.** Neither can be
   dismissed by a local check; each is internally consistent.
3. **The system must commit to one.** Perception does not return a
   set of hypotheses; it returns a percept. Commitment is part of
   the work.
4. **The commitment requires sideways communication.** No single
   layer has the information to decide. Low-level edge agencies and
   high-level shape agencies must talk to each other directly, not
   only through a parent.
5. **Heterarchy, not hierarchy, is the right shape.** Strict
   top-down trees cannot represent this lateral talk; agencies must
   be able to address peers.
6. **The chosen reading is provisional.** It can flip later if a
   different agency wins the argument. Perceptual commitment is not
   final commitment.
7. **The same image can therefore mean different things in different
   contexts.** Identical inputs do not guarantee identical outputs.

These seven items are what the rest of this file tests against the
implementation plan.

## What the implementation already absorbs

### 1 — A blackboard for shared interpretation

`blackboard.md` in the deliberation phase
([`03-runtime-pipeline.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/03-runtime-pipeline.md))
is the plan's broadcast surface: any agency can read what any other
agency has written. This is the substrate on which sideways
communication *could* happen, even if the protocol on top is not
heterarchical in the strict sense.

### 2 — Multiple perception agencies on the same stimulus

[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md)
ships `intake-bee`, `issue-kind-detector`, `ambiguity-detector`, and
`urgency-detector` as parallel perception agencies. Figure 3-3's
premise — that perception is a chorus rather than a soloist — is
honoured at the agency-catalogue level. `ambiguity-detector` in
particular is the closest the plan comes to naming the figure's
"two equally good readings" condition.

### 3 — Frames as the layer where commitment happens

Frame selection in `frames.ts`
([`03-runtime-pipeline.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/03-runtime-pipeline.md),
[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md))
is exactly the moment the plan commits to *one* reading of the
stimulus. Picking `bug.frame.yml` over `feature.frame.yml` is the
operational analogue of choosing cube-plus-cube over cube-minus-cube.

### 4 — A `novel` frame for the case where no reading dominates

`novel.frame.yml`
([`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md))
fires when no other frame matches strongly, and makes an analogy
pass mandatory. This is the plan's acknowledgement that not every
stimulus has an obvious reading.

### 5 — Critics that attack the chosen interpretation

`critics/*.md` and `agency.code.diff-skeptic` are licensed to
challenge the current settled reading. The plan's commitment to a
frame is not unconditional; an objection of sufficient strength
forces re-deliberation in the `criticize` tail.

## What the implementation does not yet take into account

### A — No parallel hypotheses carried through the pipeline

The plan picks one frame and proceeds. There is no schema for
"the workspace currently holds two competing interpretations of the
stimulus, both alive, both being deliberated against." The
pipeline mapping in
[`03-runtime-pipeline.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/03-runtime-pipeline.md)
moves from `activate` to `deliberate` with a single selected frame.
Minsky's "the system holds both readings until commitment" is
collapsed into "the system commits at frame-selection time."

### B — No lateral protocol between agencies

The signal/handoff schemas in
[`09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md)
describe broadcast signals and directed handoffs, but there is no
peer-to-peer protocol — no way for `agency.perception.edge-finder`
(if it existed) to talk *directly* to `agency.perception.shape-fitter`
without going through the blackboard. The blackboard is the only
lateral surface, and it is bulletin-board-shaped, not
conversation-shaped.

### C — Heterarchy is not named

The word *heterarchy* appears nowhere in
`FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/`. The plan's hierarchies are
strict: families contain members, frames contain slots, families
nest inside the workflow phases. There is no documented mechanism
for an agency to address another agency *as a peer* rather than as
a child, parent, or anonymous reader of the blackboard.

### D — No commitment-with-flip-back protocol

Figure 3-3's commitment is provisional: a reader can flip from
cube-plus-cube to cube-minus-cube mid-perception. The plan's frame
selection happens once per run; there is no schema for "we settled
on frame X at step 3, but enough new objections accumulated by step
7 that we should re-select." Re-selection requires a new run, not a
mid-run flip.

### E — Identical inputs produce identical outputs by design

The runtime is deterministic where it can be: the same stimulus,
the same agencies, the same memory state yield the same trace. This
is a virtue for auditability, but it is the inverse of Figure 3-3's
seventh idea — that the same image can mean different things in
different contexts. Context-dependence is currently carried only by
frames and K-lines; the plan does not name *interpretive
context* as a separate axis from those.

### F — `ambiguity-detector` flags, but no protocol picks up the flag

`agency.perception.ambiguity-detector` exists to *mark* missing
slots in the chosen frame
([`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md)).
It does not branch the pipeline into parallel hypotheses. The flag
is consumed by the same `deliberate → criticize → censor → settle`
chain that handles the unambiguous case. The figure's central act —
choosing between two equally good readings — has no dedicated
phase.

### G — No record of *which* reading was chosen, and what the alternative was

A settlement records the outcome; it does not record "we chose
reading A over reading B, and here is why." Without that record,
later K-line retrieval cannot say "last time we faced this
ambiguity, we picked A and it worked" — the kind of credit
assignment Figure 3-3's commitment-with-revision picture would
otherwise enable.

## Implication for the plan (no changes proposed here)

Figure 3-3 is the figure that asks the implementation to support
*more than one live reading at a time* and to give agencies a way to
argue with their peers. The current plan handles the *first* part
(ambiguity is detected) and the *last* part (commitment happens at
frame selection), but skips the middle: it does not carry parallel
hypotheses through the pipeline, and it does not give agencies a
peer-to-peer protocol.

The gaps above are recorded here as analysis, not as a change
request. Any move to close them — a `hypotheses:` field on the
workspace, a peer-to-peer message kind in
[`09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md),
a documented heterarchy protocol, a mid-run frame-reselection step in
[`02-workflow-design.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/02-workflow-design.md) —
would touch the manifest schema in
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md),
the activation protocol in
[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md),
and the identity scopes in
`THE-SOCIETY-OF-REPO/02-protocols/01-identity.md`, and so falls
under the "stop and ask" rules in
[`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md) §12 and
[`CLAUDE.md`](../../../../../CLAUDE.md).
