# Figure 3-2 — Implementation analysis (SOR / FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION)

**Source figure:** [3-2.md](3-2.md) — *Tower and ruins.*
**Analysis target:** [`FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/)
(plan-of-record for `.forgejo-society/` and
`.forgejo/workflows/forgejo-society.yaml`).

This file inventories the ideas carried by Figure 3-2 and checks each
one against the implementation plan. The aim is to mark, plainly, what
the plan already absorbs and what it does not yet name.

---

## The ideas Figure 3-2 actually carries

Pulled from the figure caption and Minsky's surrounding text in §3.1
*Conflict*:

1. **Construction is patient, sequential, and expensive.** A tower
   is the product of many ordered, careful steps.
2. **Destruction is fast, single-step, and cheap.** One swat undoes
   all of it.
3. **The reward profiles are asymmetric.** A wrecker gets an
   immediate, vivid payoff; a builder gets a delayed, abstract one.
4. **Asymmetry produces persistent rivalry.** Because the cheap action
   is satisfying, BUILDER and WRECKER do not eventually merge; they
   keep competing for the same hands.
5. **Any society that contains construction must also contain its
   negation.** The figure is not just about toys; it generalises to
   any system whose outputs can be undone.
6. **The arbiter must price the asymmetry in.** A naive "loudest
   wins" rule would always pick the wrecker, because the wrecker's
   signal is cleaner and more immediate.

These six items are what the rest of this file tests against the
implementation plan.

## What the implementation already absorbs

### 1 — Friction proportional to reversibility

The danger-zone catalogue in
[`07-policies-and-safety.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/07-policies-and-safety.md)
adds censors, suppressors, and human-confirmation requirements
specifically to the regions of the repo whose damage is hard to
reverse: `workflow_mutation`, `soul_mutation`, `governance_mutation`,
`agency_mutation`, `secret_handling`, `memory_mutation`. The plan
already accepts Figure 3-2's premise — destruction is cheaper than
construction — and answers it by attaching extra steps to the cheap
side.

### 2 — A required revert path for every change

`agency.code.revert-path-finder` is a *required slot* in the
`code-change` frame
([`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md),
[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md)).
No candidate action settles without a known way to undo it. This is
the operational answer to "destruction is one swat": every build
ships with its own pre-arranged un-build.

### 3 — Candidate-future branches as a default

The branch-and-settle default in
[`03-runtime-pipeline.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/03-runtime-pipeline.md)
and
[`07-policies-and-safety.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/07-policies-and-safety.md)
means most changes land on `society/<stimulus_id>/candidate-<n>`
before they touch `main`. Reality revision is reserved for the merge
step. This makes destructive edits to accepted reality slower than
they would otherwise be — which is the right direction given Figure
3-2's asymmetry.

### 4 — Suppressors that fire on cheap, dangerous outputs

`suppressor.workflow-diff-keywords`, `suppressor.soul-file-diff`, and
`suppressor.high-entropy-string`
([`07-policies-and-safety.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/07-policies-and-safety.md))
fire on candidate outputs that share the wrecker's shape: a small
diff with large reach. The plan thus already prices in the
asymmetry at the output layer.

### 5 — Approval gate for the most expensive losses

`governance/approval-gate.yml` reserves the most irreversible actions
(write_workflow, write_governance, write_soul_file, payment_any,
cloud_egress_outside_allowlist, delegate_to_other_society,
enable_runtime) for explicit human approval. The arbiter cannot
choose the wrecker for these actions on its own.

## What the implementation does not yet take into account

### A — No explicit cost or reward field on candidates

The schemas in
[`09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md)
describe `candidate_action`, `objection`, and `validation_result`,
but none of them carries a numeric or categorical *cost of
reversal*. Friction is added by danger-zone matchers and censor
rules, not by a per-candidate estimate of how expensive the reverse
operation would be. The arbiter cannot weigh "this build took six
steps" against "this wreck took one."

### B — No reinforcement asymmetry in credit assignment

`credit-assignment.ts` writes to `evolution/reinforcement-log.md`
([`03-runtime-pipeline.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/03-runtime-pipeline.md)),
but the plan does not say that constructive contributions and
destructive ones should be reinforced on different curves. With no
asymmetric pricing, any future learning loop risks Minsky's failure
mode: the cheap, vivid signal of a successful "wreck" outcompetes
the slow, abstract signal of a successful "build" in the
reinforcement log.

### C — No notion of *delayed* payoff

The settlement layer
([`03-runtime-pipeline.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/03-runtime-pipeline.md))
closes one run; it does not record that a particular build will not
pay off until a later run. There is no `expected_payoff_horizon`
field, no way for `patch-imaginer` to say "this is worth doing even
though it will not look valuable until three stimuli from now." The
plan resolves each run on its own merits, which is exactly the
condition under which Minsky's asymmetry bites hardest.

### D — No `simplifier` / `pruner` agency

[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md)
contains no agent whose job is *legitimate* destruction —
deleting dead code, retiring stale agencies, pruning superseded
K-lines beyond the existing `forgetting-critic`. The plan treats
destruction as something to be slowed, not as something to be
*done well*. WRECKER, in the figure, is not a malfunction; the plan
has no role for a well-formed wrecker.

### E — No cost model on memory churn

`memory_mutation` is a danger zone, but the plan does not price
in-place rewrites of memory against append-only growth. Memory
that grows unboundedly is one kind of cost; memory that loses prior
decisions is another. Figure 3-2's asymmetry applies to memory as
much as to code, and the plan currently only guards against the
loss side.

### F — The cheapest-but-dangerous output has no special class

A diff that adds one line to a workflow file is small, fast to
produce, and potentially fatal. The plan handles this via the
`workflow_mutation` danger zone, which is a *path* matcher. There is
no second axis — say, `effort_to_produce` vs `effort_to_revert` —
that would let a censor refuse a candidate purely on the grounds
that its production cost was suspiciously low compared to the
damage it could cause.

## Implication for the plan (no changes proposed here)

Figure 3-2 is the figure that explains *why* destruction needs to be
treated as a structural counterpart of construction rather than as a
defect. The implementation plan accepts the premise on the safety
side — it adds friction to the cheap path — but does not yet carry
the asymmetry into the data model, the credit-assignment loop, or
the agency catalogue.

The gaps above are recorded here as analysis, not as a change
request. Any move to close them — a `cost_of_reversal` field on
`candidate_action`, an asymmetric reinforcement curve in
`evolution/reinforcement-log.md`, a first-class `simplifier` or
`pruner` role in the agency catalogue — would touch the schemas in
[`09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md),
the manifests in
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md),
the safety layer in
[`07-policies-and-safety.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/07-policies-and-safety.md),
and the identity scopes in
`THE-SOCIETY-OF-REPO/02-protocols/01-identity.md`, and so falls
under the "stop and ask" rules in
[`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md) §12 and
[`CLAUDE.md`](../../../../../CLAUDE.md).
