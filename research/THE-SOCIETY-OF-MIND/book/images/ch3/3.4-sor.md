# Figure 3-4 — Implementation analysis (SOR / FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION)

**Source figure:** [3-4.md](3-4.md) — *EAT, PLAY, SLEEP as icon rows.*
**Analysis target:** [`FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/)
(plan-of-record for `.forgejo-society/` and
`.forgejo/workflows/forgejo-society.yaml`).

This file inventories the ideas carried by Figure 3-4 and checks each
one against the implementation plan. The aim is to mark, plainly, what
the plan already absorbs and what it does not yet name.

Figure 3-4 is a visual restatement of Figure 3-1 — the same top row of
rival drives, redrawn in the vocabulary the child actually uses. The
analysis here therefore overlaps with
[`3.1-sor.md`](3.1-sor.md) and only flags what the icon-row redrawing
adds *on top of* that figure.

---

## The ideas Figure 3-4 actually carries

Pulled from the figure caption and Minsky's surrounding text in §3.5
*Destructiveness*:

1. **The same top-level structure as Figure 3-1.** EAT, PLAY, SLEEP
   as mutually-exclusive drives competing for the body.
2. **Each top-level agency is a market of concrete options.** EAT is
   not a single appetite; it is bowl, loaf, sandwich, glass, bottle.
   PLAY is doll, dog, blocks, toy car, build-and-knock-over. SLEEP
   is pillow, bed, chair.
3. **Conflict between drives is also conflict between specifics.**
   When EAT wins, the choice between bowl and sandwich is itself a
   sub-conflict; PLAY winning sets off the doll-vs-blocks
   sub-conflict, and so on.
4. **The vocabulary of a drive is grounded in concrete objects, not
   abstract categories.** The figure deliberately replaces names with
   pictograms to make the point that the agencies the child actually
   uses are *thing-shaped*, not concept-shaped.
5. **The child's market changes as the inventory changes.** Add a new
   toy and PLAY-WITH-ANIMALS gains a member. The market is editable.

These five items are what the rest of this file tests against the
implementation plan.

## What the implementation already absorbs

### 1 — Families as markets of concrete specialists

The first-ship catalogues in
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md)
are organised exactly as Figure 3-4 organises EAT or PLAY: a family
is a row, and the members of the row are specific, named workers
(`patch-imaginer`, `diff-skeptic`, `kline-retriever`, and so on).
The figure's idea that a drive is "a market of options" is honoured
at the family level.

### 2 — The market is editable

The `self-modification` frame and
`agency.meta-admin.differentiation-broker`
([`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md))
are the plan's mechanism for adding, splitting, or retiring members
of a family — the operational equivalent of "give the child a new
toy and PLAY grows."

### 3 — Conflict between specifics is already the deliberation loop

The `deliberate → criticize → censor → settle` chain
([`02-workflow-design.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/02-workflow-design.md))
already mediates between members of the same family arriving at
different candidates. Figure 3-4's "doll vs blocks once PLAY has
won" sub-conflict has a direct analogue at the within-family level.

### 4 — Pictogram-like polynemes

Path polynemes and label polynemes
([`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md))
are not literal pictograms, but they serve the same role: small,
concrete tokens (`workflow-file`, `soul-file`, `secret-file`,
`kind:bug`, `kind:security`) that wake agencies without going
through an abstract category layer. The plan accepts Figure 3-4's
"thing-shaped vocabulary" claim at the activation layer.

## What the implementation does not yet take into account

Figure 3-4's main contribution beyond Figure 3-1 is the *top row* —
the drives EAT, PLAY, SLEEP themselves — drawn as visible, addressable
units. The implementation gaps it surfaces are mostly the same ones
[`3.1-sor.md`](3.1-sor.md) records under gaps A, B, D and F. They are
not repeated in full here; instead, this file flags only what the
icon-row redrawing adds.

### A — Top-level drives still have no manifest

[`3.1-sor.md`](3.1-sor.md) §A already records that the plan has no
`agencies/drives/` family, no body-level scheduler, and no analogue
of EAT, PLAY, SLEEP. Figure 3-4 sharpens this gap by showing that
the missing top row is not an abstraction — it is a *visible,
inventoried* level. The plan has no addressable unit at the
"top-row" tier at all.

### B — No inventory model for what a family contains right now

Figure 3-4's icon rows are *snapshots of current inventory* — these
are the toys the child has today. The plan stores agency manifests
in `agencies/<family>/*.md`, which is durable and listable, but it
does not surface "what the family currently offers" as a first-class
view at runtime. There is no `family.inventory` field consulted
during deliberation; the runtime simply loads whatever manifests
exist. The figure's "this is the menu right now" framing is not
represented.

### C — No grounded-vocabulary requirement

The figure is explicit that the agencies the child uses are
*thing-shaped*. The plan has no schema rule that enforces this. A
new agency may be named in any way and may carry any prose body. The
discipline of "if you cannot draw the agency as a pictogram, it is
too abstract" is editorial only; nothing in
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md)
or in the manifest schema rejects an over-abstract agency.

### D — Sub-conflict-after-winning is not modelled

In the figure, once EAT wins it must still choose between bowl and
sandwich; once PLAY wins it must still choose between doll and
blocks. The plan handles within-family contention as part of the
single deliberation loop; it does not stage "drive selected →
inventory consulted → specific chosen" as distinct steps. The
two-stage shape Figure 3-4 emphasises is collapsed into one stage.

### E — No mechanism by which the *physical* environment edits the family

A new toy appears in the room and PLAY-WITH-ANIMALS gains a member,
without any deliberate act of governance. The plan's family
membership only changes through the `self-modification` frame
([`07-policies-and-safety.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/07-policies-and-safety.md)
gates this through `agency_mutation` requiring human confirmation).
There is no analogue of "the environment quietly grows the
inventory." This is correct policy for a self-hosted forge — the
gating is a safety feature, not an oversight — but it does mean
Figure 3-4's most casual mechanism (inventory grows by accretion)
has no operational counterpart.

## Implication for the plan (no changes proposed here)

Figure 3-4 is mostly a redrawing of Figure 3-1, so its implications
for the plan are largely those already recorded in
[`3.1-sor.md`](3.1-sor.md). The one feature it adds is the
*inventory* framing: a drive is what it currently contains, the
contents are visible, and they can change. The implementation honours
this at the family level and gates the editing through the
`self-modification` frame; it does not provide a runtime inventory
view, does not enforce thing-shaped naming, and does not model the
two-stage "drive then specific" choice.

The gaps above are recorded here as analysis, not as a change
request. Any move to close them — a `family.inventory` view at
runtime, a schema rule that rejects over-abstract agency names, a
two-stage selection in
[`02-workflow-design.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/02-workflow-design.md),
or an `agencies/drives/` family with body-resource arbitration —
would touch the manifest schema in
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md),
the activation protocol in
[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md),
and the identity scopes in
`THE-SOCIETY-OF-REPO/02-protocols/01-identity.md`, and so falls
under the "stop and ask" rules in
[`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md) §12 and
[`CLAUDE.md`](../../../../../CLAUDE.md).
