# Figure 2-1 — Implementation analysis (SOR / FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION)

**Source figure:** [2-1.md](2-1.md) — *Agents by themselves and agents
in a bureaucracy.*
**Analysis target:** [`FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/)
(plan-of-record for `.forgejo-society/` and
`.forgejo/workflows/forgejo-society.yaml`).

This file inventories the ideas carried by Figure 2-1 and checks each
one against the implementation plan. The aim is to mark, plainly, what
the plan already absorbs and what it does not yet name.

---

## The ideas Figure 2-1 actually carries

Pulled from the figure caption and Minsky's surrounding text in §2.1
*Components and connections*:

1. **Same parts, different mind.** The left and right panels list the
   identical agents (PUT, ADD, SEE, GET, GRASP, FIND-PLACE, BEGIN,
   END, FIND, MOVE, RELEASE). Only the right panel can build a tower.
   The competence lives in the wiring, not the roster.
2. **A roster is not a society.** An unsorted catalogue of agents,
   however complete, does no work. Listing the parts is not the same
   as connecting them.
3. **Connections are themselves first-class.** The right-hand tree
   makes parent/child relations, and the implied "calls" between
   them, into visible objects you can point at.
4. **Hierarchy as the simplest non-trivial wiring.** The figure does
   not argue for any particular topology — it argues that *some*
   topology is necessary, and uses a strict tree as the cheapest
   example.
5. **The same agent in two roles.** BEGIN, ADD and END appear in the
   loose list as peers of MOVE and GRASP, and in the tree as
   *managers of* MOVE and GRASP. Position in the wiring changes
   what an agent is for, without changing what it is.
6. **A bureaucracy is reorganisable.** Because the agents are the
   same on both sides, the difference between "no tower" and "tower"
   is a graph edit, not a personnel change. Rewiring is the unit of
   evolution.
7. **The diagram itself is a contract.** Once the tree is drawn,
   BUILDER's job is defined: route activation to BEGIN, then ADD,
   then END. The picture is the spec.
8. **Two failure modes, not one.** A mind can fail by lacking parts
   (the right agent is missing) *or* by lacking connections (the
   right agents are present but unwired). The figure forces the
   second failure mode into view.

These eight items are what the rest of this file tests against the
implementation plan.

## What the implementation already absorbs

### 1 — Agents exist as individually addressable units

[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md)
gives every agency a stable id of the form
`agency.{family}.{name}` and a manifest under
`.forgejo-society/agencies/<family>/<name>.md`. The "loose list" on
the left of the figure — agents as named, addressable things — is
straightforwardly represented.

### 2 — Connections exist, and they have schemas

The wiring is not left implicit. The plan provides several distinct
connection mechanisms, each with its own field:

- `activates_on:` (signals, paths, labels, phrases) — bottom-up
  triggering;
- `excite:` / `inhibits:` between units — lateral influence;
- frames and polynemes
  ([`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md))
  — coordinated co-activation of groups;
- handoff and signal schemas
  ([`09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md))
  — typed messages between units.

So the right-hand panel of the figure — "the same agents, but wired" —
has multiple concrete realisations in the plan, not just one.

### 3 — Wiring is auditable in git

[`00-overview.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/00-overview.md)
and the canonical thesis "capability is granted by files and audited
by git" mean that every edge in the right-hand panel — every
`activates_on`, `excite`, `inhibits`, every frame member list — is a
diff in a tracked file. The graph edit Minsky points at is, in this
plan, a pull request.

### 4 — Position changes role

[`11-mapping-sor-to-implementation.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/11-mapping-sor-to-implementation.md)
shows the same primitives (agency, critic, censor, K-line) playing
different roles depending on where they sit in the pipeline. A
`critic` invoked in `criticize` and a `critic` invoked inside an
`agency.meta-admin.*` self-review serve different functions while
being the same kind of unit. Figure 2-1's "same agent, two roles" is
honoured at this level.

### 5 — Two failure modes are distinguishable

The runtime separates *no candidate fired* (a roster gap — no agency
matched the signal) from *candidates fired but produced no
settlement* (a wiring/decision gap — units activated but did not
compose into a valid handoff). See `perceive` / `activate` vs
`settle` in
[`03-runtime-pipeline.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/03-runtime-pipeline.md).
The plan can therefore tell "missing part" from "missing connection"
without confusing them.

## What the implementation does not yet take into account

These are the parts of Figure 2-1 that the plan currently leaves
implicit, partial, or simply absent.

### A — There is no rendered wiring diagram

The right-hand panel of Figure 2-1 is a single picture of the whole
society. The plan has no equivalent artifact. The wiring is *present*
— scattered across manifest fields, frame definitions, and signal
schemas — but it is never *gathered* into a single, browsable graph
view. Nothing in
[`02-workflow-design.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/02-workflow-design.md)
or
[`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md)
specifies a "render the agency graph" step, and no folder under
`.forgejo-society/` is the canonical home for such a diagram. The
roster is gatherable (`ls agencies/*/`); the bureaucracy is not.

### B — Connections are not themselves named objects

In the figure, the *edges* between BUILDER and BEGIN/ADD/END are as
real as the nodes. In the plan, connections are *attributes of
nodes*: `excite:` is a list inside an agency manifest, `activates_on:`
is a list inside another. There is no
`.forgejo-society/connections/<id>.md` or `connection.{name}` scope
in [`THE-SOCIETY-OF-REPO/02-protocols/01-identity.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-INTRODUCTION/THE-SOCIETY-OF-REPO/02-protocols/01-identity.md).
A connection cannot carry its own authority line, its own budget, or
its own description. Edits to wiring are therefore always edits to a
node file, never to an edge file.

### C — No "manager" node distinct from "worker" nodes

Figure 2-1 distinguishes BUILDER (which manages) from MOVE / GRASP
(which act). The plan has families (folders), members (manifests),
and an assembly family for output composition, but it does not have
a *manager kind* — a unit whose declared job is to route activation
to a named set of children in a specified order. The closest
analogue, the `agencies/meta-admin/` family in
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md),
manages *the society's own evolution*, not the per-run routing of
work to workers.

### D — The "same agents, rewired" experiment is not first-class

Because connections are stored as attributes of nodes, you cannot
hold the roster constant and swap the wiring as an atomic change.
The experiment Figure 2-1 dramatises — "here are the same parts in
two arrangements" — requires touching every node manifest. No
mechanism in
[`07-policies-and-safety.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/07-policies-and-safety.md)
or
[`09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md)
supports a "wiring profile" or "topology variant" that could be
swapped in or out while leaving the agency catalogue untouched.

### E — Wiring has no separate authority gate

Adding an agency goes through manifest review with an authority line
(`read`, `draft`, `propose`, `act`, `govern`, `human`). Adding a
*connection* — appending one id to an `excite:` list — goes through
the same gate, because the connection lives inside the node's
manifest. There is no distinct gate for "rewire the society" as
opposed to "introduce a new agent." The figure's argument that
connections are first-class would naturally suggest one; the plan
does not have it.

### F — The two failure modes are distinguishable but not surfaced

Gap E above (roster gap vs wiring gap) is *detectable* in the
runtime, but neither
[`03-runtime-pipeline.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/03-runtime-pipeline.md)
nor
[`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md)
specifies that a settlement record carry a field like
`failure_mode: missing-agency | missing-connection | …`. The
information is implicit in logs; it is not a structured field a
critic or censor can key on.

### G — Hierarchy is one wiring among many, but only one is named

The figure is careful to show a tree because a tree is the cheapest
example, not because trees are privileged. The plan likewise has
non-tree wiring (lateral `excite`/`inhibit`, frame co-activation,
signal-driven activation), but its narrative consistently leans on
the tree-shaped pipeline phases of
[`02-workflow-design.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/02-workflow-design.md).
There is no documentation page that catalogues the *topologies* a
Forgejo Society instance is allowed to grow into, or that says when
a non-tree shape (a cycle, a lattice, a recurrent bundle) is
acceptable. The plan can express more shapes than it explains.

## Implication for the plan (no changes proposed here)

Figure 2-1 is the figure that makes *wiring* the unit of intelligence.
The implementation plan absorbs the spirit of this — agencies are
addressable, connections exist, and edits are tracked in git — but
keeps the wiring as a *property of nodes* rather than as an
independently-named, independently-governable object. The roster is
visible in the filesystem; the bureaucracy is not.

The gaps above are recorded here as analysis, not as a change
request. Any move to close them — for example, a `connection.*`
identifier scope, a rendered agency-graph artifact, a "wiring profile"
swap mechanism, or a `failure_mode` field on settlement records —
would touch the manifest schema in
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md),
the activation protocol in
[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md),
the handoff schemas in
[`09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md),
and the identity scopes in
[`THE-SOCIETY-OF-REPO/02-protocols/01-identity.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-INTRODUCTION/THE-SOCIETY-OF-REPO/02-protocols/01-identity.md),
and so falls under the "stop and ask" rules in
[`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md) §12 and
[`CLAUDE.md`](../../../../../CLAUDE.md).
