# Figure 17-2 — Implementation analysis (SOR / FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION)

**Source figure:** [17-2.md](17-2.md) — *Sequences of teaching-selves.*
**Analysis target:** [`FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/)
(plan-of-record for `.forgejo-society/` and
`.forgejo/workflows/forgejo-society.yaml`).

This file inventories the ideas carried by Figure 17-2 and checks each
one against the implementation plan. The aim is to mark, plainly,
what the plan already absorbs and what it does not yet name.

---

## The ideas Figure 17-2 actually carries

Pulled from the figure caption and Minsky's surrounding text in
§17.1 *Sequences of teaching-selves*:

1. **Development is staged.** A mind passes through a discrete series
   of organisations — *Stage 1*, *Stage 2*, *Stage 3* — rather than
   growing as one undifferentiated capacity.
2. **Each stage teaches the next.** The transition from one stage to
   the next is not maturation in isolation: the older stage is the
   teacher of the younger-shaped successor.
3. **Earlier stages persist.** Stages do not overwrite their
   predecessors. The earlier organisation *remains available* and can
   be re-entered as a fallback resource.
4. **The current mind is layered, not monolithic.** What runs *now* is
   the latest stage standing on top of all the prior stages, each
   still wired in.
5. **The teacher relationship is directional and asymmetric.** The
   *teaches* arrow points forward only; later stages do not retrain
   earlier ones in this picture.
6. **A single outline encloses all stages.** The encompassing *Self*
   is drawn as the loose envelope around the chain — unity is the
   *shape of the persistence*, not a separate part.
7. **Continuity of identity is structural.** "Being the same person"
   is what the continued availability of the earlier stages feels
   like from inside, not a fact about any one stage.
8. **Versioning is intrinsic to mind.** The figure presupposes that a
   mind is *something with a history of versions of itself*, each of
   which once was the present and is now a retained predecessor.

These eight items are what the rest of this file tests against the
implementation plan.

## What the implementation already absorbs

### 1 — History as the substrate

[`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md)
treats Git as the system's memory: every run, settlement, K-line, and
manifest revision is a commit, and the commit graph is the audit
trail. Earlier "stages" of the society are therefore *literally
retained* — every prior version of every agency manifest is reachable
by SHA. This matches idea 3 (earlier stages persist) at the storage
level.

### 2 — Manifests evolve through named transitions

[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md)
routes manifest changes through the `self-modification` frame and
`agency.meta-admin.differentiation-broker`. A change from one agency
shape to another is a proposed, reviewed transition rather than an
overwrite. That captures idea 8 (versioning is intrinsic) as a
governance fact, not just a storage fact.

### 3 — Settlements are the durable record of a run's lesson

[`03-runtime-pipeline.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/03-runtime-pipeline.md)
ends each run in `settle`, producing a settlement object that is
written to memory. A settlement is the operational form of "what this
stage learned that the next stage can read." It matches the *teaches*
arrow of idea 2 at run granularity — one run's settlement is
available to the next run.

### 4 — K-lines as retrieval of prior configurations

[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md)
and `agency.memory.kline-retriever` re-activate past activation
patterns by id. This is the closest operational analogue to idea 3:
an earlier organisation can be re-entered on demand, not just stored.

### 5 — The Society itself has an identity that spans versions

[`00-overview.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/00-overview.md)
and [`11-mapping-sor-to-implementation.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/11-mapping-sor-to-implementation.md)
treat the Society of Repo as a single addressable thing with an
`sor.*` identity that persists across changes to its constituents.
That is the operational counterpart of idea 6 — the encompassing
outline that contains all stages.

## What the implementation does not yet take into account

These are the parts of Figure 17-2 that the plan currently leaves
implicit, partial, or absent. None of them are urgent; all of them
are real.

### A — No first-class notion of a *stage*

The plan has *runs*, *settlements*, *manifest revisions*, and
*K-lines*, but it has no object called a *stage* — a named, bounded
period during which the society had a particular organisation.
Figure 17-2's whole grammar depends on stages being addressable
("Stage 2 teaches Stage 3"). The identity protocol in
`THE-SOCIETY-OF-REPO/02-protocols/01-identity.md` does not include a
`stage` scope, and no implementation document carves runs into
developmental epochs.

### B — *Teaches* is not a typed relation

Idea 2 requires a directional, asymmetric *teaches* link from an
earlier organisation to a later one. The plan has settlements and
commit history, both of which are *records*, but neither is a typed
"X taught Y" edge between two configurations of the society. Nothing
in [`09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md)
declares a `teaches` handoff or signal kind.

### C — Earlier organisations are stored, not *runnable*

Git retains every prior manifest, but the runtime does not currently
support "run the society as it was at commit `<sha>`." Idea 3
("remains available") in Minsky's figure means *usable as a
fallback*, not merely *inspectable*. The plan has no documented
mechanism — e.g. a `runtime.profile: stage-2` selector — for
re-instantiating an earlier whole-society configuration on demand.

### D — No layering of stages within a single run

The figure shows the present mind as the *latest* stage *standing on*
its predecessors. The plan runs one configuration at a time: the
manifests checked into the working tree are the society. There is no
notion of a layered runtime where, say, *Stage 3* handles the request
but falls back to *Stage 2* on uncertainty. Fallback today is between
*agencies*, not between *eras of the society*.

### E — Self-modification is not framed as teaching

`agency.meta-admin.differentiation-broker` (idea 2 in the "already
absorbs" list) names the *act* of changing the society, but does not
name the relationship between the pre-change and post-change
configurations. Figure 17-2's emphasis is that the *old* shape is the
*teacher* of the new shape — the change carries pedagogy with it.
The implementation treats a manifest change as a `propose → act`
transition, not as a teach-the-successor act, and so does not record
*what the old shape was trying to convey* to the new one.

### F — Identity-as-persistence is not modelled

Idea 7 ("continuity of identity is what the persistence of older
stages feels like from inside") would, operationally, be a property
of the Society of Repo as a whole: its `sor.*` identity is *defined
by* the chain of stages it retains, not merely *associated with* it.
The implementation gives the SOR an id and a manifest, but does not
derive that identity from a recorded stage chain. There is no
"identity-by-history" check anywhere in
[`07-policies-and-safety.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/07-policies-and-safety.md).

### G — Stage transitions are not authority-bearing events

The authority registry uses `read`, `draft`, `propose`, `act`,
`govern`, `human`. A *stage transition* — moving the whole society
from one organisation to the next — would, in the spirit of the
figure, be a `govern`-level event with `human` involvement. The plan
does not currently distinguish "ordinary manifest change" from
"stage transition," so this stronger authority gate is not invoked
even when, in retrospect, a sequence of small `propose → act` changes
amounted to one.

### H — No retrospective view across stages

Figure 17-2 is, in part, a *retrospective* diagram: it can only be
drawn after several stages have passed. The plan provides no
documented surface that renders the society's own developmental
history as a chain of stages — only commit logs and settlement
archives. The summary-tier work in
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md)
collapses *signals within a run*, not *runs within a developmental
arc*.

## Implication for the plan (no changes proposed here)

Figure 17-2 asks the system to treat its own history as a chain of
*teaching predecessors*, not merely as an archive. The implementation
plan honours the storage and governance of change (Git, settlements,
`self-modification`, differentiation-broker) but does not yet honour
the *developmental* reading of that change: stages as addressable
units, *teaches* as a typed relation, earlier stages as re-runnable,
and identity as derived from the retained chain.

The gaps above are recorded here as analysis, not as a change
request. Any move to close them — for example, a `stage` identifier
scope, a `teaches` handoff kind, a `runtime.profile` selector that
re-instantiates a past configuration, or a `govern`/`human` gate
specific to stage transitions — would touch the identity protocol in
`THE-SOCIETY-OF-REPO/02-protocols/01-identity.md`, the handoff
schemas in [`09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md),
the state/memory model in [`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md),
and the safety policies in [`07-policies-and-safety.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/07-policies-and-safety.md),
and so falls under the "stop and ask" rules in
[`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md) §12 and
[`CLAUDE.md`](../../../../../CLAUDE.md).
