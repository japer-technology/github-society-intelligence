# Cover — *The Society of Mind*

## What the image shows

The front cover of the 1986 Simon & Schuster trade paperback edition of
Marvin Minsky's *The Society of Mind*. Against a pale yellow ground,
the title is stacked in large blue serif capitals — `THE / SOCIETY / OF
/ MIND` — separated by thin red rules. Below the title sits a
pen-and-ink illustration of an unfinished sphere: a wireframe ball of
intersecting panels and struts, partly assembled, with a smaller solid
sphere floating beside it. Small human figures stand on a desert-like
plane around the construction, suggesting a work-in-progress at
enormous scale. The author's name, **MARVIN MINSKY**, appears in red
caps above the rule, with the byline "COFOUNDER OF THE ARTIFICIAL
INTELLIGENCE LABORATORY, MIT" below. A short cover-blurb attributed to
Isaac Asimov, *Information Week*, reads: "270 brilliantly original
essays on … how the mind works."

## What it illustrates

The image is the book's first visual statement of its thesis: a mind is
something assembled out of many small, separately built parts, and the
construction is never quite finished. The half-built sphere and the
small workers around it stand in for the society of agents that the
book will go on to describe — none of them by themselves a mind, but
together making one possible.
