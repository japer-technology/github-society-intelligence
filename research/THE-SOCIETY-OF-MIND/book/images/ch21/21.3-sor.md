# Figure 21-3 — Implementation analysis (SOR / FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION)

**Source figure:** [21-3.md](21-3.md) — *Chaining Trans-frames.*
**Analysis target:** [`FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/)
(plan-of-record for `.forgejo-society/` and
`.forgejo/workflows/forgejo-society.yaml`).

This file inventories the ideas carried by Figure 21-3 and checks each
one against the implementation plan.

---

## The ideas Figure 21-3 actually carries

Pulled from the figure and the surrounding text of §21.3
*Trans-frames*:

1. **Chaining as a primitive operation.** Two Trans-frames can be
   joined by sharing an endpoint; three or more can be strung end-to-
   end without changing the frame schema.
2. **Identity of slots across frames.** Frame N's Destination *is*
   frame N+1's Origin. Not a copy, an identity. The chain is held
   together by shared slot bindings, not by transport between frames.
3. **Collapsibility.** The chain Boston → New York → Washington has
   a well-defined collapsed form Boston → Washington with the same
   shape. A chain of N frames is itself a one-frame thing at a
   coarser grain.
4. **Realm-independence of chaining.** The same chaining works for
   spatial movement, information flow, and ownership transfer — the
   point that earns the Trans-frame.
5. **Composition as bead-stringing.** No special connector is needed;
   chaining is the trivial consequence of slot identity plus the
   shared skeleton.
6. **The chain is itself a candidate for a K-line.** Once a useful
   chain has been built, the pattern of bindings is exactly the kind
   of thing memory should re-evoke later.

## What the implementation already absorbs

### 1 — A chain-of-phases at the workflow level

The phase chain `guard → normalize → guardrail → perceive → activate →
deliberate → criticize → censor → settle → act → remember → report`
([`03-runtime-pipeline.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/03-runtime-pipeline.md))
is a chain of well-named stages. The output of each phase is the
input of the next, which is the workflow-level analogue of the
figure's bead-stringing.

### 2 — Collapsed-form artefacts

Summary tiers
([`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md)
under `agencies/assembly/`) collapse a long run into shorter briefs.
That is collapsibility for outputs, which honours idea 3 at the level
of human-readable artefacts.

### 3 — K-lines as the memory of useful patterns

[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md)
defines K-lines as recordings of the mental state that produced a
successful outcome. The "successful chain" of idea 6 is exactly the
kind of thing a K-line is for, even if no current K-line records a
chain explicitly.

## What the implementation does not yet take into account

### A — Chaining as a frame-level operation does not exist

The plan chains *phases* and *signals*; it does not chain *frames*.
There is no operation in
[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md)
that takes two frame instances and identifies a slot of one with a
slot of the other. Frames are selected, filled, settled — and then
the next stimulus brings up a fresh frame. The figure's "string of
frames" is not representable.

### B — Slot identity across frames is not a concept

The schema's `slots.<name>` does not have a `bound_to:` or `aliases:`
field that would let frame B declare "my `origin` slot is the same
binding as frame A's `destination` slot." Without that, idea 2 cannot
be expressed: the runtime can only *copy* values between frames, not
*identify* slots.

### C — No collapsibility for frame chains

Summary tiers collapse *signals* and *briefs*; they do not collapse
*frame chains* into a coarser-grained frame instance. There is no
operation that takes (A→B, B→C, C→D) and emits A→D as a single
collapsed frame of the same kind. Hierarchy works on outputs, not on
frame structures.

### D — Realm-independence of chaining is asserted but not exercised

Because (per
[`21.2-sor.md`](21.2-sor.md), gap A) the plan has no Trans-frame, it
cannot demonstrate that one chaining machinery works across realms.
The workflow chain is realm-specific: it chains *cognitive phases*,
not Trans-shaped events. Idea 4 has no operational analogue.

### E — Chains are not first-class memory objects

A K-line in the plan re-evokes a mental state — the set of active
agencies, symbols, and frames at success. The figure points at a
narrower thing: the *chain of bindings* that joined N frames together.
The K-line schema does not have a `frame_chain:` field, and the
retrieval critic (`critic.kline-retrieval`, indirectly) does not look
up patterns by chain shape. The plan can remember the *state*; it
cannot remember the *path*.

### F — Composition discipline is at the phase boundary only

Idea 5 (no special connector is needed; composition is trivial given
the shared skeleton) requires the shared skeleton from Figure 21-2.
Without it, the plan's composition has to be supplied by hand at
each phase boundary — by the `next_phase:` field on signals and by
explicit hand-offs documented in
[`09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md).
Composition exists, but it is not trivial.

## Implication for the plan (no changes proposed here)

Figure 21-3 is the figure where the Trans-frame earns its keep: once
the skeleton exists, chains are free. The plan has chaining at the
workflow level and collapsibility at the output level, but it does
not yet have either at the frame level — because, per
[`21.2-sor.md`](21.2-sor.md), it does not yet have the Trans-frame.

Adding frame chaining would mean adding a `bound_to:` field on slots,
a collapse operation on frame instances, and a K-line schema field
for chain shape — all in
[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md).
These fall under the "stop and ask" rules in
[`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md) §12 and
[`CLAUDE.md`](../../../../../CLAUDE.md).
