## What the image shows

A small diagram with two circles connected by an arrow. The left circle is labelled *ORIGIN*; the right circle, *DESTINATION*. Above the arrow that joins them, two alternative labels appear — *TRAJECTORY* or *DIFFERENCE*.

## What it illustrates

The Trans-frame is the minimal skeleton shared by transportation in space, transmission of information, and transfer of ownership — Schank's P-Trans, M-Trans, and A-Trans. Origin and Destination are the two endpoint pronomes; the arrow between them carries either a path (Trajectory) or a change (Difference). Because the same skeleton fits all three realms, the same chaining skill applies to all of them — the point that [21-3.md](21-3.md) makes explicit.
