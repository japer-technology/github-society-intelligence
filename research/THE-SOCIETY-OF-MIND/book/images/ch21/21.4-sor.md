# Figure 21-4 — Implementation analysis (SOR / FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION)

**Source figure:** [21-4.md](21-4.md) — *The Builder society for "put apple
in pail".*
**Analysis target:** [`FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/)
(plan-of-record for `.forgejo-society/` and
`.forgejo/workflows/forgejo-society.yaml`).

This file inventories the ideas carried by Figure 21-4 and checks each
one against the implementation plan.

---

## The ideas Figure 21-4 actually carries

Pulled from the figure and the surrounding text of §21.4
*communication among agents*:

1. **A single Trans decomposes into a tree of agents.** TRANS → GET,
   PUT-in → FIND, GRASP / RELEASE → LOOK-for, MOVE-arm-to.
2. **Middle managers carry no object information.** GET, PUT-in,
   FIND name kinds of operation; they do not know it is *apple* or
   *pail* they are operating on.
3. **Object information lives only at the leaves.** *apple* and
   *pail* appear as annotations on the leaf sub-trees, not as
   arguments threaded through every parent.
4. **The same sub-tree (FIND → LOOK-for + MOVE-arm-to) is reused
   under different objects.** Two FIND sub-trees appear, identical in
   structure, differing only in leaf annotation.
5. **Decomposition is the only thing the parent does.** TRANS's role
   is to wake GET then PUT-in; it has no primitive competence of its
   own.
6. **The "how" of object-binding is left to a later mechanism.** The
   figure deliberately stops at the question "how does the leaf know
   it is *apple* and not *pail*?" — that question is answered by
   Figure 21-5.
7. **Hierarchy as a unit of action, not just of organisation.** The
   tree is what gets activated as one Trans; it is not an org chart
   of standing roles.

## What the implementation already absorbs

### 1 — Narrow specialists per family

The agency families in
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md)
ship deliberately small members: `patch-imaginer`, `diff-skeptic`,
`kline-retriever`, `conscious-presenter`, etc. These are leaf-shaped
specialists in the figure's sense.

### 2 — Activation as the dominant control mechanism

[`02-workflow-design.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/02-workflow-design.md)
and the polyneme mechanism in
[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md)
make activation / inhibition the way one unit wakes another. That is
the right machinery for the figure's "TRANS turns on GET, GET turns
on FIND" pattern, even if the topology is currently flat.

### 3 — Critics and censors play named roles per stimulus

For a given stimulus a frame names `default_critics:` and
`default_censors:` rather than fixing them globally. This honours
idea 7 weakly: the *set of active units* depends on the stimulus,
not on a standing org chart.

## What the implementation does not yet take into account

### A — Recursive agency composition is absent

This is the same gap as Figure 1-2's gap B: the plan has one level of
nesting (family → member), not the multi-level tree shown in the
figure (TRANS → GET → FIND → LOOK-for). A manifest cannot declare
`composed_of: [ <child-agency-id>, ... ]`, and no runtime loader
descends through such a tree. Figure 21-4's whole shape requires
recursion the schema does not currently express.

### B — Middle-manager agencies have no place in the catalogue

Idea 2 (managers that *carry no object information*) requires a kind
of agency whose entire content is "wake these children in this
order." The plan has no `kind: router` or `kind: orchestrator`
agency; every agency has a prose body that becomes its prompt and is
expected to do work. A FIND-style unit that just activates LOOK-for
and MOVE-arm-to has no schema home.

### C — Object information has no agreed leaf-only convention

In the figure, *apple* is bound only at the leaves; parents never
see it. In the plan, every agency receives the full context bundle
(per
[`09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md)'s
`useful_context`). There is no discipline that says "this slot value
is for the leaf only; the parent must not consume it." Object scoping
is not enforceable.

### D — Sub-tree reuse under different bindings is not expressible

Idea 4 (the same FIND sub-tree used twice, once for *apple* and once
for *pail*) requires the ability to instantiate the same sub-agency
twice in the same run with different bindings. The plan loads an
agency manifest once per stimulus and runs it once. There is no
notion of "two instances of `agency.code.find` with different
parameterisations alive in the same settlement."

### E — Ordered activation within a tree is not declarative

The figure implies an order: GET before PUT-in; FIND before GRASP.
The plan's families are sets; ordering is supplied only by the outer
phase chain. A manifest cannot say "activate my children in this
order" because there are no children to activate.

### F — A Trans-shaped decomposition is not a stimulus shape the plan recognises

The figure decomposes one Trans of an apple. The plan's frames
classify stimuli as `bug`, `feature`, `code-change`, etc. — not as
"this is a Trans whose Object is an apple." Without the Trans-frame
(gap A of [`21.2-sor.md`](21.2-sor.md)), the decomposition has
nothing to attach to at the top.

## Implication for the plan (no changes proposed here)

Figure 21-4 is Figure 1-2 applied to a concrete task. The plan
absorbs the *specialist-leaves* part of the figure (narrow agencies)
but does not yet absorb the *tree* part (recursive composition,
object-free managers, leaf-only binding, ordered activation).

Closing these gaps would mean adding a recursive `composed_of:` field
and a router-only agency kind to
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md)
and to the activation protocol in
[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md)
— the same changes flagged by Figure 1-2, and so falling under the
"stop and ask" rules in [`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md) §12
and [`CLAUDE.md`](../../../../../CLAUDE.md).
