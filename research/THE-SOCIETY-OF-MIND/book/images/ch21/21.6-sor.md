# Figure 21-6 — Implementation analysis (SOR / FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION)

**Source figure:** [21-6.md](21-6.md) — *A fully populated
Trans-frame.*
**Analysis target:** [`FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/)
(plan-of-record for `.forgejo-society/` and
`.forgejo/workflows/forgejo-society.yaml`).

This file inventories the ideas carried by Figure 21-6 — the
Trans-frame drawn as a bridge from Boston to New York with every
surrounding pronome filled — and checks each one against the
implementation plan.

---

## The ideas Figure 21-6 actually carries

1. **The bridge is the load-bearing structure.** ORIGIN →
   TRAJECTORY/DIFFERENCE → DESTINATION is the part that must exist
   for anything else to mean anything. Boston, RT. 84, location
   change, New York.
2. **Surrounding pronomes fill in the rest.** CAUSE, OBSTACLE,
   EFFECT, VEHICLE, INSTRUMENT, ACTOR, OBJECT, TIME, PLACE, ACTION,
   GOAL, METHOD — each is its own slot, each can be filled
   independently.
3. **Simultaneous activation of many slots.** Understanding a single
   sentence ("Jack drove from Boston to New York on the turnpike
   with Mary") fills many slots at once, not serially.
4. **Slot values are tokens, not free text.** *JACK*, *CAR*, *PAST*,
   *DRIVE* — short tokens that other frames and procedures can
   reuse. This is the pronome substrate from
   [21-1.md](21-1.md) put to work.
5. **Some slots co-refer.** ACTOR and CAUSE are both *JACK*; OBJECT
   and VEHICLE are both *CAR*. The figure presupposes that the same
   token can occupy several slots at once.
6. **The frame ties knowledge of *things* to knowledge of *using
   them*.** VEHICLE knows about cars-as-objects; METHOD knows about
   driving-as-procedure. The frame is what connects the two
   bodies of knowledge.
7. **A populated frame is the record of an understood event.** Once
   all slots are filled (or marked unfilled), the act of
   understanding is, in Minsky's sense, complete.
8. **Defaults and unfilled slots are both legal.** PLACE = "in the
   car" is a default reading of "drove"; nothing in the sentence
   stated it. The frame supplies defaults where the input is silent.

## What the implementation already absorbs

### 1 — A frame manifest with named slots

[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md)
defines `slots:` as a list of named, required-or-optional fields per
frame. The `code-change` frame names six slots; the
`security-sensitive` frame adds three more. The shape "a frame is a
set of named slots that get filled" is in the plan.

### 2 — Settlement as a populated frame

[`03-runtime-pipeline.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/03-runtime-pipeline.md)
and
[`09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md)
record the per-run outcome as a settlement document. The settlement
is, structurally, a populated frame: each required field carries a
value (or is explicitly empty), and the act of producing it is the
act of "understanding what this run is."

### 3 — Defaults via frame manifests

A frame's `default_actions`, `default_critics`, `default_censors`,
and `default_frame` (on polynemes) supply defaults when the stimulus
is silent. This is the operational form of Figure 21-6's
PLACE = "in the car" reading.

### 4 — Slot filling by named agencies

`slots.<>.filled_by:` declares which agencies are responsible for
producing each slot's value. This matches the figure's idea that
each pronome has a definite source — the slot is not magic; some
agency owns it.

### 5 — Multiple slots filled per run

Each run of the pipeline fills many slots together (workspace +
settlement + signals). The plan does not insist on filling slots
serially, which is consistent with the figure's "many at once."

## What the implementation does not yet take into account

### A — No canonical pronome vocabulary

As noted in the analysis of Figure 21-1, the plan does not ship a
canonical role-slot inventory (ORIGIN, DESTINATION, ACTOR, OBJECT,
…). Each frame names its own slots. Figure 21-6's "every pronome
filled at once" therefore has no plan-side analogue: there is no
fixed pronome list to *be* fully populated.

### B — Slot values are prose, not tokens

The figure's slot fillers are short tokens (*JACK*, *CAR*, *PAST*).
The plan's slot fillers are free-form Markdown (`relevant_files`,
`proposed_patch`). Tokens that can co-refer across slots and across
frames are not first-class; the same `JACK` appearing in ACTOR and
CAUSE has no schema-level identity.

### C — No cross-slot co-reference

Figure 21-6 puts the same token in ACTOR and CAUSE, and the same
token in OBJECT and VEHICLE. The plan has no `co-refers:` or
`same_as:` field linking two slots' fillers. If two slots happen to
hold the same string, that is coincidence at the prose level, not
structure.

### D — The bridge is not distinguished from the surround

The figure marks the ORIGIN → arrow → DESTINATION span as
load-bearing and treats the surrounding pronomes as decoration. The
plan's frame schema has only `required: true|false`; it has no
notion of "this slot is the load-bearing axis of the frame; this
one is supplementary." All required slots are equal.

### E — No structural connection between *thing* and *use*

Figure 21-6's VEHICLE → CAR and METHOD → STEERING, ETC. presume
that an agency knowing about cars-as-objects can be wired to an
agency knowing about driving-as-procedure. The plan has no
"thing/use" pairing: agencies are categorised by family (perception,
memory, code, safety, identity, integration, assembly, meta-admin),
not by object-knowledge / procedure-knowledge.

### F — Defaults are per-frame, not per-pronome

Defaults in the plan attach to a frame (`default_actions`,
`default_critics`). Figure 21-6's defaults attach to a *pronome
within a frame* — PLACE defaults to "in the car" *for the
drive-Trans-frame specifically*. The plan has no
`slots.<>.default:` field.

### G — Unfilled-but-legal slots are not represented

The figure tolerates partially populated frames as a normal reading
of an underspecified sentence. The plan's `required: true` slots
must be filled before settlement; there is no "unfilled but
acceptable" tier for slots that are nice-to-have when the stimulus
is silent.

### H — No "moment of understanding" artefact

Figure 21-6 captures the *snapshot* of a frame just-populated, the
output of a single act of comprehension. The plan's settlement is
close but is *outcome-shaped* (proposed patch, risks, revert path)
rather than *comprehension-shaped* (here is the populated set of
pronomes that represent what we now believe this stimulus to be).

## Implication for the plan (no changes proposed here)

Figure 21-6 is the figure where Chapter 21 cashes in: a single frame,
fully populated, *is* the moment of understanding. The plan honours
the surface — named slots, defaults, slot-owning agencies, per-run
artefacts — but does not honour the substrate: a canonical pronome
vocabulary, tokenised slot values, cross-slot co-reference, and a
load-bearing/surround distinction.

The gaps above are recorded here as analysis, not as a change
request. Any move to close them — shipping a pronome vocabulary, a
tokenised slot-value schema, a `co-refers:` field, per-pronome
defaults, or a comprehension-shaped artefact alongside the
settlement — would touch the frame schema in
[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md),
the handoff and signal schemas in
[`09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md),
and the identity scopes in
`THE-SOCIETY-OF-REPO/02-protocols/01-identity.md`, and so falls under
the "stop and ask" rules in
[`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md) §12 and
[`CLAUDE.md`](../../../../../CLAUDE.md).
