# Figure 5-1 — Implementation analysis (SOR / FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION)

**Source figure:** [5-1.md](5-1.md) — *The self as a remote-controlled puppet.*
**Analysis target:** [`FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/)
(plan-of-record for `.forgejo-society/` and
`.forgejo/workflows/forgejo-society.yaml`).

This file inventories the ideas carried by Figure 5-1 and checks each
one against the implementation plan. The figure is a cartoon of the
homunculus the chapter sets out to dismantle, so several of its
"ideas" are *negative claims* — things the plan should *not* contain.
The analysis is written accordingly.

---

## The ideas Figure 5-1 actually carries

Pulled from the figure caption and Minsky's surrounding text in §5.3
*The remote-control self*:

1. **The naïve self as a single inner operator.** A small unified
   figure stands in for "you", separate from the agencies it directs.
2. **The world reaches the operator only as displays.** Sensation
   arrives on a screen of panes — coffee, wheel, music, stage — never
   as the world itself.
3. **The body reaches the operator only as wires.** Action is mediated
   by a small set of named effectors (face, mouth, fire, feet); the
   operator does not act on the world directly.
4. **A clean inside/outside boundary.** The oval frames an interior in
   which the operator lives; everything outside is *world*.
5. **Centralised control.** All wires converge on one hand. There is
   no second decider inside the loop.
6. **A fixed effector vocabulary.** The wires are countable and
   labelled; the puppet cannot grow a new limb during the scene.
7. **No model of the operator itself.** The puppet has no inner
   screen; it is shown as opaque to itself.
8. **The figure as *reductio*.** Minsky draws the homunculus literally
   so that the reader rejects it. The intended takeaway is that no
   such single inner pilot exists — only further agencies.

These eight items are what the rest of this file tests against the
implementation plan.

## What the implementation already absorbs

### 1 — There is no single inner operator

The plan is structurally pluralist. Cognition is distributed across
agency families — perception, memory, code, safety, identity,
integration, assembly, meta-admin — defined in
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md).
No phase in
[`03-runtime-pipeline.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/03-runtime-pipeline.md)
hands the whole run to one unit. The cartoon homunculus has no
counterpart in the runtime.

### 2 — The "screen" is real, and it is the workspace

The plan does pass perception through a structured intermediate: the
workspace and signal schemas described in
[`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md)
and [`09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md).
What the figure mocks (a screen between the self and the world) is
honoured at the right grain: agencies see *signals*, not raw input.
The honesty is that the screen has no single viewer.

### 3 — Effectors are a small, named, audited set

The "wires" of the figure correspond to the plan's `act` phase and
the authority levels `read`, `draft`, `propose`, `act`, `govern`,
`human` registered in `THE-SOCIETY-OF-REPO/01-governance/authority-registry.md`.
Only units with `act` may write to the world, and the catalogue of
acting units is small and named. Figure 5-1's "countable effectors"
intuition is matched, with the difference that no single hand holds
the wires.

### 4 — A public self-model exists, and it is not the runtime

The plan distinguishes the *self-model* (`AGENTS.md`, `APPEND_SYSTEM.md`,
`identity/` agencies such as `agency.identity.spock-self-model`,
`agency.identity.tone-stabilizer`, `agency.identity.soul-file-guardian`)
from the *deciding machinery* (the workflow, the agencies, the
critics and censors). The self-model is something the society
*maintains*, not something that *runs* the society. This is the
inversion Minsky is after: the "self" is an artefact of the mind, not
its operator.

### 5 — Self-modification is governed, not centralised

Edits to the self-model run through the `self-modification` frame
([`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md),
[`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md))
with `agency.identity.soul-file-guardian` at `govern` authority and
`suppressor.soul-file-diff` in the censor layer. The puppet's
inability to grow new limbs without help is, in the plan, the
correct posture: the society can change its own effectors, but only
through a settlement.

## What the implementation does not yet take into account

These are the parts of Figure 5-1 that the plan leaves implicit,
partial, or genuinely absent. The figure is a *reductio*, so several
of these gaps are about *not yet having the language to refuse the
homunculus explicitly*.

### A — No first-class statement that there is no central self

The plan is pluralist by *construction*, but no document carries a
clause of the form "no agency, critic, or censor may take the role of
a global self; the self-model is descriptive, not executive." The
absence of a homunculus is implicit in the schema rather than asserted
as a constraint. Nothing in
[`07-policies-and-safety.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/07-policies-and-safety.md)
would catch a future unit that quietly began to centralise.

### B — The boundary of the "inner screen" is not modelled

Figure 5-1's oval — the line between *what the mind sees* and *what
the world is* — has no explicit counterpart. The workspace contains
signals, frames, K-lines, and settlements
([`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md),
[`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md))
but there is no schema field that marks "this is the perceived view"
versus "this is the world". Agencies that need to reason about *what
the society currently believes it perceives* must reconstruct that
view from logs.

### C — No introspective view of the effector set

The "wires" in the figure are, in the plan, the union of units with
`act` authority. There is no generated artefact that lists them as a
single inventory at runtime — no `effectors.json`, no enumeration
surfaced to identity agencies. A unit such as
`agency.identity.spock-self-model` that wanted to answer "what can we
currently *do* in the world?" would have to walk the manifests.

### D — No agency owns "this is what the puppet would look like"

The figure works by drawing the *wrong* model of self. The plan has
no unit whose job is to maintain the *wrong* models the society must
resist — no `critic.homunculus-detector`, no policy that flags prose
or manifests slipping into "the agent decided" singular voice. The
vocabulary discipline lives in [`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md)
and the style guide; it is not yet a runtime check.

### E — "Sensation arrives only as displays" is asymmetric in the plan

Perception agencies are catalogued in
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md);
their outputs are signals. But the plan does not distinguish *raw
arrival* (a webhook payload, a file change) from *displayed signal*
(a normalised, typed observation). The `normalize` phase in
[`02-workflow-design.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/02-workflow-design.md)
is the closest analogue, but its output is not labelled as "the
screen" anywhere a downstream agency can reason about it as such.

### F — The figure carries a developmental claim the plan does not

Minsky uses the puppet image because children, and lay adults,
*default* to it. The plan has no concept of "the model the society
defaults to when it has not yet learned better" — no manifest
attribute that marks an agency as *naïve-default* versus *trained-up*.
The progression Figure 5-1 implies (puppet → society) has no
counterpart in the differentiation pathway managed by
`agency.meta-admin.differentiation-broker`.

### G — No surfacing for users who *do* think there is one self

The figure is pedagogical: it exists to be rejected. The plan has no
"explain-the-society" surface aimed at users who still expect a
single inner agent. The closest material lives in
`FORGEJO-SOCIETY-INTRODUCTION/` and `FORGEJO-SOCIETY-PROMOTION/`,
which are documentation pillars, not runtime affordances. A user
querying the running system gets a response shaped by
`agency.identity.spock-self-model`; they do not get a structural view
of *which* agencies contributed to it.

## Implication for the plan (no changes proposed here)

Figure 5-1 is the figure the chapter draws in order to throw it away.
The implementation plan honours the figure's *conclusion* — there is
no single inner pilot — by being pluralist throughout. It does not
yet honour the figure's *pedagogy* — the active, named refusal of
the homunculus, the explicit modelling of the perceived view, and the
introspectable inventory of effectors that together would let the
society answer "no, it is not like that" in its own voice.

The gaps above are recorded here as analysis, not as a change
request. Any move to close them — for example, a `critic.homunculus`
clause in [`07-policies-and-safety.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/07-policies-and-safety.md),
a `perceived_view` field in the signal schema in
[`09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md),
an `effectors.json` introspection artefact in
[`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md),
or a new identifier scope for "view-of-self" objects in
`THE-SOCIETY-OF-REPO/02-protocols/01-identity.md` — would touch the
manifest schema in [`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md),
the protocols above, and the governance vocabulary, and so falls
under the "stop and ask" rules in
[`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md) §12 and
[`CLAUDE.md`](../../../../../CLAUDE.md).
