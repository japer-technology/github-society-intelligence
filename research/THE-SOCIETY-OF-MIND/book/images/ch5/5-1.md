# Figure 5-1 — The self as a remote-controlled puppet

**File:** `ch5/5-1.png`
**Appears in:** [../../som-5.3.md](../../som-5.3.md) — *The remote-control self*

## What the image shows

A whimsical line drawing of a small marionette-like figure standing
inside a large oval "screen" suspended in front of it. Around the
screen, four panes display sensory snippets — musical notes, a coffee
cup, a wheel, a stage with a piano. Outside the oval, four real-world
agents reach in along curving wires from the corners: a stylised face
in profile (top left), a smiling mouth (top right), an open flame
labelled by a curling line (lower left), and a pair of legs and feet
walking (lower right). The wires terminate at the central puppet,
which holds them out like reins.

## What it illustrates

The naïve picture of the *self* that Minsky is about to dismantle:
a tiny operator inside the head pulling strings on a screen-image of
the world. The drawing's cartoon excess is the point. By rendering
the homunculus literally, the figure makes its impossibility visible
and prepares the reader for the chapter's argument that there is no
inner pilot — only more agencies.
