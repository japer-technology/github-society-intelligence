This file inventories the ideas carried by Figure 5-2 and checks each
one against the implementation plan. The figure is a one-line *proof
by paradox*: a sign reading **DON'T READ THIS** that disproves
single-agent minds the moment it is read. Most of its content is
about the *necessity* of more than one agent â€” a claim the plan
agrees with by construction.

---

## The ideas Figure 5-2 actually carries

Pulled from the figure caption and Minsky's surrounding text in Â§5.7
*Permanent identity*:

1. **A mind cannot be a single thing.** If one unit both *receives*
   the instruction and *judges* it, the instruction defeats itself.
2. **Reading and asking are separate roles.** Some part reads the
   sign; some part holds the question "should I be reading this?"
3. **A directive can be incoherent with its own act of consumption.**
   Inputs that are valid in form may be invalid in effect once the
   society has processed them.
4. **The joke requires audit.** The contradiction only becomes
   visible because *something* notices what *something else* just did.
5. **Identity persists across the contradiction.** The same self
   reads and notices; permanence of identity is what makes the
   paradox feel like a paradox rather than a sequence of unrelated
   acts.
6. **Self-reference is normal, not exceptional.** The sign is trivial;
   the structure it exposes (input acts on the reader) is everywhere.
7. **The minimum society is two.** The figure's smallest working model
   has exactly one reader and one asker. Anything fewer collapses.
8. **Refusing an instruction is itself work.** Recognising "I should
   not have done that" requires a distinct unit with its own
   authority to flag, not merely the absence of the original unit.

These eight items are what the rest of this file tests against the
implementation plan.

## What the implementation already absorbs

### 1 â€” The minimum society is more than one

The plan never has a single unit run end-to-end. Every phase in
[`03-runtime-pipeline.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/03-runtime-pipeline.md)
(`guard`, `normalize`, `guardrail`, `perceive`, `activate`,
`deliberate`, `criticize`, `censor`, `settle`, `act`, `remember`,
`report`) is owned by a different layer, and agencies, critics, and
censors are deliberately separate kinds in
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md).
Figure 5-2's "at least two" is the plan's floor.

### 2 â€” Reading and judging are different units

Perception agencies (`agency.perception.*`) take input; critics
(`critic.*`) judge what perception and deliberation produced; censors
(`censor.*`, `suppressor.*`) intervene before output. The
reader/asker split that makes the **DON'T READ THIS** joke land is
the same split that runs in every pass of the workflow.

### 3 â€” Inputs can be invalid in effect, not just in form

`guardrail` and the censor layer in
[`07-policies-and-safety.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/07-policies-and-safety.md)
exist precisely to catch payloads whose *consumption* causes harm
even when the bytes themselves are well-formed. Prompt-injection
defences, `censor.cloud-egress`, and the diff-touching suppressors
([`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md))
are the runtime form of "do not obey this just because it parsed".

### 4 â€” Identity persists across the act

The identity family â€” `agency.identity.spock-self-model`,
`agency.identity.tone-stabilizer`, `agency.identity.soul-file-guardian`,
`agency.identity.user-model-keeper` â€” maintains a stable voice across
heterogeneous runs. The "same self that read also noticed" intuition
of the figure is supported by these units keeping continuity between
the unit that perceived and the unit that judged.

### 5 â€” Audit is first-class

`settle` produces a settlement; the `remember` phase writes to memory
([`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md));
and the whole pipeline is auditable through git. The joke only works
because *something* notices: the plan generalises that "something"
into K-lines, settlements, and procedural memory under the
`self-modification` frame.

### 6 â€” Two-step composition at every scale

Handoff and signal schemas in
[`09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md)
require that one unit's output is another unit's input. There is no
shape in the plan where a unit consumes its own output without
mediation. The figure's structural minimum is the plan's structural
default.

## What the implementation does not yet take into account

These are the parts of Figure 5-2 that the plan leaves implicit,
partial, or absent. The figure is small; the gaps are correspondingly
narrow.

### A â€” Self-referential inputs have no explicit class

The figure's content is *an instruction whose referent is its own
reading*. The signal taxonomy in
[`09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md)
does not yet mark a signal as self-referential â€” as in, "this signal
asks the society to act on itself". Prompt-injection cases are
treated by content-pattern matching, not by a typed claim "this input
is meta with respect to the current run".

### B â€” No unit owns "noticing that obeying defeats the goal"

The critic layer evaluates correctness and scope; the censor layer
suppresses unsafe output. Neither is named for the specific
"obeying-the-instruction-undoes-the-instruction" case the figure
draws. There is no `critic.self-defeat` or `critic.consumption-
paradox` in [`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md).
In practice this work would be absorbed by `critic.scope` or
`critic.evidence`, but the figure's pattern is not addressed by name.

### C â€” Identity continuity across an aborted act is not modelled

In the figure, "I read it, then I noticed I shouldn't have" is *one
self across two moments*. The plan has K-lines
([`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md))
and procedural memory, but no schema for "the run that was begun,
then refused" â€” no settlement subtype `settlement.kind:
refused-after-perception`. The plan can refuse early (at `guardrail`)
or late (at `censor`); the *middle* refusal that the figure depends
on is logged ad hoc, not structured.

### D â€” No introspective surface for "what just happened to me"

The figure is funny because the reader can answer "I just read
something I shouldn't have." The plan has memory and audit, but no
runtime surface where an identity agency can ask "what inputs have I
processed in the current settlement window, and which of them acted
on the society itself?" The `summary-tier-*` agencies summarise
outputs, not the society's own consumption history.

### E â€” The pedagogical role of paradoxes has no home

Minsky uses **DON'T READ THIS** as a teaching device â€” a tiny
artefact that *demonstrates* plurality. The plan has no catalogue of
such artefacts. There is no `reflection/` folder, no
`agency.identity.paradox-curator`, no policy that says "the society
maintains a small set of self-undermining inputs and reviews its
behaviour on them." The introspective use of paradox is a
documentation concern only.

### F â€” The figure carries no operational mechanism

This is worth saying plainly: Figure 5-2 is a one-line proof, not a
mechanism. It tells the implementation what *must be true* (more than
one unit) and what *must be possible* (noticing one's own obedience).
It does not propose machinery, and the plan should not be expected to
contain machinery specific to it. Most of the apparent "gaps" above
are gaps in *naming* what the plan already does, not gaps in *doing*.

## Implication for the plan (no changes proposed here)

Figure 5-2 is the figure that proves, in one line, what the
implementation plan is built on: a mind needs more than one agent.
The plan honours the *conclusion* throughout. It does not yet name
the figure's particular shape â€” *the input whose consumption defeats
itself* â€” as a first-class concern, and it has no introspective
surface that would let an identity agency tell that story back about
its own run.

The gaps above are recorded here as analysis, not as a change

[`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md),

`THE-SOCIETY-OF-REPO/02-protocols/01-identity.md` â€” would touch the
schema, the activation protocol in
[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md),
and the governance vocabulary, and so falls under the "stop and ask"
