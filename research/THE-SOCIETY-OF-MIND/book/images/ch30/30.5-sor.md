# Figure 30-5 — Implementation analysis (SOR / FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION)

**Source figure:** [30-5.md](30-5.md) — *CAUSE, FREE WILL, CHANCE.*
**Analysis target:** [`FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/)
(plan-of-record for `.forgejo-society/` and
`.forgejo/workflows/forgejo-society.yaml`).

Figure 30-5 places a small *FREE WILL* between a large *CAUSE* and a
large *CHANCE*. Discoveries migrate outward — anything found to
follow a law joins CAUSE, anything found to be random joins CHANCE —
and the middle box shrinks. The chapter argues the middle box must
be retained anyway, because responsibility depends on it.

---

## The ideas Figure 30-5 actually carries

1. **Three categories, not two.** Behaviour is sorted into caused,
   willed, or chance.
2. **The middle category is residual.** It is what is left after
   *caused* and *random* have taken their share.
3. **The middle category shrinks over time.** Every successful
   explanation migrates something out of it.
4. **The middle is psychologically necessary.** Praise, blame and
   responsibility presuppose it; it cannot be discarded even as it
   shrinks.
5. **Sizes encode a stance.** Drawing CAUSE and CHANCE large and
   FREE WILL small is itself the argument.
6. **The connecting bars imply migration paths.** Things move between
   boxes; the diagram is about *flow*, not just classification.
7. **The figure is honest about its own pressure.** It admits that
   the middle is under attack from both sides.

---

## What the implementation already absorbs

### 1 — A clear "caused" track

The deterministic parts of the runtime — the workflow phases in
[`02-workflow-design.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/02-workflow-design.md),
the pipeline order in
[`03-runtime-pipeline.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/03-runtime-pipeline.md),
the policy gates in
[`07-policies-and-safety.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/07-policies-and-safety.md)
— are the plan's *CAUSE* box. Anything that always follows the same
path lives here.

### 2 — A clear "human" track

The `human` authority level
([`07-policies-and-safety.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/07-policies-and-safety.md))
is the plan's analogue of *FREE WILL*: a choice point that the
machinery itself refuses to resolve and routes to a person. It is
small, deliberate, and located between the deterministic and the
under-determined.

### 3 — Responsibility-bearing settlements

Settlements record *who decided what, with what evidence*
([`03-runtime-pipeline.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/03-runtime-pipeline.md),
[`09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md)).
That captures the chapter's insistence that responsibility must be
locatable — without it, praise and blame have nowhere to land.

### 4 — Auditability of migrations

Git history is the migration record. When a step that used to be a
human decision is encoded into a critic or a policy, the change is
visible in the commit log
([`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md)).
The figure's "things move from FREE WILL into CAUSE" claim has an
operational mirror: an `act`-level rule today was a `human`-level
escalation yesterday.

## What the implementation does not yet take into account

### A — There is no *CHANCE* track

The plan acknowledges deterministic flow and human decision but has
no first-class treatment of *non-deterministic* behaviour from
language-model agencies. Sampling temperature, stochastic ordering,
and retry randomness exist in practice; nothing in
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md)
or
[`09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md)
tags a signal as *chance-sourced* the way an authority level tags a
decision as `human`. The right-hand box of Figure 30-5 has no
schema-level analogue.

### B — No measurement of the middle's size

The figure's whole argument depends on noticing that the middle is
shrinking. The plan has no metric that counts "how many decisions
this run required `human` authority." Nothing in
[`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md)
tracks the ratio of escalations over time. The migration is real;
the measurement is absent.

### C — Migration is one-way only

Git history happily records "this used to be `human`, now it is
`act`." It does not record "this used to be `act`, now it must
become `human` again" — for example, after a regression that proves
a critic was wrong. Authority levels are designed to be tightened,
not loosened, in
[`07-policies-and-safety.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/07-policies-and-safety.md).
Figure 30-5's flows are bidirectional in principle.

### D — No defence of the middle as a policy

The chapter argues that the middle category must be *preserved* even
as it shrinks. The plan has no rule that protects `human` authority
from being optimised away — no minimum ratio, no critic that fires
when "everything is now automated" is detected. The middle is
preserved only by individual manifest authors not deleting their
`human` escalations.

### E — No critic for "you are pretending this was a free choice"

A subtle reading of the figure is that some things claimed for FREE
WILL are really CAUSE or CHANCE in disguise. The plan has no critic
that audits settlements for this — e.g. "you marked this `human` but
the human had no real options." `critic.evidence` and
`critic.scope` come closest but address different questions.

### F — Sizes do not appear in the schema

The figure's stance is encoded by *box size*. Authority levels in
the plan are an enum; they have no associated weight or proportion.
Two manifests with the same authority level read as equally weighty,
which is not what Figure 30-5 says about the realm it sits in.

## Implication for the plan (no changes proposed here)

Figure 30-5 is the *responsibility* figure: a small, defended middle
between caused and random behaviour. The plan owns the small middle
(via the `human` authority level) and the leftward migration (via
git history). It does not name the right-hand *CHANCE* track, does
not measure the middle's shrinkage, does not allow loosening, and
does not protect the middle as a matter of policy. Closing those
gaps would touch the safety surface in
[`07-policies-and-safety.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/07-policies-and-safety.md),
the signal schema in
[`09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md),
the state and memory layout in
[`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md),
and the authority registry under
`THE-SOCIETY-OF-REPO/01-governance/`, and so falls under the "stop
and ask" rules in
[`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md) §12 and
[`CLAUDE.md`](../../../../../CLAUDE.md).
