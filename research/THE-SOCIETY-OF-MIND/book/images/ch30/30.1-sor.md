# Figure 30-1 — Implementation analysis (SOR / FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION)

**Source figure:** [30-1.md](30-1.md) — *Epigraph from G. K. Chesterton
closing the section on mental models.*
**Analysis target:** [`FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/)
(plan-of-record for `.forgejo-society/` and
`.forgejo/workflows/forgejo-society.yaml`).

Figure 30-1 is a boxed quotation, not a diagram of mechanism. The
analysis below treats the epigraph as a *stance* the chapter takes
about models, and asks where that stance is — and is not — visible in
the implementation plan.

---

## The ideas Figure 30-1 actually carries

1. **Sentimentality as a working substrate.** The quote licenses
   imperfect, affect-laden models as practical tools, not as defects
   to be removed.
2. **Action requires commitment beyond evidence.** A "perfectly
   rational lover" never marries; a "perfectly rational army" runs
   away. Models that refuse to commit do not yield action.
3. **Practicality is the test.** A model is judged by whether
   another agency can use it to do something, not by whether it
   matches reality.
4. **The chapter's licence to use approximate self-models.** The
   epigraph closes §*mental models*; it justifies the rest of
   Chapter 30 building on incomplete world models, self-models, and
   models of models.
5. **An external authority is invoked.** The epigraph hands the
   chapter's stance to a named human (Chesterton) rather than
   defending it internally — a rhetorical move about *whose voice*
   the section ends on.
6. **No mechanism is depicted.** Unlike most figures in the book,
   30-1 contains no agents, no arrows, no boxes — only a citation in
   italic type.

## What the implementation already absorbs

### 1 — Acting under uncertainty is the default

[`03-runtime-pipeline.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/03-runtime-pipeline.md)
and [`09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md)
do not require a unit to be "certain" before it emits a signal;
confidence is a field, not a gate. The `settle` step in
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md)
chooses an action over an evidence set that is explicitly allowed to
be partial. This matches the epigraph's claim that workable models
need not be complete to be acted on.

### 2 — Practicality as the test of a unit

The first-ship catalogues in
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md)
are scoped by *what they let the run do next*, not by whether they
hold a faithful theory of their domain. `patch-imaginer` is justified
by its output (a proposed diff), not by accuracy.

### 3 — External authority is preserved as a level

Authority levels include `human`
([`07-policies-and-safety.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/07-policies-and-safety.md)),
which is the operational analogue of "this stance comes from outside
the society." A quoted human voice in a manifest, or a `human`-level
override, is a first-class part of governance rather than a special
case.

## What the implementation does not yet take into account

### A — No representation of an "epigraph" or motto in a manifest

The figure's whole content is an *attributed quotation* that frames a
section. The plan has no slot for an agency, family, or workflow to
carry a motto, an epigraph, or an attributed quotation. A unit cannot
declare:

```yaml
epigraph:
  text: "..."
  attributed_to: "..."
```

So the rhetorical move Figure 30-1 makes — handing a stance to a
named external voice at the close of a section — is not modelled.

### B — "Sentiment" is not a property the schema knows about

Confidence, budget, authority and family are first-class fields in
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md);
affect is not. The epigraph's substantive claim — that *sentiment*
is what gets things done — has no operational analogue. There is no
`agency.fear.*`, no `agency.attachment.*`, no signal field for
emotional charge, and the first-ship roster does not include affect
units beyond the narrowly-scoped `critic.fear.*` and
`blast-radius-fear`.

### C — No agency is allowed to be "irrational on purpose"

Critics and censors are written as filters that *raise* the rigour of
a candidate action
([`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md),
[`07-policies-and-safety.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/07-policies-and-safety.md)).
Nothing in the plan declares a role whose job is to *commit* under
under-evidenced conditions — the operational counterpart of the
"lover" or the "army" in the quote. The closest existing roles
(`agency.code.patch-imaginer`, `agency.code.revert-path-finder`) are
imaginers, not committers.

### D — No "stop deliberating" pressure

The epigraph implies that a rational process which never terminates
is worse than an imperfect one that does. The plan has
`budget.max_tool_calls` and `budget.max_wall_clock_s`
([`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md))
as hard caps, but no *positive* pressure to act before exhausting
them — no "if you have deliberated past threshold T, settle on the
current best candidate" rule in the workflow phase chain in
[`02-workflow-design.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/02-workflow-design.md).

### E — Source attribution for prose stances

The repository quotes Minsky, Chesterton, and others throughout the
introduction essays, but the implementation plan does not require
that *agency manifests* attribute the stance they encode. A manifest
can carry an arbitrary prose body
([`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md))
with no field for `sources:` or `derived_from:`. Figure 30-1's whole
point is that the *source* of a stance is part of the stance.

## Implication for the plan (no changes proposed here)

Figure 30-1 is a stance-setting epigraph, not a diagram. The
implementation plan absorbs its weaker reading (units may act on
incomplete evidence) but not its stronger one (sentiment, commitment,
and attributed external voice are first-class parts of how a society
gets things done). Closing those gaps would touch the manifest schema
in
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md),
the safety surface in
[`07-policies-and-safety.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/07-policies-and-safety.md),
and the workflow phase chain in
[`02-workflow-design.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/02-workflow-design.md),
and so falls under the "stop and ask" rules in
[`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md) §12 and
[`CLAUDE.md`](../../../../../CLAUDE.md).
