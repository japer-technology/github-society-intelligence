# Figure 30-3 — Implementation analysis (SOR / FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION)

**Source figure:** [30-3.md](30-3.md) — *Models of models nested
inside Mary's brain (model of self, model of model of world).*
**Analysis target:** [`FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/)
(plan-of-record for `.forgejo-society/` and
`.forgejo/workflows/forgejo-society.yaml`).

Figure 30-3 extends [30-2.md](30-2.md) by adding two new sub-regions
inside the world model: a *self-model* (body / mind) and a *model of
the model itself* (physical / mental). The analysis below checks each
layer against the plan.

---

## The ideas Figure 30-3 actually carries

1. **Models are recursive.** The world model contains other models,
   including a model of itself.
2. **A self-model is one of the contained things.** The agent's
   picture of itself is *inside* its picture of the world.
3. **The self-model is partitioned.** Body and mind appear as two
   contained ovals.
4. **There is a meta-model.** A *model of the world model* sits
   alongside the self-model, partitioned into *physical* and
   *mental*.
5. **Recursion stops by drawing.** Only two extra levels are shown;
   the figure does not chase the regress further.
6. **The labels of the inner partitions echo the outer ones.** *body*
   maps to *physical*, *mind* to *mental* — the meta-model uses the
   self-model's own vocabulary.
7. **Direct examination is impossible.** The figure asserts that
   Mary cannot inspect the world or herself directly; the nested
   ovals are *what she can inspect*.

---

## What the implementation already absorbs

### 1 — One level of reflection is named

The `self-modification` frame in
[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md)
and `agency.meta-admin.differentiation-broker` in
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md)
treat the society as an object the society can change. That is one
level of "model of the model," in the sense of *the society
reasoning about its own units*.

### 2 — Summaries are models of memory

The summary tiers in
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md)
and the memory layout in
[`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md)
are explicit *condensed pictures of stored material* — a model of
memory rather than memory itself. This matches the figure's
"model-of-the-model" shape at the storage layer.

### 3 — Repository introspection is permitted

Agencies can read `.forgejo-society/` and the workflow file itself
([`03-runtime-pipeline.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/03-runtime-pipeline.md),
[`07-policies-and-safety.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/07-policies-and-safety.md)).
The society can therefore look at its own manifests, which is the
operational form of "Mary can examine her self-model."

## What the implementation does not yet take into account

### A — Self-model has no schema

The plan has no `self_model:` block, no `agencies/self/` family, and
no signal kind that carries a current self-description. Manifests
describe individual units; nothing in
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md)
or
[`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md)
holds the society's picture of itself as a single addressable object
the way Figure 30-3 draws *Mary's model of herself*.

### B — Body / mind partition has no operational analogue

The figure splits the self-model into *body* (substrate) and *mind*
(activity). The plan has substrate (workspace, memory, runner,
forge) and activity (agencies, critics, censors), but they are not
unified under a "self-model" with two named halves. There is no
`self.substrate` vs `self.activity` field in any schema, and no
agency whose job is to keep that two-part picture current.

### C — Meta-model (model of the model) is not first-class

The summary tiers do model *memory*; nothing models *the society's
own model of itself*. There is no `memory.meta-model.*` or
`agency.meta-admin.self-portrait`. The figure's lower oval —
*Mary's model of her model of the world*, with its *physical /
mental* partition — has no counterpart in
[`11-mapping-sor-to-implementation.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/11-mapping-sor-to-implementation.md).

### D — Reflection is bounded at one level, by silence

Figure 30-3 stops at two extra levels deliberately, by drawing. The
plan stops at one level *implicitly*, because no recursion is named.
There is no field that says "depth-of-reflection allowed = 1" and no
critic that fires when an agency tries to reason about its own
reasoning about its own reasoning. The shape is enforced by absence
rather than by policy in
[`07-policies-and-safety.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/07-policies-and-safety.md).

### E — Vocabulary re-use across levels is not enforced

The figure makes its central point partly by *re-using vocabulary*:
the meta-model's *physical / mental* mirror the self-model's *body /
mind*. The plan has no mechanism that requires the same names at
different levels of reflection. A society could describe its
substrate as "infrastructure" in one manifest and as "host" in
another with no critic noticing.

### F — "Direct examination is impossible" is not declared

The chapter's stance — Mary's only access to herself is via her
models — has no operational counterpart. Agencies in the plan
*directly* read each other's manifests and signals; nothing forces
self-knowledge to flow through a `self-model` retrieval step. The
figure presupposes an opacity the plan does not enforce.

## Implication for the plan (no changes proposed here)

Figure 30-3 is the *recursion* figure. It claims a society needs a
self-model and a model-of-its-model, and that both are partitioned
and bounded. The plan absorbs only the weakest reading: the society
can look at its own manifests, and memory can be summarised. Naming
a self-model layer, partitioning it, and giving it a meta-layer would
touch the manifest schema in
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md),
the memory layout in
[`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md),
and the identity scopes in `THE-SOCIETY-OF-REPO/02-protocols/01-identity.md`,
and so falls under the "stop and ask" rules in
[`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md) §12 and
[`CLAUDE.md`](../../../../../CLAUDE.md).
