# Figure 30-4 — Implementation analysis (SOR / FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION)

**Source figure:** [30-4.md](30-4.md) — *Body and mind as two realms,
joined by a thin link.*
**Analysis target:** [`FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/)
(plan-of-record for `.forgejo-society/` and
`.forgejo/workflows/forgejo-society.yaml`).

Figure 30-4 reduces the lower oval of [30-3.md](30-3.md) to its most
stripped-down form: two boxes — BODY (Physical Realm) and MIND
(Mental Realm) — joined by a single short bar. It is a diagram about
the *shape of self-reports*, not a metaphysical claim.

---

## The ideas Figure 30-4 actually carries

1. **Self-reports cluster into two domains.** When the agent
   describes itself, descriptions sort into a physical and a mental
   pile.
2. **The two clusters are unequal in obvious connection.** The link
   between them is one thin bar — there is little to say about how
   the realms join.
3. **The shape is dumbbell, not gradient.** Few intermediate
   categories appear between the two boxes.
4. **The figure is about reports, not about being.** It models the
   *structure of what Mary says* about herself, not a claim about
   what Mary *is*.
5. **The labels are caricatures.** BODY and MIND are deliberately
   broad; the figure is honest about its own coarseness.
6. **It motivates a problem.** Anything that depends on connection
   between body and mind — voluntary action, sensation — has to be
   carried across one thin bar.

---

## What the implementation already absorbs

### 1 — Substrate and activity are kept distinct

[`00-overview.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/00-overview.md)
and
[`02-workflow-design.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/02-workflow-design.md)
clearly separate substrate (Forgejo, runner, workspace, memory,
filesystem) from activity (workflow phases, agencies, critics,
censors). The forge is the body; the units that run inside it are
the mind. This matches Figure 30-4's two-realm shape at the
project's largest scale.

### 2 — The connection between them is narrow and named

The censor surface in
[`07-policies-and-safety.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/07-policies-and-safety.md)
is exactly the thin bar in the figure. Anything an agency wants to do
*to* the substrate (write a file, push a commit, call out) goes
through a small, named, audited set of censors. The figure's claim
that the link between realms is narrow is mirrored by the plan
keeping it narrow on purpose.

### 3 — Self-reports are textual artefacts

Settlements
([`03-runtime-pipeline.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/03-runtime-pipeline.md))
and summaries
([`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md))
are how a run reports on itself. They are written, stored, and read
back — which matches the figure's framing that *self-knowledge is
report-shaped*.

## What the implementation does not yet take into account

### A — No explicit "body / mind" vocabulary

The plan never names the substrate / activity split as *body* and
*mind*. They are not fields, they are not authority levels, and no
manifest declares which realm it belongs to. The two-realm picture
is implicit in the directory layout but not first-class in any
schema in
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md).

### B — The "thin bar" is not enumerated

Figure 30-4's whole point is that *the connector is small and worth
counting*. The plan has many censors and many tool surfaces
([`07-policies-and-safety.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/07-policies-and-safety.md));
nothing collates them into a single "this is the entire body / mind
interface" listing. A future reviewer cannot ask "show me everything
that crosses the boundary" without grepping.

### C — No critic for "this report is all-physical or all-mental"

The figure presupposes that self-reports are biased toward one
realm. There is no critic in
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md)
that watches a settlement and flags "this run reported only what it
ran, not what state it ran in" or vice versa. The first-ship roster
focuses on evidence and scope, not on the *balance* of physical vs
mental content in self-reports.

### D — No representation of the dumbbell shape itself

Figure 30-4 is, geometrically, a claim that the *shape* of
self-knowledge matters: two clusters and a thin bar. The plan has no
schema for "shape of a settlement" or "topology of a summary." The
shape is whatever the prose body and signals happen to produce.

### E — Voluntary action across the bar is unnamed

The figure motivates the gap between *deciding* (mind) and *moving*
(body). In the plan, the analogue is the gap between `settle`
(decision) and `act` (execution)
([`02-workflow-design.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/02-workflow-design.md),
[`03-runtime-pipeline.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/03-runtime-pipeline.md)).
That gap is *present*, but it is not named as a body / mind boundary
crossing; the audit log records the censor that fired, not the
realm-crossing event as such.

### F — No "models of the dumbbell"

Figure 30-3 supplies the meta-layer that produces Figure 30-4. The
plan supplies neither the dumbbell nor a model of it; see gap C in
[30.3-sor.md](30.3-sor.md). 30-4's shape is therefore unreachable
even in principle from current schemas.

## Implication for the plan (no changes proposed here)

Figure 30-4 is the *interface* figure: two realms, one thin bar. The
implementation plan already keeps the bar narrow (censors) and the
realms separate (substrate vs activity), which is the figure's
operational content. It does not give the realms names, enumerate
the bar as a single surface, or model the dumbbell shape itself.
Closing those gaps would touch the manifest schema in
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md),
the safety surface in
[`07-policies-and-safety.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/07-policies-and-safety.md),
and the runtime pipeline in
[`03-runtime-pipeline.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/03-runtime-pipeline.md),
and so falls under the "stop and ask" rules in
[`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md) §12 and
[`CLAUDE.md`](../../../../../CLAUDE.md).
