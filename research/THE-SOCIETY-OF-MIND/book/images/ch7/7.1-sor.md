# Figure 7-1 — Implementation analysis (SOR / FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION)

**Source figure:** [7-1.md](7-1.md) — *A difference-engine.*
**Analysis target:** [`FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/)
(plan-of-record for `.forgejo-society/` and
`.forgejo/workflows/forgejo-society.yaml`).

This file inventories the ideas carried by Figure 7-1 and checks each
one against the implementation plan. The aim is to mark, plainly, what
the plan already absorbs and what it does not yet name.

---

## The ideas Figure 7-1 actually carries

Pulled from the figure caption and Minsky's surrounding text in §7.8
*Difference-engines*:

1. **Two separately maintained representations.** ACTUAL INPUTS feed a
   SITUATION; IDEAL INPUTS feed a GOAL-DESCRIPTION. The figure
   insists these are *different streams*, not one annotated view.
2. **A goal is a representation, not a wish.** GOAL-DESCRIPTION is a
   structure with the same kind of shape as SITUATION — comparable
   slot for slot.
3. **Difference as the unit of work.** A central node, DIFFERENCES,
   computes the *gap* between situation and goal. Cognition advances
   by reducing this gap, not by executing a plan.
4. **The difference vector is itself addressable.** The fan-out shows
   DIFFERENCES as a *thing* — something that can be inspected,
   logged, and routed, not a transient intermediate.
5. **Agent selection is driven by the difference, not by the goal.**
   Each output arrow carries one difference signal to one agent. The
   agents do not consult the goal; they consult what is missing.
6. **Agents are specialised by difference-type.** The column of
   AGENT triangles implies a catalogue indexed by *kind of
   difference* ("wrong colour", "missing block", "block in wrong
   place"), not by domain.
7. **Goal-pursuit without any agent knowing what a goal is.** The
   society as a whole is goal-directed; no individual member needs to
   represent the goal. This is the figure's central claim.
8. **An implied loop.** A difference-engine only makes sense if it
   re-measures after each agent fires. SITUATION must be re-read,
   DIFFERENCES recomputed, and a new agent selected, until the
   difference vector is empty.
9. **Bridge from GPS to society.** The figure redraws Newell and
   Simon's general problem-solver as a society fragment, asserting
   that classical means-ends analysis is *one shape an agency can
   take*, not a competitor to society architecture.

These nine items are what the rest of this file tests against the
implementation plan.

## What the implementation already absorbs

### 1 — Situation as a first-class file

[`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md)
gives every run a `state/mind/issues/<n>/workspace.md` described as the
"human-readable current situation", plus a `blackboard.md` for layered
cognitive state and `percepts.jsonl` for raw observations. The
SITUATION box on the left of Figure 7-1 has a clear operational
counterpart: the workspace tree is what the runtime perceives the
world to be.

### 2 — Goal-shaped slots inside frames

[`11-mapping-sor-to-implementation.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/11-mapping-sor-to-implementation.md)
makes the mapping explicit: *"difference engines | implicit in frame
`slots:` (required slots == differences from desired state)"*.
[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md)
defines `slots:` with `required: true|false` and `filled_by: [<agent-id>, ...]`.
An unfilled required slot is, in Minsky's sense, a difference between
the current situation and what the frame expects. The mapping is
honest about being *implicit* rather than named.

### 3 — Failure conditions as named differences

The same frame schema carries `failure_conditions: [<named-objection>, ...]`.
A failure condition is a *named* way in which SITUATION can diverge
from GOAL-DESCRIPTION. The plan therefore has a vocabulary for
talking about specific differences, even though it has no node that
computes them as a vector.

### 4 — Agent selection by activator

[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md)
routes activation through polynemes that `excite:` and `inhibit:`
agencies based on `paths`, `labels`, and `phrases`, and through frame
selection that picks `default_actions` and `default_critics`. Figure
7-1's "fan-out from a central node to specialised agents" has an
analogue here: activation is fanned out from perception to agencies.
The mechanism exists; what it dispatches on is different (see gap C).

### 5 — Re-measurement across the loop

[`03-runtime-pipeline.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/03-runtime-pipeline.md)
documents the chain `perceive → activate → deliberate → criticize →
censor → settle → act → remember → report`. After `act`, the next
stimulus triggers a fresh `perceive`. The pipeline therefore supports
*between-run* re-measurement, which is the coarsest form of Figure
7-1's loop.

### 6 — Goal-pursuit without per-agent goals

Most first-ship agencies in
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md)
(`patch-imaginer`, `diff-skeptic`, `kline-retriever`, …) are written
as specialists that respond to what is in front of them. They are not
written as goal-bearers. The society's goal-directedness is meant to
emerge from frames, settlement criteria, and approval gates, not from
any single agency. The figure's central claim is honoured in spirit.

## What the implementation does not yet take into account

These are the parts of Figure 7-1 that the plan currently leaves
implicit, partial, or simply absent. None are urgent; all are real.

### A — No GOAL-DESCRIPTION object

There is no file or schema for the goal-description analogue of
`workspace.md`. The desired state of a run is scattered across:

- the stimulus payload (`state/runs/<run>/stimulus.json`),
- the matching frame's `slots:` and `failure_conditions:`,
- the active settlement's pending fields, and
- the human's prose in the original issue or comment.

Nothing in `state/` or `workspace/` is named "goal", "ideal", or
"target-state". Figure 7-1's insistence that GOAL-DESCRIPTION is its
own box, fed by its own input stream, is not represented.

### B — No DIFFERENCES node

There is no runtime module that produces a structured difference
vector between situation and goal. `lifecycle/settle.ts` decides
whether the workspace is *ready* to act on, and critics raise
*objections*, but neither emits "the following slots are still empty
and the following invariants are still violated, expressed as a
typed list". `state/.../objections.jsonl` is the closest artefact,
and it is shaped as critic output, not as a difference computation.

### C — Activation is not dispatched by difference-type

Polynemes excite agencies on `paths`, `labels`, and `phrases`. Frames
pick `default_actions` from `matches`. None of these are *differences*.
The plan therefore cannot say:

```yaml
on_difference:
  - kind: missing-slot
    slot: proposed_patch
    excite: [ agency.code.patch-imaginer ]
  - kind: failed-invariant
    invariant: no-secret-in-diff
    excite: [ critic.evidence, censor.cloud-egress ]
```

The schema has no `on_difference:` and no taxonomy of difference
kinds. Agent selection is *correlated* with differences (a missing
patch tends to wake the patch-imaginer), but not *driven* by them.

### D — No in-run re-measurement loop

The pipeline phases run in a fixed sequence per stimulus. There is no
phase that, after `act`, recomputes SITUATION, recomputes DIFFERENCES,
and re-enters `deliberate` if differences remain. Iteration happens
*across* runs, not *within* one. Figure 7-1's tight inner loop —
"fire one agent, re-measure, pick the next" — is not a shape the
runtime supports today.

### E — No catalogue of difference-typed specialists

[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md)
indexes agencies by *family* (perception, memory, code, safety,
identity, integration, assembly, meta-admin). It does not index any
agency by the *kind of gap it closes*. There is no
`agency.code.fill-missing-tests` whose entire reason for existing is
"the `tests` slot is empty"; there is `patch-imaginer`, which produces
patches regardless of which slot is the binding constraint.

### F — `act` consults the settlement, not the gap

[`03-runtime-pipeline.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/03-runtime-pipeline.md)
shows `act` reading the settlement and `governance/approval-gate.yml`.
The settlement is the agreed outcome, not a residual difference. A
difference-engine would have `act` consume the difference vector,
pick the most-reducible one, and dispatch the corresponding agent.
The plan dispatches on settlement contents instead.

### G — IDEAL INPUTS as a stream has no equivalent

ACTUAL INPUTS has an obvious analogue: webhooks, repo state, prior
memory. IDEAL INPUTS does not. The plan has no notion of a continuous
stream of "what the situation should be" — no goal sensors, no
periodic ingestion of policy or contract documents into a desired-state
tree. Goal information enters only as part of a stimulus or as a
static frame.

### H — GPS-shaped agencies are not a named pattern

The figure asserts that a difference-engine *is* a society fragment,
i.e. that means-ends analysis is one legitimate internal shape for
an agency. The implementation plan has no named pattern for
"means-ends agency" — no template that says: a comparator member, a
difference-emitter member, a fan-out of difference-typed worker
members, and a re-measurement trigger. Building one today would
require composing pieces by hand.

## Implication for the plan (no changes proposed here)

Figure 7-1 is the figure that asks the implementation to treat *the
gap* — not the goal, not the plan — as the primary thing the runtime
manipulates. The current plan honours the *outcome* of difference-
reduction (specialists, frames, settlement criteria) while leaving the
*mechanism* (a goal-description object, a difference vector, a
within-run re-measurement loop, and a difference-typed dispatch) in
the implicit register.

The gaps above are recorded here as analysis, not as a change
request. Any move to close them — for example, a `goal.md` companion
to `workspace.md`, a `differences.jsonl` artefact and module, an
`on_difference:` field on agencies, a `reduce` phase between `act`
and `remember`, or a difference-typed agency taxonomy — would touch
the state layout in [`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md),
the pipeline in [`03-runtime-pipeline.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/03-runtime-pipeline.md),
the manifest schema in [`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md),
the activation protocol in [`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md),
and the SOR-to-implementation mapping in
[`11-mapping-sor-to-implementation.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/11-mapping-sor-to-implementation.md),
and so falls under the "stop and ask" rules in
[`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md) §12 and
[`CLAUDE.md`](../../../../../CLAUDE.md).
