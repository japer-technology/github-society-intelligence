# Figure 27-4 — Implementation analysis (SOR / FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION)

**Source figure:** [27-4.md](27-4.md) — *Epigraph from Emily Dickinson
("sequence ravelled out of reach").*
**Analysis target:** [`FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/)
(plan-of-record for `.forgejo-society/` and
`.forgejo/workflows/forgejo-society.yaml`).

Figure 27-4 is an epigraph, not a diagram. The ideas it carries are
those Minsky uses it to license at the head of the section on
*exceptions to logic*. This file inventories those ideas and checks
each against the implementation plan.

---

## The ideas Figure 27-4 actually carries

1. **Reasoning is a sequence.** Thoughts are joined to prior
   thoughts; the chain itself is the unit of interest.
2. **The sequence ravels.** Long chains come apart in ordinary use,
   not as a pathology.
3. **The cause is not malice but exception.** Facts that hold *in
   general* fail at the boundary, and the chain rests on those
   facts.
4. **Local consistency is preserved.** Each link, taken alone, still
   looks sound; the failure is a property of the chain, not of any
   single step.
5. **Islands of consistency are the practical refuge.** Where the
   facts hold, reasoning is reliable; outside, it is not.
6. **Boundaries must be marked.** An island is only useful if its
   coastline is visible.
7. **Pursuing complete logic is a category error.** No real-world
   statement is exception-free, so chains built as if it were are
   guaranteed to ravel.
8. **The poetic image is itself the argument.** Dickinson's *balls
   upon the floor* substitutes for a proof; the chapter accepts the
   image as load-bearing.

## What the implementation already absorbs

### 1 — Bounded scope per frame

Frames (see
[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md)
and
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md))
are the plan's "islands of consistency." A frame names a small,
coherent context — `code-change`, `security-sensitive`,
`self-modification` — and may lower authority within it. Reasoning
is meant to happen *inside* a frame, not across the whole society
in one chain.

### 2 — Boundaries are marked

Danger zones in
[`07-policies-and-safety.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/07-policies-and-safety.md)
enumerate paths and content patterns where the ordinary rules no
longer apply: `workflow_mutation`, `soul_mutation`,
`governance_mutation`, `agency_mutation`, `secret_handling`,
`memory_mutation`. These are the marked coastlines of the islands.

### 3 — Short chains by construction

The workflow phase chain (`guard → normalize → guardrail → perceive
→ activate → deliberate → criticize → censor → settle → act →
remember → report`) is intentionally short and named. There is no
phase that performs open-ended chained inference; each step's
outputs are written as signals and read by the next, which keeps
the sequence from quietly extending.

### 4 — Local consistency, audited

Every step records its result — `signals.jsonl`, `objections.jsonl`,
`guardrail.json`, settlement files — so a chain that turns out to
have raveled can be reconstructed by reading back, link by link.
The plan does not prevent ravelling; it makes the unravelled
sequence available for inspection.

### 5 — Reversion as the response to ravelling

The `code-change` and `security-sensitive` frames require
`agency.code.revert-path-finder` as a slot filler
([`07-policies-and-safety.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/07-policies-and-safety.md)).
The plan does not assume chains hold; it assumes any chain may
need to be backed out, and refuses to act without an explicit
revert path. That is Dickinson's premise turned into an operational
precondition.

## What the implementation does not yet take into account

### A — No formal concept of an *island of consistency*

The plan has *frames*, *danger zones*, and *agencies*, but it does
not have a primitive that says "this set of facts is treated as
exception-free for the duration of this scope." A frame lowers
authority; it does not declare which facts may be relied on. There
is no `frame.invariants:` list a critic could check, and no
mechanism by which crossing out of a frame voids derivations that
depended on those invariants.

### B — Chains across frames are not detected

Dickinson's ravel happens *between* the thought-before and the
thought-after — across the boundary. The plan has no detector that
notices when a settlement's reasoning depends on signals produced
under one frame's invariants but acted on under another. Cross-
frame inference is allowed silently.

### C — No exception-tracking on inherited facts

The plan's memory
([`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md))
stores K-lines, decisions, and settlements as durable facts. There
is no field on a memory item that records *known exceptions*, *known
boundaries of validity*, or *expiry conditions*. A fact recorded
during one stimulus is available, with no qualification, during the
next.

### D — No critic for "the chain is too long"

[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md)
catalogues critics by content (`critic.evidence`, `critic.scope`,
`critic.security`, …), not by chain shape. There is no critic that
warns *this settlement's justification depends on N intermediate
inferences, several of which are outside the active frame*.
Dickinson's worry — that the *length* and *crossing* of the chain
is itself the problem — has no first-class voice.

### E — No marker for "outside the island"

When an agency is asked to act on a stimulus that falls outside
every defined frame, the plan defaults to read/draft authority via
the authority registry, but it does not raise a distinct *outside
known consistency* signal. The condition Dickinson is describing —
that the operator is now in territory where ordinary reasoning is
unreliable — is not represented.

### F — Poetic / non-propositional evidence has no home

The plan accepts manifests, signals, diffs, and settlements. It has
no slot for an *image* or *quotation* that, in Minsky's chapter,
does real work in framing what counts as a reasonable response.
The epigraph's status as *load-bearing* in the source has no
operational analogue.

### G — Settlements do not declare their scope of validity

A settlement records what was decided and why
([`03-runtime-pipeline.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/03-runtime-pipeline.md),
[`09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md)).
It does not record *under which conditions this decision is meant
to hold* — which is precisely the marking Dickinson's argument
calls for. A settlement is therefore as available outside its
island as inside.

## Implication for the plan (no changes proposed here)

Figure 27-4 is the figure that licenses the chapter's argument
against logic-completeness by quoting a poem. The plan honours the
*defensive* posture — bound the scope, mark the dangerous edges,
require a revert path — but does not yet honour the *epistemic*
posture: that some facts hold only inside an island, that crossing
out voids derivations, and that chain length is itself a hazard
worth a critic.

Any move to close the gaps above — a `frame.invariants:` list, a
cross-frame inference detector, an `exceptions:` field on memory
items, a `critic.chain-length` or `critic.cross-frame`, an
*outside-known-consistency* signal kind, or a `scope_of_validity`
field on settlements — would touch the manifest schema in
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md),
the frame and K-line model in
[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md),
the memory layout in
[`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md),
and the signal schema in
[`09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md),
and so falls under the "stop and ask" rules in
[`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md) §12 and
[`CLAUDE.md`](../../../../../CLAUDE.md).
