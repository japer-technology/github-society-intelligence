# Figure 27-3 — Implementation analysis (SOR / FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION)

**Source figure:** [27-3.md](27-3.md) — *Censor and suppressor in the
stream of states.*
**Analysis target:** [`FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/)
(plan-of-record for `.forgejo-society/` and
`.forgejo/workflows/forgejo-society.yaml`).

This file inventories the ideas carried by Figure 27-3 and checks each
against the implementation plan. The aim is to mark, plainly, what the
plan already absorbs and what it does not yet name.

---

## The ideas Figure 27-3 actually carries

1. **Two intercepting agents live on the same trajectory.** A censor
   at the branch point, a suppressor on the last arrow.
2. **The trajectory is a *fan-out* from a previous state.** The
   present state is one of several possible next states, selected at
   a branch.
3. **A censor acts at the branch.** It deflects the choice toward an
   acceptable next state, before any candidate action is formed.
4. **A suppressor acts at the action edge.** It blocks an already-
   chosen candidate at the last possible moment.
5. **Earlier is cheaper.** A cut at the branch avoids forming the
   bad candidate at all; a cut at the action edge wastes the work
   already done.
6. **Earlier is harder to learn.** A censor must recognise *previous
   states* — a much larger class than the single edges a suppressor
   watches.
7. **The two work together, not as substitutes.** The figure shows
   *both* present. Censors do not retire suppressors; they reduce
   how often suppressors fire.
8. **The selection of next state is itself the cognitive event.**
   The figure makes the *branch* a labelled mechanism, not an
   incidental step.
9. **State multiplicity is finite and enumerable.** The figure draws
   five candidate next states. A censor has to discriminate among a
   bounded set, not over an open world.

## What the implementation already absorbs

### 1 — Censors and suppressors as separate layers on one path

[`07-policies-and-safety.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/07-policies-and-safety.md)
gives censors and suppressors separate rows in the layered safety
model. Censors fire *before tool grant* and *mechanically alter the
tool surface*; suppressors fire on each candidate output. The
figure's "two intercepts at two distinct points" is the schema's
shape.

### 2 — Censor acts before the candidate forms

The plan's censors do not wait for a diff; they alter what tools an
agency may use, before the agency runs. This is the operational
equivalent of "deflect at the branch": the bad next state is made
unreachable by removing the means to reach it.

### 3 — Mechanical, not advisory

`07-policies-and-safety.md` is explicit: *a safety agent should not
merely advise the LLM; it should mechanically alter the tool
surface.* This matches the figure's reading of the censor as a
structural change to the choice set, not a comment about it.

### 4 — Both layers present, neither retired

The plan ships both layers in the first-ship safety model, and both
log to the audit trail. Censor blocks go to `signals.jsonl`,
suppressor activations to `objections.jsonl`. The figure's
co-existence is reproduced.

### 5 — Bounded next-state set

The default tool surface is computed deterministically from
`authority_registry`, `write_policy`, `rights_registry`,
`danger_zones`, and active censors
([`07-policies-and-safety.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/07-policies-and-safety.md)).
The agency therefore selects its next action from an enumerable,
bounded set of allowed tools — the figure's five-branch fan-out in
operational form.

### 6 — Reality revisions as branches

[`07-policies-and-safety.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/07-policies-and-safety.md)
makes every write to `main` go through a `society/<stimulus_id>/
candidate-<n>` branch by default. Candidate futures are explicitly
enumerated, named, and retained as *rejected hypotheses* under
`memory/failure/rejected-candidates/`. The figure's "several possible
next states" idea is honoured at the reality-revision scale.

## What the implementation does not yet take into account

### A — No representation of the *previous state* a censor matches

The figure's censor recognises a *previous state* and steers away
from a known-bad next state. The plan's censors match on the
*stimulus* and the *candidate tool list*, not on a snapshot of the
cognitive condition that precedes the choice. There is no
`previous_state` object recorded between phases that a censor can
inspect; perception writes signals, activation reads them, but
nothing aggregates them into the *state* Minsky's censor would key
off.

### B — Censors do not enumerate the possible next states

The figure shows five branches with one selected. The plan's
censors *subtract* from a tool surface; they do not produce or
inspect a list of *candidate next steps*. There is no
`possible_next_states` artefact between `deliberate` and `act`. The
censor never sees the alternatives it is deflecting toward; it only
sees the disallowed options it removes.

### C — Earlier-is-cheaper is not measured

The figure's central argument is one of cost: censoring at the
branch is cheaper than suppressing at the edge. The plan tracks
token, tool-call, and wall-clock budgets per agency
([`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md))
but does not record *where on the path* a saving was made. A run
that was deflected by a censor and a run that was suppressed at the
edge are not comparable by cost in the current state schema.

### D — No automatic promotion from suppressor to censor

The figure invites the inference: *if this suppressor keeps firing
on the same edge, learn a censor for the previous state.* The plan
has no mechanism by which repeated `objections.jsonl` entries for a
single suppressor cause a new censor candidate to be drafted — even
as a ticket. The transfer of intercept from late to early is left
entirely to human review.

### E — Branch selection is not an audited event

In the figure the branch is a labelled mechanism — the censor sits
*there*. In the plan, the choice of next tool happens inside the
model and is observable only through which tool was actually
invoked. The *deflection* itself — "wanted to do X, did Y instead" —
is not a signal kind, because *what the model wanted* is not
represented.

### F — Censor coverage of the state space is unmeasured

The figure shows the censor handling five branches; in practice the
branch fan-out is wider. The plan does not estimate *how much of the
state space* a given censor covers, or report when a stimulus class
falls outside any censor's known set. There is no notion of *blind
spots* in the censor catalogue.

### G — No notion of "an acceptable next state"

The figure's censor steers *toward* acceptable next states. The
plan's censors only steer *away from* disallowed ones. There is no
manifest field that lists, for a censor, the set of next steps it
considers good — only the set of tools it removes. Positive
direction is left implicit in the agency catalogues.

## Implication for the plan (no changes proposed here)

Figure 27-3 is the figure that turns the safety story into a *layout
problem*: where on the path is each intercept, and what does it
cost to put it there? The plan adopts the right *layers* and the
right *positions*, but does not yet record the geometry the figure
makes central — the previous state, the enumerated next states, the
deflection event, and the cost saving from moving an intercept
earlier.

Any move to close the gaps above — a `previous_state` snapshot, a
`possible_next_states` artefact between `deliberate` and `act`, a
`deflected_from/deflected_to` signal kind, a censor-coverage report,
or an automated proposer that drafts censors from recurring
suppressor activations — would touch the signal schema in
[`09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md),
the censor and suppressor catalogues in
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md)
and
[`07-policies-and-safety.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/07-policies-and-safety.md),
the runtime pipeline in
[`03-runtime-pipeline.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/03-runtime-pipeline.md),
and the memory layout in
[`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md),
and so falls under the "stop and ask" rules in
[`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md) §12 and
[`CLAUDE.md`](../../../../../CLAUDE.md).
