# Figure 20-6 — Implementation analysis (SOR / FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION)

**Source figure:** [20-6.md](20-6.md) — *transmitters, connectors,
receivers.*
**Analysis target:** [`FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/)
(plan-of-record for `.forgejo-society/` and
`.forgejo/workflows/forgejo-society.yaml`).

This file inventories the ideas carried by Figure 20-6 and checks each
one against the implementation plan. The aim is to mark, plainly, what
the plan already absorbs and what it does not yet name.

---

## The ideas Figure 20-6 actually carries

Pulled from the figure caption and Minsky's surrounding text in §20.9
*Distributed memory*:

1. **Three explicit roles: transmitter, connector, receiver.** The
   memory substrate decomposes into senders, routers, and listeners.
2. **Transmitters are K-line-like.** They broadcast a stored state.
3. **Receivers are recognisers tuned to combinations.** They fire
   on patterns of incoming signal, not on single sources.
4. **Connectors are the mediating web.** They carry traffic without
   themselves choosing what to send or what to recognise.
5. **The three are independently learnable.** *What to send*, *how
   it reaches its destination*, and *what to recognise* are
   separable problems.
6. **Many-to-many fan-out.** A single transmitter reaches several
   receivers; a single receiver listens to several transmitters.
7. **Continued flow outward.** Receivers are not terminals; they
   feed onward to other agents.

These seven items are what the rest of this file tests against the
implementation plan.

## What the implementation already absorbs

### 1 — K-lines as transmitters

[`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md)
and the `agency.memory.*` family in
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md)
treat K-lines as the unit that re-broadcasts a past state. The
`kline-retriever` agency is operationally a transmitter in Figure
20-6's sense.

### 2 — Signals as the carried traffic

The signal envelope defined in
[`09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md)
is the wire-level object that flows between agencies, which maps to
the arrows in Figure 20-6.

### 3 — Activation-on-patterns as receiver behaviour

`activates_on:` clauses in agency manifests
([`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md))
let an agency fire on a *combination* of incoming signals, labels,
paths, and phrases. Receivers in the figure's sense exist.

### 4 — Continued onward flow

[`03-runtime-pipeline.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/03-runtime-pipeline.md)
shows that signals propagate across phases; receivers feed
`deliberate`, `criticize`, `censor`, etc. The "to other agents"
arrows in Figure 20-6 are honoured at the pipeline level.

### 5 — Many-to-many is the normal case

Multiple perception agencies can fire on one input, and a single
downstream agency can wake on combinations of upstream signals. The
fan-out / fan-in topology of the figure is the plan's default.

## What the implementation does not yet take into account

### A — *Connector* is not a named role

The plan has agencies (transmitter / receiver shapes) and the signal
bus (the wire), but no documented unit that owns *routing* as its
sole job. The runtime delivers signals based on `activates_on:`
clauses; there is no `agency.memory.connector` or `service.router`
documented as a separable concern.

### B — The three independent learning problems are fused

Figure 20-6 separates "what to send", "how it reaches its
destination", and "what to recognise" so they can be improved
independently. In the plan, these three are co-located inside each
agency's manifest (prompt body, `activates_on:`, output behaviour).
There is no schema slot that names which of the three is being
modified by a given change.

### C — No tunable threshold per receiver

Receivers in the figure fire on weighted combinations. The plan's
`activates_on:` clauses are largely boolean or pattern-match; the
plan does not document per-agency thresholds, weights, or
confidence scores on incoming signals.

### D — Routing is not a first-class observable

Because connectors are not named, the trail of "which path did this
signal take from transmitter to receiver" is not recorded as such.
The settlement records which agencies fired, not which routing path
delivered the activation.

### E — Memory side is more developed than the routing side

The `memory` family is explicitly documented; an equivalent
`routing` or `connector` family is not. As a consequence, changes
to how signals reach receivers tend to be made *inside* receiver
manifests rather than in a dedicated routing layer.

### F — No protocol for transmitter / receiver pairing audit

Figure 20-6 implies a learnable pairing: this transmitter usefully
wakes this receiver. The plan keeps K-lines and keeps signal logs,
but does not document a pairing-level audit object that says "these
two units are reliably co-active."

## Implication for the plan (no changes proposed here)

Figure 20-6 says that memory is *three* separable parts, and that the
middle part — routing — deserves its own name so the other two can
be reasoned about cleanly. The implementation plan has the endpoints
and has the wire, but does not yet name the connector layer or
separate the three learning problems.

The gaps above are recorded as analysis. Any move to close them — an
`agency.memory.connector` family, a `signal.weight` / `threshold`
field, a routing-path entry in the settlement audit, or a documented
split between *transmit*, *route*, and *receive* concerns — would
touch the manifest schema in
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md),
the frames protocol in
[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md),
the state model in
[`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md),
the handoff schemas in
[`09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md),
and the identity scopes in `THE-SOCIETY-OF-REPO/02-protocols/01-identity.md`,
and so falls under the "stop and ask" rules in
[`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md) §12 and
[`CLAUDE.md`](../../../../../CLAUDE.md).
