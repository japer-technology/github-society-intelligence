# Figure 20-4 — Implementation analysis (SOR / FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION)

**Source figure:** [20-4.md](20-4.md) — *the nemeic spiral of an
individual mind.*
**Analysis target:** [`FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/)
(plan-of-record for `.forgejo-society/` and
`.forgejo/workflows/forgejo-society.yaml`).

This file inventories the ideas carried by Figure 20-4 and checks each
one against the implementation plan. The aim is to mark, plainly, what
the plan already absorbs and what it does not yet name.

---

## The ideas Figure 20-4 actually carries

Pulled from the figure caption and Minsky's surrounding text in §20.6
*The nemeic spiral*:

1. **A nemeic network is roughly hierarchical but not strictly so.**
   Most connections respect levels; many short-circuit them.
2. **Higher nemes are more language-linked.** Items near speech are
   easier to put into words because public speech disciplines them.
3. **Lower nemes are more idiosyncratic.** Concrete, action-near units
   are harder to communicate and more private to the individual.
4. **Cross-connections and shortcuts are normal.** Exceptions and
   bypasses are part of the structure, not flaws in it.
5. **Control spirals down into detail when work goes well.** Routine
   processing pushes activity toward concrete units.
6. **Control spirals up into diagnosis when it does not.** When
   something fails, attention moves toward higher, more
   describable layers.
7. **The direction of spiral is observable in behaviour.** Whether
   the system is "executing" or "explaining" is a function of which
   way control is currently moving.
8. **Naming and reportability are graded, not binary.** Units differ
   smoothly in how well they can be spoken about.

These eight items are what the rest of this file tests against the
implementation plan.

## What the implementation already absorbs

### 1 — Polynemes and K-lines as the nemeic substrate

[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md)
adopts polynemes and K-lines as first-class objects. The figure's
"nemeic network" is the right name for the graph these objects form
in the runtime.

### 2 — Family hierarchy in `.forgejo-society/agencies/`

[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md)
groups agencies by family (perception, memory, code, safety, identity,
integration, assembly, meta-admin). This is a coarse hierarchy that
matches Figure 20-4's "roughly hierarchical."

### 3 — Reportable output as a layered concern

The `agency.assembly.*` family — including the *summary tiers* and the
`conscious-presenter` — exists explicitly to produce reportable
artefacts, which corresponds to the "near-language" top of the
spiral. The plan already separates what can be spoken about from
what cannot.

### 4 — Concrete action units at the base

`act` is owned by integration agencies that perform low-level
operations (writing files, opening PRs, calling services). These map
to the bottom of the spiral — the concrete `GET / GRASP / PUT / MOVE`
end of Figure 20-4.

### 5 — Diagnosis as its own path

When a run fails, [`07-policies-and-safety.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/07-policies-and-safety.md)
routes through critics, censors, and meta-admin agencies — i.e.
upward, toward more describable units. This is the operational form
of "spiral up into diagnosis."

### 6 — Reportability via `conscious-presenter`

Promoting a state into something that can be shown to a human is an
explicit job in the plan, not an emergent property. The "near-speech"
end of Figure 20-4 has an owner.

## What the implementation does not yet take into account

### A — Hierarchy is two-tier, not graded

Family → member is the only nesting. The figure shows a smooth
gradient from `LANGUAGE` down to `MOVE`. The plan has no documented
notion of "this agency is closer to speech than that one" beyond the
prose distinction between `assembly` and `integration` families.

### B — Shortcuts and cross-connections have no schema

A polyneme that bypasses a level — directly waking an integration
agency from a language-near frame — is not specified in
[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md).
The plan's activation graph is implicit in `activates_on:` clauses,
not declared as a graph with documented shortcuts.

### C — "Reportability" is not a per-agency property

No manifest field says "this agency's output is suitable for human
display" or "this agency's signals are internal-only." The plan
infers reportability from family membership, which is coarser than
Figure 20-4's gradient.

### D — Direction of spiral is not tracked

The plan does not record, per run, whether control moved primarily
downward (toward `act`) or upward (toward `assembly` and
`meta-admin`). The settlement object captures the outcome; it does
not capture the *shape* of the run.

### E — No notion of "private" units

Figure 20-4's lower nemes are idiosyncratic and hard to communicate.
The plan does not distinguish *public* signals from *private* internal
state. Everything that an agency emits is in principle inspectable
the same way.

### F — Spiral-up on failure is policy, not architecture

[`07-policies-and-safety.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/07-policies-and-safety.md)
describes escalation through critics and censors, but the *spiral*
quality — keep going up until something can be named — is not a
declared invariant. A run that fails low and dies low is
indistinguishable in the schema from a run that fails low and is
re-described upward into a usable diagnosis.

### G — No vocabulary for "near-language" / "near-action"

The canonical vocabulary in `THE-SOCIETY-OF-REPO/` and the
implementation documents do not include the *language-end* /
*action-end* axis that Figure 20-4 makes central. Anyone wanting to
talk about position on the spiral has to invent terms.

## Implication for the plan (no changes proposed here)

Figure 20-4 says that the mind's structure is a graded, shortcut-rich
spiral with two natural directions of flow. The implementation plan
honours the *ends* of the spiral (`act` and `assembly`) but encodes
the middle as a two-tier hierarchy and does not track which way
control is currently moving.

The gaps above are recorded as analysis. Any move to close them — a
`spiral_position` or `reportability` property on agencies, a declared
shortcut graph, a `spiral_direction` field on settlements, or a
public/private signal classification — would touch the manifest
schema in
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md),
the frames protocol in
[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md),
the policies in
[`07-policies-and-safety.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/07-policies-and-safety.md),
the state model in
[`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md),
and the identity scopes in `THE-SOCIETY-OF-REPO/02-protocols/01-identity.md`,
and so falls under the "stop and ask" rules in
[`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md) §12 and
[`CLAUDE.md`](../../../../../CLAUDE.md).
