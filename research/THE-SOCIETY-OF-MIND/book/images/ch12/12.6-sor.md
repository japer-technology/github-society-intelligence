## The ideas Figure 12-6 actually carries

1. **A concept is incomplete without a function.** *Block-arch* and
   *hand-change* together are the meaning of *arch*; either alone is
   not.
2. **The link between structure and function is itself a stored
   object.** The curved arrow is a thing, not an inference.
3. **The two halves live in different vocabularies.** *Block-arch*
   speaks of geometry; *hand-change* speaks of action. The bridge
   crosses realms.
4. **Naming requires the bridge.** *Arch* attaches to the pair, not
   to either node.
5. **The bridge is one-to-one in this minimal case.** One structure,
   one function ā€” establishing the schema before Figure 12-7 widens
   it.
6. **Bridges are learnable independently of either side.** A new
   bridge can connect existing nodes; no node needs to change.

`linked: [ <record-id>, ... ]` with link types including `derived_from`,

## What the implementation does not yet take into account

### A ā€” No `structure ā†” function` link type

The catalogue of link types
([`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md))
includes `supersedes`, `derived_from`, `contradicts`, `cites`,
`reinforces`, `analogous_to`, `learned_from` ā€” but no
`realises`, `served_by`, `function_of`, or `structure_of`. The
specific link in Figure 12-6 has no canonical name.

### B ā€” Concepts and procedurals are not paired in schema

`memory/concepts/` and `memory/procedural/` live in different
subtrees with different write rules. There is no schema that says *a
concept entry should carry a list of procedural entries that realise
it* (or vice versa). The pairing is implementable as ad-hoc `linked:`
entries; it is not modelled.

### C ā€” Names attach to nodes, not to pairs

Figure 12-6's claim is that the word *arch* attaches to the
structureā†”function pair. In the plan, an identifier names a single
record. There is no construct like a *named bridge* (e.g.
`bridge.arch` with two endpoints) and no way to retrieve "the bridge
called X" as a unit.

### D ā€” One-to-one minimality is not enforced

The plan permits many links per record. Figure 12-6's minimal one-to-
one case is a *starting* configuration that should be marked as such
("this concept has exactly one function link, awaiting widening").
No such marker exists, so the difference between Figure 12-6 (minimal
bridge) and Figure 12-7 (cross-linked web) cannot be represented as
two states of the same concept.

## Implication for the plan (no changes proposed here)

Figure 12-6 introduces the structural primitive ā€” *the bridge* ā€” that
the next two figures generalise. The plan has the raw materials
(concepts, procedurals, typed links, cross-family polynemes) but has
not named the bridge itself. Any change to add a `realises` /
`function_of` link type, a conceptā†”procedural pairing schema, or a
first-class `bridge.<name>` identifier would touch
`THE-SOCIETY-OF-REPO/02-protocols/01-identity.md` (identifier scopes),
[`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md)
(link catalogue), and
[`11-mapping-sor-to-implementation.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/11-mapping-sor-to-implementation.md)
(mapping rows), and so falls under the "stop and ask" rules in
[`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md) Ā§12 and
