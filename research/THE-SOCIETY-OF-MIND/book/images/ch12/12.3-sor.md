# Figure 12-3 — Implementation analysis (SOR / FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION)

**Source figure:** [12-3.md](12-3.md) — *Near-arch with an unsupported
top; the second counterexample, prompting an enforcement clause.*
**Analysis target:** [`FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/)
(plan-of-record for `.forgejo-society/` and
`.forgejo/workflows/forgejo-society.yaml`).

---

## The ideas Figure 12-3 actually carries

1. **Counterexamples come in *kinds*.** This one fails not by
   forbidden touching but by missing support — a different failure
   mode from Figure 12-2.
2. **Learning is the addition of an *enforce* clause.** "The standing
   blocks must support the top." A required positive relation is
   added.
3. **Enforcement and prevention are dual.** Both shape the uniframe;
   they are complementary moves, not the same move under a different
   name.
4. **A description grows by accreting heterogeneous clauses.** *Two
   standing blocks; must not touch; must support the top.* The
   uniframe is a small list, not a single predicate.
5. **The functional test remains the arbiter.** *Push the car
   through* still decides which clauses are needed; the clauses are
   not justified internally.
6. **Each clause has its own provenance.** Each rule was triggered by
   a specific failed scene. The history matters for revision.

## What the implementation already absorbs

### 1 — Required vs forbidden distinctions exist at the slot level

Frame `slots` carry `required: true|false`
([`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md)).
"The top slot must be filled by something supported by the leg slots"
maps directly to a required slot with a filler constraint.

### 2 — Critics enforce; censors prevent

The split between `critics/` (objections to inadequate work) and
`censors/` (refusal to take an unsafe action)
([`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md))
is the operational dual of Minsky's enforce/prevent. A critic says
*you have not supported the top*; a censor says *you must not let
the standing blocks touch*.

### 3 — Frames already accrete heterogeneous clauses

`code-change.frame.yml` requires `user_goal`, `relevant_files`,
`proposed_patch`, `tests`, `risks`, `final_user_response`
([`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md)).
That is a small heterogeneous list, the same shape as the growing
arch uniframe.

### 4 — Settlement decides under a functional test

Settlements move to `authorised` only when critics and censors are
satisfied
([`03-runtime-pipeline.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/03-runtime-pipeline.md)).
The functional test ("does the work actually work?") is what gates
acceptance, mirroring *push the car through*.

## What the implementation does not yet take into account

### A — Clauses lack typed provenance

Each clause in a frame is just a line in a YAML file. There is no
`from_rejection: <settlement_id>` field linking the clause back to
the counterexample that justified it. The history Minsky requires
in §3 of this chapter is not represented.

### B — Required-filler constraints across slots

The plan supports `required: true|false` on a slot, but not relational
constraints between slots (e.g. *the filler of `top` must be
supported by the filler of `legs`*). Figure 12-3's enforcement is
inter-slot; the schema today is per-slot.

### C — No taxonomy of failure modes

The plan logs failures into `memory/failure/<iso8601>-<topic>.md` as
prose
([`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md)).
There is no structured field saying *this failure was a
missing-required, that one was a forbidden-co-occurrence*. The
distinction Figure 12-3 draws from Figure 12-2 cannot be queried.

### D — Enforce/prevent split is implicit, not declared

`critics/` vs `censors/` carries the duality at the unit level. But
within a single frame's `failure_conditions`, there is no tagging
that says *this condition is enforce-shaped, that one is
prevent-shaped*. A reader of a frame file cannot easily tell which
clauses are constructive requirements and which are exclusions.

## Implication for the plan (no changes proposed here)

Figure 12-3 supplies a second discrimination — *enforce*, against
Figure 12-2's *prevent* — and asks that the resulting clauses carry
their reasons with them. The plan has the right *vocabulary* at the
unit level (critic vs censor) but not the right *schema* inside
frames (no provenance, no inter-slot constraints, no failure-mode
typing). Any change to add these would touch
[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md)
and [`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md),
and so falls under the "stop and ask" rules in
[`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md) §12 and
[`CLAUDE.md`](../../../../../CLAUDE.md).
