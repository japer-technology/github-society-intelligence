# Figure 12-9 — Implementation analysis (SOR / FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION)

**Source figure:** [12-9.md](12-9.md) — *A box pressed into service as
a chair; partial bridges and flagged gaps.*
**Analysis target:** [`FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/)
(plan-of-record for `.forgejo-society/` and
`.forgejo/workflows/forgejo-society.yaml`).

---

## The ideas Figure 12-9 actually carries

1. **A concept can be *stretched* over a new instance.** The box is
   not a chair, yet some chair-bridges still fire.
2. **Partial bridge satisfaction is informative.** Two clauses hold,
   two fail; the partial pattern *is* the diagnosis.
3. **Failed clauses are reported, not hidden.** *No back to support
   body. No room to bend knees.* Absence is named.
4. **Reformulation is opportunistic.** The reasoning succeeds because
   the box happens to have a flat top and parallel sides.
5. **Substitution leaves a trace.** A user of the improvised chair
   knows *which chair-affordances are missing*; the action carries
   its caveats.
6. **A uniframe is also a checklist.** The same bridge-set used for
   recognition is reused for *fitness assessment of an alternative*.
7. **Question marks belong in the representation.** The image shows
   them above the box; the meaning is *not yet decided*.

## What the implementation already absorbs

### 1 — Settlement outcomes include `partial`

`outcome: success|partial|failed` on K-lines
([`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md))
and corresponding states on settlements
([`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md))
already carry the *partial* category. Figure 12-9's box-as-chair
maps to a partial settlement.

### 2 — Critics produce named objections

`objections.jsonl` per issue
([`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md))
records critic and suppressor objections. *No back to support body*
would arrive as such an objection.

### 3 — Caveat on K-lines

`decision.caveat: <one-line or null>` on K-line records
([`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md))
preserves a known limitation alongside a remembered success. That
is the operational analogue of *which chair-affordances are missing*.

### 4 — Spock as conscious presenter

The plan keeps Spock — the visible voice — as the single channel to
the user ([`00-overview.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/00-overview.md)).
A box-as-chair finding would be reported through Spock with its
caveats intact.

## What the implementation does not yet take into account

### A — No partial-match score against a concept

The plan has *partial settlements* but no notion of *partial bridge
satisfaction against a concept's checklist*. There is no field on a
concept that enumerates required bridges and no runtime step that
returns "n of m satisfied; here are the failures."

### B — No "stretch" or "substitution" frame

`code-change`, `bug`, `feature`, `security-sensitive`,
`self-modification`, `novel`
([`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md)) —
none of these is *try this concept on a thing it was not designed
for, report what fits and what doesn't*. Figure 12-9's competence
has no frame.

### C — Absent features are not named in percepts

`percepts.jsonl` records what *is* observed. There is no convention
for asserting *the chair-back is absent here* as a positive percept.
Absence currently shows up only through failure to satisfy a
required slot, which is silent unless a critic complains.

### D — Question-mark state has no representation

The image's question marks above the box mean *I am not yet sure*.
The plan has `forming | pending | authorised | executing | blocked`
on settlements
([`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md))
but none of these means *recognised as a candidate of concept X with
partial fit, awaiting decision*. *Forming* is too generic.

### E — Reformulation traces are not stored

Spock can report caveats, but the trail *we tried concept X on
target Y; here is the bridge-coverage report* is not preserved. Such
a trace would belong somewhere between `memory/episodic/` and
`memory/analogies/` but has no schema today.

## Implication for the plan (no changes proposed here)

Figure 12-9 names the move *use what you know, on something you
don't, and keep track of the gap*. The plan has the pieces (partial
outcomes, critic objections, K-line caveats, Spock as caveat-bearing
presenter) but lacks the wiring (bridge checklists, a stretch frame,
absence-as-percept, a coverage-report trace). Any change to add
these would touch
[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md),
[`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md),
and [`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md),
and so falls under the "stop and ask" rules in
[`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md) §12 and
[`CLAUDE.md`](../../../../../CLAUDE.md).
