# Figure 12-3 — Near-arch with an unsupported top

![figure](12-3.png)

**File:** `ch12/12-3.png`
**Appears in:** [../../som-12.1.md](../../som-12.1.md) — *A block-arch scenario*

## What the image shows

Two upright blocks set apart with a third long block lying on the
ground beside them rather than across their tops. The caption reads,
*Two standing blocks and a lying block. The standing blocks must not
touch. They must support the lying block.*

## What it illustrates

Another counterexample that meets the previous refined description
yet still does not behave like an arch. The figure justifies adding
an *enforcement* clause — the top must be supported by the
standing blocks — and so demonstrates the second of the three
moves (enforcement, prevention, tolerance) that Minsky uses to
characterise uniframe construction.
