# Figure 12-4 — Implementation analysis (SOR / FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION)

**Source figure:** [12-4.md](12-4.md) — *Arch with a wedge-shaped top;
a positive instance that forces tolerance in the uniframe.*
**Analysis target:** [`FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/)
(plan-of-record for `.forgejo-society/` and
`.forgejo/workflows/forgejo-society.yaml`).

---

## The ideas Figure 12-4 actually carries

1. **Tolerance is the third move.** After enforce and prevent comes
   *loosen* — admit a wider range of fillers for a slot.
2. **A positive instance can be the agent of revision, not only
   confirmation.** The wedge-topped arch is a *yes*, and that *yes*
   forces a change.
3. **Slot fillers can be disjunctions.** "The other thing may be a
   WEDGE OR A BLOCK." The slot is no longer single-typed.
4. **The uniframe is now complete enough to act.** Enforce + prevent +
   tolerate yields a workable description for the running example.
5. **Generality is bounded by the examples seen, not by reasoning.**
   The slot admits *wedge* and *block* because both were observed; it
   does not yet admit *cylinder*.
6. **Naming the slot in the description matters.** Calling it *the
   other thing* instead of *the lying block* is itself the
   generalisation move.

## What the implementation already absorbs

### 1 — Frame slots can be filled by many agencies

`slots.<slot-name>.filled_by: [ <agent-id>, ... ]`
([`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md))
is already a list. A `top` slot whose filler can come from either
`agency.shape.block-shaper` or `agency.shape.wedge-shaper` is exactly
representable.

### 2 — Concepts as candidates

`memory/concepts/<concept-id>.md`
([`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md))
holds "candidate abstractions awaiting governance." A widened concept
("arch tops can be wedges as well as blocks") would be promoted here
before being absorbed.

### 3 — Append-only widening

A widening is recorded as a new entry that supersedes an older one
via `supersedes:`
([`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md)).
This preserves the history of *was narrower, became wider, here is
why*.

### 4 — Polyneme `excite` is multi-target

A path or label polyneme already excites several agencies with
weights ([`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md)).
The mechanism for *one stimulus, several possible specialists* is
present.

## What the implementation does not yet take into account

### A — Tolerance has no runtime trigger

The plan can *describe* a widened slot but has no path by which an
observed positive instance promotes a slot from single-typed to
disjunctive. As with Figure 12-2 and 12-3, the change happens through
hand-edited YAML under governance, not through a recognised "this
positive example does not match — widen, don't reject" event.

### B — `filled_by` lists agencies, not value-shapes

`filled_by` names which *agencies* may fill the slot, not which
*kinds of value* are acceptable in it. Figure 12-4's claim is about
the shape of the filler (block-or-wedge), independent of who supplies
it. The schema cannot express *the top slot accepts values of type
block-or-wedge*.

### C — Positive observations are not separated by their effect

The plan classifies outcomes by success/failure
([`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md)).
It does not classify positive successes into *those that confirm an
existing description* vs *those that force it to widen*. The latter
are Figure 12-4's specific contribution and have no marker.

### D — Generality bounded by observation is not enforced

A widened slot in a hand-edited frame can be widened arbitrarily —
nothing in the plan refuses to admit *cylinder* unless a cylinder has
been seen. The discipline of "tolerate only what you've observed" is
left to the editor, not the runtime.

### E — No slot-rename when its content generalises

Figure 12-4 changes the slot's *name* from *the lying block* to *the
other thing* as part of the generalisation. The plan's slot names are
identifiers; renaming a slot is a schema change requiring a
superseding frame file. The vocabulary-broadening move that
accompanies tolerance is captured only as a wholesale frame
supersession.

## Implication for the plan (no changes proposed here)

Figure 12-4 completes the enforce/prevent/tolerate trio and asks for a
runtime that can *widen on demand* under disciplined conditions. The
plan has the static shape (multi-filler slots, append-only
supersession) but not the dynamic step (positive-instance-driven
widening, value-shape typing on fillers, observation-bounded
generality). Any change to introduce these would touch
[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md)
and [`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md),
and so falls under the "stop and ask" rules in
[`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md) §12 and
[`CLAUDE.md`](../../../../../CLAUDE.md).
