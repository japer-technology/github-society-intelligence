# Figure 12-12 — Implementation analysis (SOR / FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION)

**Source figure:** [12-12.md](12-12.md) — *Arch as uniframe with a top
slot containing an embedded accumulation (BLOCK OR WEDGE).*
**Analysis target:** [`FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/)
(plan-of-record for `.forgejo-society/` and
`.forgejo/workflows/forgejo-society.yaml`).

---

## The ideas Figure 12-12 actually carries

1. **Uniframes and accumulations compose.** A description can be
   uniform in most of its parts and accumulative in one of them.
2. **The composition is local.** Only one slot — *top* — is opened
   to an accumulation; the legs remain strictly typed.
3. **The accumulation lives *inside* a slot.** It is a property of
   the filler, not a sibling of the concept.
4. **A slot can carry a constraint *and* a list.** The legs are
   typed and bear the side note *Must not touch*; the top is typed
   as a list of alternatives.
5. **Hybrid representation is the *normal* end-state.** Few real
   concepts are pure uniframes or pure accumulations.
6. **The accumulation node is itself named.** *BLOCK OR WEDGE* is
   addressable, not anonymous; it could be extended without touching
   the surrounding uniframe.
7. **The hybrid is a settled outcome of the chapter.** Figures 12-1
   to 12-11 are the path; Figure 12-12 is where the description
   rests.

## What the implementation already absorbs

### 1 — `filled_by` is a list of allowed fillers

`slots.<slot-name>.filled_by: [ <agent-id>, ... ]`
([`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md))
is already a list at the slot level. The shape "this slot accepts
several alternatives" is representable as several filler agencies.

### 2 — Slots may differ in their constraints

Each slot has its own `required:` flag and its own `filled_by:` list.
Mixing a tightly-typed slot with a loosely-typed one within the
same frame is supported.

### 3 — Failure conditions per frame

`failure_conditions: [ <named-objection>, ... ]`
([`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md))
holds per-frame side notes such as *the legs must not touch*.

### 4 — Append-only widening of the alternatives list

Adding a new entry to a `filled_by` list is a normal frame edit
under governance, mirroring the *extend the accumulation without
touching the surrounding uniframe* property.

## What the implementation does not yet take into account

### A — No named accumulation node inside a frame

In Figure 12-12, the *BLOCK OR WEDGE* node has its own identity. In
the plan, the list inside `filled_by` is anonymous; it has no id and
no separate file. Extending or referencing *the set of acceptable
top-shapes* requires hand-editing the frame's YAML.

### B — `filled_by` lists agencies, not value-shapes

As noted for [12.4-sor.md](12.4-sor.md), `filled_by` names *who* may
fill, not *what shape* the value must take. Figure 12-12's accumulation
is over value-shapes (block, wedge); the plan represents it indirectly
via the producer agencies.

### C — Hybrid representation is not declared

The plan has neither *uniframe* nor *accumulation* as a kind on a
`memory/concepts/` entry. Without those kinds, the *hybrid* in
Figure 12-12 cannot be declared either. The structure is buildable
out of frames, agencies, and links; it is not *recognisable* as a
hybrid in any registry.

### D — Inter-slot constraints (carried from earlier figures)

The side note *Must not touch* still needs an inter-slot rule
between the two `leg` slots. As called out in [12.3-sor.md](12.3-sor.md),
the schema today supports per-slot, not inter-slot, constraints.

### E — No promotion path from `novel` to hybrid

A genuinely new arch shape would fire `novel.frame.yml`
([`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md))
and be re-analogised. There is no path by which a `novel` outcome
can promote a new alternative *into the accumulation slot of an
existing frame*. The settled hybrid form has no growth mechanism
inside the runtime.

### F — Repeated slots with shared rule

Figure 12-12 has two *LEG* slots and a single side note that
applies to both. Frame slots in the plan are individually named; a
constraint shared across two structurally identical slots has to be
repeated. The notion of a *slot kind* (one schema, two instances) is
absent.

## Implication for the plan (no changes proposed here)

Figure 12-12 is where the chapter rests: real concepts mix uniframe
discipline with accumulation tolerance, slot by slot. The plan can
*assemble* such a representation from existing parts but cannot
*declare* it, *grow* it from observation, or *address* the
accumulation node inside a slot. Any change to introduce a named
accumulation primitive, value-shape typing on slot fillers, inter-
slot constraints, slot kinds, or a `novel→hybrid` promotion path
would touch
[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md),
[`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md),
and `THE-SOCIETY-OF-REPO/02-protocols/01-identity.md`, and so falls
under the "stop and ask" rules in
[`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md) §12 and
[`CLAUDE.md`](../../../../../CLAUDE.md).
