# Figure 12-2 — Implementation analysis (SOR / FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION)

**Source figure:** [12-2.md](12-2.md) — *Near-arch with touching
standing blocks; a counterexample that prompts an exclusion.*
**Analysis target:** [`FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/)
(plan-of-record for `.forgejo-society/` and
`.forgejo/workflows/forgejo-society.yaml`).

---

## The ideas Figure 12-2 actually carries

1. **A near-miss is more instructive than a positive example.** The
   scene that *almost* fits is what forces the description to sharpen.
2. **Counterexamples come in shapes the current description cannot
   reject.** The blocks-touching configuration matches the old rule
   yet fails the functional test.
3. **Learning is the addition of a *prevent* clause.** "The standing
   blocks must not touch." A new constraint is appended; nothing
   already known is overwritten.
4. **The label stays the same; the body changes.** Both Figure 12-1
   and Figure 12-2 are captioned *Two standing blocks and a lying
   block*. The change is internal.
5. **Discrimination is a separate competence from recognition.** The
   work of telling *arch* from *near-arch* is its own skill, not a
   by-product of recognising *arch*.
6. **Generalisation proceeds by exclusion, not by induction.** The
   uniframe shrinks the set of things called *arch*; it does not
   enlarge a pattern.

## What the implementation already absorbs

### 1 — Critics as the place where near-misses are caught

[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md)
lists critics including `critic.evidence`, `critic.scope`, and
`critic.diff-skeptic`. A critic exists precisely to say *this looks
right but isn't*; that is the operational role of Figure 12-2.

### 2 — Frames carry `failure_conditions`

[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md)
gives every frame a `failure_conditions: [ <named-objection>, ... ]`
list. This is the schema slot where an exclusion like *standing
blocks must not touch* would live for a code-shape concept.

### 3 — Append-only correction

[`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md)
makes corrections append a superseding record rather than overwrite
the old one. Figure 12-2's "add an exclusion, keep the rest" is
already the house style for memory.

### 4 — Rejected candidates are preserved

`memory/failure/rejected-candidates/<settlement_id>.yml`
([`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md))
keeps closed-without-merge branches as *rejected hypotheses*. A
near-miss is exactly this kind of record: a thing that almost worked
and was excluded.

---

## What the implementation does not yet take into account

### A — Counterexamples are not linked to the concept they refute

A `rejected-candidates/` entry records *the branch and the rejecting
settlement* but not *the concept whose description it caused to
narrow*. The link from a near-miss to the resulting exclusion clause
is missing. A future reader of `memory/concepts/<id>.md` cannot
trace back to the rejection that shaped it.

### B — Frame `failure_conditions` are static

`failure_conditions` is a hand-authored list in each `*.frame.yml`.
There is no runtime path by which an observed near-miss promotes a
new `failure_conditions` entry into the frame. The "prevent" clause
in Figure 12-2 arrives by learning; in the plan it arrives by editing
a YAML file under governance, which is the right safety posture but
not the same mechanism.

### C — No "near-miss" classification

The plan distinguishes outcomes `success | partial | failed`
([`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md)).
None of these match Figure 12-2's *almost-but-not-quite-and-here's-why*.
A near-miss is neither success nor failure; it is a partial match
that should be remembered specifically as a discrimination cue.

### D — Discrimination is not a named competence

There is no `critic.discrimination` whose only job is to tell
near-cases apart, and no agency family `agencies/discrimination/`.
The work is folded into general critics. Figure 12-2's claim that
*telling apart* is its own skill is therefore not named in the plan.

## Implication for the plan (no changes proposed here)

Figure 12-2 names a competence — *learn from the case that almost
fits* — that the plan currently handles only as part of generic
critic activity and through hand-edited `failure_conditions`. Any
move to close the gaps (a near-miss outcome class, a runtime path
from rejection to frame narrowing, a discrimination critic, or a
back-link from `memory/failure/rejected-candidates/` to
`memory/concepts/`) would touch
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md),
[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md),
and [`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md),
and so falls under the "stop and ask" rules in
[`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md) §12 and
[`CLAUDE.md`](../../../../../CLAUDE.md).
