# Figure 12-10 — Implementation analysis (SOR / FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION)

**Source figure:** [12-10.md](12-10.md) — *Accumulations: furniture
and money as trees of dissimilar leaves.*
**Analysis target:** [`FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/)
(plan-of-record for `.forgejo-society/` and
`.forgejo/workflows/forgejo-society.yaml`).

---

## The ideas Figure 12-10 actually carries

1. **Some concepts resist a uniframe.** *Furniture* and *money* have
   no compact description that covers all members.
2. **An *accumulation* is a first-class representation.** When no
   unifying description exists, the list is the concept.
3. **Members of an accumulation may live in different ontologies.**
   *Coin* is physical; *credit* is contractual. The category
   survives the mismatch.
4. **Tree depth is one.** Root and leaves; no internal structure
   binds the leaves to one another.
5. **The leaves remain individually meaningful.** Each leaf is itself
   recognisable (a chair is a chair); the accumulation does not
   absorb their identity.
6. **The boundary between uniframe and accumulation is itself a
   judgement.** Figure 12-10 is the figure that says *here is where
   uniframing stops paying*.
7. **An accumulation is open.** New leaves can be added without
   restructuring; nothing prevents future entries.

## What the implementation already absorbs

### 1 — Append-open catalogues throughout

Every memory subtree
([`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md))
admits new files without revising existing ones. The "open list"
property is the default.

### 2 — `agencies/assembly/*` and summary tiers

`agency.assembly.summary-tier-1` and its peers
([`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md))
are explicit assemblers — units whose job is to gather many narrower
outputs into a single brief. They are the operational ancestors of
*assemble a category from heterogeneous members*.

### 3 — Polynemes already list members by enumeration

`label-polynemes.yml` and `phrase-polynemes.yml`
([`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md))
match by simple membership in a list. No unifying predicate is
required. *Kind:question* matches `question`, full stop.

### 4 — Heterogeneous memory subtrees coexist

`memory/` already mixes records of different shapes (`episodic/`,
`semantic/`, `procedural/`, `failure/`, `klines/`, `decisions/`).
The repository itself is an example of accumulation tolerated at
the top level.

## What the implementation does not yet take into account

### A — No `accumulation` kind for concepts

`memory/concepts/` holds candidates "awaiting governance"
([`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md))
but does not distinguish *uniframe-shaped* from *accumulation-shaped*
concepts. A future *furniture* concept would be written as if it
admitted a description, even when it does not.

### B — No decision rule for "stop trying to uniframe"

Figure 12-10's boundary judgement — *uniframing stops paying here* —
has no operational mechanism. There is no critic
(`critic.uniframe-cost`) and no meta-admin pass that says *we have
tried N times to find a shared description and should now settle
for a list*.

### C — Accumulations are not addressable as units

Even if several concept files were treated as members of *furniture*,
there is no `accumulation.<name>` identifier scope
(`THE-SOCIETY-OF-REPO/02-protocols/01-identity.md` does not list one)
and no schema that says *these files are members of accumulation X*.
The set has no name.

### D — Cross-ontology membership has no carrier

The members of *money* span physical and contractual ontologies.
Frames, polynemes, and K-lines do not carry ontology tags. A
representation that admits *coin and credit are both members of
money* without flattening either side is not provided.

### E — Membership is unweighted

An accumulation has members; some members are central, others
peripheral. The plan has no `centrality` or `prototypicality` field
on a record's membership in any set.

## Implication for the plan (no changes proposed here)

Figure 12-10 names a category of concepts the plan currently cannot
recognise: those that are honestly lists. The plan can store the
members but cannot mark the set, decide when to stop unifying, or
preserve cross-ontology membership. Any change to add an
`accumulation` identifier scope, an `accumulation` kind under
`memory/concepts/`, or a critic that judges uniframing cost would
touch `THE-SOCIETY-OF-REPO/02-protocols/01-identity.md`,
[`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md),
and [`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md),
and so falls under the "stop and ask" rules in
[`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md) §12 and
[`CLAUDE.md`](../../../../../CLAUDE.md).
