# Figure 12-5 — Implementation analysis (SOR / FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION)

**Source figure:** [12-5.md](12-5.md) — *Two panels listing every
difference between an arch and a row; the "don't notice too much"
figure.*
**Analysis target:** [`FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/)
(plan-of-record for `.forgejo-society/` and
`.forgejo/workflows/forgejo-society.yaml`).

---

## The ideas Figure 12-5 actually carries

1. **A scene admits many descriptions simultaneously.** A learner
   could record any of a dozen relations and call them all true.
2. **Most observable differences are redundant.** *A above B*, *A
   touches B*, *A supported by B* refer to overlapping facts.
3. **A good description selects a minimal essential subset.** The
   work is choosing what *not* to record, not enumerating what could
   be.
4. **Redundancy has structure: some relations entail others.**
   Support entails touching entails proximity. The entailment graph
   is itself part of the concept.
5. **A negative panel and a positive panel together reveal the
   difference space.** Discrimination requires both halves to be
   present in inspectable form.
6. **Noticing too much is a failure mode.** The learner that records
   everything is no better off than the learner that records nothing.
7. **The right granularity depends on the contrast at hand.** A
   different counterexample would justify keeping different relations.

## What the implementation already absorbs

### 1 — Percept channels are structured

`percepts.jsonl` in `state/mind/issues/<n>/`
([`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md))
holds typed records, not free observation. Each record states one
thing; the schema constrains noise.

### 2 — Critic catalogue contains a `scope` critic

`critic.scope` ([`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md))
objects when a proposed change drags in more than the stimulus
warrants. That is the operational shape of *don't notice too much*
at the action layer.

### 3 — Polynemes are deliberately small catalogues

[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md)
ships short path/label/phrase polyneme tables, not exhaustive ones.
The plan implicitly endorses *select a minimal essential subset*.

### 4 — Summary tiers compress

`agencies/assembly/*` and the summary-tier mechanism mentioned in
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md)
collapse signals into briefs. This is the operational version of
reducing a long observation list to the few entries that matter.

## What the implementation does not yet take into account

### A — No representation of entailment among percepts

Figure 12-5's central claim — *A supported by B entails A above B and
A touches B* — requires a graph of entailments between observation
types. The plan has typed relational links between *records*
(`supersedes`, `cites`, `analogous_to`)
([`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md))
but not between *kinds of observation*. The runtime cannot say
*these three percepts are redundant given that one*.

### B — `critic.scope` acts on actions, not on descriptions

The scope critic limits what a settlement *changes*. It does not
limit what a frame or a concept *records*. A frame today may name
ten failure conditions, several of which are entailments of others,
and nothing in the plan flags the redundancy.

### C — No structured "difference panel"

Figure 12-5 requires *two* parallel descriptions — positive and
negative — laid side by side. The plan stores positives in
`workspace/active-settlements/` and negatives in
`memory/failure/rejected-candidates/` but never pairs them at the
same level of granularity for direct comparison.

### D — Granularity is fixed per pillar, not per contrast

Percept granularity is determined by which `perceive`-phase agency
produced the record. There is no mechanism by which the *contrast
being investigated* dictates the granularity at which observations
are kept. Figure 12-5 says the right grain depends on the question;
the plan picks grain in advance.

### E — Redundancy pruning is not a named pass

No agency or phase is responsible for *removing percepts that are
implied by other percepts already recorded*. `agencies/meta-admin`
covers differentiation and self-modification
([`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md))
but not de-duplication of the observation layer.

## Implication for the plan (no changes proposed here)

Figure 12-5 turns *what to leave out* into a first-class problem. The
plan has the instinct (small catalogues, scope critic, summary tiers)
but not the apparatus (entailment among percept kinds, paired
positive/negative panels, contrast-driven granularity, a redundancy
pruning pass). Any change to add these would touch
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md),
[`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md),
and [`09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md),
and so falls under the "stop and ask" rules in
[`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md) §12 and
[`CLAUDE.md`](../../../../../CLAUDE.md).
