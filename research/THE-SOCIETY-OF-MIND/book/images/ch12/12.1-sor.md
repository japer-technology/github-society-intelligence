# Figure 12-1 — Implementation analysis (SOR / FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION)

**Source figure:** [12-1.md](12-1.md) — *Two standing blocks and a
lying block; the first instance of the block-arch.*
**Analysis target:** [`FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/)
(plan-of-record for `.forgejo-society/` and
`.forgejo/workflows/forgejo-society.yaml`).

This file inventories the ideas carried by Figure 12-1 and checks each
one against the implementation plan.

---

## The ideas Figure 12-1 actually carries

1. **A concept begins as a single positive instance.** Before anything
   is general, there is one concrete scene the learner has seen.
2. **An instance is structured, not atomic.** It is three blocks in a
   named relation, not an undifferentiated impression.
3. **The instance is named externally.** The label *arch* is given by
   a teacher; the learner does not coin it.
4. **The instance carries an implied test.** *Push the toy car
   through* — a functional probe that the description has not yet
   captured.
5. **The instance is the seed of a uniframe.** Everything later in the
   chapter — exclusions, supports, tolerances — is editing of this one
   description.
6. **Image and caption together are the record.** Neither the picture
   alone nor the words alone are enough; the pair is the unit of
   memory.
7. **The scene is a candidate, not a fact.** Whether *arch* really
   means *this* is to be decided by further examples.

## What the implementation already absorbs

### 1 — Instances arrive as structured percepts

The `perceive` phase in
[`03-runtime-pipeline.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/03-runtime-pipeline.md)
turns a Forgejo event into a normalised `stimulus.json` and a series
of percept records in `state/mind/issues/<n>/percepts.jsonl`
([`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md)).
An incoming stimulus is therefore stored as parts in named relations,
not as a free-form blob — the operational analogue of "three blocks
in a configuration."

### 2 — Externally supplied labels drive activation

Labels on issues and PRs route into `label-polynemes.yml`
([`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md)).
A teacher-supplied tag — `bug`, `feature`, `self-modification` — is
the operational counterpart of the adult saying *arch*: a word that
seeds the frame.

### 3 — Candidate, not fact

`workspace/active-settlements/<settlement_id>.yml`
([`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md))
holds settlements in `forming → pending → authorised → executing`.
A first observation enters as `forming`, exactly the status of a
single positive instance before any generalisation is justified.

### 4 — Image-and-caption-as-unit at the record level

Episodic records in `memory/episodic/<iso8601>-issue-<n>.md` and
event records in `memory/events/` carry both raw payload and the
normalised description. The pairing of *what happened* with *how we
named it* is preserved.

## What the implementation does not yet take into account

### A — No "first instance" status on a concept

The implementation has no `memory/concepts/<id>.md` entry that is
explicitly marked as *seeded by exactly one positive instance, not
yet generalised*.
[`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md)
mentions `concepts/` as "candidate abstractions awaiting governance"
but does not record the count or identity of supporting instances.
A reader cannot tell, from a concept file, whether it rests on one
example or twenty.

### B — Functional probes are not first-class

The block-arch instance carries an implicit test: *can a car pass
through?* The plan has critics (`critic.evidence`, `critic.scope`)
that test *settlements*, not tests that travel with a concept and
re-fire whenever the concept is invoked. There is no
`memory/concepts/<id>.probe.yml` describing the function the concept
must perform.

### C — Teacher-attribution on labels is missing

Polynemes record *what* label fired, not *who* applied it. The
distinction between a label supplied by a maintainer (teacher) and
one applied by an agency (self) is the difference between Minsky's
external naming and internal hypothesis. The plan does not carry
this provenance.

### D — A single observation does not yet differentiate

[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md)
routes splitting and naming through `agency.meta-admin.differentiation-broker`,
but the broker is described as acting on accumulated material. The
"one instance, named, stored" pattern of Figure 12-1 has no dedicated
handler; it would currently pass through `perceive` and `remember`
without leaving a marker that *here a new concept was seeded*.

## Implication for the plan (no changes proposed here)

Figure 12-1 is the figure that says *a concept can begin honestly
with one example*. The plan can carry that example as a stimulus and
as an episodic record, but it cannot yet say *this is the first
positive instance of concept X, and X has a functional probe attached
to it*. Adding such a marker would touch `memory/concepts/` schema in
[`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md)
and the responsibilities of `agency.meta-admin.differentiation-broker`
in [`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md),
and so falls under the "stop and ask" rules in
[`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md) §12 and
[`CLAUDE.md`](../../../../../CLAUDE.md).
