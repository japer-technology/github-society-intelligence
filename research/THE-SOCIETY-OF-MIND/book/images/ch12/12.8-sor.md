**Source figure:** [12-8.md](12-8.md) — *Chair as structure-plus-
function: part-by-part mapping from chair-parts to body-needs.*
**Analysis target:** [`FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/)
(plan-of-record for `.forgejo-society/` and
`.forgejo/workflows/forgejo-society.yaml`).

---

## The ideas Figure 12-8 actually carries

1. **A concept is part-decomposed on both sides.** *Chair* has parts
   (back, seat, legs); *sitter* has parts (body, knees, height). The
   bridge is between parts, not wholes.
2. **Multiple bridges per concept are normal.** Four labelled links
   connect the two halves; recognising a chair invokes all of them.
3. **The same part may serve more than one function.** *Chair-Legs*
   both *maintain seat height* and *give room for knees*.
4. **Function language refers to a *consumer*.** The right-hand side
   is the sitter — an entity whose requirements anchor the function
   names.
5. **Recognition is not enough; the concept must be *usable*.** The
   bridge-set is what lets a learner *act* upon the recognition.
6. **Bridges carry semantics, not just edges.** Each link has a
   short phrase ("supports body", "maintains seat height") that the
   diagram preserves.
7. **The mapping is total enough to test.** Every chair-part has at
   least one outgoing link; every body-need has at least one
   incoming.

## What the implementation already absorbs

### 1 — Parts as named slots

Frame `slots:` ([`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md))
decomposes a situation into named pieces, each with its own
filler. The "back / seat / legs" decomposition of Figure 12-8 maps
naturally to slots within a `chair.frame.yml` (were one to exist).

### 2 — Multiple links per record

Records carry `linked: [ ... ]`
([`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md))
and the link list is unbounded. Many bridges per concept is
representable.

### 3 — User-model keeper as the *consumer* anchor

`agency.identity.user-model-keeper`
([`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md);
[`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md))
maintains a model of the human whose needs the system serves. That
is the operational counterpart of *the sitter* — the consumer whose
requirements give the function-side its meaning.

### 4 — Settlement tests usability, not only recognition

A settlement is `authorised` only when its critics and censors say
the proposal is fit to act on
([`03-runtime-pipeline.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/03-runtime-pipeline.md)).
*Usable, not merely recognised* matches the gate.

## What the implementation does not yet take into account

### A — Concepts cannot be part-decomposed

`memory/concepts/<concept-id>.md` is a single markdown file
([`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md)).
There is no `parts: [ <part-id>, ... ]` schema. Figure 12-8's "chair-
back, chair-seat, chair-legs" sub-structure would have to be
expressed as prose or as several separate concept files with ad-hoc
links — neither of which makes the part structure queryable.

### B — Links lack semantic labels

`linked:` carries link *types* (`supersedes`, `cites`, `reinforces`,
…) but not free-text phrases per edge. Figure 12-8's "maintains seat
height" is a per-link semantic phrase, not a type. The plan loses
the bridge label when it stores the bridge.

### C — No part-to-part mapping schema

Figure 12-8 maps *parts of one concept* to *parts of another*. The
plan has links between records (whole-to-whole) but not links
between *sub-elements* of records. A bridge from `chair-legs` to
`sitter-knees` would require both records to expose stable sub-IDs,
which the current schema does not require.

### D — Totality is not checked

Figure 12-8 implies every chair-part has at least one outgoing
function link. The plan does not run such a coverage check on a
concept's bridges. There is no critic whose job is *flag any part
without a function link*.

### E — Consumer-perspective rewriting is missing

The user-model keeper models the human, but no agency rewrites a
concept's bridges from the consumer's point of view ("re-state these
links in the sitter's vocabulary"). The asymmetry Figure 12-8 draws
between *what the chair has* and *what the sitter needs* is not a
deliberate operational move.

## Implication for the plan (no changes proposed here)

Figure 12-8 sharpens Figure 12-6's bridge by demanding *part-level*
detail and *consumer-anchored* function names. The plan represents
wholes well and parts only inside frames; it represents links as
typed edges and not as phrased bridges; it tests settlements for
usability but not concepts for coverage. Any change to introduce
part-IDs on concepts, free-phrase bridge labels, or a coverage
critic would touch
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md),
[`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md),
and the identifier rules in
`THE-SOCIETY-OF-REPO/02-protocols/01-identity.md`, and so falls under
the "stop and ask" rules in
[`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md) §12 and
[`CLAUDE.md`](../../../../../CLAUDE.md).
