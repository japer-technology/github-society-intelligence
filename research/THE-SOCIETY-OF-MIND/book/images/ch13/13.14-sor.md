# Figure 13-14 — Implementation analysis (SOR / FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION)

**Source figure:** [13-14.md](13-14.md) — *The short block shrinks
at the frontier.*
**Analysis target:** [`FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/)
(plan-of-record for `.forgejo-society/` and
`.forgejo/workflows/forgejo-society.yaml`).

This file inventories the ideas carried by Figure 13-14 and
checks each one against the implementation plan.

---

## The ideas Figure 13-14 actually carries

From the caption and §13.6 *The frontier effect*:

1. **A salient landmark distorts nearby measurements.** The long
   block's end pulls the short block's edge in.
2. **The procedure prefers a describable answer to an accurate
   one.** "Stop where the long block stops" is easier to specify
   than "extend by N more units."
3. **Distortion is systematic, not random.** The shrinkage is
   reproducible at the frontier and absent elsewhere.
4. **The error reveals what the procedure was using as
   anchors.** When the procedure fails, it tells you what its
   inputs really were.
5. **Local information dominates global geometry.** The figure's
   *length* of the short block is given up to preserve the
   *coincidence* of two endpoints.
6. **The failure is not the perceiver's; it is the procedure's
   anchoring strategy.** The child could see the right answer if
   the procedure asked for it.
7. **The diagnostic is the artefact, not the apology.** The
   drawing itself shows the strategy; no introspection is
   needed.

## What the implementation already absorbs

### 1 — Artefacts as diagnostic of procedure

Every run's settlements, signals, and patches are on disk
(point 7 has a structural home in
[`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md)).
A maintainer reading the artefacts can in principle reconstruct
the anchoring choices a unit made.

### 2 — Critics that compare claims with artefacts

`critic.evidence` checks that what an agency reports is
supported by what it produced. This is the start of point 4 —
errors reveal inputs — at a coarse grain.

### 3 — Per-run isolation

Each activation is its own settlement; systematic effects can
be detected if a maintainer aggregates several. Point 3
(distortion is systematic) is observable in principle.

## What the implementation does not yet take into account

### A — Landmark / non-landmark distinction has no representation

Point 1's central asymmetry — that some features are landmarks
and others are not — is absent. The plan has labels, paths, and
phrases as triggers
([`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md))
but no concept of *salience*. A `landmark_signal:` field is not
in
[`09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md).

### B — No critic for "snap-to-landmark" distortion

Point 2 — that procedures prefer describable answers — would
correspond to a critic that flags outputs whose values
coincide suspiciously with salient input boundaries. No such
critic exists; `critic.evidence` checks support, not
suspicious convenience.

### C — Systematic-error detection requires manual aggregation

Point 3 says the distortion is reproducible. The plan has no
aggregator across settlements that says "in N runs near input
region R, the same kind of error appears." Detecting it
requires reading git by hand. See also Figure 13-13 gap D.

### D — Anchoring strategy is not declared

Point 4 — errors reveal anchors — is only useful if anchors
were declarable in the first place. An agency's manifest does
not list "the features I use as anchors when measuring or
positioning." Without that declaration, the artefact-as-
diagnostic is hard to read.

### E — Trade-off between describability and accuracy is not modelled

Point 5's choice — preserve a coincidence at the cost of a
length — is a *policy*. The plan has no place to write that
policy explicitly. An agency could in principle prefer accuracy
or describability; the plan does not ask, and so the choice is
buried in the prose.

### F — The strategy belongs to the procedure, not the unit

Point 6 distinguishes "the perceiver" from "the perceiver's
anchoring strategy." In plan terms, the unit is the agency and
the strategy is — at best — inside the prose. There is no
schema for "this agency uses strategy S, which it could
replace with strategy S' without changing what it is."

## Implication for the plan (no changes proposed here)

Figure 13-14 is the figure that earns the *frontier effect* —
the systematic distortion produced when a procedure prefers a
describable answer. The implementation plan honours
artefact-as-evidence, but does not yet honour the concepts
(salience, anchoring strategy, describability/accuracy
trade-off) that would make the distortion nameable.

The gaps above are recorded here as analysis, not as a change
request. Any move to close them — for example, a `salience:`
field on signals, a `critic.snap-to-landmark`, a declared
`anchoring_strategy:` block on agency manifests, or an
aggregator agency that detects systematic per-region errors —
would touch
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md),
[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md),
[`09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md),
and the identity scopes in
`THE-SOCIETY-OF-REPO/02-protocols/01-identity.md`, and so falls
under the "stop and ask" rules in
[`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md) §12 and
[`CLAUDE.md`](../../../../../CLAUDE.md).
