# Figure 13-9 — Implementation analysis (SOR / FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION)

**Source figure:** [13-9.md](13-9.md) — *Older children's drawings
of people.*
**Analysis target:** [`FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/)
(plan-of-record for `.forgejo-society/` and
`.forgejo/workflows/forgejo-society.yaml`).

This file inventories the ideas carried by Figure 13-9 and checks
each one against the implementation plan.

---

## The ideas Figure 13-9 actually carries

From the caption and §13.4 *Children's drawing-frames*:

1. **Development can leave the schema alone and change the
   procedure.** The same feature list could still apply; what
   has shifted is the bookkeeping that walks it.
2. **A small procedural revision yields visibly different
   outputs.** Two drawings, both produced by the same
   underlying frame, look qualitatively different.
3. **Maturity shows up as separability.** The hallmark of the
   advance is that previously fused parts (head, body) are now
   distinct.
4. **Earlier output is not deleted by the new procedure.** The
   older drawings remain valid as a record of where the schema
   was; they are not retroactively wrong.
5. **The new output is richer in details the schema permitted
   all along.** Fingers, hair, clothes were always allowable
   slots; what changed is the procedure that fills them.
6. **Development is reversible in principle, not in practice.**
   The same child could in principle draw the earlier picture
   again; the procedural improvement is monotone but not
   exclusive.

## What the implementation already absorbs

### 1 — Procedure and schema are separable

[`03-runtime-pipeline.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/03-runtime-pipeline.md)
distinguishes the pipeline (procedure) from the manifests
(schema). The pipeline can be updated without rewriting every
agency. Point 1 is honoured at the pipeline scale.

### 2 — Past outputs preserved by git

Older settlements and signals remain in git history; new runs
do not erase them. Point 4 holds.

### 3 — Differentiation as a planned mechanism

The `self-modification` frame and
`agency.meta-admin.differentiation-broker` in
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md)
provide a sanctioned route for splitting one unit into two.
Point 3 (separability) has an operational analogue at the
agency scale.

## What the implementation does not yet take into account

### A — Procedures within a frame cannot evolve independently

Since frames currently have no first-class walk (see Figure 13-8
gap A), there is no in-frame procedure to revise. The
implementation can refine *the pipeline* and refine *individual
manifests*; it cannot refine *the procedure that walks a
frame's slots* because that procedure is not addressable.

### B — Maturity is not tracked

Point 3 (separability as the hallmark of advance) would benefit
from a record on each frame or agency: "previous bookkeeping
known to conflate X with Y; current bookkeeping known to
separate them." No such record exists in
[`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md).
Differentiation events are visible in git but not classified as
maturity steps.

### C — Same-schema, different-procedure runs cannot be diffed

Two settlements produced by the same frame under different
procedures (the old and new bookkeeping) cannot currently be
labelled as such. A maintainer can compare them by hand; the
runtime has no `procedure-version:` field on a settlement.

### D — Schemas do not advertise slots that are typically under-filled

Point 5 — that the older drawings populate slots the younger
drawings ignored — suggests a useful metric: per-slot fill
rates over recent runs. The plan does not collect such metrics.
A slot can sit empty for many runs without flagging.

### E — Reversibility is not modelled

Point 6 — the advance is monotone but not exclusive — would
need a way to say "this frame can be walked under v1 or v2 of
its bookkeeping; default is v2." The plan has no
`procedure_version:` selector and no fallback path. A revised
procedure simply replaces the previous one in the manifest.

### F — No "developmental" classification of changes

Git records that a manifest changed; it does not record
*why* in any machine-readable way. The category "this change
makes previously fused slots separable" is not a first-class
kind of edit, despite being the central category in Figure
13-9.

## Implication for the plan (no changes proposed here)

Figure 13-9 is the figure that earns *bookkeeping advances by
itself*. The implementation plan honours pipeline-level
evolution and agency-level differentiation, but does not yet
honour the within-frame procedural maturation that this figure
makes vivid.

The gaps above are recorded here as analysis, not as a change
request. Any move to close them — for example, a
`procedure_version` field on frames, per-slot fill-rate
collection in
[`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md),
or a "maturity step" settlement kind — would touch
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md),
[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md),
and the identity scopes in
`THE-SOCIETY-OF-REPO/02-protocols/01-identity.md`, and so falls
under the "stop and ask" rules in
[`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md) §12 and
[`CLAUDE.md`](../../../../../CLAUDE.md).
