This file inventories the ideas carried by Figure 13-3 and checks

## The ideas Figure 13-3 actually carries

From the caption and §13.2 *Boundaries*:

1. **The mind adds boundaries the world omits.** A single
   continuous object is partitioned into three regions purely on
   the strength of a template.
2. **A partition can be supported by no perceptual cue at all.**
   The dashed lines in the right-hand drawing do not trace any
   edge, shadow, or material change.
3. **The template carries the partition.** Without the
   body-and-supports frame, the partition has nothing to attach
   to.
4. **Function imposes structure.** The reason to cut here rather
   than there is that *support* must lie below *body*, not that
   the object shows a seam.
5. **One physical object can be many functional parts.** Identity
   at the matter level does not constrain identity at the
   description level.
6. **The added boundary is conventional, not discovered.** A
   different template (say, *left-half + right-half*) would slice
   the same object differently with equal legitimacy.

## What the implementation already absorbs

### 1 — Frames carry partitions through slots

A frame in
[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md)
attaches named slots to whatever it activates over. When
`frame.regression-debug` is in force, *regression*, *suspect-commit*,
and *covering-test* become addressable parts of the situation even
if the underlying diff is a single hunk. Points 1 and 3 are honoured
at the level of *meaning*.

### 2 — Multiple descriptions of the same artefact

[`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md)
allows the same blob (a file, a diff, a settlement) to be referenced
by many records — a critic's note, a censor's verdict, an agency's
summary. The matter is single; the descriptions are several. Point
5 has an operational form.

## What the implementation does not yet take into account

### A — Partitions over `perceive` output cannot be invented

`perceive` returns signals whose shape is fixed by
[`09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md).
A downstream frame cannot split one signal into three pieces and
hand each piece to a different slot. The act of "drawing a dashed
line through a single thing" has no representation in the
handoff schema.

### B — No critic for unsupported partitions

Figure 13-3's partition is unsupported by any perceptual cue. The
plan currently has no critic that asks "is this partition justified
by the inputs, or is it imposed by the template?" `critic.evidence`
checks claims against artefacts; it does not check whether the
*shape* of those claims was free or earned.

### C — Conventionality of partitions is not recorded

Point 6 — that the partition is one of many possible — has no
operational analogue. A frame's slots are presented as the way to
read the situation. There is no field on a frame manifest that
says "this partition is one valid template among others
(`frame.left-half-right-half`, etc.)."

### D — Function-imposed structure is not first-class

The reason *support* sits below *body* is functional, not
topological. In the plan, slot constraints are written into the
frame body as prose. There is no schema for "this slot stands in a
*bears* relation to that slot," and so the runtime cannot check
whether a candidate partition respects the function.

### E — Re-partition does not survive between phases

If `activate` or `deliberate` did invent a partition over a
`perceive` signal, that partition would have to be re-derived at
every subsequent phase, because signals are immutable handoffs.
There is no place in the workspace to record "the partition we
adopted for this run."

## Implication for the plan (no changes proposed here)

Figure 13-3 makes the case that perception is partly inventive:
boundaries can be supplied by the description rather than by the
scene. The implementation plan honours multiple descriptions over
the same artefact, but does not yet honour the inventive
boundary-drawing that this figure is about.

The gaps above are recorded here as analysis, not as a change
request. Any move to close them — for example, a re-partition hook
on signals in
[`09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md),
a `critic.partition-justification`, or a function-typed slot
constraint in
[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md)
— would touch the manifest schema in
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md)
and the identity scopes in
`THE-SOCIETY-OF-REPO/02-protocols/01-identity.md`, and so falls
under the "stop and ask" rules in
[`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md) §12 and
[`CLAUDE.md`](../../../../../CLAUDE.md).
