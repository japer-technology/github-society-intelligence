## The ideas Figure 13-10 actually carries

From the caption and §13.4 *Children's drawing-frames*:

1. **The decisive change is removing a check.** Step 2 used to
   ask "is one already drawn?"; the new step does not ask.
2. **Removing a check makes outputs more disciplined, not less.**
   Counter-intuitively, dropping a guard *increases* separability
   because the guard was the source of conflation.
3. **A handful of words rewrites behaviour.** The revision is
   three lines of italic, not a redesign.
4. **The loop structure stays.** Steps 1 and 3 form a generic
   "consider next; stop when done" frame.
5. **Defaults dominate.** When the procedure does not ask
   whether a feature has been drawn, the default is *draw it*.
   Defaults are policy.
6. **Each feature is drawn once and exactly once.** The new
   procedure encodes one-pass enumeration without explicit
   counting.

## What the implementation already absorbs

### 1 — Defaults as policy

[`07-policies-and-safety.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/07-policies-and-safety.md)
treats defaults as something to write down: authority defaults,
budget defaults, refusal defaults. Point 5 (defaults dominate)
is honoured as a design principle.

### 2 — Generic loop structure at the pipeline scale

The phase chain in
[`03-runtime-pipeline.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/03-runtime-pipeline.md)
has its own "consider next; stop when done" outline — the run
moves through phases until `settle` or refusal. Point 4 (loop
shape that survives revision) is honoured at one level.

### 3 — Small-edit-large-effect

Most manifest tweaks in
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md)
are small. Point 3 (few words rewrite behaviour) is consistent
with how the plan expects edits to look.

## What the implementation does not yet take into account

### A — "Removing a check" as a sanctioned kind of change

Most safety work in
[`07-policies-and-safety.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/07-policies-and-safety.md)
is about *adding* checks (censors, refusal paths, budgets). The
figure's central move — *removing* a check because the check
was wrong — has no special category. A removed-check edit
looks like any other diff and risks being treated as a
weakening. Point 2 (removing a guard can improve discipline) is
not recognised by the change vocabulary.

### B — In-frame loops do not exist

Steps 1 and 3 of the revised procedure form an internal loop
over a frame's slots. As noted under Figure 13-8 gap A, frames
have no in-frame procedure schema. A "for each remaining slot,
draw it" loop has nowhere to live.

### C — "One-pass enumeration" is not declarable

Point 6 — each feature drawn once and exactly once — would
correspond to a slot-fill policy of `unique-per-pass`. There is
no such policy field on frames. The runtime cannot warn when an
agency would fill the same slot twice in one pass, or skip a
slot when it should not.

### D — Default-`act` is risky and undocumented

Point 5 says that when no check is asked, the default is *do
it*. In the plan, defaults at the agency boundary lean toward
`draft` or `propose`, not `act`. The figure's revision works
because the omitted check is downstream of safety, not at the
authority boundary. The plan does not currently distinguish
"safe to default-act" steps (interior bookkeeping) from
"unsafe to default-act" steps (external effects); both look
like manifest edits.

### E — Procedure changes are not versioned distinctly from manifest changes

Figure 13-10 highlights a *procedure* change. The plan has no
separate kind for it; the diff in git treats it as any other
edit. As noted under Figure 13-9 gap F, the lack of
classification makes it hard to find or reason about all the
procedure changes that have happened.

## Implication for the plan (no changes proposed here)

Figure 13-10 is the figure that earns the lesson *the right
edit can be a deletion*. The implementation plan honours
defaults as policy and pipeline-level loops, but does not yet
honour in-frame loops, one-pass enumeration, or the sanctioned
removal of a check that was wrong.

The gaps above are recorded here as analysis, not as a change
request. Any move to close them — for example, a
`procedure-revision: remove-check` change category in
[`07-policies-and-safety.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/07-policies-and-safety.md),
a `unique-per-pass` slot policy in
[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md),
or a distinct settlement kind for procedure changes — would
touch
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md)
and the identity scopes in
`THE-SOCIETY-OF-REPO/02-protocols/01-identity.md`, and so falls
under the "stop and ask" rules in
[`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md) §12 and
[`CLAUDE.md`](../../../../../CLAUDE.md).
