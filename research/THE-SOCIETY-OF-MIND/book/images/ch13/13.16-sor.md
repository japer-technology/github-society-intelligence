# Figure 13-16 — Implementation analysis (SOR / FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION)

**Source figure:** [13-16.md](13-16.md) — *Single arch and double
arch, parsed two ways.*
**Analysis target:** [`FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/)
(plan-of-record for `.forgejo-society/` and
`.forgejo/workflows/forgejo-society.yaml`).

This file inventories the ideas carried by Figure 13-16 and
checks each one against the implementation plan.

---

## The ideas Figure 13-16 actually carries

From the caption and §13.7 *Duplications*:

1. **Parts do not always sum cleanly.** Sometimes the same
   material is counted under two descriptions; sometimes one
   part serves two structures.
2. **Two descriptions of the same matter can both be correct.**
   The lintel is *body* under one parse and *support-bearer*
   under another; both readings stand.
3. **Sharing is sometimes the right answer.** The middle
   column of a double-arch should not be duplicated in the
   accounting — it is genuinely one part used twice.
4. **Structural and functional parsings can disagree on
   count.** Structurally there is one column; functionally
   there are two roles. Either disagreement may be correct,
   depending on the question.
5. **Equality of expressions can hide overlap.** "*Arch =
   body + supports*" looks additive but is not: the regions
   overlap at their boundaries.
6. **Two arches sharing a column is not a degenerate case.**
   It is the normal case for connected structures, and the
   uniframe should accommodate it.
7. **Choosing structural vs functional description is itself a
   decision.** The figure invites the reader to recognise that
   the choice has cost.
8. **A shared part has two parents.** The middle column
   participates in two arches, which is a violation of
   strict tree structure.

## What the implementation already absorbs

### 1 — Multiple descriptors per blob

[`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md)
allows the same artefact (a file, a diff, a settlement) to be
referenced by many records. Point 2 (two descriptions of the
same matter) has a structural home.

### 2 — Agencies grouped by family, with cross-family signals

The catalogues in
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md)
let several agencies react to the same trigger. Point 6 (a
single thing playing multiple roles) is honoured for
*activations*.

### 3 — Settlement audit trail does not double-count effects

The act phase in
[`03-runtime-pipeline.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/03-runtime-pipeline.md)
applies one set of changes per settlement; downstream readers
see one event, not two. Point 3 (sharing is correct) is
honoured for the *write side*.

## What the implementation does not yet take into account

### A — Shared parts across structures are not first-class

Point 8 — one part with two parents — has no analogue. An
agency belongs to one family folder under `agencies/`. A
critic belongs to one parent unit it scrutinises. A frame
slot has one home. The "middle column" idiom — a unit owned
by two structures — would need to be expressible, and is not.

### B — Structural-vs-functional parsing is not declarable

Point 4 — that structural and functional counts may disagree
— is the kind of thing a frame should be able to declare.
The plan has no `parse_mode: structural | functional` field,
and so cannot ask "under which parse should this slot be
counted?"

### C — Overlap on slot boundaries has no representation

Point 5 — that body and supports overlap at the dashed line —
would correspond to a slot relationship like
`overlaps_with:`. Frame slots in
[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md)
are listed flatly; they do not declare overlap, adjacency, or
exclusion.

### D — No critic for "this thing is being double-counted"

Point 1 — parts sum cleanly sometimes and not others —
suggests a critic whose job is to flag when an artefact is
referenced by two structures that both claim ownership. No
such critic exists in
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md).

### E — The cost of choosing a parse is not surfaced

Point 7 — the choice of structural or functional has cost —
would benefit from a settlement field "this run chose
structural parse over functional; the trade-off was X." No
such field exists. The chosen parse is invisible after the
fact.

### F — Connected-structure cases are not the normal case in the schema

Point 6 — that shared parts are normal, not exceptional — is
not reflected in the plan's defaults. Catalogues assume
tree-like ownership (an agency is in one family). Genuine
sharing would require either symbolic links across folders
or a `also_member_of:` field, neither of which exists.

### G — Equality of expressions hiding overlap is undetected

Point 5's deeper warning — that "*A = B + C*" can mislead
when B and C overlap — applies to summary tiers. A brief that
collapses two signals as "*summary = signal1 + signal2*" does
not currently check whether the signals describe overlapping
matter. The arithmetic in summaries is implicitly disjoint.

## Implication for the plan (no changes proposed here)

Figure 13-16 is the figure that earns the *duplications* topic
by showing that good descriptions sometimes need to share, to
double-count, or to overlap. The implementation plan honours
multiple descriptors over the same matter, but does not yet
honour shared ownership, overlap, or structural-vs-functional
parse choice — the substantive moves this figure is about.

The gaps above are recorded here as analysis, not as a change
request. Any move to close them — for example, an
`also_member_of:` field on manifests, a `parse_mode:` on
frames, an `overlaps_with:` on slots, a
`critic.double-counting`, or a recorded parse-choice on
settlements — would touch
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md),
[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md),
[`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md),
the mapping in
[`11-mapping-sor-to-implementation.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/11-mapping-sor-to-implementation.md),
and the identity scopes in
`THE-SOCIETY-OF-REPO/02-protocols/01-identity.md`, and so falls
under the "stop and ask" rules in
[`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md) §12 and
[`CLAUDE.md`](../../../../../CLAUDE.md).
