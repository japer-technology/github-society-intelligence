# Figure 13-8 — Implementation analysis (SOR / FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION)

**Source figure:** [13-8.md](13-8.md) — *The child's drawing
procedure as a checklist.*
**Analysis target:** [`FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/)
(plan-of-record for `.forgejo-society/` and

This file inventories the ideas carried by Figure 13-8 and checks
each one against the implementation plan.

---

## The ideas Figure 13-8 actually carries

From the caption and §13.3 *Seeing and believing*:

1. **A schema becomes behaviour through a procedure.** The
   feature-network of Figure 13-7 only produces a drawing once a
   numbered procedure walks it.
2. **Each step both checks and acts.** Step 2 asks "is there a
   body yet?" and either skips or draws.
3. **Existence checks use feature descriptions, not identity.**
   "Is there a large closed figure?" answers yes when the head's
   circle is present — irrespective of whether it was drawn *as*
   a body.
4. **The procedure is satisfied by the first match.** No step
   says "find another large closed figure"; the first one
   encountered is taken.
5. **Side effects accumulate visibly.** Each step adds to the
   in-progress drawing; the final artefact is the sum.
6. **The procedure is short.** Five steps suffice. Frames are
   walked by small scripts.
7. **The error is in the bookkeeping, not in the steps.** Each
   step is locally reasonable; the trouble is the missing
   distinct-identity check.

## What the implementation already absorbs

### 1 — Workflows as numbered procedures over schemas

The phase chain `guard → normalize → guardrail → perceive →
activate → deliberate → criticize → censor → settle → act →
remember → report` in
[`03-runtime-pipeline.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/03-runtime-pipeline.md)
is a numbered walk over the run's schema. Point 1 (schema +
procedure produces behaviour) is the design.

### 2 — Steps that both check and act

Most phases combine a check with an effect: `guardrail` may
refuse; `criticize` records or vetoes; `settle` accepts or holds.
Point 2 is honoured at the workflow scale.

### 3 — Visible accumulation

[`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md)
preserves all signals and settlements in git; each step's
contribution is on disk. Point 5 holds.

### 4 — Short, pointed procedures

The pipeline is twelve phases; agency budgets in
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md)
keep individual unit procedures small. Point 6 is honoured.

## What the implementation does not yet take into account

### A — Within-agency procedures are not first-class

A frame's slot-walk — Figure 13-8's checklist over Figure 13-7's
network — has no schema in
[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md).
A frame names slots but does not declare the *order* in which
slots are visited, nor the check-then-act loop that visits them.
The drawing procedure of Figure 13-8 would, in plan terms, have
nowhere to live except inside an agency's prose.

### B — "First-match" semantics are not declared

Point 4 — the procedure accepts the first match — is critical
for understanding why the conflation in Figure 13-6 happens.
Frames in the plan do not declare match policy
(`first`, `unique`, `disjoint`). Without that declaration, a
critic cannot tell whether reuse of a filler is allowed or a
bug.

### C — Existence checks distinct from identity checks

Point 3 — checks by feature description, not identity — is
exactly the kind of distinction that should be expressible in a
slot constraint. The plan has neither feature-checks nor
identity-checks as schema primitives; both collapse into the
prose body of the agency.

### D — Procedure-as-artefact is missing

The workflow phases are first-class (they live in the workflow
yaml); within-frame procedures are not. There is no
`frame.<name>.procedure.md` companion to a frame's slot list.
Point 1 is honoured at the pipeline scale and absent at the
frame scale.

### E — Bookkeeping errors as a separate kind

The figure makes vivid that the steps can be perfect while the
*bookkeeping* is wrong. The plan's critic vocabulary
(`critic.evidence`, `critic.scope`, `critic.diff-skeptic`) does
not include a critic whose only job is to detect bookkeeping
slips — accepted-as-distinct fillers that share identity, or
slots over-served by a single filler. See also Figure 13-6 gap
A.

## Implication for the plan (no changes proposed here)

Figure 13-8 is the figure that earns *frames are walked by
procedures*. The implementation plan honours the pipeline as a
procedure over the run, but does not yet honour procedures over
the slots of an individual frame — and so cannot represent the
match policies, bookkeeping rules, and feature-vs-identity
distinctions that produce or prevent the conflation of Figure
13-6.

The gaps above are recorded here as analysis, not as a change
request. Any move to close them — for example, an
in-frame `walk_order`, a `match_policy` field, a
`critic.bookkeeping`, or a frame-level procedure manifest —
would touch
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md),
[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md),
[`09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md),
and the identity scopes in
`THE-SOCIETY-OF-REPO/02-protocols/01-identity.md`, and so falls
under the "stop and ask" rules in
[`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md) §12 and
[`CLAUDE.md`](../../../../../CLAUDE.md).
