This file inventories the ideas carried by Figure 13-11 and
checks each one against the implementation plan.

---

## The ideas Figure 13-11 actually carries

From the caption and §13.5 *Learning a script*:

1. **Expertise is the absence of deliberation.** Knowing is
   contrasted with thinking, not equated with it.
2. **Practice produces a kind of knowledge that does not require
   reconsideration.** The expert has stored the answer, not the
   reasoning.
3. **Speed is a side effect.** The expert is fast because
   nothing is being weighed, not because the weighing is faster.
4. **The epigraph asserts an empirical claim about cognition.**
   It is not metaphor; the chapter will model it.
5. **Deliberation and competence can be in tension.** The unit
   that thinks may be slower than the unit that has compiled
   away the question.

## What the implementation already absorbs

### 1 — Deliberation as a distinct phase

[`03-runtime-pipeline.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/03-runtime-pipeline.md)
names `deliberate` as a separate phase. The plan does not
conflate "running" with "considering"; deliberation is a
nameable, skippable step. Point 1 has a structural home.

### 2 — K-lines as compiled prior activations

[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md)
defines K-lines as records of useful prior activations, with
`kline-retriever` as the agency that brings them back. This is
the operational shape of "stored answers" — point 2 in its
implementation form.

### 3 — Budgets that punish unnecessary deliberation

`budget.max_tool_calls` and `budget.max_wall_clock_s` in
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md)
make slow deliberation expensive, biasing the system toward
already-known answers. Point 3 (speed as side effect) has a
weak shadow here.

## What the implementation does not yet take into account

### A — No "skip deliberation when K-line is present" rule

The phase chain runs `deliberate → criticize → censor` for
every activation. There is no recognised path that says "this
situation matches a recent K-line so closely that `deliberate`
can be bypassed." Knowing-without-thinking, the epigraph's
claim, has no operational form.

### B — Scripts are not first-class

Figure 13-11 prepares Figure 13-12's distinction between a
*program* (deliberative) and a *script* (compiled). The plan
has K-lines but no `script.<name>` artefact that bundles a
compiled action sequence. K-lines reactivate; they do not
*run*.

### C — Expertise is not measured

There is no metric on a unit that reports "this agency now
resolves N% of its activations from prior K-lines without
deliberating." Without that metric, the runtime cannot tell
whether practice is producing the expected compilation.

### D — Deliberation and competence tension is not surfaced

Point 5's tension — that the deliberating unit may be slower
than the compiled unit — has no representation. The runtime
cannot say "this agency has a compiled script available; using
it would be faster than its current deliberation path." No
critic or censor reports the lost speed.

### E — The figure is an epigraph; the plan has no epigraph slot either

A minor but real observation: the figure announces a *posture*
the chapter will then earn. The plan has manifests, schemas,
and prose, but no first-class "design posture" record that
later mechanisms refer back to. The figure's role — frame the
section — has no implementation counterpart.

## Implication for the plan (no changes proposed here)

Figure 13-11 is the figure that earns the contrast between
*thinking* and *knowing*. The implementation plan honours both
sides — `deliberate` and K-lines — but does not yet let one
displace the other, nor measure how often it should.

The gaps above are recorded here as analysis, not as a change
request. Any move to close them — for example, a
`deliberate.skip_if_kline_match` policy in
[`07-policies-and-safety.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/07-policies-and-safety.md),
a `script.<name>` artefact, or an "expertise rate" metric in
[`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md)
— would touch
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md),
[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md),
and the identity scopes in
`THE-SOCIETY-OF-REPO/02-protocols/01-identity.md`, and so falls
under the "stop and ask" rules in
[`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md) §12 and
[`CLAUDE.md`](../../../../../CLAUDE.md).
