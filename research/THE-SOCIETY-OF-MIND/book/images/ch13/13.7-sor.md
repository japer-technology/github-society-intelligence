# Figure 13-7 — Implementation analysis (SOR / FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION)

**Source figure:** [13-7.md](13-7.md) — *A feature-network for
"person".*
**Analysis target:** [`FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/)
(plan-of-record for `.forgejo-society/` and
`.forgejo/workflows/forgejo-society.yaml`).

This file inventories the ideas carried by Figure 13-7 and checks
each one against the implementation plan.

---

## The ideas Figure 13-7 actually carries

From the caption and §13.3 *Seeing and believing*:

1. **A concept is a list of slots, not a picture.** *Person* is
   represented by HEAD, EYES, MOUTH, BODY, ARMS, LEGS — labelled
   places to be filled.
2. **Each slot carries a feature description.** *Large closed
   figure*, *small closed figures*, *curve* — the slot expects a
   filler of a specified shape.
3. **Relations between slots are part of the schema.** EYES and
   MOUTH attach to HEAD; ARMS and LEGS attach to BODY's upper
   and lower parts.
4. **Slots can share feature descriptions without sharing
   identity.** HEAD and BODY both demand *large closed figure*,
   which is the precondition for the conflation made vivid in
   Figure 13-6.
5. **Anchoring is positional and qualitative, not metric.**
   *Upper part* and *lower part* are not coordinates.
6. **The whole network is small.** Seven slots are enough to
   build a recognisable schema for *person*. Frames are economical
   by design.
7. **The network is the description; there is no image hiding
   behind it.** Minsky's point is that nothing more visual is
   needed.

## What the implementation already absorbs

### 1 — Frames as slot bundles

[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md)
defines frames as named bundles of slots with expected fillers.
This is point 1 directly. Frames such as `frame.regression-debug`
or `frame.self-modification` are small, intentional networks.

### 2 — Slot expectations carried in the manifest

Each frame's slot fillers are written into its manifest body.
Point 2 is honoured: slots come with descriptions, however
narrowly typed.

### 3 — Activation as the way slots are filled

The polyneme machinery in
[`06-*`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md)
lets several agencies contribute to filling slots when a frame
fires. There is no picture-in-the-middle; the frame *is* the
representation. Point 7 holds.

### 4 — Small, pointed catalogues

The first-ship catalogues in
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md)
follow the same economy: few slots, each tight. Point 6.

## What the implementation does not yet take into account

### A — Inter-slot relations are not first-class

Point 3 — that *eyes attach to head*, *arms attach to body's
upper part* — has no schema in
[`06-*`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md).
Slots are listed; the relations between them live only in the
prose body. A frame cannot declare
`relations: [ {child: eyes, parent: head, anchor: inside} ]`
and the runtime cannot validate that fillers respect it.

### B — Shared feature descriptions across slots are not detected

Point 4 — two slots demanding the same feature — is the seed of
the conflation in Figure 13-6. The plan does not scan a frame
for "two slots whose expected filler descriptions overlap." No
critic flags it, no schema field warns of it. The trap that
Figure 13-7 sets is therefore representable but unseen.

### C — Qualitative anchors have no type

Point 5's *upper part* / *lower part* would need a small
vocabulary of qualitative anchors (`inside`, `above`, `attached`,
`adjacent`). The frame schema has no such vocabulary and no
validator that checks fillers against it. Anchors are prose.

### D — No "fill same feature with different identities" check

The bookkeeping rule absent from Figure 13-6 — that two slots
demanding *large closed figure* must be filled with two
*different* large closed figures — has no operational form. The
runtime does not currently distinguish "satisfied by reuse" from
"satisfied by a fresh filler."

### E — Frames are not introspected as networks

Point 1's *network* view of a frame — slots plus relations — is
not surfaced in any tool, view, or settlement. A maintainer
cannot ask "render `frame.regression-debug` as a graph and check
that no two slots accept the same filler under the same
identity." The data to do so is not present in the schema (gaps
A and B).

## Implication for the plan (no changes proposed here)

Figure 13-7 is the figure that earns the *frame* as a unit of
description. The implementation plan honours frames as slot
bundles, but does not yet honour the relations between slots,
the qualitative anchors, or the shared-filler trap that the
figure sets up for Figure 13-6 to exploit.

The gaps above are recorded here as analysis, not as a change
request. Any move to close them — for example, a `relations:`
block on the frame schema, a qualitative-anchor vocabulary, or a
`critic.frame-shape` — would touch
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md),
[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md),
and the identity scopes in
`THE-SOCIETY-OF-REPO/02-protocols/01-identity.md`, and so falls
under the "stop and ask" rules in
[`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md) §12 and
[`CLAUDE.md`](../../../../../CLAUDE.md).
