# Figure 13-15 — Implementation analysis (SOR / FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION)

**Source figure:** [13-15.md](13-15.md) — *The child mid-stroke at
the long block's edge.*
**Analysis target:** [`FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/)
(plan-of-record for `.forgejo-society/` and
`.forgejo/workflows/forgejo-society.yaml`).

This file inventories the ideas carried by Figure 13-15 and
checks each one against the implementation plan.

---

## The ideas Figure 13-15 actually carries

From the caption and §13.6 *The frontier effect*:

1. **The procedure runs out of guidance mid-step.** With no
   nearby landmark, "where do I stop?" has no easy answer.
2. **The system asks itself a question it cannot answer in its
   own terms.** The question-marked arrows are the unit's
   acknowledgement of an empty slot.
3. **The easy answer wins over the right answer.** "Stop where
   the long block stops" is reachable; "extend by N more units"
   is not.
4. **The frontier effect is a procedural shortcut, not a
   perceptual error.** The visual system is fine; the
   step-by-step procedure is what reaches for the wrong anchor.
5. **The moment of failure is observable from inside.** The
   in-progress drawing exposes the missing decision — it is not
   the post-hoc artefact that diagnoses, it is the pause.
6. **Local landmarks beat absent global ones, every time.** When
   the procedure must produce an output, it will use the
   nearest specifiable thing.
7. **Falling back to a landmark is rational under bounded
   budget.** Refusing to stop would be worse; the unit completes
   the run by accepting the distortion.

## What the implementation already absorbs

### 1 — Budgets force termination

`budget.max_tool_calls` and `budget.max_wall_clock_s` in
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md)
make unbounded deliberation impossible. Point 7 (falling back is
rational under bounded budget) is enforced by design.

### 2 — Settle as the place where "good enough" becomes "finished"

`settle` in
[`03-runtime-pipeline.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/03-runtime-pipeline.md)
makes the call to accept and act on the workspace. The
operational analogue of "the child completes the drawing" is a
settled run. The decision to accept is recorded, not hidden.

### 3 — Censors as a no-act path

[`07-policies-and-safety.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/07-policies-and-safety.md)
allows censors to block the `act` phase. Point 7's "refusing
would be worse" assumption is not always true in the plan — a
censor may decide refusal *is* the right outcome.

## What the implementation does not yet take into account

### A — Mid-step "I don't know" has no representation

Point 2's question-marked arrows have no schema. An agency can
produce a settlement or refuse, but it has no in-run state
called "this step is undefined under the current frame." The
plan treats decision-points as silent unless the unit emits a
signal.

### B — "Easy answer" tracking is absent

Point 3 — that the easy answer wins — would correspond to a
critic that records, for each output, the alternatives the unit
*could* have chosen and the apparent reason it chose this one.
No critic in
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md)
collects "rejected nearby answers." A snap-to-landmark
selection is invisible after the fact.

### C — The pause itself is not first-class

Point 5 — that the in-progress moment is the diagnostic — would
require a representation of agency state mid-deliberation, not
just at completion. The plan persists signals and settlements;
it does not persist "this agency considered three candidates
and was hesitating between two." Diagnostics are post-hoc only.

### D — No metric for "distortion to complete on budget"

Point 7's trade-off — accept distortion rather than fail to
finish — has no recorded shape. A run is `settled` or
`refused`; there is no `settled_with_compromise` flag and no
field that says "this output was the budget-feasible answer,
not the best answer."

### E — Refusal vs distorted-completion is not policy-driven

The plan implicitly prefers settled runs (the workflow is
designed to reach `act`). It does not let a maintainer write
"prefer refusal over distortion in domain D" as a policy. The
choice between Figure 13-15's distorted completion and an
explicit "I cannot stop here" refusal is not a policy decision
the plan currently exposes.

### F — Bounded-rationality is implicit, not declared

The whole figure is a bounded-rationality argument: under
bounded budget, the procedure does the best it can. The plan
enforces budgets but does not document the *reasoning* about
bounded rationality. Maintainers reading
[`07-policies-and-safety.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/07-policies-and-safety.md)
do not encounter the design posture "when budgets bind,
expect describable answers to outcompete accurate ones."

## Implication for the plan (no changes proposed here)

Figure 13-15 is the figure that earns the *moment* of the
frontier effect — the visible pause when a procedure exhausts
its local guidance. The implementation plan honours budgets and
settlement but does not yet honour the in-deliberation state,
the rejected alternatives, or the compromise-on-budget category
that this figure invites.

The gaps above are recorded here as analysis, not as a change
request. Any move to close them — for example, a mid-run
"undefined-step" signal in
[`09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md),
a `critic.rejected-candidates`, a `settled_with_compromise`
outcome in
[`03-runtime-pipeline.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/03-runtime-pipeline.md),
or an explicit refusal-vs-distortion policy in
[`07-policies-and-safety.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/07-policies-and-safety.md)
— would touch
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md),
[`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md),
and the identity scopes in
`THE-SOCIETY-OF-REPO/02-protocols/01-identity.md`, and so falls
under the "stop and ask" rules in
[`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md) §12 and
[`CLAUDE.md`](../../../../../CLAUDE.md).
