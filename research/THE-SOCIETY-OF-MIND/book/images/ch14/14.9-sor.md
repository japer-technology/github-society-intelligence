# Figure 14-9 — Implementation analysis (SOR / FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION)

**Source figure:** [14-9.md](14-9.md) — *Description fed by sensation and expectation.*
**Analysis target:** [`FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/)
(plan-of-record for `.forgejo-society/` and
`.forgejo/workflows/forgejo-society.yaml`).

---

## The ideas Figure 14-9 actually carries

1. **Two equal inputs feed one description.** Sensation and
   Expectation arrive symmetrically.
2. **Perception is bidirectional.** Memory reaches forward into
   what is currently being recognised.
3. **The Description is the joint product.** Neither stream alone
   produces it.
4. **Expectation has a source distinct from sensation.** Whatever
   feeds the right-hand arrow is a different organ from whatever
   feeds the left-hand arrow.
5. **Combination is the runtime's central operation.** The
   interesting work is not arriving, it is being assembled.
6. **A single named artefact is updated by both sides.** *The
   description* is one thing, not two parallel records.

## What the implementation already absorbs

### 1 — Expectation streams exist as named units

`agency.memory.prior-decision-resonator` and
`agency.memory.kline-retriever` in
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md)
are the right-hand arrow. They surface prior decisions and
reactivate K-lines whose `restore_when` matches the current
stimulus. Frames in
[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md)
also carry expectations as defaults.

### 2 — Sensation streams exist as named units

`agency.perception.intake-bee`,
`agency.perception.issue-kind-detector`,
`agency.perception.urgency-detector` are the left-hand arrow. They
read `stimulus.json` and write `percepts.jsonl`.

### 3 — A shared workspace as the Description

`state/mind/issues/<n>/workspace.md` and `blackboard.md` in
[`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md)
are the *single artefact* both streams update. The plan calls them
"the human-readable face of the layered state," which is exactly
the figure's *Description* box.

### 4 — `activate` is the join point

[`03-runtime-pipeline.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/03-runtime-pipeline.md)
makes `activate` the phase that reads percepts *and*
`memory/klines/**` *and* `memory/analogies/**`. The arrows meet
there.

## What the implementation does not yet take into account

### A — The two streams are not weighted against each other

Both streams emit signals, but nothing in
[`09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md)
records *which side a signal came from*. A future critic could
ask "is the current description dominated by expectation when
sensation disagrees?" — but only if the signal source is typed as
`sensory` vs `expectation-derived`. Today it is not.

### B — No "expectation override" signal

Figure 14-9's hazard is that expectation can *outvote* sensation
(seeing what one expected to see). The plan has scope and
evidence critics, but no critic whose specific job is to flag
"this description has more expectation than evidence behind it."

### C — The Description is one file, but its provenance is not
recorded

`workspace.md` is updated by many agencies. The file does not
carry per-line attribution of "this line came from sensation,
this line came from a K-line." A reader cannot tell where any
particular claim originated. The blackboard is layered by phase,
not by source-stream.

### D — Expectation is monotonic within a run

Once frames and K-lines are activated, they bias every subsequent
phase. Sensation cannot *turn off* an active expectation within a
run — only critic objections can, and they object to the
candidate, not to the prior. The figure's symmetric arrows are
asymmetric in time.

## Implication for the plan (no changes proposed here)

Figure 14-9 is the figure the plan most closely already
implements. The remaining gaps are about *legibility* and
*balance*: the two streams exist, they meet, but the runtime
cannot yet ask which one is winning. The gaps above are recorded
as analysis, not as a change request. Any move to close them —
for example, a `signal.source_kind: sensory|expectation`, a
`critic.expectation-dominance`, or per-line provenance on
`workspace.md` — would touch the signal schema and the workspace
write rules, and so falls under the "stop and ask" rules in
[`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md) §12 and
[`CLAUDE.md`](../../../../../CLAUDE.md).
