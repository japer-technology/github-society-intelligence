## The ideas Figure 14-16 actually carries

1. **The same template, different labels.** A spatial template is
   reused for a non-spatial comparison.
2. **Cross-realm correspondence.** *Tall/Short* lines up with one
   spatial axis; *Wider/Thinner* with the other.
3. **Some cells settle the question, others do not.**
   *Taller-and-wider* yields *more*; *taller-and-thinner* yields
   *unsure*.
4. **"Unsure" is a first-class outcome.** Failure to combine is
   itself a recorded cell, not a missing one.
5. **Comparisons are typed by axis.** *Taller* is on the height
   axis; *wider* is on the width axis; their combination is
   well-defined precisely because the axes are.
6. **The chapter's larger claim.** A single uniframe (interaction-
   square) can move between realms by relabelling.

## What the implementation already absorbs

### 1 ŌĆö Frames as relabelled templates

[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md)
ships seven distinct frames (`question`, `bug`, `feature`,
`code-change`, `security-sensitive`, `self-modification`, `novel`).
Several of them share the *shape* of slots (each carries a
*relevant region* and a *required action*); the plan therefore
already reuses templates with different labels, even though it
does not name the shared template.

### 2 ŌĆö Confidence and "unsure" as data

Handoffs in
[`09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md)
carry a `confidence Ōłł [0,1]` field and a `status` enum that
includes `deferred`. The plan has a way to say "outcome not
settled by this combination," which is the figure's *unsure*
cell.

### 3 ŌĆö Non-spatial reasoning lives in critics

`critic.evidence`
([`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md))
already evaluates "do these claims combine to support this
conclusion?" ŌĆö the same kind of question Figure 14-16 asks about

## What the implementation does not yet take into account

### A ŌĆö No way to declare "this is the same template as that one"

The plan has no `extends:` relation between frames (same gap as
Figure 14-12, here viewed through the interaction-square lens). A
spatial-comparison reasoning structure cannot be re-instantiated
as a tall-vs-thin reasoning structure by simply relabelling axes,
because there is no parent template to relabel.

### B ŌĆö *Unsure* is per-handoff, not per-combination

The plan can record that *an agency* is unsure (via `confidence`
or `status: deferred`). It cannot record that *a particular pair
of causes* is intrinsically unsure ŌĆö i.e., that *the cell* is
unsure, regardless of which agency was asked. The figure's
*taller-and-thinner ŌćÆ unsure* is a property of the combination,
not of the reasoner.

### C ŌĆö Transitivity / comparison logic has no agency

The figure is part of Minsky's *Society of More*. The plan does
not ship an agency family for *comparison* (more/less/same).
`critic.evidence` handles claims-and-support; nothing handles
ordering relations. The plan therefore cannot easily express why
*taller and wider* settles a comparison and *taller and thinner*
does not.

### D ŌĆö Cross-realm reuse is not measured

The same gap as Figure 14-12 (D): when the plan happens to reuse
a shape, that reuse is not counted, so the shape never accrues
the status of *uniframe*. Figure 14-16's whole point ŌĆö *here is
the same template applied to something completely different* ŌĆö
cannot be detected by the runtime.

## Implication for the plan (no changes proposed here)

Figure 14-16 says the same combinator that governs motion also
governs comparison: relabel the axes, the structure is the same.
The plan reuses shapes informally and does not record either the
shape or the reuse. The gaps above are recorded as analysis, not
as a change request. Any move to close them ŌĆö for example, a
shared `interaction-square` template with axis-relabelling, a
*combination-level* unsure outcome, or an agency family for
ordering relations ŌĆö would touch the frame, signal, and manifest
schemas and the identity scopes, and so falls under the "stop and
ask" rules in

[`CLAUDE.md`](../../../../../CLAUDE.md).
