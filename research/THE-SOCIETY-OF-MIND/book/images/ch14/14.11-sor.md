# Figure 14-11 — Implementation analysis (SOR / FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION)

**Source figure:** [14-11.md](14-11.md) — *Four sides of the Block-Arch as four obstacles.*
**Analysis target:** [`FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/)
(plan-of-record for `.forgejo-society/` and
`.forgejo/workflows/forgejo-society.yaml`).

---

## The ideas Figure 14-11 actually carries

1. **A trap is a conjunction, not a single thing.** Four obstacles
   cooperate to produce one effect.
2. **Each obstacle blocks one named direction.** Left blocks
   leftward motion; top blocks upward motion; and so on.
3. **The floor counts.** Adding the *bottom* obstacle is the
   reformulation move; without it, the trap is not a trap.
4. **The space of acceptable motions is finite and enumerable.**
   The two-dimensional motion space is exactly four directions.
5. **The hand is the bearer of the blocked capability.** The
   figure separates the *agent* (hand) from the *constraints*
   (sides).
6. **Closure is the property to be detected.** What matters is not
   any single obstacle but the fact that *all* directions are
   blocked.

## What the implementation already absorbs

### 1 — Censors as per-direction blockers

[`07-policies-and-safety.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/07-policies-and-safety.md)
ships censors that each block one *kind* of action: cloud egress,
workflow-file edits, secret access. Each censor is "an obstacle
on one side."

### 2 — Suppressors fire per candidate output

`act.ts` runs suppressors on each candidate. Each suppressor
disables one branch of action, the way each obstacle in the
figure disables one direction.

### 3 — `inhibits:` on manifests

[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md)
allows an agency manifest to declare `inhibits: [<agent-id>, ...]`,
emitting inhibitory signals at activation. This is the wiring by
which one component disables another — the *---o* of the figure.

## What the implementation does not yet take into account

### A — No "all directions blocked" detector

The plan has many things that block individual directions and no
agency that *detects the conjunction*. There is no
`agency.safety.no-move-possible` whose job is to fire when every
candidate action has been suppressed and every direction inhibited.
A trapped run today produces a *blocked* settlement
([`07-policies-and-safety.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/07-policies-and-safety.md))
without naming *trapped* as a distinct cognitive state.

### B — The "floor" obstacle has no canonical name

The figure's reformulation move is recognising that the *bottom*
side is also an obstacle, not just the visible upright pillars.
The plan does not have a habit of asking "what is the obstacle we
are not naming because it is the default?" — for example, the
default that *do nothing* is always available. *Do nothing* is a
direction, and nothing in the runtime currently treats it as one
that can also be inhibited.

### C — The motion space is implicit, not enumerated

The figure works because the motion space has exactly four
elements and each can be labelled. The plan's "directions" — the
set of effects an agency might have — is open-ended. Without an
enumeration, *closure* cannot be detected; you cannot say "all
directions are blocked" without first being able to count them.

### D — Agent and constraint are not separated at the manifest
level

In the figure, the hand is distinct from the four sides. In the
plan, an agency's authority, tools, and inhibitions all sit on
the same manifest. There is no separate *constraint object* that
the agency "feels" as a wall. Constraints are properties *of* an
agency, not entities *around* one.

## Implication for the plan (no changes proposed here)

Figure 14-11 names a state — *trapped* — and shows that it is
constructed from a conjunction of per-direction inhibitions. The
plan can produce that state (when everything is censored a
blocked settlement is written) but cannot *recognise* it as a
distinct cognitive condition with its own signal. The gaps above
are recorded as analysis, not as a change request. Any move to
close them — for example, an enumerated effect-space schema, a
*no-move-possible* signal, or a *do-nothing-as-direction*
convention — would touch the manifest, signal, and censor
documents, and so falls under the "stop and ask" rules in
[`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md) §12 and
[`CLAUDE.md`](../../../../../CLAUDE.md).
