# Figure 14-12 — Implementation analysis (SOR / FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION)

**Source figure:** [14-12.md](14-12.md) — *The two-dimensional container uniframe.*
**Analysis target:** [`FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/)
(plan-of-record for `.forgejo-society/` and
`.forgejo/workflows/forgejo-society.yaml`).

---

## The ideas Figure 14-12 actually carries

1. **Stripping surface details yields a reusable template.** The
   arch becomes a plain rectangle.
2. **A *uniframe* is a frame with all the unnecessary specifics
   removed.** Identity is preserved by what is kept, not by what is
   lost.
3. **The template has labelled positions, not labelled contents.**
   Each side is a *direction*, not an object.
4. **The interior denotes a region.** What is enclosed matters; how
   it is filled does not.
5. **The same uniframe applies to many domains.** Boxes, rooms,
   abstract enclosures, sets of constraints.
6. **Uniframes are nameable, storable, and citable.** They are
   small enough to reuse and stable enough to refer to.

## What the implementation already absorbs

### 1 — Frames are nameable templates

[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md)
defines frames as YAML files with a `name`, a `slots` block, and a
list of defaults. The first-ship catalogue (`question`, `bug`,
`feature`, `code-change`, `security-sensitive`,
`self-modification`, `novel`) is a small fixed library of
templates. The figure's "small reusable shape" idea has a direct
operational form.

### 2 — Slot positions, not slot contents

A frame's `slots` are *named positions* filled by `filled_by:
[<agent-id>, ...]`. The frame declares the shape; the agencies
fill it. This is the labelled-positions / unlabelled-contents
distinction.

### 3 — Analogies tree for transported templates

`memory/analogies/`
([`11-mapping-sor-to-implementation.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/11-mapping-sor-to-implementation.md))
exists for cross-domain transports of patterns. A uniframe that
proves to apply in many domains belongs there.

## What the implementation does not yet take into account

### A — No *uniframe* kind distinct from *frame*

The plan has one kind of frame and uses it for one kind of work
(matching a stimulus and seeding defaults). Figure 14-12's
uniframe is a different artefact: a *content-free template* that
many concrete frames could share. The schema in
[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md)
has no `extends:` or `template_of:` field by which a concrete
frame could declare *I am one filling of this uniframe*.

### B — Stripping is not a documented operation

The figure's move is to *take an existing structure and remove its
specifics* to find the template inside. The plan has no agency
whose job is to abstract repeated frames into a uniframe.
`agency.meta-admin.*` exists, but the first-ship roles do not
include "uniframe extractor."

### C — Interior is not a typed region

Figure 14-12 cares that the rectangle has an *interior* — "the
region enclosed." Frames in the plan have slots (named fillers)
and defaults, but no notion of a *region* the frame governs. The
closest analogue is the set of paths a frame's `any_paths` match,
but those are *triggers*, not *contents*.

### D — Reuse across domains is not measured

A uniframe earns its name by being applied in multiple realms.
The plan does not track *how many distinct domains* a frame is
fitting. There is no field on a frame that says "instantiated by
these N other frames." Without that, recognising that a frame has
quietly become a uniframe cannot be automated.

## Implication for the plan (no changes proposed here)

Figure 14-12 turns "frame" into a layered concept: concrete
frames as instances, uniframes as their shared template. The
plan has the lower layer and a place to store the upper, but no
schema by which the two are linked. The gaps above are recorded
as analysis, not as a change request. Any move to close them —
for example, an `extends:` field on frames, a `uniframe.schema.json`,
or an `agency.meta-admin.uniframe-extractor` — would touch the
frame schema and the identity scopes, and so falls under the
"stop and ask" rules in
[`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md) §12 and
[`CLAUDE.md`](../../../../../CLAUDE.md).
