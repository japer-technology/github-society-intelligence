---

## The ideas Figure 14-15 actually carries

1. **Two independent causes, one combined effect.** The grid
   enumerates every pair.
2. **Each cause owns one axis.** Horizontal is one cause;
   vertical is the other.
3. **The cell is the unit of combined meaning.** Each of the nine
   cells stands for a distinct combined outcome.
4. **A *no-motion* centre.** The combination "neither cause
   fires" is itself a named state.
5. **Reach by combination.** The corner cells (NE, NW, SE, SW)
   are unreachable by either cause alone.
6. **One template, many uses.** Wrist, shoulder, hip, ankle,
   thumb, eye — the same 3×3 shape governs each.

---

## What the implementation already absorbs

### 1 — Signals can combine

[`09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md)
allows many signals from many sources within one cycle. An agency's
activation in `activate.ts` is a function of the signal set, so
*combined cause → combined effect* is, in the loosest sense,
expressible.

### 2 — Frames as joint-cause selectors

A frame's `matches` block fires when any of its triggers match.
When the triggers are independent (a `label` *and* a `path`), the
selected frame is a function of two causes. The plan has the
ingredients for axis-shaped reasoning.

### 3 — Layered blackboard

`blackboard.md`
([`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md))
is documented as "layered cognitive state." Layers are a step
towards axes.

## What the implementation does not yet take into account

### A — No two-axis combinator object

Figure 14-15 is a *table* — two axes, one cell per intersection.
None of the plan's schemas have this shape. There is no
`interaction.schema.json` whose body is *"axis A: <values>; axis
B: <values>; cell: <outcome>."* Combined-cause reasoning happens
inside an agency's prose, not in a typed object.

### B — Causes are not labelled as causes

The plan's signals have `name`, `energy`, `evidence`,
`suggested_effects` — but no `axis:` or `cause_of:` annotation. A
critic cannot ask *"are these two signals on the same axis or
different axes?"* because the axis is not declared anywhere.

### C — A *no-motion* state has no canonical signal

The figure's centre cell — "neither cause fires" — is itself a
state worth naming. The plan has no signal whose meaning is
*"neither candidate cause is active right now."* Silence is the
default, not a record.

### D — Corner cells (combined-only outcomes) have no schema

Outcomes that *cannot* be reached by any single cause are exactly
the most interesting outputs of an interaction-square. The plan
does not have a place to record "this outcome required signals X
*and* Y; neither alone would produce it." Co-occurrence is not
captured by signals or by K-lines as a typed relation.

### E — Reuse of the template is not modelled

The figure's payoff is one template, many bodies. The plan would
need an `interaction-square` object that could be *instantiated*
with different axis labels (Move/Left-Right, More/Tall-Thin, …).
Today, each pairwise reasoning task would need its own bespoke
critic.

## Implication for the plan (no changes proposed here)

Figure 14-15 is the smallest possible *interaction model*: two
causes, nine outcomes, one template. The plan has the ingredients
(signals, frames, blackboard layers) but not the combinator. The
gaps above are recorded as analysis, not as a change request. Any
move to close them — for example, an `interaction.schema.json`,
an `axis:` field on signals, or an `agency.assembly.combiner` —
would touch the signal and frame schemas and the identity scopes,
and so falls under the "stop and ask" rules in
[`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md) §12 and
[`CLAUDE.md`](../../../../../CLAUDE.md).
