# Figure 14-6 — Implementation analysis (SOR / FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION)

**Source figure:** [14-6.md](14-6.md) — *Solving the nine-dot puzzle by leaving the square.*
**Analysis target:** [`FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/)
(plan-of-record for `.forgejo-society/` and
`.forgejo/workflows/forgejo-society.yaml`).

---

## The ideas Figure 14-6 actually carries

1. **The solution requires crossing a self-imposed line.** Strokes
   must extend past the implied boundary.
2. **The cheating-feeling is informative.** A perceived ethical
   barrier marks a real cognitive constraint, even when no rule is
   broken.
3. **Reformulation is a discrete move, not a refinement.** The
   solver does not search inside the square more carefully; they
   leave the square.
4. **Start and end are explicit.** Once the boundary dissolves, the
   path needs anchored endpoints to be tractable.
5. **The solver must overrule the eye.** Recognition of the trap is
   followed by a deliberate choice to ignore it.
6. **A solution is also a re-description of the problem.** The
   four-line path *retroactively* shows what the problem actually
   permitted.

## What the implementation already absorbs

### 1 — Self-modification as a guarded crossing

The `self-modification` frame in
[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md)
exists precisely because some moves cross a line the rest of the
system silently obeys (touching `agencies/`, `policies/`,
`governance/`, `AGENTS.md`). The plan acknowledges that *some
problems require leaving the box*, and routes those moves through
extra controls.

### 2 — Approval-gate as the ethical-barrier mechanism

[`07-policies-and-safety.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/07-policies-and-safety.md)
makes the approval-gate the place where "this candidate crosses a
line that requires human authority" is *settled* rather than
ignored. The cheating-feeling becomes a structured request.

### 3 — Settlement as a re-description

`workspace/active-settlements/<settlement_id>.yml`
([`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md))
is the artefact in which a candidate action *re-describes the
problem it solves*. Once a settlement is written, the run's final
view of the stimulus differs from its initial view. The figure's
"solution restates the problem" idea has an operational form here.

## What the implementation does not yet take into account

### A — Crossings are only handled when they touch governed files

The plan flags crossings that touch `agencies/`, `policies/`, or
`governance/`. It does not flag crossings that touch nothing
governed but still leave the *frame's expected region* — for
example, a `bug.frame.yml` run that proposes a feature change. The
implied square exists at the level of *every frame*, not only at
the level of self-modification.

### B — No "reframe" move at the agency level

Figure 14-6's hard step is reframing — recognising that the
boundary is voluntary. The plan does not have an
`agency.meta-admin.reframer` that, on seeing repeated objections
inside one frame, proposes switching to a different frame. The
runtime picks a frame in `activate` and rarely revisits the
choice within a single run.

### C — The cheating-feeling is not represented

The plan has objections, signals, and inhibitions. It has no
signal whose name is, in effect, *"I am suspicious of my own
default."* Critics object to *candidates*; nothing objects to the
*frame* itself once it has been chosen.

### D — Endpoints are not always anchored

The figure makes its solution legible by labelling `start` and
`end`. The plan's runs have a clear start (`stimulus.json`) but
the end-condition for a single agency loop is governed by
`budget.max_tool_calls` and `budget.max_wall_clock_s`
([`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md))
rather than by a named target. *Reach this kind of state* is not a
field on a manifest.

## Implication for the plan (no changes proposed here)

Figure 14-6 names the move that gets past Figure 14-5: deliberately
treating an implicit boundary as crossable. The plan handles this
only for the explicit boundary of governed files. The gaps above
are recorded as analysis, not as a change request. Any move to
close them — for example, an `agency.meta-admin.frame-reconsider`,
a per-frame *"this region was meant for me"* signal, or named
target-state fields — would touch the manifest, frame, and
activation schemas, and so falls under the "stop and ask" rules in
[`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md) §12 and
[`CLAUDE.md`](../../../../../CLAUDE.md).
