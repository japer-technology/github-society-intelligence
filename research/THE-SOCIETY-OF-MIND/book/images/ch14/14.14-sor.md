# Figure 14-14 — Implementation analysis (SOR / FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION)

**Source figure:** [14-14.md](14-14.md) — *The Move agency with inhibitory box-frame.*
**Analysis target:** [`FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/)
(plan-of-record for `.forgejo-society/` and
`.forgejo/workflows/forgejo-society.yaml`).

---

## The ideas Figure 14-14 actually carries

1. **A parent agency composed of four direction-subagents.** *Move*
   contains Move-Left, Move-Right, Move-Up, Move-Down as named
   members.
2. **Obstacle-to-subagent wiring by side.** Each side of the box
   maps to exactly one subagent it inhibits.
3. **Inhibition as the operative primitive.** *---o* is the
   relation; activation falls out of *absence of inhibition*.
4. **Trapped = all subagents inhibited.** The state of the parent
   is the conjunction of its members' suppression.
5. **Missing-obstacle ⇒ free direction.** Remove one side and the
   matching subagent fires automatically.
6. **Negative thinking as a strategy.** Imagine the full box, then
   look for the missing inhibitor; *that* is the way out.

---

## What the implementation already absorbs

### 1 — Inhibition is a first-class relation

[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md)
puts `inhibits:` on the manifest schema, and
[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md)
puts `excite:` and `inhibit:` on polynemes. Inhibition is not
modelled as an exception; it is a normal kind of edge in the
activation graph.

### 2 — Per-effect blockers

Censors and suppressors
([`07-policies-and-safety.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/07-policies-and-safety.md))
each disable one kind of action. They are the *---o* arrows of
the figure, attached to the right candidate-output direction.

### 3 — A *blocked* settlement when everything is suppressed

The fail-closed rule in
[`07-policies-and-safety.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/07-policies-and-safety.md)
says: if any safety layer blocks the run, write a blocked
settlement rather than silently doing nothing. The all-sides-
inhibited terminal state of the figure has an operational form.

## What the implementation does not yet take into account

### A — No composed_of relation between parent and direction-children

The figure's *Move* is a parent that *contains* four named
subagents. The plan has families (folders) and members, but the
manifest schema has no `composed_of: [<agency-id>, ...]` field by
which Move would *declare* its children. This is the same gap
recorded against Figure 1-2; here it appears in the form of
"per-direction children of a single parent."

### B — Inhibition-by-conjunction is not detected

The figure's interesting state is *all four arrows present at
once*. The plan emits per-agency inhibitions and per-censor blocks
but does not aggregate them into "every member of family *F* has
been inhibited." No signal is named `agency.<family>.all-inhibited`.

### C — Missing-obstacle ⇒ free-direction is not exploited

Negative thinking, in Minsky's sense, is *"compare the current
inhibition pattern against the expected full set."* The plan does
not store *expected inhibition patterns* anywhere. K-lines remember
which agencies fired together; they do not remember which agencies
were inhibited together, or which were *expected to be inhibited
but were not.*

### D — Direction-subagents have no canonical naming convention

The figure assumes Move-Left, Move-Right, Move-Up, Move-Down are
addressable. The plan's identifier scheme
(`{scope}.{kind}.{name}`, per
[`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md)) does not reserve a
suffix for *direction* or *option* within an agency family. Any
attempt at this pattern would have to invent that convention.

### E — Suppression is not a "wake the alternative" signal

In the figure, if the top side is missing, Move-Up *fires
automatically*. In the plan, suppression of one candidate does
not excite another. Removing a censor for one direction yields
"this direction is no longer blocked," not "this direction is now
suggested." The asymmetry is real and intentional today; Figure
14-14 says it could be otherwise.

## Implication for the plan (no changes proposed here)

Figure 14-14 is the operational diagram of *trapped*: it says
trapped is not a feeling but a wiring pattern, and it says the
way out is to look at the wiring rather than at the goal. The
plan has the wires (inhibition, censors, suppressors) but not the
diagrams that would let it reason about the *shape* of its own
inhibition. The gaps above are recorded as analysis, not as a
change request. Any move to close them — for example, a
`composed_of:` field, an *all-inhibited* signal per family, or an
`expected_inhibition_pattern` on frames — would touch the
manifest, signal, frame, and censor schemas, and so falls under
the "stop and ask" rules in
[`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md) §12 and
[`CLAUDE.md`](../../../../../CLAUDE.md).
