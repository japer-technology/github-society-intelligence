## The ideas Figure 14-5 actually carries

1. **A constraint the viewer adds to the problem.** The square is
   not in the picture; it is supplied by the eye.
2. **Default assumption as silent input.** The puzzle is solvable
   only when one notices that the silent input is there.
3. **Problem statement is part of the problem.** How the puzzle is
   presented determines which solutions feel admissible.
4. **The figure deliberately invites the trap.** Square arrangement
   primes the wrong default; the puzzle's difficulty is half
   typographic.
5. **The solution is locked behind a recognition, not a search.**
   The hard step is noticing that a constraint was assumed; the
   geometry afterwards is easy.
6. **Two kinds of constraint exist: stated and inferred.** The
   instruction text states some; the picture's shape suggests
   others; only the stated ones are binding.

## What the implementation already absorbs

### 1 — Scope as a critic concern

[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md)
makes *scope* one of the named `objection.kind` values in
[`09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md).
Critics can flag when a candidate action is treating the problem as
narrower or wider than the stimulus warrants.

### 2 — Frame defaults are visible and overridable

[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md)
makes a frame's `default_actions`, `default_critics`, and
`default_censors` explicit fields in the YAML. The plan does not
hide its defaults — they live as data, where they can at least be
read and audited.

### 3 — Ambiguity detection

`agency.perception.ambiguity-detector`
([`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md))
flags missing slots in the chosen frame. That is one half of "noticing
what the problem statement leaves unsaid."

## What the implementation does not yet take into account

### A — No critic for *inferred* constraints

The plan flags missing slots (ambiguity-detector) and out-of-scope
candidates (scope objections). Neither catches the harder
nine-dot-style failure: a constraint that *was never stated* but
that every agency is silently honouring. There is no
`critic.implicit-constraint` whose job is to ask "what are we
treating as fixed that the user never fixed?"

### B — Frame defaults are not flagged as such at use-time

A frame's defaults are visible in the YAML, but once they are
applied to a stimulus, the resulting workspace does not annotate
which fields came from defaults vs from the stimulus. A downstream
critic cannot easily distinguish "the user said this" from "the
frame assumed this." The square is invisible in the same way.

### C — Problem-statement framing is opaque

The `normalize` step
([`03-runtime-pipeline.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/03-runtime-pipeline.md))
produces `stimulus.json` from the raw event. The transformations it
applies (label inference, kind detection, urgency estimation) are
themselves silent inputs to every later phase. The plan does not
require `stimulus.json` to record *which fields are observed* vs
*which are inferred*.

### D — No "puzzle restatement" move

The figure's solution begins with restating the problem ("the square
is not part of the rules"). The plan has no agency that proposes
*alternative framings of the same stimulus* before deliberation
begins. Frame selection picks one frame; it does not generate two
and compare.

## Implication for the plan (no changes proposed here)

Figure 14-5 names a failure mode the plan is not yet armed against:
agencies that obey a constraint nobody wrote down. The gaps above
are recorded as analysis, not as a change request. Any move to
close them — for example, a `critic.implicit-constraint`, a
`provenance:` field on stimulus and workspace fields, or a frame
selection that produces *candidates* rather than a single choice —
would touch the manifest, frame, and stimulus schemas, and so falls
under the "stop and ask" rules in
[`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md) §12 and
[`CLAUDE.md`](../../../../../CLAUDE.md).
