## The ideas Figure 14-3 actually carries

1. **A two-poled distinction as a reusable shape.** Each dumbbell is
   a goal-side paired with a means-side, drawn the same way every
   time.
2. **Five realms, one schema.** FUNCTION/STRUCTURE,
   ENDS/MEANS, CONCLUSION/PREMISE, EFFECT/CAUSE, BODY/SUPPORT all
   share the same top/bottom geometry.
3. **The pairing is the unit.** A pole on its own ŌĆö *ends* without
   *means*, *effect* without *cause* ŌĆö is incomplete.
4. **Asymmetry between the poles.** The top pole is the *what*; the
   bottom pole is the *how*. They are not interchangeable.
5. **Recognising the recurrence enables analogy.** Once the shape is
   visible, a move that works in one realm is a candidate move in
   the others.
6. **Distinctions are themselves objects.** Each dumbbell is a thing
   with a name; one can talk *about* it, not only *with* it.

## What the implementation already absorbs

### 1 ŌĆö Slot pairs in frames

[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md)
defines frame `slots` with `required` and `filled_by`. The
`code-change` frame in particular pairs `user_goal` (the *ends*) with
`proposed_patch` (the *means*) and `risks` (the *effect*) with
`relevant_files` (the *cause-bearing region*). The dumbbell shape is
implicit in those slot lists.

### 2 ŌĆö Cause/effect language in signals and objections

[`09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md)
gives `objections` a `kind` enum and `claims` an `evidence` array.
*Claim* and *evidence* are themselves a dumbbell ŌĆö a CONCLUSION
paired with a PREMISE ŌĆö even though the schema does not name them
that way.

### 3 ŌĆö Analogies as a memory tree

`memory/analogies/`
([`11-mapping-sor-to-implementation.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/11-mapping-sor-to-implementation.md))
is where transported shapes would live. The plan acknowledges that
analogy is durable cognition rather than ad-hoc rhetoric.

## What the implementation does not yet take into account

### A ŌĆö No `distinction` / `dyad` kind

The figure's whole subject is the dyad ŌĆö a top pole bound to a
bottom pole. None of the manifest `kind:` values
(`worker | critic | censor | suppressor | narrator | assembler | meta`)
represents a *distinction*. A dumbbell cannot be created, stored, or
referenced as a first-class object.

### B ŌĆö Slot pairing is unstructured

Frame slots are a flat list. There is no field that says "these two
slots are a dyad of kind *ends/means*." The pairing in `code-change`
(`user_goal` Ōåö `proposed_patch`) is convention only; nothing in
[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md)
makes the pairing machine-readable.

### C ŌĆö Cross-realm reuse is not a runtime move

The figure's payoff is that recognising the shape in one realm
suggests it in another. No agency in
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md)
is tasked with "if dyad *D* works in domain *X*, propose *D* in
domain *Y*." Analogy transport happens only when a human or a
K-line carries it.

### D ŌĆö Asymmetry of poles is implicit

The plan has no field that records "this pole is the *what*, that
pole is the *how*." Without that, a future *distinction* object
could not refuse a malformed dyad (effect-without-cause,
conclusion-without-premise).

## Implication for the plan (no changes proposed here)

Figure 14-3 makes *the distinction itself* a first-class object. The
plan reuses dyad-shaped pairs (claim/evidence, goal/patch,
cause/effect language in objections) but does not give them a name,
a schema, or a transport mechanism. The gaps above are recorded as
analysis, not as a change request. Any move to close them ŌĆö for
example, a `distinction.schema.json`, a `pole_role` annotation on
slots, or a `critic.dyad-completeness` ŌĆö would touch the manifest
and frame schemas and the identity scopes, and so falls under the
"stop and ask" rules in
[`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md) ┬¦12 and
[`CLAUDE.md`](../../../../../CLAUDE.md).
