# Figure 14-2 — Implementation analysis (SOR / FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION)

**Source figure:** [14-2.md](14-2.md) — *Removing a leg from the table.*
**Analysis target:** [`FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/)
(plan-of-record for `.forgejo-society/` and
`.forgejo/workflows/forgejo-society.yaml`).

This file inventories the ideas carried by Figure 14-2 and checks each
one against the implementation plan.

---

## The ideas Figure 14-2 actually carries

1. **Structure ↔ function as paired columns.** Each structural
   change on the left has a definite functional consequence on the
   right.
2. **Localised causality.** *Remove left leg* implicates *left side
   falls* — the consequence respects the position of the change.
3. **Failure as a route to understanding.** Knowing how the table
   fails is part of knowing how it works.
4. **Counterfactual reasoning made explicit.** The figure entertains
   a deletion (remove a leg) without performing it; the consequence
   is read off, not observed.
5. **Symmetry that exposes structure.** Two symmetric inputs produce
   two symmetric outputs; the symmetry is part of the diagnostic.
6. **A two-column table as the schema for that pairing.** The
   geometry of the figure is itself the data structure being
   proposed.

## What the implementation already absorbs

### 1 — Reverse-path reasoning as a first-class agency

[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md)
ships `agency.code.revert-path-finder` and `agency.code.blast-radius-fear`.
The first names the way back; the second names what falls when this
change goes in. Both are operational forms of "remove this part, see
what fails."

### 2 — Reversion as a required slot

[`07-policies-and-safety.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/07-policies-and-safety.md)
makes `revert_path` a required slot for `code-change` frames
(via [`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md)).
Settlement cannot complete without that paired "if this fails, here
is the undo" being written down. The figure's "remove leg → side
falls" pair has a near-direct counterpart in "apply patch → here is
the revert."

### 3 — Counterfactual without performance

The candidate-future branch model in
[`03-runtime-pipeline.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/03-runtime-pipeline.md)
is the runtime's mechanism for entertaining a change without
adopting it. The branch is the imagined deletion; merge is the
adoption.

## What the implementation does not yet take into account

### A — No structured "structural change → functional effect" record

The figure's two-column table is a small typed object: *delta on the
left, predicted consequence on the right*. The handoff and signal
schemas in
[`09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md)
have `claims`, `objections`, `candidate_actions` — but no
`predicted_effects` or `failure_predictions` field on a candidate
action. Blast-radius-fear's output is prose, not a structured
left-column/right-column pairing.

### B — No deliberate fault-injection critic

Figure 14-2 *removes a part* to learn. The first-ship critic
catalogue in
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md)
includes evidence, scope, and contradiction critics; none of them
*propose deletions and read off the consequences*. There is no
`critic.fault-injector` that, given a candidate action, removes one
of its components and asks what fails.

### C — Symmetry is not exploited

The figure relies on the symmetry between left-leg and right-leg
removal to make its point. The plan has no notion of paired
counterfactuals — `act.ts` does not run a candidate with `X` and
again without `X` to compare outcomes. Reasoning is per-candidate,
not per-pair.

### D — Failure knowledge is not a named memory subtree

The `memory/` layout in
[`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md)
includes `episodic/`, `semantic/`, `procedural/`, `decisions/`, and
K-lines — but only an implicit *failure* notion (`failure_conditions`
on frames). There is no `memory/failures/` archive where the
"removing X causes Y to fall" records would live, indexed for reuse.

## Implication for the plan (no changes proposed here)

Figure 14-2 turns *understanding by failure* into a small structured
table. The plan absorbs the idea of reversion and blast-radius, but
treats them as prose claims rather than as typed predictions. The
gaps above are recorded as analysis, not as a change request. Any
move to close them — for example, a `predicted_effects` field on
candidate actions, a `critic.fault-injector`, or a `memory/failures/`
tree — would touch the manifest, handoff, and memory schemas, and so
falls under the "stop and ask" rules in
[`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md) §12 and
[`CLAUDE.md`](../../../../../CLAUDE.md).
