# Figure 14-9 — Description fed by sensation and expectation

![figure](14-9.png)

**File:** `ch14/14-9.png`
**Appears in:** [../../som-14.3.md](../../som-14.3.md) — *Seeing squares*

## What the image shows

Three labels arranged on a horizontal line: **Sensation** on the
left and **Expectation** on the right, both pointing inward with
arrows to a central box labelled **Description**.

## What it illustrates

The correction to [14-8.md](14-8.md). What we see is not a pure
relay of sensory data but a description assembled jointly from two
streams: the evidence arriving from the eye, and the expectations
arriving from memory. The figure is Minsky's compact statement
that perception is bidirectional, and it sets up the chapter's
later discussion of how recognition can succeed from partial
evidence.
