# Figure 1-3 — Implementation analysis (SOR / FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION)

**Source figure:** [1-3.md](1-3.md) — *Unpacking the ADD agent.*
**Analysis target:** [`FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/)
(plan-of-record for `.forgejo-society/` and
`.forgejo/workflows/forgejo-society.yaml`).

This file inventories the ideas carried by Figure 1-3 and checks each
one against the implementation plan. Where Figure 1-2 introduced a
single parent with three children, Figure 1-3 takes one of those
children (ADD) and unfolds it again — and then again — into FIND,
GET, PUT and their sub-workers SEE, GRASP, MOVE, RELEASE. The ideas
the figure carries are therefore mostly about *depth*, not about
*membership*.

---

## The ideas Figure 1-3 actually carries

Pulled from the figure caption and Minsky's surrounding text in §1.4
*The world of blocks*:

1. **Decomposition is not a one-step move.** What looks like a leaf
   at level *n* turns out to be an agency at level *n+1*. The tree
   does not bottom out at the first opening.
2. **A worker named ADD has *its own* BEGIN/ADD/END shape.** FIND
   opens the step (locate a block), GET/PUT carry the body (acquire
   and place), and the loop terminates implicitly when no block is
   available to FIND.
3. **Sub-agencies have shared vocabulary, not shared identity.** SEE
   appears under FIND and (in spirit) under PUT; the same *kind* of
   primitive is reused without collapsing the parents into one.
4. **Sensorimotor pairing is structural.** FIND pairs SEE with GRASP;
   PUT pairs MOVE with RELEASE. Perception and action are bound
   together at the smallest grain Minsky is willing to draw.
5. **The tree is uneven on purpose.** FIND, GET, PUT do not all
   decompose into the same number of children. The figure refuses to
   make the hierarchy regular.
6. **Recursion is the explanatory engine.** The figure adds no new
   primitive over Figure 1-2; it only re-applies the same operation
   (an agent is really an agency) one more level down.
7. **Reuse of primitives across parents implies a library.** SEE,
   GRASP, MOVE, RELEASE behave like a shared inventory of operations
   that any higher agency can compose. They are not owned by ADD.
8. **Depth carries cost.** Each level of unpacking is a level of
   coordination — activation, sequencing, termination — that some
   higher unit has to maintain. The figure makes that cost visible
   by drawing it.

These eight items are what the rest of this file tests against the
implementation plan.

## What the implementation already absorbs

### 1 — Specialists at the leaves

The first-ship catalogues in
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md)
favour narrow workers (`agency.code.patch-imaginer`,
`agency.memory.kline-retriever`, `agency.perception.ambiguity-detector`)
over broad ones. At the leaf grain, the plan agrees with Figure 1-3:
small, named, single-purpose units.

### 2 — Perception and action are separated by family

The figure binds SEE to GRASP at one node; the plan keeps the
analogous capabilities in separate families — `agencies/perception/`
and `agencies/code/` — and lets them meet in the workflow phases
`perceive → activate → deliberate` documented in
[`02-workflow-design.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/02-workflow-design.md)
and
[`03-runtime-pipeline.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/03-runtime-pipeline.md).
The pairing is honoured at the pipeline scale rather than inside a
single agency, but the *split* between sensing and acting is real.

### 3 — A shared library of primitives

Tool groups in `config/tools.yml` referenced from
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md)
(`read`, `grep`, and the rest) are the closest plan analogue to
"SEE / GRASP / MOVE / RELEASE as shared verbs." Many agencies declare
the same tool group; the tool group is owned by no one agency. This
matches idea 7 at the tool layer, even though it is not modelled as
an agency-of-agencies layer.

### 4 — Activation as the only coupling

Across all depths, the plan uses signals, frames, polynemes, and
inhibition (see
[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md))
as the way one unit causes another to run. That is the right
machinery for Figure 1-3's recursive "turn on" relationship, even if
the plan currently uses it across a flat membership rather than
across a tree.

### 5 — Stable ids that survive splitting

The id convention `agency.<family>.<name>` documented in
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md)
and the identity protocol referenced from
[`11-mapping-sor-to-implementation.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/11-mapping-sor-to-implementation.md)
means that if a leaf such as `agency.code.patch-imaginer` later
splits into narrower workers, the parent id can remain and the
children can be added with longer names. The naming discipline
permits the kind of deepening Figure 1-3 illustrates, even if the
schema does not yet make it ergonomic.

## What the implementation does not yet take into account

These are the parts of Figure 1-3 that the plan currently leaves
implicit, partial, or simply absent. They are recorded as analysis,
not as a change request.

### A — No multi-level decomposition

The single most direct reading of Figure 1-3 — *ADD → {FIND, GET,
PUT} → {SEE, GRASP, MOVE, RELEASE}* — needs at least two levels of
nesting. The plan supports one level (family → member) and no more.
There is no manifest field such as

```yaml
composed_of:
  - <child-agency-id>
  - <child-agency-id>
```

and no runtime loader in
[`03-runtime-pipeline.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/03-runtime-pipeline.md)
that would descend through such a tree. The recursion that Figure
1-3 *is* cannot be represented in any current manifest.

### B — No "parent agency" identity for ADD-shaped units

Even at one level, `code` is a folder and an `agency:` enum value,
not an addressable manifest (the same gap recorded against Figure
1-2). At two levels, the absence multiplies: there is no
`agency.code.add-block` manifest that could *own* `find-block`,
`get-block`, `put-block` as children, and no way to "turn on the
ADD-block agency" without naming each child individually.

### C — Shared primitives are not modelled as agencies

The figure's SEE and GRASP behave like reusable verbs that several
parents can invoke. The plan's reusable layer is the tool group, not
an agency. There is no shared sub-agency such as
`agency.code.read-file` that `patch-imaginer`, `diff-skeptic`, and
`revert-path-finder` would each compose. Reuse happens through tool
groups defined in `config/tools.yml` and through the polynemes
described in
[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md),
which is a different shape of reuse.

### D — Perception and action paired at the smallest grain

Figure 1-3 binds SEE to GRASP inside FIND — a single node holds both
the look and the take. The plan keeps perception and code in
separate families and couples them only through the
`perceive → activate → deliberate → act` phase chain
([`03-runtime-pipeline.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/03-runtime-pipeline.md)).
There is no agency-local "see-and-grasp" pair. Whether that pairing
is worth introducing is a design question; what is true today is
that the plan does not represent it.

### E — Uneven decomposition is not first-class

Figure 1-3 is deliberately irregular: FIND, GET, PUT do not all
expand to the same shape. The plan's families are uniform sets of
members with the same manifest schema. Anything resembling "this
agency unpacks into two workers, that one unpacks into three, and
the third does not unpack at all" cannot be declared in a manifest;
it would have to be smuggled in by writing different numbers of
sibling files.

### F — Depth is not budgeted

Each new level of decomposition in the figure adds a level of
coordination. The plan's budgets (`budget.max_tool_calls`,
`budget.max_wall_clock_s`) in
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md)
limit a unit's local work but say nothing about the cost of *being a
parent of N children that are themselves parents of M children*.
There is no policy in
[`07-policies-and-safety.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/07-policies-and-safety.md)
that limits recursion depth, because there is no recursion to limit.

### G — Termination at deeper levels

Figure 1-2 made END an explicit member. Figure 1-3 implicitly
inherits the same need at each level: FIND must decide "no more
blocks", PUT must decide "released". The plan handles termination
once, at the run level, in `settle` and in the settlement schema
referenced from
[`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md)
and
[`09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md).
Agency-local termination — and therefore *nested* agency-local
termination — is not modelled.

### H — Memory of a successful decomposition

The figure assumes that *the way ADD unpacks* is itself reusable
knowledge: next time the system needs to add a block, the same
decomposition tree is available. The plan stores reusable cognitive
patterns as K-lines and frames
([`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md),
[`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md)),
but a K-line currently records a *configuration of active units*, not
a *parent-children-grandchildren shape*. There is no "this is the
ADD-block decomposition; restore it" kind of memory.

## Implication for the plan (no changes proposed here)

Figure 1-3 is mostly a re-application of Figure 1-2's operation. Its
distinct contribution is the assertion that the operation is
*indefinitely repeatable* — that any leaf, looked at closely, becomes
an agency. The implementation plan honours the *leaf grain*
(specialists, named, narrow) and the *outer pipeline grain*
(sequenced phases with termination), but does not yet honour the
*middle grain* where a worker is itself a sequenced, terminating,
multi-child unit.

Closing any of the gaps above — a `composed_of` field, a recursive
agency loader, a depth budget, an agency-local termination role, a
K-line shape that records a decomposition tree — would touch the
manifest schema in
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md),
the activation model in
[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md),
the memory schemas in
[`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md)
and
[`09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md),
and the identity scopes in
`THE-SOCIETY-OF-REPO/02-protocols/01-identity.md`, and so falls under
the "stop and ask" rules in
[`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md) §12 and
[`CLAUDE.md`](../../../../../CLAUDE.md).
