# Figure 1-4 — Implementation analysis (SOR / FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION)

**Source figure:** [1-4.md](1-4.md) — *Agent versus agency.*
**Analysis target:** [`FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/)
(plan-of-record for `.forgejo-society/` and
`.forgejo/workflows/forgejo-society.yaml`).

This file inventories the ideas carried by Figure 1-4 and checks each
one against the implementation plan. Figure 1-4 is not a diagram of a
particular agency; it is a diagram of a *perspective*. The same
hexagon is an agent from outside and an agency from inside. The ideas
to test, therefore, are mostly about vocabulary, addressability, and
the runtime's capacity to hold both views at once.

---

## The ideas Figure 1-4 actually carries

Pulled from the figure caption and Minsky's surrounding text in §1.4
*The world of blocks* and §1.6 *Agents and Agencies*:

1. **"Agent" and "agency" are perspectives on one object.** The
   distinction is not a type difference; it is a viewpoint
   difference.
2. **From outside, an agency presents as a single addressable unit.**
   Whatever happens inside is irrelevant to the caller — turning it
   on causes its outputs to appear.
3. **From inside, the same unit is a population.** The behaviour the
   outside sees is produced by many smaller workers cooperating.
4. **Activation is the outer interface.** The only thing the outside
   layer does is turn the unit on; the inside layer is responsible
   for everything else.
5. **The fan-out is unbounded in principle.** Minsky's hexagon
   pyramid trails into "etc." — there is no special bottom level and
   no special top level; the structure is recursive on both sides.
6. **The same identity persists across views.** The hexagon at the
   apex is the *same* hexagon whether named as an agent or as an
   agency. There is one id, two readings.
7. **Cognitive unity is a perspective effect.** What looks like a
   single coherent mental act is, on inspection, a coordination
   among smaller acts. Coherence is produced, not given.
8. **Choosing the right grain is a runtime concern.** A caller that
   needs only the outcome should not have to descend into the
   society; a critic that needs to assign blame must be able to
   descend.

These eight items are what the rest of this file tests against the
implementation plan.

## What the implementation already absorbs

### 1 — A single vocabulary word covers both views

[`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md) and the canonical vocabulary
referenced from
[`11-mapping-sor-to-implementation.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/11-mapping-sor-to-implementation.md)
use *agency* for the unit and use it consistently — the same word
applies to the BUILDER-shaped thing seen from outside and to the
BEGIN/ADD/END-shaped thing seen from inside. The naming discipline
is at least consistent with idea 1, even though it does not provide
a separate outside-view word.

### 2 — Stable ids that do not vary with viewpoint

The id convention `agency.<family>.<name>` documented in
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md)
gives each unit one persistent name. Whatever the runtime does with
that unit — activate it, audit it, replace it — the id is the same.
This matches idea 6 at the level of naming.

### 3 — Activation as the dominant outer verb

[`02-workflow-design.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/02-workflow-design.md)
and
[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md)
make signals, frames, and polynemes the way one unit causes another
to run. The outside view of any agency is "what signals turn it on
and what outputs come back." That is exactly the outside view
Figure 1-4 names.

### 4 — Observability from outside without descent

The `report` step in
[`03-runtime-pipeline.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/03-runtime-pipeline.md),
together with the settlement and signal schemas in
[`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md)
and
[`09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md),
gives the rest of the system a view of *what an agency did* without
requiring it to look inside that agency. That is the outside-view
contract working as intended.

### 5 — Critics and censors as inside-view inspectors

Critics and censors in
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md)
and the safety pipeline in
[`07-policies-and-safety.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/07-policies-and-safety.md)
are the parts of the runtime that *look in* — they inspect candidate
actions, objections, and signals rather than only consuming final
outputs. The plan has at least the beginnings of the inside view
that idea 8 demands.

### 6 — Cognitive unity as something the workflow produces

The phase chain `guard → normalize → guardrail → perceive → activate
→ deliberate → criticize → censor → settle → act → remember → report`
in
[`03-runtime-pipeline.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/03-runtime-pipeline.md)
explicitly stages the coordination among many smaller units into
something the caller experiences as a single response. The unity of
a Forgejo Society run is not assumed — it is assembled by `settle`
and emitted by the narrator. That is idea 7 made operational at the
run scale.

## What the implementation does not yet take into account

These are the parts of Figure 1-4 that the plan currently leaves
implicit, partial, or simply absent.

### A — No separate word for the outside view

The figure depends on being able to say "this is an *agent* (outside)
that is also an *agency* (inside)." The canonical vocabulary uses
*agency* for both. There is no term — and therefore no manifest
field, no schema annotation, no audit category — for "the agency as
seen by callers, ignoring its interior." Idea 1, the figure's
central distinction, is consistent in naming but missing in
mechanism.

### B — Inside and outside are not separately addressable

A caller cannot say "treat `agency.code` as a single unit, opaque,
and give me its result" because `agency.code` is a folder, not an
addressable unit (the same gap recorded against Figures 1-2 and
1-3). The runtime can address members, but it cannot address the
whole-as-one. Idea 2 — the outside view as a first-class handle —
is therefore not implementable today.

### C — The interior is not addressable as a population either

Symmetrically, a critic cannot say "open `agency.code` and tell me
which member produced this objection" because there is no
`agency.code` to open. Members are flat siblings under a folder;
there is no parent through which to descend. Idea 3 — the inside
view as a population belonging to a named whole — is also not
implementable today.

### D — Activation cannot be addressed at the outer grain

[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md)
defines activation in terms of signals, frames, paths, and phrases.
None of those address "the whole agency as a single thing." There
is no `activates_on.parent_agency` or `excite_family:` field. The
outer verb Figure 1-4 highlights — *turn the agency on* — has no
direct schema realisation; activation always reaches members
directly.

### E — Authority and budget are member-scoped

`authority` in the manifest schema is one of the six legal levels
(read, draft, propose, act, govern, human) and is set on each
member. Budgets are member-scoped too
([`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md)).
There is no agency-level authority ("the code agency may at most
propose") or agency-level budget ("the code agency as a whole has
this many tool calls per run"). The outside view, if it existed,
would naturally carry policy of its own; today it carries none.

### F — No depth-agnostic addressing

The figure's recursion ("etc.") implies that any node, at any depth,
should be referable by id alone. The plan's ids are flat
(`agency.<family>.<name>`) with two segments after the scope. There
is no convention for `agency.code.add-block.find-block.see` and no
loader that would resolve such a name. Idea 5 — recursion on both
sides — has no naming convention behind it.

### G — Grain selection is not a runtime concern

Idea 8 says the runtime should decide *how deep to look*. The plan
does not have this choice: critics see what members emit, the
narrator sees what settlement collects, and there is no policy in
[`07-policies-and-safety.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/07-policies-and-safety.md)
or
[`03-runtime-pipeline.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/03-runtime-pipeline.md)
that selects a depth at which to read or report. Depth is fixed at
"one" because the schema offers no other.

### H — Memory cannot remember "the BUILDER, abstracted"

K-lines and frames in
[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md)
and the memory shapes in
[`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md)
record which members were active. They do not record "the agency
that was operating, treated as a unit." A K-line that wanted to say
"restore the configuration in which `agency.code` was the active
unit, however that was internally composed at the time" cannot be
expressed.

## Implication for the plan (no changes proposed here)

Figure 1-4 is the figure that names the rule the other two figures
demonstrate: the agent/agency distinction is a difference of
viewpoint, not of kind. The implementation plan honours the
*outcome* of holding both views — coherent responses produced by a
society of narrow workers — at the run scale, but it does not yet
honour the *mechanism* — a single addressable unit that can be
treated as one from outside and as many from inside — at the agency
scale.

Closing any of the gaps above — a separate outside-view term, a
parent manifest that *is* the family, parent-level activation,
agency-level authority and budget, deeper ids, a depth selection
policy, K-lines that remember the parent grain — would touch the
manifest schema in
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md),
the activation and memory protocols in
[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md),
the state and memory shapes in
[`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md),
the handoff and signal schemas in
[`09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md),
the safety policies in
[`07-policies-and-safety.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/07-policies-and-safety.md),
and the identity scopes in
`THE-SOCIETY-OF-REPO/02-protocols/01-identity.md`, and so falls under
the "stop and ask" rules in
[`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md) §12 and
[`CLAUDE.md`](../../../../../CLAUDE.md).
