# Figure 1-2 — Implementation analysis (SOR / FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION)

**Source figure:** [1-2.md](1-2.md) — *The BUILDER agent and its three
sub-agents.*
**Analysis target:** [`FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/)
(plan-of-record for `.forgejo-society/` and
`.forgejo/workflows/forgejo-society.yaml`).

This file inventories the ideas carried by Figure 1-2 and checks each
one against the implementation plan. The aim is to mark, plainly, what
the plan already absorbs and what it does not yet name.

---

## The ideas Figure 1-2 actually carries

Pulled from the figure caption and Minsky's surrounding text in §1.4
*The world of blocks*:

1. **An agent that is really an agency.** A unit that looks atomic
   from outside but, on inspection, is a small society.
2. **Hierarchical decomposition as a primitive.** One stated capability
   ("build a tower") expands into a fixed set of narrower workers
   (BEGIN, ADD, END).
3. **"Turning on" as the only thing the parent does.** BUILDER has no
   competence of its own; activating BUILDER activates its children.
4. **Sequenced sub-agents.** BEGIN → ADD → END is an order, not a set.
   The composition itself carries control flow.
5. **A terminator sub-agent.** END is not a worker that *does* part of
   the tower — it *decides whether the loop continues*. The stop
   criterion is itself a member of the agency.
6. **A loop body distinct from initialisation.** ADD is the repeated
   step; BEGIN runs once. The figure presupposes that "do once" and
   "do until" are different roles inside one agency.
7. **Cheaper, narrower specialists.** Each child knows less than
   BUILDER would have to know if it tried to act directly.
8. **Names as scaffolding for later differentiation.** Once BEGIN,
   ADD, END have names, any of them can later be replaced or split
   without renaming BUILDER. The hierarchy is the substrate of
   evolution.

These eight items are what the rest of this file tests against the
implementation plan.

## What the implementation already absorbs

### 1 — "Agency" as a first-class word

[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md)
defines `agency` as a manifest kind and groups manifests by *family*
(perception, memory, code, safety, identity, integration, assembly,
meta-admin). Each family directory under `.forgejo-society/agencies/`
is, in Minsky's sense, a *coarse* agency built from finer ones.
Figure 1-2's BUILDER → children pattern is therefore represented at
exactly **one** level of depth: family → member agency.

### 2 — Narrow specialists over generalists

The first-ship catalogues are deliberately small and pointed:
`patch-imaginer`, `diff-skeptic`, `revert-path-finder`,
`blast-radius-fear`, `kline-retriever`, `conscious-presenter`. This
matches Minsky's "cheaper, narrower workers" criterion. The plan does
not ship a single "code agent"; it ships a handful of code
sub-specialists.

### 3 — Activation as the dominant control mechanism

[`02-workflow-design.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/02-workflow-design.md)
and [`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md)
make activation, inhibition, and polynemes the way one unit wakes
another. This is the right machinery for "turning on" — Figure 1-2's
core verb — even though the figure itself uses it only between a
parent and its three children.

### 4 — A sequence at the pipeline scale

The workflow's phase chain `guard → normalize → guardrail → perceive →
activate → deliberate → criticize → censor → settle → act → remember →
report` is itself a BEGIN/ADD/END-shaped composition. `normalize` is
the once-only opener, the `deliberate / criticize / censor` middle is
the repeated body of cognition, and `settle / act / remember` are the
closers that decide the run is complete. The figure's *shape* is
honoured at the workflow level.

### 5 — Termination conditions as named roles

`settle` is owned by `lifecycle/settle.ts` and the settlement schema
([`03-runtime-pipeline.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/03-runtime-pipeline.md)),
which decides whether the workspace is ready to act on. That is the
plan's END — an explicit, named "are we done?" role rather than an
implicit loop condition.

### 6 — Names that support later differentiation

[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md)
gives every unit a stable id (`agency.code.patch-imaginer`,
`agency.assembly.summary-tier-1`) and routes splits through the
`self-modification` frame and `agency.meta-admin.differentiation-broker`.
This is the operational form of Minsky's claim that *naming the
children enables later evolution of the parent*.

## What the implementation does not yet take into account

These are the parts of Figure 1-2 that the plan currently leaves
implicit, partial, or simply absent. None of them are urgent; all of
them are real.

### A — A coarse agency is not itself a manifest

In the figure, BUILDER is a node in the diagram — addressable by name,
with its own identity. In the plan, "code family" is a *folder*
(`agencies/code/`) and a `agency:` enum value on each member manifest.
There is no `agencies/code/code.agency.md` that *is* the family, the
way `agencies/code/patch-imaginer.md` *is* `patch-imaginer`. As a
result:

- nothing in the runtime can "turn on the code agency" as a unit;
- the family has no manifest body, no authority line, no budget, no
  `activates_on`;
- `inhibits:` and `excite:` can only target members, never families.

Figure 1-2's central distinction — *BUILDER is a thing you can point
at* — is therefore present in the prose but missing from the schema.

### B — No recursive composition

`THE-SOCIETY-OF-REPO/02-protocols/13-hierarchy-and-summaries.md` maps
to `agencies/assembly/*` and to *summary tiers*, which is hierarchy
applied to **outputs** (signals collapsed into briefs). It is not
hierarchy applied to **agents themselves**. The plan supports one
level of nesting (family → member). The figure's "BUILDER → BEGIN /
ADD / END, and ADD itself decomposes further" idiom is not
representable today. A manifest cannot declare:

```yaml
composed_of:
  - <child-agency-id>
  - <child-agency-id>
  - <child-agency-id>
```

There is no `composed_of`, no nested-agency schema, and no runtime
loader that descends through such a tree.

### C — Sequence as a property of an agency

Figure 1-2's BEGIN → ADD → END is *ordered*. The plan's families are
*sets*: members of `agencies/perception/` all activate when their
signals match, in no declared order. Ordering is supplied only by the
outer workflow phases, never by an agency's own structure. There is
no field that says "within this agency, run X before Y, then loop on
Z." Anything that needs sequenced internal sub-steps today has to be
written as a *pipeline*, not as an *agency*.

### D — A loop-with-terminator inside a single agency

END is a *member of BUILDER* whose only job is to decide whether to
keep going. The closest analogue in the plan is `critic.scope` or the
`settle` phase, but both of those operate at the run level, not inside
one agency. The plan has no notion of:

- "this agency contains a worker step and a terminator step";
- "the terminator step is itself a sub-agent that can be replaced";
- "the loop count is bounded by an agency-local budget, not by the
  global `budget.max_tool_calls`."

`budget.max_tool_calls` and `budget.max_wall_clock_s` from
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md)
limit a unit; they do not give it an internal `while not END` loop
with a named END.

### E — "Does nothing on its own" is not enforceable

Minsky's BUILDER has *no* primitive competence — it is pure
activation. In the plan, every agency manifest has a prose body that
*becomes the agent prompt*. A unit with no body of its own (a pure
router that only excites children) is not a first-class shape; it
would have to masquerade as a normal agency with an empty prompt. The
schema does not distinguish `kind: assembler` (which exists) from
`kind: router-only`, and the workflow does not have a phase that runs
"pure activator" agencies.

### F — Granularity vocabulary: *agent* vs *agency*

The figure depends on the distinction "this looks like an agent but
is really an agency." [`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md) and the
canonical vocabulary in `THE-SOCIETY-OF-REPO/` use *agency* for the
unit and do not provide a separate word for the externally-atomic
view. The plan inherits this. Without that vocabulary, the recursive
question "is this an agent or an agency?" — which is the entire point
of Figure 1-2 — cannot be asked precisely in any current implementation
document.

### G — Activation by *naming a parent*

In the figure, the operator says "turn on BUILDER" and three things
happen. In the plan, activation is driven by *signals*, *labels*,
*paths*, *phrases*, and *frames* — never by naming an agency directly.
There is no `activates_on.parent_agency: agency.code` field, and no
mechanism by which exciting a family excites its members in a
declared order. This is the operational gap that follows from A, B
and C together.

### H — Initialisation as a distinct role

BEGIN exists because *choosing where to start the tower* is a
different kind of work from *adding a block*. The plan handles
initialisation implicitly inside `perceive` and `activate`. No agency
catalogue currently names a "BEGIN-style" role per family — e.g. a
`agency.code.start-here` that picks the entry file before
`patch-imaginer` runs. The work happens, but it is fused into other
units rather than separated out, which is exactly the fusion Minsky
is arguing against in §1.4.

## Implication for the plan (no changes proposed here)

Figure 1-2 is the figure that earns the word *agency* by making one.
The implementation plan honours the *outcome* of that figure
(specialists, named, evolvable) at the workflow scale, but does not
yet honour the *mechanism* (a recursive, sequenced, terminator-bearing
unit that is itself addressable) at the agency scale.

The gaps above are recorded here as analysis, not as a change
request. Any move to close them — for example, a `composed_of` field,
a `kind: router`, an `agency.<family>.<family>.md` parent manifest, or
an agency-local loop with a named terminator — would touch the
manifest schema in [`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md),
the activation protocol in [`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md),
and the identity scopes in `THE-SOCIETY-OF-REPO/02-protocols/01-identity.md`,
and so falls under the "stop and ask" rules in
[`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md) §12 and
[`CLAUDE.md`](../../../../../CLAUDE.md).
