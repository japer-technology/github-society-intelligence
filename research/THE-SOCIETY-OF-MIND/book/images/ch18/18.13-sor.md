# Figure 18-13 — Implementation analysis (SOR / FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION)

**Source figure:** [18-13.md](18-13.md) — *Six arrangements that all
mean five.*
**Analysis target:** [`FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/)
(plan-of-record for `.forgejo-society/` and
`.forgejo/workflows/forgejo-society.yaml`).

This file inventories the ideas carried by Figure 18-13 and checks
each one against the implementation plan.

---

## The ideas Figure 18-13 actually carries

1. **One meaning, many realisations.** *Five* is the same answer
   reached by counting, by finger-matching, by shape recognition,
   by pick-once tagging, by recitation.
2. **Meaning is a family of skills, not a definition.** The figure
   shows only the shape-recognition member of the family; the rest
   are implied.
3. **Cross-connection is the source of robustness.** If one skill
   is broken or unavailable, the others still reach the same
   answer.
4. **Equivalence is a verdict, not a perception.** Recognising the
   six tiles as "all five" is itself a separate act of judgement on
   top of the individual recognitions.
5. **Perceptual variation is wide, conceptual identity is narrow.**
   Six distinct images collapse onto one concept; the collapse is
   the work.
6. **A concept is a small society of methods.** Each method has
   different costs, different failure modes, different evidence.
7. **Naming the concept is a separate act from any one method.**
   The label *five* is not produced by counting alone; it is the
   shared output of the family.
8. **Substitution heals breakage.** Lose one method (e.g. cannot
   count beyond three), another method (e.g. quincunx recognition)
   still yields the answer.

## What the implementation already absorbs

### 1 — Multiple critics produce one verdict

[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md)
and the `criticize` step in
[`02-workflow-design.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/02-workflow-design.md)
have independent critics emitting independent signals that collapse
into a single accept/reject verdict at `settle`. The plan does run
several methods toward one answer.

### 2 — Evidence schema treats sources as a list

The handoff schema in
[`09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md)
accepts multiple evidence items per claim. A single conclusion can
be supported by several independent sources, which is the *family
of methods* pattern at the data layer.

### 3 — Settlement as the named verdict

[`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md)
makes the settlement the place where the *one* outcome is recorded.
That is the operational analogue of "naming the concept *five*"
once the contributing methods have spoken.

## What the implementation does not yet take into account

### A — No notion of *equivalence class of methods*

The plan has many critics and many evidence items but no record
that says "these N methods are different paths to the same answer."
Figure 18-13's six tiles are an equivalence class; the plan has no
schema for declaring "these critics are interchangeable" or "these
evidence types substitute for each other." Substitutability is not
representable.

### B — No graceful fallback when one method is unavailable

A real "five-family" stays functional when one member is broken.
The plan's `criticize` either runs all listed critics or fails. If
one critic is offline, there is no rule saying "fall back to the
other members of its equivalence class." The redundancy that
matters in the figure is absent here.

### C — Single answer, no width

The settlement records one verdict and the list of contributing
signals. It does not record *how many methods reached the same
answer* as a number — the analogue of *width of agreement*. A
verdict supported by six methods looks the same as one supported by
one.

### D — Concept identity is not first-class

Figure 18-13 collapses six images onto one concept (*five*). The
plan collapses many signals onto one settlement, but it does not
have a *concept registry* in which a stable id stands for "the
thing we are deciding about." K-lines in
[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md)
come closest but address *recall of mental state*, not *concept
identity across runs*.

### E — Counting is not a primitive

Several of Minsky's *five*-methods are explicitly numerical
(counting, reciting). The plan has no counting primitive in its
agencies: there is no `agency.code.counter` or
`agency.perception.tally`. Numerical answers are reached, when
they are, by general-purpose code agencies rather than by a named
counting skill.

### F — Cross-method audit is implicit

A reader of a settlement can see which critics ran and what they
said, but not "this approval is the agreement of three independent
families of method." The cross-connection structure exists in
fact, not in record.

### G — No measure of method cost

The figure implicitly says different methods have different costs
(counting is slow, shape-recognition is fast). The plan's
`budget.max_tool_calls` and `budget.max_wall_clock_s` in
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md)
are per-agency limits; there is no per-method cost stored on
signals so that future runs can prefer cheaper paths to the same
answer.

## Implication for the plan (no changes proposed here)

Figure 18-13 is the figure that turns *meaning* into *redundancy*:
the same answer reached by many roads. The implementation does
collapse many signals into one verdict, but it does not yet treat
methods as members of an equivalence class, does not fall back when
one method is unavailable, and does not register concepts as
first-class things.

Closing the gaps — an `equivalence_class:` field on critics,
fallback rules in `criticize`, a width-of-agreement field on
settlements, a concept registry, per-method cost on signals — would
touch the manifest schema in
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md),
the handoff schema in
[`09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md),
the memory model in
[`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md),
and the identity scopes in
`THE-SOCIETY-OF-REPO/02-protocols/01-identity.md`, and so falls
under the stop-and-ask rules in
[`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md) §12 and
[`CLAUDE.md`](../../../../../CLAUDE.md).
