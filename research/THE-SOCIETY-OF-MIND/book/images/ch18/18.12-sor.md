# Figure 18-12 — Implementation analysis (SOR / FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION)

**Source figure:** [18-12.md](18-12.md) — *Bundle of chains, chain of
bundles.*
**Analysis target:** [`FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/)
(plan-of-record for `.forgejo-society/` and
`.forgejo/workflows/forgejo-society.yaml`).

This file inventories the ideas carried by Figure 18-12 and checks
each one against the implementation plan.

---

## The ideas Figure 18-12 actually carries

1. **Composition is recursive.** Chain and bundle (Figure 18-11) can
   be nested inside each other to arbitrary depth.
2. **Two distinct nestings.** *Bundle of chains* (several complete
   proofs supporting one claim) and *chain of bundles* (a sequence
   of steps, each step itself supported by several pieces) are
   different structures with different failure modes.
3. **Serial weakens, parallel strengthens — independently at each
   level.** The arithmetic of reliability composes through the
   levels in opposite directions.
4. **Reliability is a property of the whole tree, not of one
   layer.** A bundle of chains can be very strong even if every
   individual chain is weak; a chain of bundles can be weak even if
   every bundle is strong.
5. **Argument shape is the same vocabulary as mechanical shape.**
   The figure deliberately reuses the linkage language for proofs.
6. **The two nestings are not interchangeable.** Bundle-of-chains
   and chain-of-bundles do *not* simplify to the same structure;
   the order of nesting carries meaning.
7. **Naming the nesting is a separate act.** The figure labels the
   shape (`BUNDLE OF CHAINS`, `CHAIN OF BUNDLES`) before discussing
   it; the label is part of the design vocabulary.
8. **Practical arguments are mixed.** Real reasoning is neither
   purely serial nor purely parallel; the mix is what makes a case
   persuasive.

## What the implementation already absorbs

### 1 — Chain at the top, bundle inside one step

The phase chain in
[`03-runtime-pipeline.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/03-runtime-pipeline.md)
is a chain, and the `criticize` step inside it is a bundle of
critics (see
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md)
and
[`11-mapping-sor-to-implementation.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/11-mapping-sor-to-implementation.md)).
The plan therefore already implements one instance of *chain of
bundles* — a sequence of phases, with at least one phase made of
parallel checks.

### 2 — Evidence on every claim

[`09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md)
requires non-empty evidence on every handoff. Each step in the
chain therefore has its own internal bundle (the cited sources),
which is a second, smaller instance of the same pattern.

### 3 — Critics + censors as nested layers

[`07-policies-and-safety.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/07-policies-and-safety.md)
runs censors after critics. Read as Figure 18-12, this is a short
chain (`criticize → censor`) of bundles (the critic set, the censor
set).

### 4 — Settlement records the chain

[`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md)
collapses the run into a settlement; the settlement preserves the
order of phases (chain) and the verdicts of the critic set (bundle).
The two-level shape survives into git.

## What the implementation does not yet take into account

### A — Nesting is hard-coded, not declarative

The chain-of-bundles shape exists because the workflow yaml hard
codes it. There is no schema in which an agency declares
"`composed_of` is a bundle of chains" or "this critic is itself a
chain of three sub-checks." The two nestings Figure 18-12 names are
not values that anything in `.forgejo-society/` can take.

### B — Depth is limited to two

Today the plan supports phases (level 1) and, inside `criticize`,
critics (level 2). It cannot represent a critic that is itself a
chain of sub-critics, or a sub-critic that is itself a bundle.
Recursive nesting — Figure 18-12's entire point — stops at one
fold.

### C — *Bundle of chains* has no analogue

The plan's chain-of-bundles (pipeline with bundled critics inside)
is present, but its mirror image is not. Nothing in the plan says
"approve this act if *any one* of three complete, independent
critic-chains accepts it." There is no `any-of:` over named chains,
only `all-of:` implied by the listed critics.

### D — Reliability arithmetic is not recorded

Even where bundles exist, the plan does not record *how many
independent paths supported a claim* or *how much weaker a chain
became at its weakest link*. There is no field for the strength of
support, only for its presence. The composition-of-reliability
arithmetic Figure 18-12 depends on cannot be computed from a
settlement record.

### E — Order of nesting is invisible in identifiers

A `bundle-of-chains` and a `chain-of-bundles` made of the same
parts have the same set of activations and the same handoffs, but
different meanings. The identity scheme in
`THE-SOCIETY-OF-REPO/02-protocols/01-identity.md` has no field that
records "this composite is a bundle-of-chains, not a
chain-of-bundles." The shape that distinguishes the two — and so
the difference in reliability — is unnameable.

### F — Mixed-shape reports

`report` in
[`02-workflow-design.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/02-workflow-design.md)
lists steps and verdicts. It does not render the shape of the
underlying argument (e.g. "this approval is a chain of length 12 in
which step 4 was a bundle of width 3"). A reviewer who wants to
know *how reliable* an act is must reconstruct the topology from
the trace.

### G — No tooling for the failure-mode mirror

Figure 18-12's punchline is that one nesting is fragile in places
the other is robust. The plan offers no tool for asking "which of
these two compositions should I prefer?" because it has no
representation for "these two compositions."

## Implication for the plan (no changes proposed here)

Figure 18-12 forces the question: *which composition?* The plan
answers, for now, "the one in the yaml" — a chain of (at most two)
bundles, hard-coded. The recursive, choosable, measurable
composition the figure depicts is not yet representable.

Closing the gaps — a `composed_of` field, an `any-of:` quorum, a
`shape:` discriminator, strength on signals, deeper nesting — would
touch the manifest schema in
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md),
the handoff schema in
[`09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md),
the runtime pipeline in
[`03-runtime-pipeline.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/03-runtime-pipeline.md),
and the identity scopes in
`THE-SOCIETY-OF-REPO/02-protocols/01-identity.md`, and so falls
under the stop-and-ask rules in
[`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md) §12 and
[`CLAUDE.md`](../../../../../CLAUDE.md).
