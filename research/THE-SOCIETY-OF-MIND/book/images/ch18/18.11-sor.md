# Figure 18-11 — Implementation analysis (SOR / FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION)

**Source figure:** [18-11.md](18-11.md) — *Chain versus bundle.*
**Analysis target:** [`FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/)
(plan-of-record for `.forgejo-society/` and
`.forgejo/workflows/forgejo-society.yaml`).

This file inventories the ideas carried by Figure 18-11 and checks
each one against the implementation plan.

---

## The ideas Figure 18-11 actually carries

1. **Two primitive topologies of support.** Serial (chain) and
   parallel (bundle) are the irreducible ways to link parts between
   two endpoints.
2. **Asymmetric reliability.** A chain fails when *any* link fails; a
   bundle fails only when *every* path fails.
3. **Redundancy as deliberate design.** Brakes, gear, curb are
   *independently chosen* paths to the same outcome (the car stays).
4. **Independence is the load-bearing property.** Bundles work only
   if their members can fail separately. Correlated paths collapse
   into a single chain.
5. **Convergence at the endpoints.** A bundle is defined by sharing
   *A* and *B*; the middle is parallel, but the conclusions are the
   same.
6. **Strong arguments are bundles.** Several independent lines of
   reasoning supporting one claim are exactly the parallel topology.
7. **Weak arguments are chains.** A single line of reasoning is a
   chain in the realm of inference, with weakest-link semantics.
8. **The topology travels across realms.** Mechanical, inferential,
   organisational reliability all obey the same rule.

## What the implementation already absorbs

### 1 — Chain semantics at the pipeline

The phase chain in
[`03-runtime-pipeline.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/03-runtime-pipeline.md)
fails closed at every step — *any* phase failure halts the run. The
plan unambiguously treats the workflow as a serial chain.

### 2 — Multiple critics in one phase

[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md)
and [`11-mapping-sor-to-implementation.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/11-mapping-sor-to-implementation.md)
list `evidence-critic`, `scope-critic`, `cost-critic`,
`privacy-critic`, `risk-critic`, `overconfidence-critic`,
`source-quality-critic`, `staleness-critic`. All run in the
`criticize` step. This is a *bundle of critics* whose joint
veto-power is stronger than any single one.

### 3 — Censors plus critics

[`07-policies-and-safety.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/07-policies-and-safety.md)
adds censors after critics. Critics challenge, censors forbid. The
two layers are parallel safety paths against unsafe `act` outputs.

### 4 — Evidence-bearing handoffs

[`09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md)
requires *non-empty* evidence on every claim. The runtime law "no
evidence, no trust" forbids the weakest single-link inference and
forces, at minimum, the appearance of a bundle.

### 5 — Strong-argument shape inside `criticize`

Because each critic emits an independent signal, an act survives
`criticize` only if it satisfies many independent checks. The
topology Minsky calls a *strong argument* is the topology of
`criticize`.

## What the implementation does not yet take into account

### A — Independence is asserted, not measured

A bundle works only if its paths are independent. The plan ships
several critics but has no field that records *what each one looks
at*, no schema that detects when two critics share inputs and so
correlate, and no run-time check that says "these three approvals
all rest on the same evidence id — treat them as one." Critics that
look like a bundle may, in practice, be one chain wearing three
labels.

### B — No first-class *bundle* primitive

The plan composes critics by *listing them* in `criticize`. There is
no `bundle:` declaration that names the parallel set, no minimum
quorum (`require: at-least 2 of {evidence, scope, source-quality}`),
no maximum tolerated dissent. A bundle is implied by enumeration,
not declared.

### C — No quorum semantics for agencies

For critics, the implicit rule is *all must pass*. For agencies
themselves, there is no equivalent rule. The plan cannot say
"activate three independent code agencies; if any two agree, accept
the patch." Voting and majority rules are absent. Bundles of
agencies are not a primitive.

### D — Chain-only at the pipeline level

The workflow itself is a chain. There is no "run perceive and
remember in parallel" branch, no parallel arm for redundant
perception agencies. The single failure of any phase still halts
the run; there is no bundled alternative path.

### E — No record of correlated failure

Minsky's lesson is that brakes, gear, and curb fail *for different
reasons*. The plan's settlement record
([`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md))
captures *which* checks failed but not *whether their failure modes
were independent*. Cross-run analysis cannot easily distinguish "we
have a bundle" from "we have one chain pretending to be three."

### F — No degraded-mode bundle

A real bundle keeps the car parked even if one of three paths is
already known to be broken. The plan has no concept of "run with
the cost-critic offline because it has been failing; require the
remaining critics to compensate." Bundles in `criticize` are
all-or-nothing.

### G — Strong-argument shape is not surfaced in reports

The `report` step
([`02-workflow-design.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/02-workflow-design.md))
lists what happened, not the *argument shape*. A reader cannot see
that an act was approved because three independent critics agreed,
versus approved because one critic ran and the others timed out.

## Implication for the plan (no changes proposed here)

Figure 18-11 names the smallest non-trivial design decision the plan
has to make: are these parts in series or in parallel? The
implementation already uses both — the pipeline is a chain, the
critics are a bundle — but it does not *declare* which is which, and
it does not measure the independence on which the bundle's strength
depends.

Adding a `bundle:` primitive, quorum semantics, an independence
field on critics, parallel branches in the workflow, or degraded-mode
operation would touch the manifest schema in
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md),
the runtime pipeline in
[`03-runtime-pipeline.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/03-runtime-pipeline.md),
and the safety policies in
[`07-policies-and-safety.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/07-policies-and-safety.md),
and so falls under the stop-and-ask rules in
[`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md) §12 and
[`CLAUDE.md`](../../../../../CLAUDE.md).
