# Figure 18-6 — Implementation analysis (SOR / FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION)

**Source figure:** [18-6.md](18-6.md) — *One arch, many roles; chains of
roles.*
**Analysis target:** [`FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/)
(plan-of-record for `.forgejo-society/` and
`.forgejo/workflows/forgejo-society.yaml`).

This file inventories the ideas carried by Figure 18-6 and checks each
one against the implementation plan.

---

## The ideas Figure 18-6 actually carries

1. **One structure, many readings.** A single arch is *bridge*,
   *table*, and *tunnel* depending on the role it is asked to fill.
   The substrate does not change; the use does.
2. **Role is a property of context, not of the part.** The arch
   carries no field "I am a bridge." Whatever calls it decides.
3. **Chaining is itself a family of operations.** *Road*, *shelves*,
   *arcade* are three different chainings of the same primitive,
   each with its own end-to-end semantic.
4. **Repetition produces a new kind.** A row of bridges is not "more
   bridge" — it is a *road*, a thing with its own name and its own
   properties.
5. **Chains exist in several realms at once.** Structural (arch in
   space), functional (use in time), conceptual (name in thought).
   The same six panels carry all three.
6. **Weakest-link logic travels with the chain.** Whichever realm the
   chain lives in, breaking one link breaks the whole.
7. **Repair is per-link.** The unit of fixing matches the unit of
   chaining, not the unit of naming.
8. **Naming the chain is a separate act from building it.** *Road*,
   *shelves*, *arcade* are names imposed on a structural pattern
   after the fact; the pattern is what carries the weight.

## What the implementation already absorbs

### 1 — Same unit, different uses

A manifest in `.forgejo-society/agencies/` is a single artefact that
can be activated by several `activates_on` triggers, in different
phases, with different outputs. The unit (the agency) is one;
[`02-workflow-design.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/02-workflow-design.md)
and [`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md)
let it appear inside more than one frame.

### 2 — Pipelines as named chains

The phase chain
`guard → normalize → guardrail → perceive → activate → deliberate →
criticize → censor → settle → act → remember → report`
([`03-runtime-pipeline.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/03-runtime-pipeline.md))
is the one chain the plan does name. It is the *road* in Minsky's
sense: a sequence whose meaning is more than the sum of its members.

### 3 — Weakest-link semantics at run level

`02-workflow-design.md` makes the workflow fail closed: any phase
that fails halts the run. That is the chain reading of reliability
applied to the pipeline.

### 4 — Critics as a chain of evidence demands

[`07-policies-and-safety.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/07-policies-and-safety.md)
and [`09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md)
require evidence at every handoff. A handoff that loses its evidence
breaks the chain, and the `criticize` step rejects it.

## What the implementation does not yet take into account

### A — No vocabulary for *role-of-an-agency*

Figure 18-6's central trick is that the same arch is called *bridge*
in one frame and *table* in another. The plan gives each agency an
id (`agency.code.patch-imaginer`) but no separate field for *the role
it is playing in this run*. There is no `role:` on a handoff, no
`as: bridge|table|tunnel` modifier on an activation. The arch / use
distinction is missing from the schema.

### B — Pipeline is the only first-class chain

The phase chain in `03-runtime-pipeline.md` is hard-coded. The plan
has no notion of a *user-declared* chain of agencies (the way *road*
declares a chain of arches). Nothing in `.forgejo-society/` says
"this set of agencies, run in this order, *is* a chain with its own
name." Chaining as a primitive that can be applied at will is
absent.

### C — No naming of emergent composites

A row of bridges becoming a *road* is an act of *renaming the
composite*. The plan can record outputs through
`agencies/assembly/*` and summary tiers, but it does not let a chain
of activations be addressed by a single id (`chain.code.bug-fix`,
say). Composites are recorded, not named.

### D — Realm of the chain is implicit

Structural, functional, conceptual chains all look the same in the
figure but differ in what they support. The plan distinguishes
phases (functional) and signal kinds
([`09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md))
but not "this chain is structural" vs "this chain is conceptual."
Anyone reading a run record cannot tell which realm a given chain
operates in.

### E — Repair granularity is not stated

Weakest-link logic only helps if the unit of repair matches the
unit of chaining. The plan replaces *agencies* (manifest files) and
*workflows* (yaml). It has no concept of "replace this one link in
the deliberate phase chain" because the deliberate phase is not
itself made of named replaceable links.

### F — Same agency, multiple roles, single audit trail

If one manifest were used as *bridge* in one run and *table* in
another, today's audit trail records two activations of the same id
with no role field to distinguish them. The collapse-into-git rule
([`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md))
keeps the *trace* but loses the *reading*.

## Implication for the plan (no changes proposed here)

Figure 18-6 says: *one part, many uses; one use, many chainings*. The
implementation honours the first half weakly (one manifest can fire in
several phases) and the second half not at all (only the pipeline is
named as a chain).

Closing the gaps — adding a `role:` field, allowing user-named chains
of agencies, recording the realm of a chain, distinguishing the unit
of repair from the unit of activation — would touch the manifest
schema in [`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md),
the handoff schema in [`09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md),
and the identity scopes in
`THE-SOCIETY-OF-REPO/02-protocols/01-identity.md`, and so falls under
the stop-and-ask rules in
[`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md) §12 and
[`CLAUDE.md`](../../../../../CLAUDE.md).
