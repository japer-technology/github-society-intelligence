# Figure 23-2 — Implementation analysis (SOR / FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION)

**Source figure:** [23-2.md](23-2.md) — *The duplication problem.*
**Analysis target:** [`FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/)
(plan-of-record for `.forgejo-society/` and
`.forgejo/workflows/forgejo-society.yaml`).

This file inventories the ideas carried by Figure 23-2 and checks each
one against the implementation plan. The aim is to mark, plainly, what
the plan already absorbs and what it does not yet name.

---

## The ideas Figure 23-2 actually carries

Pulled from the figure caption and Minsky's surrounding text in §23.2
*Differences and duplicates*:

1. **A difference-detector is a third agency.** Comparing *A* and *Z*
   is not done by *A* or *Z*; it is done by a separate unit that
   receives both as inputs.
2. **Two parallel input streams.** The detector requires two
   well-aligned bundles of signals, one from each side, arriving in
   compatible form.
3. **Alignment is fragile.** Anything that is not the difference
   being looked for looks *like* a difference. The detector cannot
   distinguish signal from skew on its own.
4. **The producing machinery must match.** Not only the outputs of
   *A* and *Z*, but the subagencies that produced those outputs, must
   be essentially identical, or noise dominates.
5. **Comparison appears to demand duplication.** Taken naively, this
   says: to compare two states of mind you need two copies of the
   mind that produced them.
6. **Duplication is implausible at the brain's scale.** Minsky uses
   the figure to set up a problem, not to endorse the duplication.
   The figure is the *cost statement* of naïve difference detection.
7. **Some shared substrate is required.** The way out of the
   duplication problem (developed in later sections) is that the two
   sides share most of their machinery and differ only in a small,
   localised part.
8. **Spurious mismatches are the dominant failure mode.** When the
   substrate is *not* shared, the detector's output is mostly noise,
   not insight.

These eight items are what the rest of this file tests against the
implementation plan.

## What the implementation already absorbs

### 1 — Comparison is delegated to dedicated units

The plan does not ask the producer of a patch to evaluate it.
`agency.code.diff-skeptic` "attacks the proposed patch on its own
terms"
([`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md)),
and the `critic.*` and `censor.*` families exist precisely to play
the role of the third agency in Figure 23-2.

### 2 — Inputs to the comparison are explicit artefacts

`perceive` writes a workspace snapshot, `deliberate` writes proposals,
and `settle` writes the candidate settlement
([`03-runtime-pipeline.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/03-runtime-pipeline.md),
[`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md)).
Critics receive both sides through the same on-disk channels and the
same handoff/signal schemas
([`09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md)),
which is the plan's version of "two parallel streams in compatible
form."

### 3 — Normalisation as a defence against skew

[`02-workflow-design.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/02-workflow-design.md)
includes a `normalize` phase before any deliberation. Its job is to
canonicalise the inputs the comparison agencies will see, which is
the plan's structural answer to the alignment fragility in item 3.

### 4 — One workspace, one runtime

[`00-overview.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/00-overview.md)
and
[`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md)
place all agencies in the same `.forgejo-society/` tree, reading the
same memory and signals. The producing machinery is, in effect,
shared by construction: the plan has *one* mind, not two, so the
"shared substrate" requirement of item 7 is partially satisfied at
the architectural level.

### 5 — Policies that bound spurious mismatches

[`07-policies-and-safety.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/07-policies-and-safety.md)
gives censors and policy gates the authority to drop or quarantine
outputs that fail evidence or scope checks. This caps the cost of
item 8 — the detector's noise — even though it does not eliminate the
underlying cause.

## What the implementation does not yet take into account

These are the parts of Figure 23-2 that the plan currently leaves
implicit, partial, or absent. None of them are urgent; all of them
are real.

### A — No paired (A, Z) input contract for critics

Critics receive proposals and workspace state, but the schema in
[`09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md)
does not define a "compare-these-two" handoff with a left side, a
right side, and an explicit alignment manifest. Each critic
improvises that pairing in its prompt body, which is exactly the
ad-hoc arrangement Figure 23-2 warns against.

### B — Provenance of inputs is partial

The figure says the *producing* subagencies on both sides must match,
not just their outputs. The run record stores which agencies fired
(`state/mind/issues/<n>/activation.jsonl`), but a critic does not
receive, as part of its input, the lineage of agencies that produced
each side of the comparison. It cannot check whether *A* and *Z* were
produced by compatible machinery.

### C — No detector for "this is mostly skew"

Item 8 is the dominant failure mode. The plan has critics that find
real defects (`diff-skeptic`, `blast-radius-fear`) and censors that
enforce policy, but no unit whose job is to declare *"these two
inputs were not aligned enough to be compared"* and to suppress the
comparison itself. The plan trusts that `normalize` was sufficient;
it does not check.

### D — Shared substrate is assumed, not asserted

The architectural fact that all agencies share `memory/`, `state/`,
and the same runtime is what makes the "duplication problem" mostly
moot for this plan. But that fact is nowhere asserted as a
*requirement* of comparison. If a future change introduced
out-of-process agencies, federated runners, or per-agency private
state (the kind of move suggested elsewhere in the
[transition plans](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-INSTALLATION/transition-plan/)),
nothing in the comparison schema would notice that the substrate had
stopped being shared.

### E — No bound on comparison count

Figure 23-2 implies that each new pair-comparison is expensive
because alignment must be re-established. The plan's budgets in
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md)
(`max_tool_calls`, `max_wall_clock_s`) bound a single unit's cost.
They do not bound *how many distinct A↔Z comparisons* a run may
perform, which is the cost axis the figure highlights.

### F — Differences are not stored, so duplicates cannot be detected

If two critics independently raise the *same* difference between *A*
and *Z*, the plan has no mechanism to recognise them as duplicates,
because differences are not stored as objects (see also gap A in
[`23.1-sor.md`](23.1-sor.md)). The cost Minsky describes — comparison
machinery being run twice over near-identical inputs — can therefore
happen silently.

### G — Analogy is built on situations, not on aligned pairs

`memory/analogies/*.yml`
([`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md))
stores cross-domain mappings between *situations*. The figure points
at a different object: an aligned (*A*, *Z*) pair plus a record of
which subagencies on each side were treated as "the same". The plan
has no schema for that aligned pair, so analogies cannot yet be
indexed by the comparison they came from.

## Implication for the plan (no changes proposed here)

Figure 23-2 is the cost statement that follows from making
*difference* the unit of thought (Figure 23-1). The implementation
plan sidesteps the brunt of the duplication problem by running one
mind on one substrate, which is structurally the right move.
What it does not yet do is name the *paired comparison* as a
first-class handoff, deliver lineage as part of that handoff, or
defend against the case where alignment has silently failed.

The gaps above are recorded here as analysis, not as a change
request. Any move to close them — a `compare:` handoff kind in
[`09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md),
a lineage block on critic inputs, an alignment-check unit before
critics fire, or a stored-differences tree under
[`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md)
— would touch handoff schemas, the manifest schema in
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md),
the memory layout, and the identity scopes in
`THE-SOCIETY-OF-REPO/02-protocols/01-identity.md`, and so falls under
the "stop and ask" rules in
[`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md) §12 and
[`CLAUDE.md`](../../../../../CLAUDE.md).
