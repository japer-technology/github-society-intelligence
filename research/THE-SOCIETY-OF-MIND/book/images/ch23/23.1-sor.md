# Figure 23-1 — Implementation analysis (SOR / FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION)

**Source figure:** [23-1.md](23-1.md) — *Procedure and Difference
between two situations.*
**Analysis target:** [`FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/)
(plan-of-record for `.forgejo-society/` and
`.forgejo/workflows/forgejo-society.yaml`).

This file inventories the ideas carried by Figure 23-1 and checks each
one against the implementation plan. The aim is to mark, plainly, what
the plan already absorbs and what it does not yet name.

---

## The ideas Figure 23-1 actually carries

Pulled from the figure caption and Minsky's surrounding text in §23.1
*A world of differences*:

1. **Two endpoints, named.** *A* (the situation as it is) and *Z*
   (the situation aimed at) are first-class objects that can be
   pointed to and reasoned about as wholes.
2. **Two edges between them.** A *Procedure* edge (a way to get from
   A to Z) and a *Difference* edge (a description of how A and Z
   diverge). The pair is the irreducible unit, not either edge alone.
3. **The same diamond serves many verbs.** Predicting, expecting,
   explaining, wanting, escaping, attacking, defending, and
   abstracting are different *traversals* of the same two edges, not
   different mechanisms.
4. **Direction matters.** Procedure runs forward from A; Difference
   can be read either way. The *direction* of traversal is what
   distinguishes "predict" from "explain" from "want".
5. **Difference is a kind of object.** It is not a side-effect of
   comparison; it is a thing that can be stored, named, and handed to
   another agency.
6. **Procedure is selected by difference.** "Wanting" means: take the
   difference, ask which procedure would reduce it. Differences index
   procedures.
7. **Analogy lives one level up.** Once differences are objects,
   *differences between differences* become the operands of analogy.
8. **Economy of mechanism.** A single diamond, traversed in different
   directions, replaces a long list of separate faculties.

These eight items are what the rest of this file tests against the
implementation plan.

## What the implementation already absorbs

### 1 — A and Z exist as nameable states

[`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md)
gives every run a workspace state on disk
(`state/mind/issues/<n>/…`), and `settle` produces an explicit
settlement object. *A* is the workspace as `perceive` finds it; *Z*
is the workspace the settlement asserts. Both endpoints are
addressable artefacts, not just transient values.

### 2 — Procedure has an operational home

The phase chain documented in
[`03-runtime-pipeline.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/03-runtime-pipeline.md)
— `perceive → activate → deliberate → criticize → censor → settle →
act` — is the plan's standing procedure from A to Z. Individual
agencies such as `agency.code.patch-imaginer` propose narrower
procedures inside that chain.

### 3 — Differences as frame slots

[`11-mapping-sor-to-implementation.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/11-mapping-sor-to-implementation.md)
records the mapping *"difference engines → implicit in frame
`slots:` (required slots == differences from desired state)."* Frames
in [`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md)
already treat unfilled slots as the gap between current and target,
which is one half of the diamond.

### 4 — Analogy fallback

`activate` runs an analogy pass over `memory/analogies/` when no
frame matches strongly
([`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md)
§ *Analogy fallback*). This is the operational seat for idea 7: a
place where the system reaches for cross-domain similarity when the
local difference cannot be reduced directly.

### 5 — Criticism that attacks the proposed procedure

`agency.code.diff-skeptic`
([`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md))
"attacks the proposed patch on its own terms" — i.e. compares the
proposed Z to A and asks whether the procedure really closes the
difference. This is Figure 23-1 read in the *explain* direction.

## What the implementation does not yet take into account

These are the parts of Figure 23-1 that the plan currently leaves
implicit, partial, or absent. None of them are urgent; all of them
are real.

### A — Difference is not a first-class object

The plan stores frames, klines, analogies, signals, and settlements,
but there is no `memory/differences/` and no `difference.*` identifier
scope. A difference exists only as a transient comparison inside a
critic prompt or as an unfilled frame slot. It cannot be named,
cited, or handed between agencies the way a K-line can.

### B — Predict / explain / want are fused into one direction

The pipeline runs A → Z (perceive → settle → act). It does not have
a declared *explain* mode that runs Z → A on an already-acted
situation, nor a declared *want* mode that takes a desired Z and asks
which procedure to invoke. All three traversals collapse into the
single forward run; the diamond is walked one way only.

### C — Procedures are not indexed by the differences they reduce

Agencies declare `activates_on:` against signals, paths, labels, and
phrases. None of them declare *which difference they are competent to
reduce*. There is no field such as:

```yaml
reduces_difference:
  - difference.code.missing-test-coverage
  - difference.docs.broken-link
```

so the runtime cannot answer Minsky's central question — "given this
difference, which procedure?" — by lookup. It can only answer it by
matching on surface features.

### D — No store of differences between differences

Analogy in the plan lives in `memory/analogies/*.yml` as cross-domain
mappings between situations. The plan does not store *differences
between differences*, which is where Minsky locates analogy proper.
The substrate for "this gap is like that gap" is therefore present
for situations but not for gaps themselves.

### E — Direction of traversal is not in the schema

A frame, a kline, or an agency manifest does not record whether it is
being used forward (predict), backward (explain), or as a goal-setter
(want). The same unit may serve all three uses, but the run log
([`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md))
does not note which traversal occurred, so later K-line replay cannot
distinguish "we predicted X" from "we wanted X" from "we explained
X."

### F — A and Z are asymmetric in the run record

The plan captures the *initial* perception and the *final*
settlement, but not a paired (A, Z) object with a difference between
them as its third field. Critics receive A and a proposed Z as
separate inputs; nothing in the workspace says "here is the diamond
that this run instantiates."

### G — Abstraction has no diamond-shaped seat

Idea 8 — that one diamond replaces a list of faculties — is at odds
with the plan's current shape, where predict-like work
(`patch-imaginer`), explain-like work (`diff-skeptic`,
`revert-path-finder`), and want-like work (`settle`, governance
frames) are separate units with separate manifests. The economy
Minsky claims for the diamond is not the economy the plan currently
offers.

## Implication for the plan (no changes proposed here)

Figure 23-1 argues that *difference* and *procedure* together are the
primitive of useful thought, and that almost every mental verb is a
direction of travel across that pair. The implementation plan owns
the *procedure* edge cleanly and reads the *difference* edge through
the indirect lens of frame slots, but it does not yet make
differences into first-class, storable, indexable objects, and it
does not distinguish traversal directions in the run record.

The gaps above are recorded here as analysis, not as a change
request. Any move to close them — a `difference.*` scope in the
identity protocol, a `memory/differences/` tree, a
`reduces_difference:` field on agency manifests, or a `traversal:`
field on activation records — would touch the manifest schema in
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md),
the frame and analogy protocols in
[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md),
the memory layout in
[`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md),
and the identity scopes in
`THE-SOCIETY-OF-REPO/02-protocols/01-identity.md`, and so falls under
the "stop and ask" rules in
[`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md) §12 and
[`CLAUDE.md`](../../../../../CLAUDE.md).
