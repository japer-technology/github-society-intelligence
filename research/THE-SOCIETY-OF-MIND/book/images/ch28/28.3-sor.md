# Figure 28-3 — Implementation analysis (SOR / FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION)

**Source figure:** [28-3.md](28-3.md) — *Left, right, front, and rear
brains.*
**Analysis target:** [`FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/)
(plan-of-record for `.forgejo-society/` and
`.forgejo/workflows/forgejo-society.yaml`).

This file inventories the ideas carried by Figure 28-3 and checks each
one against the implementation plan. The aim is to mark, plainly, what
the plan already absorbs and what it does not yet name.

---

## The ideas Figure 28-3 actually carries

Pulled from the figure and Minsky's surrounding text in §28.8
*Overlapping minds*:

1. **A partition is a methodological choice, not a fact.** The
   left/right cut and the front/rear cut are both available; the
   anatomy does not prefer either.
2. **Equal-arbitrariness defeats privilege.** If left/right earns each
   half a "mind," front/rear earns the same. Any cut would.
3. **A region is a *mind* only if its parts cohere.** The criterion is
   internal — the relationships among the parts must show genuine
   coherency. A drawn box is not enough.
4. **Coherency is a property to be tested, not declared.** The figure
   shows three boxes; the chapter asks which of them actually has the
   internal traffic that would justify the label.
5. **Overlap is the normal case.** The same agents and walls belong to
   more than one mini-mind at once. Membership is not partition.
6. **Agents are shared without sharing mental lives.** Two overlapping
   minds use the same components but do not see each other's contents.
   Sharing structure does not entail sharing experience.
7. **Mini-minds are local, not summed.** The whole is not a single
   mind whose parts are mini-minds; the mini-minds simply coexist
   with overlap.
8. **The frame around the figure matters.** All three panels sit in
   one outer frame. The unifying surface is the *forge*, not a meta-mind.

These eight items are what the rest of this file tests against the
implementation plan.

## What the implementation already absorbs

### 1 — Many cross-cutting groupings are first-class

[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md)
defines several orthogonal axes by which units are grouped: *kind*
(agency / critic / censor), *family* (perception, memory, code,
safety, identity, integration, assembly, meta-admin), and *frame*
([`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md)).
These are different cuts through the same population, which matches
the figure's left/right vs front/rear move at the level of
*grouping*.

### 2 — Frames as overlapping membership

Frames in [`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md)
are explicitly described as activations that pull together units from
different families. A single agency can participate in many frames.
This is the closest operational analogue to "the same agent belongs
to more than one mini-mind."

### 3 — Polynemes as multi-membership

Polynemes wake several units at once across family lines. The plan
therefore already treats *co-activation* as a real thing distinct
from folder membership — the figure's central distinction (the box
around a region is not the region's identity).

### 4 — The forge is the unifying frame

[`00-overview.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/00-overview.md)
and [`11-mapping-sor-to-implementation.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/11-mapping-sor-to-implementation.md)
keep the forge — repository, workflow, runner — as the outer
container of every grouping. There is no meta-mind agency that
"owns" all the others; there is a substrate. This matches the
figure's outer frame around its three panels.

### 5 — Critics and censors as coherency tests

`critic.scope`, `critic.evidence`, and the censor stack each test
whether a proposed action *holds together*. They are the plan's
nearest analogue to the figure's question "does this region have
genuine coherency?" — applied to candidate settlements rather than
to candidate minds, but the shape of the test is the same.

## What the implementation does not yet take into account

These are the parts of Figure 28-3 that the plan currently leaves
implicit, partial, or simply absent. None of them are urgent; all of
them are real.

### A — "Mini-mind" is not a manifest kind

The figure's load-bearing unit is the *mini-mind*: a subset of agents
whose internal traffic is dense enough to warrant the label. The plan
has *agency*, *critic*, *censor*, *frame*, and *family*, but no
schema entity that says "these units, taken together, exhibit
coherency and may be addressed as a region." A mini-mind cannot be
named, activated, inspected, or measured today.

### B — Coherency is not measured

There is no critic, censor, or report that asks how much internal
co-activation a candidate grouping shows. Nothing in
[`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md)
or [`09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md)
records signal traffic at the *between-units* level in a form that
could be summarised as "this set of units talks to itself more than
to outsiders." The figure's criterion is therefore unrepresentable.

### C — Folder partitions look authoritative

`agencies/perception/`, `agencies/code/`, etc. are filesystem
partitions, and the schema's `family:` enum hardens that partition.
The figure's whole point is that any single partition is *arbitrary*.
The plan currently presents one partition (by family) as if it were
the structure of the society, rather than as one cut through it.
Frames soften this in practice but do not contradict it on disk.

### D — No second, equally-valid partition is documented

The figure depends on showing *two* cuts of the same brain. The plan
documents one (by family). It does not also document, say, a
partition by *latency* (fast-loop vs slow-loop units), by *risk*
(read-only vs write-capable), or by *surface* (chat-facing vs
git-facing). Without a second canonical cut, the figure's
equal-arbitrariness argument has nothing to land on.

### E — Overlap is implicit, not declared

A unit's frames are listed on the unit. There is no inverse view: no
manifest, no generated index, no settlement field that says "frame
*F* currently contains units *X, Y, Z*, of which *Y* is also in
frames *G* and *H*." Overlap exists in the data but is not surfaced
as a first-class object. Nothing in the runtime can ask "what
overlaps with what?"

### F — Shared agents are not isolated from one another's contents

Minsky's claim that overlapping minds "share agents without sharing
mental lives" presupposes a per-mini-mind context boundary. The plan
gives each *run* a workspace ([`03-runtime-pipeline.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/03-runtime-pipeline.md)),
but does not give *concurrent mini-minds within a run* their own
contexts. If two regions of the society want to use `patch-imaginer`
without seeing each other's drafts, there is currently no
mechanism — the workspace is shared.

### G — The outer frame is not labelled as a non-mind

The forge plays the role of Minsky's outer frame, but the plan does
not say so. Nothing in [`00-overview.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/00-overview.md)
states *the forge is the unifying surface and is not itself an
agency*. Without that statement, future contributors may treat the
workflow as a top-level mind and start hanging "global" decisions on
it — exactly the privilege Minsky denies any single region.

### H — No vocabulary for "coherent enough to address"

The chapter's strongest move is to tie *naming a mind* to *internal
coherency*. The plan has no threshold, no review step, and no
policy in [`07-policies-and-safety.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/07-policies-and-safety.md)
that says "a grouping may be elevated to a named region only when
its internal co-activation passes such-and-such bar." Naming is
purely editorial.

## Implication for the plan (no changes proposed here)

Figure 28-3 is the figure that forbids the system from treating any
one partition of itself as *the* structure. The implementation plan
already has the raw material — frames, polynemes, families — to
support overlapping groupings, and it already declines to define a
meta-mind. What it lacks is a first-class *mini-mind* unit, a way to
*measure* the coherency that would earn one, and a documented second
partition to break the apparent authority of the family folders.

The gaps above are recorded here as analysis, not as a change
request. Any move to close them — for example, a `region:` schema
entity, a coherency metric in the settlement record, a second
canonical partition documented in
[`01-target-layout.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/01-target-layout.md),
or per-mini-mind workspace contexts in
[`03-runtime-pipeline.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/03-runtime-pipeline.md)
— would touch the manifest schema in
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md),
the activation model in
[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md),
and the identity scopes in `THE-SOCIETY-OF-REPO/02-protocols/01-identity.md`,
and so falls under the "stop and ask" rules in
[`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md) §12 and
[`CLAUDE.md`](../../../../../CLAUDE.md).
