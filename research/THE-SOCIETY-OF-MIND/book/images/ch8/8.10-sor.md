# Figure 8-10 — Implementation analysis (SOR / FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION)

**Source figure:** [8-10.md](8-10.md) — *Paired K-society and S-society layers.*
**Analysis target:** [`FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/)
(plan-of-record for `.forgejo-society/` and
`.forgejo/workflows/forgejo-society.yaml`).

This file inventories the ideas carried by Figure 8-10 and checks each
one against the implementation plan.

---

## The ideas Figure 8-10 actually carries

1. **K-society is laid out *alongside* its S-society, not above or
   inside it.** The architecture is parallel layers, not nesting.
2. **Every K-line is at most one short hop from its S-agents.** The
   layer pairing keeps memory adjacent to cognition.
3. **Signals flow in opposite directions in the two layers.**
   S-agents drive forward toward action; K-agents drive backward
   from memory toward agents.
4. **The two layers share connectivity structure.** The K-layer's
   topology mirrors the S-layer it pairs with.
5. **Memory is adjacent to cognition, not stored elsewhere and
   retrieved.** Recall is a short walk, not a lookup.
6. **The arrangement is recursive.** Another K-society can be paired
   with the first K-society, and so on — open-ended stack.
7. **Layer pairing is the architectural answer to growth.** It is
   how memory stays useful as the agent population grows.

## What the implementation already absorbs

### 1 — Memory lives in the same folder as the mind

`memory/`, `agencies/`, `state/`, `workspace/` all sit under
`.forgejo-society/`
([`00-overview.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/00-overview.md),
[`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md)).
Memory is structurally adjacent to cognition, not in a separate
database.

### 2 — One-hop link from K-line to agencies

A K-line's `activation_snapshot.active_agencies` is a direct map
from K-line id to agency ids
([`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md)).
Reactivation is one indirection: K-line → agency. Figure 8-10's
"one short hop" is honoured at this granularity.

### 3 — Same-run pairing

`klines.ts` loads K-lines in the same workflow run that fires the
agencies. Memory is consulted in the same process as cognition, not
across a boundary.

### 4 — Directional write rules

The two write trees (`state/`, `memory/`) with one-way promotion
([`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md):
"`state/` and `workspace/` may feed `memory/`; `memory/` may seed the
workspace at activation; nothing demotes") gives a coarse "signals
flow forward; memory writes flow back at settlement" shape.

## What the implementation does not yet take into account

### A — The K-society is not laid out as a layer

K-lines are records under `memory/klines/`, not agents in a parallel
society with the same family structure as `agencies/`. There is no
`klines/perception/`, `klines/code/`, … matching the agency families
one-for-one.

### B — No enforced topology mirror

The plan does not require that every agency have a corresponding
K-slot, or that the K-layer's link graph mirror the S-layer's signal
graph. Figure 8-10's claim that "the connections in the K-society
are similar to those in the S-society" has no schema enforcement.

### C — Bidirectional flow is implicit, not represented

Signals in the plan flow forward through workflow phases. K-lines
only contribute at `activate` and are written at `remember`. The
*opposite direction* of signal flow that Figure 8-10 names is a
property of the pipeline phases, not a property of two paired layers.
No record says "this signal travelled in the K-direction."

### D — K-lines lack agencies' machinery

K-lines have no `activates_on`, no `inhibits:`, no tools, no
authority, no budget. Even if they were stored next to agencies,
they could not be treated as peer agents. The K-society of the
figure is a society of *agents*; the plan's K-layer is a corpus of
records.

### E — Recursive layering has no analogue

The plan has one K-line layer. It does not contemplate
`klines/of-klines/` — a K-society whose units are themselves
K-lines, paired with the first K-layer. Figure 8-10's open-ended
stack is the part of the figure that goes furthest beyond the
current schema.

### F — "Adjacent, not retrieved" is partial

The folder co-location supports the figure's claim, but the loader
behaviour does not: `klines.ts` performs a retrieval (cue similarity
search) rather than a *walk* from agency to neighbouring K-line.
The architectural metaphor is adjacency; the operational metaphor
is still lookup.

### G — Per-agency K-neighbourhood

Figure 8-10 makes every K-line reachable from its paired S-agent in
one hop. The plan has the reverse direction (K-line lists its
agencies) but not the forward direction (agency exposes its
neighbouring K-lines as a property of itself). An agency cannot ask
"what K-lines am I adjacent to?" without scanning all of memory.

## Implication for the plan (no changes proposed here)

Figure 8-10 is the architectural figure of the chapter: it claims
that the right way to grow memory is to grow a *layer* alongside
the mind, not a store behind it. The plan honours the adjacency
intuition through folder layout but not through the structural
properties — parallel topology, opposite signal direction,
recursive layering — that the figure makes load-bearing. Closing
the gaps would touch the manifest schema in
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md),
the K-line schema and loader in
[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md),
the memory layout in
[`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md),
and the identity scopes in `THE-SOCIETY-OF-REPO/02-protocols/01-identity.md`,
and so falls under the "stop and ask" rules in
[`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md) §12 and
[`CLAUDE.md`](../../../../../CLAUDE.md).
