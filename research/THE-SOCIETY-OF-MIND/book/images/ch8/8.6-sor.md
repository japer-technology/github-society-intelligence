# Figure 8-6 — Implementation analysis (SOR / FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION)

**Source figure:** [8-6.md](8-6.md) — *Reusing Tower Builder for House Builder.*
**Analysis target:** [`FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/)
(plan-of-record for `.forgejo-society/` and
`.forgejo/workflows/forgejo-society.yaml`).

This file inventories the ideas carried by Figure 8-6 and checks each
one against the implementation plan.

---

## The ideas Figure 8-6 actually carries

1. **Skills are stratified into top, middle, and bottom level-bands.**
2. **Top-level agents are too task-specific to transfer.** Tower
   plans do not become house plans.
3. **Bottom-level agents are too apparatus-specific to transfer.**
   Hand-grippers do not become construction tools.
4. **The middle band transfers almost intact.** Selection, placement,
   checking are domain-general inside a domain-family.
5. **Transfer is by *level*, not by surface similarity.** The cue for
   reuse is structural depth, not topical resemblance.
6. **A K-line that captures a reusable skill should attach at the
   middle band.** Attaching higher binds it to the old task;
   attaching lower binds it to the old apparatus.
7. **New skills inherit by linking the same middle band into a new
   top and bottom.** Composition replaces re-implementation.
8. **The level-band itself is what evolution selects for.** Middle
   bands that transfer well become the durable units of skill.

## What the implementation already absorbs

### 1 — Coarse stratification through families

Agency families separate concerns: `assembly/` compresses, `code/`
manipulates, `safety/` constrains, `integration/` presents
([`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md)).
This is not Figure 8-6's level-band model, but it is the same kind
of move — separating concerns by depth of commitment.

### 2 — Explicit middle-band compressors

`agency.assembly.summary-tier-1` and `summary-tier-2`, together with
the hierarchy-and-summaries protocol mapped in
[`11-mapping-sor-to-implementation.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/11-mapping-sor-to-implementation.md),
implement summary tiers — a middle band between raw signals and
settled brief.

### 3 — Reusable context in K-lines

K-line `useful_context.{files_read, commands_run, prior_settlements,
prior_klines}` ([`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md))
records material that can survive change of stimulus. This is the
mechanism by which mid-task knowledge can travel to a new task.

### 4 — Frame/agency separation parallels top/bottom

Frames carry top-level expectations; agencies carry bottom-level
operations. The plan already keeps these apart, which makes a
middle-band claim *possible* even though not yet *expressible*.

## What the implementation does not yet take into account

### A — No level-band annotation on agencies

No manifest declares "I am middle-band." Family is a *kind* division;
level-band is a *depth* division. The plan has the first, not the
second. A reviewer cannot ask "which agencies are reusable across
frames?" and get a structural answer.

### B — K-lines do not record which level-band they attached to

`activation_snapshot.active_agencies` is a flat map of agent ids and
weights. It is suitable for replay, unsuitable for selecting only
the transferable middle. There is no field saying "the durable part
of this K-line is *this* subset."

### C — K-line `restore_when` matches top-level cues

`restore_when` is built from terms, labels, paths, and symbols —
typically surface features of a stimulus. Figure 8-6's matching
criterion ("the middle band of the past skill applies here") would
require matching by *agency overlap* in the middle band, which is
not how `klines.ts` selects K-lines today.

### D — No grafting primitive

The figure's House Builder *grafts* Tower Builder's middle band onto
a new top and bottom. The plan has no operation that says "load the
middle of this K-line, drop its top and bottom, attach to the
current frame's top and to a different bottom catalogue." K-lines
are loaded whole.

### E — Cross-frame K-line reuse is not encouraged

K-lines are stored under `memory/klines/<class>/` where `class`
tends to track the frame that produced them (`code-change`,
`security`, `question`, `self-modification`). A House Builder–style
reuse of a Tower Builder K-line is operationally possible but not
encouraged: nothing in the schema invites it.

### F — Evolution does not select for middle-band durability

Credit assignment updates per-K-line `reuse_count` and `decay_score`
([`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md)).
It does not separately track *which part* of the K-line survived
reuse. The middle band cannot become the unit that evolution
reinforces.

## Implication for the plan (no changes proposed here)

Figure 8-6 is the figure that earns the word *skill* — a unit
whose middle survives change of purpose. The plan honours the
*existence* of a middle band (summary tiers, reusable context) but
not the *level-band as primary classifier* on which the figure's
transfer story depends. Closing the gaps would touch the manifest
schema in
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md),
the K-line schema in
[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md),
and the credit-assignment hooks in
[`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md),
and so falls under the "stop and ask" rules in
[`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md) §12 and
[`CLAUDE.md`](../../../../../CLAUDE.md).
