# Figure 8-4 — Implementation analysis (SOR / FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION)

**Source figure:** [8-4.md](8-4.md) — *Partial states across mental divisions.*
**Analysis target:** [`FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/)
(plan-of-record for `.forgejo-society/` and
`.forgejo/workflows/forgejo-society.yaml`).

This file inventories the ideas carried by Figure 8-4 and checks each
one against the implementation plan.

---

## The ideas Figure 8-4 actually carries

1. **The mind is always in many partial states at once.** There is
   no single total state.
2. **Each division holds its state independently.** SHAPE's state
   does not pass through SUBSTANCE on its way to COLOR.
3. **Divisions are pre-existing scaffolding.** SHAPE, COLOR, SIZE,
   SUBSTANCE are named slots that exist before any specific content.
4. **One referent requires simultaneous activations across unrelated
   divisions.** A small white rubber ball is four states held at once.
5. **Divisions are addressable individually.** One can query SHAPE
   without disturbing COLOR.
6. **Partial states jointly represent a referent without a central
   integrator.** Coherence comes from co-activation, not from a
   summary node.
7. **Cross-divisional contradiction is normal.** Holding both ROUND
   and FIST simultaneously is not a malfunction; it is the *content*
   ("the ball, gripped").

## What the implementation already absorbs

### 1 — Agency families as divisions of the mind

[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md)
defines eight families (perception, memory, code, safety, identity,
integration, assembly, meta-admin). Each is a standing division with
its own catalogue and authority defaults. The names exist before any
stimulus fills them with content.

### 2 — Many partial activations coexist per stimulus

The settlement schema's `activated[]` records per-agency final
weights ([`09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md)).
A run carries dozens of partial activations simultaneously.

### 3 — Layered blackboard, not single state

[`possibility-2.md`'s *blackboard should become layered*](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-INTRODUCTION/THE-REPO-IS-THE-MIND/possibility-2.md)
is named explicitly in
[`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md):
`blackboard.md` is the layered face of state. The single-total-state
fiction is rejected at the design level.

### 4 — No central integrator

The conscious-presenter is the *output* gate, not a state integrator:
it composes a single visible response but does not collapse the
underlying state. Per-family signals remain readable in
`signals.jsonl` and the settlement.

## What the implementation does not yet take into account

### A — No first-class *division* schema

Families are folders + an enum on each manifest. No manifest declares
"I hold the SHAPE state" or "I am the carrier of the colour
division." A division in the figure is the *slot itself* — a
persistent named container — and the plan represents the slot only
through the family that fills it.

### B — Partial states are not separately addressable

There is no API like *get the current partial state of the safety
division* across runs. Per-run state is fused into `workspace.md`,
`blackboard.md`, and per-issue `signals.jsonl`. To query "what does
the perception division currently believe about stimulus X" one must
read the per-stimulus logs.

### C — Standing divisions across stimuli

Figure 8-4's divisions persist across thoughts; the same SHAPE slot
holds different content at different moments. The plan's state is
*per-stimulus* and append-only. There is no standing division that
carries content between runs and is overwritten by the next thought.
(Memory subtrees persist, but they are not divisions in the figure's
sense — they are not the *current partial state* of anything.)

### D — Frame slots are per-frame, not standing divisions

Frame `slots:` come closest to Figure 8-4's divisions — each slot is
a partial state of the situation. But slots are *frame-local*: the
`code-change` frame has `user_goal`, `relevant_files`, etc., while
the `question` frame has different slots. There is no stimulus-
independent SHAPE/COLOR/SIZE/SUBSTANCE scaffolding the mind always
holds.

### E — Cross-divisional contradiction is treated as objection

Holding ROUND-and-FIST simultaneously is, in the plan, more likely
to surface as a critic objection ("contradiction-finder") than as
ordinary partial content. The figure treats such combinations as the
normal mental shape of representing an object being acted on; the
plan tends toward a settled, contradiction-free view.

### F — No "joint reference without integrator"

The figure's deep claim is that a *referent* (the ball) is not
stored anywhere — it exists only as the joint partial states of the
divisions. The plan has settlement, `final.md`, and the conscious-
presenter's visible response — all of which *do* function as
integrators. The "referent is the co-activation" architecture is not
operationally available.

## Implication for the plan (no changes proposed here)

Figure 8-4 separates *being in a state* from *having a single state*.
The plan already carries many partial activations, but it lacks the
figure's standing-division scaffolding and treats cross-divisional
contradiction as something to settle rather than something to *hold*.
Closing the gaps would touch the manifest schema and family model in
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md),
the state layout in
[`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md),
and the frame schema in
[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md),
and so falls under the "stop and ask" rules in
[`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md) §12 and
[`CLAUDE.md`](../../../../../CLAUDE.md).
