# Figure 26-5 — Implementation analysis (SOR / FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION)

**Source figure:** [26-5.md](26-5.md) — *Epigraph from Roger Schank.*
**Analysis target:** [`FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/)
(plan-of-record for `.forgejo-society/` and
`.forgejo/workflows/forgejo-society.yaml`).

This file inventories the ideas carried by Figure 26-5 and checks each
one against the implementation plan. Figure 26-5 is an epigraph rather
than a diagram, so the "ideas it carries" are the operating principles
its single quotation states.

---

## The ideas Figure 26-5 actually carries

Pulled from Schank's quoted text and the heading it sits beneath
(§26.3 *sentence-frames*):

1. **Speakers omit the predictable.** What is "assumed or
   unessential" is left out of the surface form.
2. **Brevity is a constraint on the input, not on the meaning.** The
   meaning is larger than the sentence; the sentence is the
   compressed form.
3. **A *conceptual processor* searches for missing pieces.** There is
   a named cognitive role whose job is to fill omitted slots from
   context.
4. **The search uses a sentence *and a larger unit of discourse*.**
   Slot-filling is not local to one input; it reaches into the
   surrounding thread.
5. **A slot is what is being searched *for*.** "The needed slot" is
   the unit that organises the search. The frame defines the slot;
   the processor finds the filler.
6. **Type drives the search.** The processor looks for *a given type
   of information*, not for arbitrary text. The slot's type
   constrains what counts as a valid fill.
7. **Inference is normal, not exceptional.** Filling omitted slots is
   how comprehension works, not a special recovery mode used when
   parsing fails.

These seven items are what the rest of this file tests against the
implementation plan.

## What the implementation already absorbs

### 1 — A "conceptual processor" role exists by another name

The phase chain in [`03-runtime-pipeline.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/03-runtime-pipeline.md)
(`perceive → activate → deliberate → criticize → censor → settle`)
plays the role Schank assigns to the conceptual processor. `perceive`
collects what the input states; `activate` and `deliberate` are what
fill in what it does not. The first-ship agencies in
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md)
(`patch-imaginer`, `diff-skeptic`, `kline-retriever`) are
slot-fillers in this sense.

### 2 — Slots organise the search

The `slots:` map in
[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md)
is the structural fact that makes Schank's claim implementable. The
`code-change` frame's required `relevant_files`, `proposed_patch`,
`tests`, `risks`, and `final_user_response` are the explicit list of
"needed slots" the processor must fill, even when the issue body
mentions none of them.

### 3 — Frames carry defaults

`default_actions`, `default_critics`, `default_censors` are the
plan's form of *assumed* information attached to a frame, mirroring
Schank's claim that the assumed parts are precisely what speakers
leave out.

### 4 — Activation listens beyond the literal text

Polynemes (label, path, phrase) in
[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md)
fire on signals that are not the user's sentence — labels added by
the triage workflow, paths touched by a PR, phrases used elsewhere
in the thread. This is one operational form of Schank's "larger unit
of discourse."

## What the implementation does not yet take into account

These are the parts of Schank's principle that the plan currently
leaves implicit, partial, or simply absent.

### A — No explicit "search for filler" phase

The pipeline activates agencies and lets them act, but it has no
phase whose contract is "given an unfilled slot, find the filler."
A slot left empty by `perceive` becomes a `failure_condition` at
`settle` rather than the input to a directed search. Schank's
processor *seeks*; the plan's runtime *waits*.

### B — Slot type as a search key

Gap D from [26.2-sor.md](26.2-sor.md) recurs here. The plan's slots
declare `filled_by: [<agent-id>]` but not `type: <type-id>`.
Schank's principle relies on *type-directed* search ("a given type
of information"); the plan can only route by *named agency*. A new
agency cannot say "I fill slots of type *file-path*" generically.

### C — Discourse-scope retrieval

Polynemes match within the current stimulus (issue body, PR diff).
There is no first-class mechanism to scan the *thread* — earlier
issue comments, prior PR reviews, linked issues — for fillers of a
currently empty slot. Schank's *larger unit of discourse* maps onto
issue threads and cross-issue links that the plan does not yet
traverse. [`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md)
describes memory, but memory is *prior runs*, not *the current
discourse around this stimulus*.

### D — Brevity is not modelled as compression

The plan treats the stimulus as a complete description. Schank treats
the stimulus as a *compressed* description, with the processor's job
being to *expand* it. There is no field — `assumed_slots:`,
`omittable:` — that marks which slots a frame expects to find
omitted from the input and so always to fill from context.

### E — Inference-from-absence is not a normal mode

[`07-policies-and-safety.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/07-policies-and-safety.md)
treats unstated information as a *risk*, to be flagged and surfaced
to a `human`-authority reviewer. Schank treats it as *the ordinary
case*. The asymmetry is correct for safety-sensitive paths, but it
means low-risk slots (e.g. *which open issue does this comment
refer to?*) also fall through to the human, where Schank's principle
would have the processor resolve them silently.

## Implication for the plan (no changes proposed here)

Figure 26-5 is the epigraph that states the entire operating
principle of frame-based comprehension: speakers compress, listeners
search, slots organise the search. The implementation plan honours
*frames* and *defaults* and partial-fill tolerance, and leaves the
*search* itself — directed, type-keyed, discourse-scoped — implicit.

The gaps above are recorded here as analysis, not as a change
request. Any move to close them — a `seek-fillers` phase, a slot
type system, thread-scope retrieval, or `assumed_slots:` on the
frame schema — would touch
[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md),
[`03-runtime-pipeline.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/03-runtime-pipeline.md),
[`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md),
and the protocol material in `THE-SOCIETY-OF-REPO/02-protocols/`,
and so falls under the "stop and ask" rules in
[`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md) §12 and
[`CLAUDE.md`](../../../../../CLAUDE.md).
