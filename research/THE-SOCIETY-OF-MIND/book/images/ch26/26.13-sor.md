# Figure 26-13 — Implementation analysis (SOR / FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION)

**Source figure:** [26-13.md](26-13.md) — *A Trans-frame nested inside
a Trans-frame.*
**Analysis target:** [`FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/)
(plan-of-record for `.forgejo-society/` and
`.forgejo/workflows/forgejo-society.yaml`).

This file inventories the ideas carried by Figure 26-13 and checks each
one against the implementation plan. The aim is to mark, plainly, what
the plan already absorbs and what it does not yet name.

---

## The ideas Figure 26-13 actually carries

Pulled from the figure and Minsky's surrounding text in §26.8
*frames for verbs*:

1. **A slot can be filled by another frame.** The outer Trans-frame's
   *Actor* terminal is filled by an inner Trans-frame, not by a noun.
2. **Nesting is unbounded in principle.** The same trick that
   embeds *took the moon* under *moved it to Paris* can recurse to
   arbitrary depth.
3. **The same frame kind appears at every depth.** Both boxes are
   labelled *TRANS*. A small inventory of frame kinds covers
   arbitrarily long sentences by composition, not by enumeration.
4. **Cross-frame references resolve to outer frame slots.** *It* in
   the outer frame points to *the moon* in the inner frame; *who*
   in the inner frame points back to *the thief* in the outer.
5. **A pronoun marks a *frame boundary*, not just a referent.**
   *Who* signals "begin a new sub-frame here"; *it* signals "look
   up an already-filled slot."
6. **Nesting changes the order of comprehension.** Listeners must
   open the inner frame mid-way through the outer, fill it, then
   return to the outer. The act of comprehension is itself a stack
   discipline.
7. **The diagram itself uses nesting to *show* nesting.** The inner
   box sits under the outer's *Actor* terminal, making position in
   the diagram the operational meaning of "fills."

These seven items are what the rest of this file tests against the
implementation plan.

## What the implementation already absorbs

### 1 — A small inventory of frame kinds

[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md)
ships seven frame kinds (`question`, `bug`, `feature`,
`code-change`, `security-sensitive`, `self-modification`, `novel`).
This matches the figure's claim that a small fixed inventory
suffices, with composition doing the rest.

### 2 — Filler is named, not free-form

The `slots:` map's `filled_by: [<agent-id>]` ties slot fills to
named units. Figure 26-13's claim that the Actor *is itself a
Trans-frame* is not directly supported (see gaps), but the
underlying convention "a slot is filled by a named, schema-bound
unit" is shared.

### 3 — Cross-unit reference resolution exists at the signal level

[`09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md)
defines signals that carry stable ids and let downstream agencies
refer back to upstream outputs. This is the closest operational
match to *who* and *it* binding across the two boxes — but it
operates on signals, not on slots-of-frames.

## What the implementation does not yet take into account

These are the parts of Figure 26-13 that the plan currently leaves
implicit, partial, or simply absent. None are urgent; all are real.

### A — A slot cannot be filled by another frame

The frame schema in
[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md)
declares `filled_by: [<agent-id>]`. There is no
`filled_by_frame: [<frame-id>]` or `filled_by_kind: frame`. The
entire move that Figure 26-13 turns on — *Actor = Trans-frame* — is
not representable. Recursive composition of frames is structurally
foreclosed by the schema.

### B — No frame stack at runtime

The runtime in [`03-runtime-pipeline.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/03-runtime-pipeline.md)
selects *one* frame per stimulus. There is no stack discipline that
lets *handling a code-change frame* pause to open a
*security-sensitive* sub-frame, fill it, and resume. Frames are
flat; mid-handling escalation is done by switching frames at the
phase boundary, not by opening a nested one.

### C — Co-reference across nesting levels

The figure's *who → thief* and *it → moon* are cross-level
co-references. The plan has no co-reference layer at all
(see gap B in [26.3-sor.md](26.3-sor.md)); the cross-level case is
strictly out of reach until the single-level case is in.

### D — Pronoun-as-boundary marker

Figure 26-13 treats *who* as a signal to *open* a frame. The plan
opens frames by polyneme matching against signals, labels, paths,
and phrases — never by an explicit "begin sub-frame here" marker.
A user comment that says *the PR that closed #42, and which broke
deploy* would not be parsed as a nested structure; the plan would
fire whichever polyneme matched most strongly and proceed flatly.

### E — One frame kind across depth

The figure makes a strong claim: the *same* TRANS frame appears at
every level. The plan's frames are not designed to be self-nesting
in this sense; the `code-change` frame, for example, has slots
typed for whole patches, not for "another `code-change` frame
filling our `proposed_patch` slot."

### F — Bounded recursion / cycle protection

Even if A and B were addressed, the plan has no
`max_frame_depth:` field on a policy or budget. Self-referential
nesting (a frame whose slot can be filled by an instance of the
same frame) requires depth limits that
[`07-policies-and-safety.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/07-policies-and-safety.md)
does not currently provide. `budget.max_tool_calls` limits cost
breadth, not depth.

### G — Composition as the *normal* case

Figure 26-13's central point is that arbitrarily long sentences
*always* compose; nesting is the ordinary case, not the exception.
The plan treats one-frame-per-stimulus as the ordinary case. If
extended, nesting would have to become the default mode of
comprehension, not a feature flag.

## Implication for the plan (no changes proposed here)

Figure 26-13 is the figure that turns *frames* into a generative
system: a small set of kinds, plus the rule that a slot can be
another frame, gives unbounded coverage. The implementation plan
honours the *small inventory* half of that and does not yet honour
the *recursion* half. As a result, complex stimuli — a PR comment
that mentions another PR that closed another issue — would
currently be flattened into one frame rather than parsed as a
nested structure.

The gaps above are recorded here as analysis, not as a change
request. Any move to close them — `filled_by_frame:`, a frame-stack
at runtime, cross-level co-reference, a depth budget — would touch
the frame schema in [`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md),
the pipeline in [`03-runtime-pipeline.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/03-runtime-pipeline.md),
the policies in [`07-policies-and-safety.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/07-policies-and-safety.md),
state and memory in [`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md),
and the protocol material in `THE-SOCIETY-OF-REPO/02-protocols/`,
and so falls under the "stop and ask" rules in
[`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md) §12 and
[`CLAUDE.md`](../../../../../CLAUDE.md).
