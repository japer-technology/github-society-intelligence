# Figure 26-14 — Implementation analysis (SOR / FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION)

**Source figure:** [26-14.md](26-14.md) — *A sentence drawn as a
tabletop.*
**Analysis target:** [`FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/)
(plan-of-record for `.forgejo-society/` and
`.forgejo/workflows/forgejo-society.yaml`).

This file inventories the ideas carried by Figure 26-14 and checks each
one against the implementation plan. The aim is to mark, plainly, what
the plan already absorbs and what it does not yet name.

---

## The ideas Figure 26-14 actually carries

Pulled from the figure and Minsky's surrounding text in §26.9
*language and vision*:

1. **A whole can be perceived from separated visible parts.** The
   tabletop is read as one object even though the leg-tops are
   hidden.
2. **A verb phrase can be split by a noun phrase and remain one
   unit.** *Took … out for a walk* survives the insertion of *the
   big shaggy dog* between its halves.
3. **The same binding machinery serves vision and language.**
   Figure 26-14's whole purpose is to draw the analogy as one
   image.
4. **Modality is not the basis of structure.** Whatever recognises
   "this is still one verb phrase" is the same kind of thing as
   what recognises "this is still one tabletop."
5. **Occlusion is the rule, not the exception.** Real inputs in
   either modality are routinely interrupted, partial, or
   reordered; comprehension must work *despite* gaps, not *given*
   their absence.
6. **The bound whole is what the next stage receives.** The output
   of binding is "one verb phrase" or "one tabletop"; downstream
   processing does not see the fragments.
7. **The analogy is generative.** If language and vision share
   machinery, then frame mechanisms developed for one are
   candidates for the other; the figure is making a claim about
   transferability, not just similarity.

These seven items are what the rest of this file tests against the
implementation plan.

## What the implementation already absorbs

### 1 — Cross-surface activation already exists

Polynemes in
[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md)
fire across path-, label-, and phrase-surfaces, not just one. A
single stimulus arriving partly as a file path and partly as an
issue body activates a single frame. This is the same family of
move as "vision and language bind separated parts" — *within* the
modalities the plan currently spans.

### 2 — Settlement collapses fragments into one decision

The `settle` phase in
[`03-runtime-pipeline.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/03-runtime-pipeline.md)
collapses many partial signals into one settlement-id; downstream
phases (`act`, `remember`, `report`) see the settled whole, not the
fragments. This matches Figure 26-14's item 6: binding produces the
unit that the next stage receives.

### 3 — Memory is shared across stimulus kinds

[`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md)
stores memory as Git objects regardless of which stimulus kind
produced them. A K-line learned from a code-change run can be
retrieved by `kline-retriever` during a question run. The
*substrate* is modality-neutral, mirroring Figure 26-14's claim
that the machinery itself does not depend on the surface.

## What the implementation does not yet take into account

These are the parts of Figure 26-14 that the plan currently leaves
implicit, partial, or simply absent. None are urgent; all are real.

### A — Only one "modality" is implemented

The plan's input surface is the forge: issues, PRs, comments,
labels, paths, diffs. There is no image, audio, or external sensor
surface. Figure 26-14's vision/language analogy assumes two
modalities to bridge; the plan currently has one. The
*transferability* claim (item 7) has no second target to transfer
*to*.

### B — No "binding across an occlusion" operator

Figure 26-14's tabletop reads as one object *because the visible
parts are bound across the occluded middle*. The plan has nothing
that explicitly binds two visible signals across a missing one. If
an issue is split into two comments with an unrelated comment
between them, the plan treats them as three signals, not as one
bound thread plus an interruption.

### C — Verb-phrase splitting / discontinuous constituents

The text equivalent of Figure 26-14 — *took … out for a walk*
interrupted by *the big shaggy dog* — has no analogue in the plan's
parsing. Polynemes match contiguous patterns. There is no
mechanism for "this phrase is the head and that phrase is the
tail; bind them across whatever is between."

### D — Shared machinery across surfaces

Even within the surfaces the plan supports, polyneme kinds are
*separate* registries (`path-polynemes.yml`,
`label-polynemes.yml`, `phrase-polynemes.yml`). There is no shared
binding operator that all three subscribe to. Figure 26-14's
"vision and language share machinery" is, even at the
within-forge scale, only weakly realised.

### E — Occlusion as a normal mode

[`07-policies-and-safety.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/07-policies-and-safety.md)
treats missing or inaccessible content as a *risk* — a reason to
stop and escalate to a `human`-authority reviewer. Figure 26-14's
item 5 is the opposite: occlusion is the normal case, and
comprehension must work in spite of it. The asymmetry is correct
for safety surfaces (workflows, secrets, governance) and
overcorrected for ordinary discourse (a comment that references an
old issue the agency cannot currently read).

### F — Multi-modal frames

If item 3 were to be honoured, a single frame would need slots that
accept fillers from different surface kinds — *visible-region* +
*sentence-fragment*. The plan's frames have `filled_by:
[<agent-id>]` only. There is no slot-level type that says "this
slot accepts fillers from any modality." Multi-modal binding is
not representable.

### G — Generativity of the analogy

Figure 26-14's most ambitious claim is that the analogy itself is
generative: a frame mechanism designed for issues should be a
candidate for use on, say, diff hunks treated as visual layouts, or
review threads treated as scenes. The plan's frame catalogue is
flat per surface; there is no register that says "this frame
template was originally written for surface X and is also usable
on surface Y." Cross-surface reuse of frame designs is a manual
activity, not a schema-supported one.

## Implication for the plan (no changes proposed here)

Figure 26-14 is the figure that pushes *frame* from a linguistic
notion to a general binding mechanism, and asks whether the same
machinery serves more than one surface. The implementation plan
currently runs on one surface (the forge), binds fragments only
within a single stimulus, and treats absence as risk rather than as
the normal condition of comprehension.

The gaps above are recorded here as analysis, not as a change
request. Any move to close them — a binding operator that spans
occluded signals, a discontinuous-constituent polyneme kind, a
shared cross-surface registry, multi-modal slot types, or even an
additional input surface — would touch
[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md),
[`03-runtime-pipeline.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/03-runtime-pipeline.md),
[`07-policies-and-safety.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/07-policies-and-safety.md),
[`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md),
[`09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md),
and the protocol material in `THE-SOCIETY-OF-REPO/02-protocols/`,
and so falls under the "stop and ask" rules in
[`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md) §12 and
[`CLAUDE.md`](../../../../../CLAUDE.md).
