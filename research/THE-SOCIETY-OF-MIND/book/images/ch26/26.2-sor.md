# Figure 26-2 — Implementation analysis (SOR / FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION)

**Source figure:** [26-2.md](26-2.md) — *The present subframe.*
**Analysis target:** [`FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/)
(plan-of-record for `.forgejo-society/` and
`.forgejo/workflows/forgejo-society.yaml`).

This file inventories the ideas carried by Figure 26-2 and checks each
one against the implementation plan. The aim is to mark, plainly, what
the plan already absorbs and what it does not yet name.

---

## The ideas Figure 26-2 actually carries

Pulled from the figure and Minsky's surrounding text in §26.2
*understanding stories*:

1. **A subframe is just two things: a list of terminals and a list of
   constraints.** Nothing else is required to make a frame
   functionally useful.
2. **Terminals are typed slots, not free variables.** *Host* and
   *Gift* are role names, with role-shaped expectations attached.
3. **Constraints bind terminals to each other.** *Host likes Gift* is
   not commentary about the terminals — it is a relation that the
   frame *uses* to drive inference.
4. **A constraint is what allows inference from absence.** The reader
   concludes the kite is for Jack precisely because no sentence said
   so; the constraint fills the gap that the words leave.
5. **Frames are small.** A useful frame can have two terminals and
   one constraint. Coverage comes from many small frames, not one
   large one.
6. **Defaults live inside the frame, not in the input.** The "likes"
   relation is supplied by the frame itself; the story does not
   need to assert it.
7. **A subframe is composable.** *Present* is named *sub*frame because
   it is meant to slot into a larger frame (party, gift-giving) —
   identity and boundedness are what make composition possible.

These seven items are what the rest of this file tests against the
implementation plan.

## What the implementation already absorbs

### 1 — Frames as first-class units with named slots

[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md)
defines a frame schema with a `slots:` map and per-slot `required:`
and `filled_by:` fields. *Host* and *Gift* in Figure 26-2 map
directly onto entries of this `slots:` map. The first-ship catalogue
(`question.frame.yml`, `bug.frame.yml`, `code-change.frame.yml`,
`security-sensitive.frame.yml`, `self-modification.frame.yml`,
`novel.frame.yml`) already follows the figure's "small frame, named
terminals" shape.

### 2 — Defaults live in the frame

`default_actions`, `default_critics`, and `default_censors` in the
frame schema are exactly Minsky's *defaults attached to the frame
itself*. A frame can therefore carry information the stimulus does
not state, just as the *present* frame supplies the *likes* relation.

### 3 — Many small frames over one large one

[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md)
ships seven separately named frames rather than a single universal
frame, matching Figure 26-2's economy.

### 4 — A frame is what supplies the missing word

The `code-change` frame requires `relevant_files`, `proposed_patch`,
`tests`, `risks`, and `final_user_response` even when the user's
sentence asks only for "a fix." This is the same move the *present*
frame makes when it concludes the kite is for Jack: the slot is
known to exist whether or not the input mentions it.

## What the implementation does not yet take into account

These are the parts of Figure 26-2 that the plan currently leaves
implicit, partial, or simply absent. None are urgent; all are real.

### A — No `constraints:` field on a frame

The frame schema names slots and what fills them, but has no field
for *relations between slots*. Figure 26-2's whole point is the
single row labelled *Constraint: Host likes Gift*. The plan has no
analogue. A frame cannot declare:

```yaml
constraints:
  - relation: likes
    between: [host, gift]
```

`failure_conditions:` is the nearest field, but it lists *named
objections* used to block settlement, not *defaults that derive
unstated slot values*.

### B — Inference from absence is not a runtime operation

`failure_conditions` and `default_actions` decide what to *do* when
the frame is selected. Neither *derives a slot value* from another
filled slot. Figure 26-2's reader infers *the kite is for Jack* by
reading the *likes* edge backwards from *Gift* to *Host*. The
runtime in [`03-runtime-pipeline.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/03-runtime-pipeline.md)
has no constraint-propagation phase between `perceive` and
`deliberate`.

### C — Subframe vs frame is not a distinction in the schema

The figure carefully labels its example a *subframe*: a unit meant
to be embedded inside a larger one (party, gift-giving). The plan
treats every frame as top-level; selection is "which frame fits the
stimulus", never "which subframe fits the slot of another frame."
There is no `embedded_in:` field, and the frame catalogue is flat.

### D — Typed terminals

*Host* expects a person; *Gift* expects a thing. Neither is a free
string. The plan's `slots:` map carries `filled_by: [<agent-id>]`,
which names *who fills the slot*, not *what type the slot expects*.
A slot cannot currently declare `type: person`, `type: file-path`,
or `type: settlement-id`. Type checking of slot fillers is left to
the agency that fills them.

### E — Constraints as the joinable surface between frames

Because Figure 26-2's constraint is *named* (*likes*), two
different frames that both mention *likes* could in principle share
inferences across it. The plan's frames have no shared vocabulary
of relations; co-ordination between frames goes through
[`09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md)
signals, which are events, not relations. There is no
cross-frame *relation registry*.

## Implication for the plan (no changes proposed here)

Figure 26-2 is the figure that earns the word *frame* by reducing it
to its minimum: two terminals and one constraint. The implementation
plan honours the *terminals* half of that definition cleanly, and
leaves the *constraints* half unrepresented.

The gaps above are recorded here as analysis, not as a change
request. Any move to close them — a `constraints:` field on the
frame schema, a constraint-propagation phase in the runtime, an
`embedded_in:` field for subframes, or a typed-slot extension —
would touch the frame schema in [`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md),
the runtime pipeline in [`03-runtime-pipeline.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/03-runtime-pipeline.md),
and the protocol material in `THE-SOCIETY-OF-REPO/02-protocols/`,
and so falls under the "stop and ask" rules in
[`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md) §12 and
[`CLAUDE.md`](../../../../../CLAUDE.md).
