# Figure 19-3 — Implementation analysis (SOR / FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION)

**Source figure:** [19-3.md](19-3.md) — *K-line memorizers next to each
agency.*
**Analysis target:** [`FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/)
(plan-of-record for `.forgejo-society/` and
`.forgejo/workflows/forgejo-society.yaml`).

The figure refines 19-2 by placing the polyneme→state mapping in a
*bank of K-lines beside each listening agency*. This analysis asks
where, in the plan, those banks live.

---

## The ideas Figure 19-3 actually carries

1. **Each downstream agency has its own memorizer bank.** Not one
   global K-line store; many small local ones.
2. **K-lines mediate polyneme reception.** The polyneme does not
   reach the agency directly — it lands on a K-line first.
3. **A K-line restores a *partial* internal state.** It re-arouses
   the previous tangle inside the agency rather than computing fresh.
4. **K-lines are learned per polyneme.** One symbol per K-line per
   listening agency.
5. **Memory is stored close to where it is used.** The architectural
   principle that justifies the picture.
6. **Banks scale with the number of known polynemes.** If a listener
   learns ten polynemes, it acquires ten K-lines.
7. **K-lines belong to the listener, not to the polyneme.** Removing
   the *apple* polyneme would not delete every apple-state K-line
   inside every listener; the polyneme is a key, not the data.

## What the implementation already absorbs

### 1 — K-lines are a first-class memory kind

[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md)
and
[`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md)
define K-lines as stored partial states, with retrieval via
`agency.memory.kline-retriever`. They are not just "logs"; they are
*restorable* state, which matches the figure's idea of re-arousing a
prior tangle.

### 2 — K-lines are addressable by symbolic keys

The retriever can be summoned by frames, by stimulus features, and by
polynemes. A polyneme firing therefore *can* result in a relevant
K-line being restored — which is the path the figure shows.

### 3 — Restoration is partial, not total

The plan treats K-lines as restoring a subset of state, not the full
working memory. This matches the figure's depiction of an arrow
landing inside an agency and lighting up *some* of its internal
arrows.

---

## What the implementation does not yet take into account

### A — K-lines are not co-located with listening agencies

In the figure, the memorizer bank sits *beside* COLOR, *beside* SIZE,
*beside* SHAPE — one bank per listener. In the plan, K-lines live in
a shared `.forgejo-society/memory/klines/` directory and are mediated
by a single `kline-retriever` agency
([`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md)).
There is no `agencies/perception/colour/klines/` and no notion of
"this K-line belongs to that listener."

### B — No per-listener memorizer agency

Figure 19-3 makes the *memorizer* itself a small, distinct kind of
agent — one bank per listener. The plan has one global
`agency.memory.kline-retriever` and no `agency.<listener>.memorizer`
shape. The bank-per-listener pattern is not representable.

### C — Polyneme-to-K-line binding is not declared

The figure shows a fixed wiring: the *apple* line lands on a specific
K-line inside COLOR. In the plan, the polyneme excites the listener
agency, and the listener (an LLM-driven prompt) may or may not query
the retriever; there is no schema field of the form:

```yaml
polyneme: <symbol>
listener: <agency-id>
restores_kline: <kline-id>
```

The binding is implicit and run-time, not declared and inspectable.

### D — K-lines do not name their listener

K-line manifests describe content (what state to restore) but do not
declare *which agency they are a memory of*. Without that field, the
"K-lines belong to the listener" property of the figure is not
expressible.

### E — No learning step that creates a K-line per (polyneme, listener) pair

The figure presupposes a learning regime: the listener encounters
*apple* in some state, then a K-line is laid down beside it. The plan
records K-lines via the `remember` phase
([`03-runtime-pipeline.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/03-runtime-pipeline.md)),
but that step is run-level, not listener-local, and there is no rule
that says "if polyneme X fired and listener Y produced novel state,
mint a new K-line linking the two."

## Implication for the plan (no changes proposed here)

Figure 19-3 is an *architectural* claim about memory locality: the
right place to store a remembered partial state is *next to the
agency that will replay it*. The plan currently treats K-lines as
shared, centrally retrieved, polyneme-agnostic content. The
difference is real but not urgent; it shows up first when two
listeners need to remember different things about the same polyneme.

Adopting the figure's pattern would touch the K-line schema and
storage layout in
[`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md),
the agency schema in
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md),
and the polyneme schema in
[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md),
and so falls under the "stop and ask" rules in
[`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md) §12 and
[`CLAUDE.md`](../../../../../CLAUDE.md).
