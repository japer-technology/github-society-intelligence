# Figure 19-1 — Implementation analysis (SOR / FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION)

**Source figure:** [19-1.md](19-1.md) — *The three regions of the
language-agency.*
**Analysis target:** [`FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/)
(plan-of-record for `.forgejo-society/` and
`.forgejo/workflows/forgejo-society.yaml`).

This file inventories the ideas carried by Figure 19-1 and checks each
one against the implementation plan.

---

## The ideas Figure 19-1 actually carries

1. **Language is an agency, not a faculty.** Words are handled by a
   bounded society of agents, not by a single module.
2. **Three vertical regions.** An upper tier of *word-agents*
   (LISTENER on the input side, SPEAKER on the output side), a middle
   linking tier, and a lower tier of *other agencies* that words
   affect.
3. **LISTENER and SPEAKER are distinct roles.** Receiving words and
   producing them are different sub-agencies, not two modes of one
   unit.
4. **The middle tier is where words bind to memories and
   expectations.** It is not transport; it is the place where
   ascending sense and descending word meet.
5. **The lower tier is the rest of mind.** Anything the language
   agency wants to move — colour, shape, recollection — lives outside
   it and is reached through the middle tier.
6. **Self-application.** The duplicated *LANGUAGE AGENCY!* at the
   bottom shows that the language agency can address itself as if it
   were another downstream agency.
7. **Language as control over memory.** The unusual capacity of words
   is that they can reach back into the agency that produced them and
   re-arouse prior states.

## What the implementation already absorbs

### 1 — Language traffic is treated as inter-agency signal

[`09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md)
and the `signal` / `handoff` vocabulary make any message between
agencies a typed artifact rather than a free string. Words from the
user enter the workflow as a stimulus and become signals; words sent
back to the user leave via `conscious-presenter`. The two halves
LISTENER and SPEAKER are recognisable in the `perceive` and `report`
phases of the pipeline ([`03-runtime-pipeline.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/03-runtime-pipeline.md)).

### 2 — A middle linking layer exists in spirit

The frame/polyneme/k-line machinery in
[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md)
is precisely a middle tier that binds words (phrases, labels) to
expectations (frames) and to memory (K-lines). The
`phrase-polynemes.yml` and `directive.*` polynemes are the operational
form of "the word reaches in and selects a partial state."

### 3 — Self-addressing of the language layer

The `society <target>:` syntax (`directive.spock`, `directive.safety`,
`directive.memory`, `directive.critic`) lets a message addressed *to
the society* re-enter and steer the very units that handle language.
That is exactly the loop-back arrow the figure draws at the bottom.

## What the implementation does not yet take into account

### A — No first-class *language agency*

There is no `agencies/language/` family, no
`agency.language.listener`, no `agency.language.speaker`. Language
work is distributed across `perception/*`, `assembly/*`, and
`integration/conscious-presenter`. The figure's central box —
*LANGUAGE AGENCY* as one addressable unit — is not present in the
manifest layout described by
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md).

### B — LISTENER and SPEAKER are not separated

`perceive` ingests stimuli generally; `report` emits replies
generally. Neither is named *listener* or *speaker*, neither is owned
by a dedicated agency, and the schema does not currently support
declaring a unit as "input-side word agent" vs "output-side word
agent."

### C — The three vertical regions are not a schema concept

The plan has families (perception, memory, code, safety, identity,
integration, assembly, meta-admin), which is a horizontal taxonomy.
Figure 19-1's *vertical* division — words above, links in the middle,
non-language agencies below — has no counterpart in any folder, field,
or phase. An agency cannot declare `tier: word | link | other`.

### D — Self-reference is bounded by directives, not by structure

`directive.*` polynemes give a controlled re-entry, but only along
the four named channels (spock / safety / memory / critic). The
figure's claim is stronger: the language agency can treat *itself* as
"just another downstream agency" for any operation, not only the four
that the plan currently routes.

### E — No explicit "ascending vs descending neme" distinction

The figure splits traffic into words going *down* into the rest of
mind and effects coming *up* from the rest of mind. The polyneme
schema in
[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md)
is one-directional: polynemes excite/inhibit agencies. There is no
counterpart symbol for the upward report from an agency back into the
language layer.

## Implication for the plan (no changes proposed here)

Figure 19-1 argues that *language is a place in the architecture*,
not a capability sprinkled across it. The plan currently has the
*ingredients* of a language agency — perception, phrase polynemes,
conscious-presenter, directive channels — but does not gather them
under one addressable family with explicit listener/speaker roles,
vertical tiers, and a general self-addressing path.

Any move to close the gaps above would touch the family taxonomy and
manifest schema in
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md),
the polyneme schema in
[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md),
and the identity scopes in
`THE-SOCIETY-OF-REPO/02-protocols/01-identity.md`, and so falls under
the "stop and ask" rules in
[`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md) §12 and
[`CLAUDE.md`](../../../../../CLAUDE.md).
