# Figure 19-9 — Implementation analysis (SOR / FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION)

**Source figure:** [19-9.md](19-9.md) — *Three layers: memorizers,
intergents, recognizers.*
**Analysis target:** [`FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/)
(plan-of-record for `.forgejo-society/` and
`.forgejo/workflows/forgejo-society.yaml`).

The figure compresses the chapter's architecture into three named
layers inside *each* agency. The plan currently has analogues of two
of the three and no analogue of the middle one.

---

## The ideas Figure 19-9 actually carries

1. **Every agency has internal structure.** It is not a flat black
   box.
2. **A memorizer layer.** K-lines that can restore prior partial
   states inside this agency.
3. **A recogniser layer.** Recognisers tuned to detect that the
   agency is currently in one of those known states.
4. **An *intergent* layer in the middle.** Whatever mediates between
   memorisers and recognisers — the internal traffic of the agency.
5. **The layers are dense, not single units.** Each card-panel holds
   many small triangles; the layer is a *bank*, not one cell.
6. **Memory and recognition share an internal substrate.**
   "Restoring state" and "noticing state" are two sides of the same
   architecture.
7. **The layered shape is the same for every agency.** It is a
   schema, not a one-off design.

---

## What the implementation already absorbs

### 1 — K-lines as the memorizer layer (loosely)

K-lines in
[`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md)
play the role the figure assigns to memorisers: they restore prior
partial states. The mapping is approximate because K-lines in the
plan are central, not per-agency (see `19.3-sor.md` gap A).

### 2 — A *kind*-based vocabulary that could accept a layered schema

[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md)
already classes manifests by kind (agency / critic / censor /
frame / polyneme / K-line / procedural). Adding *recognizer* and
*intergent* as further kinds would not require inventing a new
manifest discipline, only extending an existing one.

### 3 — Internal traffic is acknowledged

The activation protocol in
[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md)
already treats `excite` / `inhibit` flows between units as the
substance of cognition, not as plumbing. That is the right
substrate for an "intergent" layer to be expressed on.

---

## What the implementation does not yet take into account

### A — No three-layer schema per agency

There is no field on a member manifest that says

```yaml
memorizers: [ ... ]
intergents: [ ... ]
recognizers: [ ... ]
```

or anything equivalent. An agency manifest is currently *one* unit
with prompt and metadata, not a small society with three named
layers.

### B — *Recognizer* is not a kind

Already recorded as `19.4-sor.md` gap A. Without recognisers as a
first-class kind, the third layer of the figure cannot be populated.

### C — *Intergent* is not a kind and has no analogue

The plan has nothing called *intergent*. Internal mediators between
"what we remember" and "what we recognise" are not separated out;
they live inside whatever LLM prompt is running.

### D — Density (banks, not singletons)

The figure draws each layer as a dense field. The plan does not yet
support "this layer is a bank of N similar units." Member manifests
are individually written and individually named; there is no
`bank:` construct that scales recognisers or memorisers up to many
at once.

### E — Reading-states-out vs describing-states

Figure 19-9 distinguishes *restoring* state (memorisers) from
*describing* state (recognisers). The plan blurs the two: a K-line
restoration and a critic verdict can both produce information, but
the distinction "this layer changes the agency, that layer reports
on the agency" is not enforced anywhere.

### F — Uniform shape across agencies

The figure's strongest claim is that *every* agency has these three
layers. The plan is heterogeneous: a `patch-imaginer` agency, a
`scope` critic, and an `agency.memory.kline-retriever` look nothing
like each other. There is no "this shape applies to all" rule.

## Implication for the plan (no changes proposed here)

Figure 19-9 promotes "agency has three layers" from a description of
one example to a *schema for all agencies*. The plan does not make
this commitment. Doing so would be a significant simplification of
the explanation of mind (one shape repeated many times) but a
significant complication of the manifest schema.

Any adoption would touch the manifest discipline in
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md),
the K-line storage layout in
[`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md),
the activation / matcher schemas in
[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md),
and the identity scopes in
`THE-SOCIETY-OF-REPO/02-protocols/01-identity.md`, and so falls under
the "stop and ask" rules in
[`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md) §12 and
[`CLAUDE.md`](../../../../../CLAUDE.md).
