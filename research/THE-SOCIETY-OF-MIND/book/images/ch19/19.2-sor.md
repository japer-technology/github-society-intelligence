# Figure 19-2 — Implementation analysis (SOR / FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION)

**Source figure:** [19-2.md](19-2.md) — *Polynemes broadcasting to
many agencies.*
**Analysis target:** [`FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/)
(plan-of-record for `.forgejo-society/` and
`.forgejo/workflows/forgejo-society.yaml`).

This is the figure that gives polynemes their definition. The plan
already adopts the word; this analysis checks how much of the figure
the word actually carries.

---

## The ideas Figure 19-2 actually carries

1. **One symbol, many private effects.** *apple* is a single line
   that reaches several agencies; each agency does something
   different with it.
2. **The polyneme itself carries no description.** It does not say
   "red"; it tells the COLOR agency to select *red*. The meaning is
   in the listener.
3. **A small fixed set of downstream agencies.** Four are shown:
   COLOR, SIZE, MATERIAL, SHAPE. Polynemes broadcast to *agencies*,
   not to a global bus.
4. **Each downstream agency holds a small selector.** Inside COLOR
   the triangle points at one of {red, green, white}; inside SIZE at
   one of {large, small, medium}; and so on.
5. **The mapping is private to each agency.** No central table says
   "*apple* → red, medium, plant, ball." The mapping is reconstructed
   by independent local choices.
6. **Different polynemes use the same downstream agencies.** *apple*,
   *golfball*, and *tomato slice* all run through COLOR / SIZE /
   MATERIAL / SHAPE; the agencies are reusable.
7. **A polyneme is a way of being *understood*, not of being *sent*.**
   The figure has no "transmitter"; the three labels just sit there
   and the downstream agencies *respond*.

## What the implementation already absorbs

### 1 — Polynemes are a first-class schema element

[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md)
defines polyneme entries with `symbol`, `match`, `excite`, `inhibit`,
and `default_frame`. The first-ship catalogues (`path-polynemes.yml`,
`label-polynemes.yml`, `phrase-polynemes.yml`) are exactly the
"horizontal lines" of the figure: one symbol, several downstream
units, no central interpreter.

### 2 — Per-listener weights

`excite: { <agent-id>: <weight 0..1> }` and the matching `inhibit`
map say *how much* each downstream unit should respond. That is the
operational form of "each agency holds its own private mapping" — the
mapping is the set of weights against that listener.

### 3 — Reusable downstream agencies

Multiple polynemes can target the same agencies. `directive.spock`,
`directive.safety`, `directive.memory`, `directive.critic` all reuse
the same families. The figure's reuse pattern (*apple*, *golfball*,
*tomato slice* all hit COLOR/SIZE/…) is therefore representable.

### 4 — Symbol without payload

A polyneme `symbol` is just an id. There is no description field on
the polyneme itself — meaning lives in the `excite`/`inhibit`
listeners. That matches the figure's central insight.

## What the implementation does not yet take into account

### A — Polynemes excite *units*, not *states inside units*

In the figure, *apple* tells COLOR to select *red* — a specific
internal alternative *within* COLOR. In the plan, a polyneme can
excite or inhibit an agency, but it cannot select a *partial state*
inside that agency. There is no field of the form:

```yaml
set_state:
  agency.perception.colour:
    selector: red
```

The selector triangle in the figure has no schema counterpart.

### B — No agency-internal alternatives

For "polyneme picks one of N states" to be meaningful, the listener
agency must declare those N states. Member manifests in
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md)
have prompts, `activates_on`, `produces`, `inhibits`, `budget`, and
authority — but no enumerated `states:` or `alternatives:` block.
Without that, COLOR-as-an-agency cannot have a "red vs green vs
white" selector to address.

### C — No notion of a *describable* polyneme target group

The figure presupposes a group {COLOR, SIZE, MATERIAL, SHAPE} that is
the natural audience of object-polynemes. The plan's polyneme entries
list listeners one by one in `excite`. There is no audience group or
"sense-feature bus" that an object polyneme could address as a unit;
adding or removing a sensory family means editing every object
polyneme by hand.

### D — Polynemes are stimulus-triggered only

The first-ship polynemes match on `paths`, `labels`, or `phrases`
present in the stimulus. The figure's polynemes are emitted *by mind
itself* — *apple* is something one agency tells the rest of the
society, not something the user typed. The plan has no path by which
a running agency emits a polyneme; only stimuli do.

### E — Weights are non-negative

`excite` and `inhibit` are separate `0..1` maps. There is no signed
weight. The figure's selectors don't need negative weights, but the
absence is relevant for Figures 19-5 and 19-6 and is recorded here as
a property of the schema.

## Implication for the plan (no changes proposed here)

The plan adopts the *name* polyneme accurately as a broadcast
activator. Figure 19-2 carries a stronger claim: a polyneme also
*selects an alternative inside each listener*. That second half — an
internal state vocabulary on each listener, plus signed weights, plus
agency-to-society emission — is absent today.

Closing these gaps would touch the polyneme schema and the agency
manifest schema in
[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md)
and
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md),
and so falls under the "stop and ask" rules in
[`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md) §12 and
[`CLAUDE.md`](../../../../../CLAUDE.md).
