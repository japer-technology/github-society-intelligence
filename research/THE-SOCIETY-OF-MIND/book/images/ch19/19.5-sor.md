# Figure 19-5 — Implementation analysis (SOR / FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION)

**Source figure:** [19-5.md](19-5.md) — *Five-out-of-six chair
recogniser.*
**Analysis target:** [`FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/)
(plan-of-record for `.forgejo-society/` and
`.forgejo/workflows/forgejo-society.yaml`).

The figure replaces the strict AND of 19-4 with a *threshold count*.
This is the first sketch of an evidence-weighing recogniser.

---

## The ideas Figure 19-5 actually carries

1. **Threshold recognition.** A recogniser can fire on *some* number
   of its inputs, not necessarily all of them.
2. **A named threshold.** *5 out of 6* is the parameter; the schema
   has to carry it.
3. **Repeated input categories matter.** Four of the six inputs are
   *LEG* — repeated features count separately rather than collapsing.
4. **Tolerance for missing parts.** A chair seen from an angle that
   hides one leg still recognises as a chair.
5. **Recognition becomes *probabilistic* in a weak sense.** The unit
   no longer requires the world to match it exactly.
6. **The recogniser is still a single unit.** One box, one output —
   the threshold lives *inside* the recogniser, not in a downstream
   judge.
7. **Threshold counting still ignores relations.** Five line-segments
   in a pile are not a chair; the figure quietly sets up Figures 19-6
   and 19-7.

## What the implementation already absorbs

### 1 — Multi-source aggregation exists at the pipeline scale

The `criticize` and `censor` phases aggregate verdicts from multiple
critics and censors before `settle`
([`03-runtime-pipeline.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/03-runtime-pipeline.md)).
A settlement decision is built from many partial signals — a
coarse-grain analogue of "look at six inputs, decide one output."

### 2 — Frames tolerate partial fills in principle

Frames in
[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md)
distinguish `required: true` from `required: false` slots, so a frame
can settle with optional slots empty. That is one form of "you don't
need every input to recognise the situation."

---

## What the implementation does not yet take into account

### A — No threshold parameter on any matcher

Polyneme `match` blocks, critic conditions, and censor conditions
are evaluated as "matches or doesn't." None of them carries a
`threshold: <n>` or `min_matches: <n>` field. The figure's defining
parameter has no schema home.

### B — Repeated inputs are not first-class

In the figure, four *LEG* wires are four inputs. In the plan,
listing the same agent four times in `excite` would be either an
error or a no-op; weights are per-agent. The "four legs, each its
own evidence" pattern cannot be expressed.

### C — Recognition aggregation is not declared per recogniser

The pipeline aggregates critics globally for the whole settlement.
There is no per-recogniser, per-claim aggregation: "this recogniser,
on this set of N inputs, fires when at least M are on." Aggregation
is one of the missing parts of "recogniser" as a kind (gap A in
`19.4-sor.md`).

### D — Settle is the only place verdicts combine

`lifecycle/settle.ts` collects critic / censor outputs. It does not
expose a per-claim threshold knob. A recogniser-style "5 of 6" is
not just absent inside agencies; it is absent at the pipeline level
too.

### E — Tolerance is governed by prompts, not by structure

In practice the plan tolerates partial information because LLM
agents are loose. That is *implicit* tolerance, mediated by prose,
not *declared* tolerance with a threshold. The figure's contribution
is to make tolerance an inspectable parameter; the plan does not.

## Implication for the plan (no changes proposed here)

Figure 19-5 is small but consequential: it introduces *the threshold
parameter* as a thing the architecture must let you name. Without it,
the plan has only the two endpoints — "matches" and "doesn't" — and
nowhere to put the middle.

Adding thresholds (per polyneme, per critic, per recogniser) would
touch the matcher schemas in
[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md)
and
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md),
and the settlement aggregator in
[`03-runtime-pipeline.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/03-runtime-pipeline.md),
and so falls under the "stop and ask" rules in
[`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md) §12 and
[`CLAUDE.md`](../../../../../CLAUDE.md).
