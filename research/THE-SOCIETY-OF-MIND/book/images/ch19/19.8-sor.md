# Figure 19-8 — Implementation analysis (SOR / FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION)

**Source figure:** [19-8.md](19-8.md) — *Recognising apple from
internal states.*
**Analysis target:** [`FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/)
(plan-of-record for `.forgejo-society/` and
`.forgejo/workflows/forgejo-society.yaml`).

The figure's claim is that the same recogniser can fire from
*sensory* inputs or from *internal-state* inputs — i.e. from
thoughts. The plan does not currently make this symmetry explicit.

---

## The ideas Figure 19-8 actually carries

1. **One recogniser shared by perception and thought.** The
   apple-neme is aroused by the same final agent whether the inputs
   came from the eyes or from internal agencies set by words.
2. **Inputs are themselves outputs of other recognisers.** SHAPE /
   COLOR / SIZE and SUBSTANCE / TASTE / STRUCTURE are not raw
   sensors; they are recognisers in their own right.
3. **Two AND-banks feed one final agent.** Sensory features cluster
   into one AND, internal-state features into another, and both feed
   the same downstream conclusion.
4. **Symbols are bridges, not labels.** The *APPLE-NEME* output is
   what later steps consume; it is not a name printed to a screen.
5. **Thought is just a different source of features.** No special
   "thinking" pathway is required.
6. **Words *set* internal states.** The right half of the figure
   presupposes that words have already arranged *Taste, Substance,
   Physical Structure* into the apple configuration.
7. **Recognisers can be composed in layers.** AND → AND → final agent
   is a small hierarchy.

---

## What the implementation already absorbs

### 1 — Stimuli and internal events share the same signal type

Signals in
[`09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md)
do not distinguish "from the user" from "from another agency": both
are events with the same envelope. The substrate is therefore
already source-agnostic in the way the figure asks for.

### 2 — Frames can match on derived state

Frame `matches` can refer to `any_signals` regardless of who emitted
the signals
([`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md)).
A frame triggered by a perception signal and a frame triggered by
an assembly-emitted signal look the same to the matcher.

### 3 — Polynemes wake the same listeners regardless of origin

A `directive.*` polyneme — emitted because of phrase matching on the
user's text — can excite the same agencies that a path polyneme
would. That is the modest, working version of "thought and percept
share machinery."

---

## What the implementation does not yet take into account

### A — No final-recogniser layer

The figure's distinguishing element is the *single triangular final
agent* that fires regardless of which AND-bank reached it. The plan
has no equivalent: no shared "concept" node that multiple distinct
matchers can converge upon. Whatever convergence happens, happens
inside an LLM prompt.

### B — Sensory vs internal symmetry is undeclared

The plan does have source-agnostic signals, but it does not *state*
that this is the property holding the apple-figure together. There
is no documented invariant that says "a recogniser shall not branch
on whether the input is sensory or internal."

### C — Internal agencies are not modelled as small recognisers

In the figure, *Taste* and *Substance* are themselves recognisers
returning *apple-y* and *fruit*. The plan has perception agencies
that return features as signals, but no `kind: recognizer` and no
"these agencies are recogniser-pyramids over their inputs" claim
(see `19.4-sor.md` gap A).

### D — Cross-bank convergence has no schema

Even granting the LLM-mediated final step, there is no field of the
form `converges_on: <symbol-id>` that two recogniser banks could
share. Convergence cannot be inspected, audited, or refused by a
critic.

### E — Words-set-internal-states is mediated, not declared

The plan permits the user to write words that change behaviour
(`society <target>:` directives), but it does not declare that
writing *apple* into the stimulus should arrange the perception
agencies into the apple configuration. The mechanism is implicit and
contextual; the figure's mechanism is wired.

## Implication for the plan (no changes proposed here)

Figure 19-8 says that *the same agent fires for the apple-I-see and
the apple-I-think*. The plan supplies the precondition (uniform
signals across sources) but not the consequence (a shared, named,
inspectable final recogniser). The gap is most visible whenever the
society is asked to reason about something whose features come from
both reading and from a tool: there is currently no architectural
guarantee that the two routes will reach the same conclusion.

Closing this gap would interact with the missing recogniser kind
(`19.4-sor.md` gap A), the polyneme-emission gap (`19.2-sor.md` gap
D), and the frames/polynemes/K-lines schemas in
[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md)
and
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md),
and so falls under the "stop and ask" rules in
[`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md) §12 and
[`CLAUDE.md`](../../../../../CLAUDE.md).
