# Figure 19-6 — Implementation analysis (SOR / FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION)

**Source figure:** [19-6.md](19-6.md) — *Weighted evidence with a
negative input.*
**Analysis target:** [`FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/)
(plan-of-record for `.forgejo-society/` and
`.forgejo/workflows/forgejo-society.yaml`).

The figure generalises 19-5: counted inputs become *weighted* inputs,
and one of them is *negative*. This is the perceptron skeleton.

---

## The ideas Figure 19-6 actually carries

1. **Signed weights on evidence.** Each input contributes a number,
   and that number can be positive or negative.
2. **A weighted sum, then a threshold.** The recogniser fires when
   the sum crosses a fixed cut.
3. **Inhibitory evidence is symmetric to excitatory evidence.** A
   table-recogniser feeding the chair-recogniser with weight *−15*
   is structurally the same as a leg feeding it with *+3*.
4. **Confounders are explicit.** The negative input names a rival
   hypothesis (*table*) and arranges for it to suppress this one.
5. **Recognisers can take recognisers as inputs.** *(Table)* is itself
   the output of another recogniser, not a raw feature.
6. **Magnitudes encode trust.** *+3* and *−15* are not labels; they
   are how much each piece of evidence is worth.
7. **The figure is still flat.** There is no internal arrangement of
   the inputs — only their summed contributions.

---

## What the implementation already absorbs

### 1 — Per-listener weights exist in polynemes

`excite: { <agent>: <weight 0..1> }` and `inhibit: { <agent>: <weight
0..1> }` in
[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md)
already split influence into two signed halves with magnitudes. This
is the closest analogue: positive evidence and negative evidence
expressed separately.

### 2 — Recognisers-feeding-recognisers, in spirit

Critics can already use the outputs of other critics in their
reasoning (mediated by `criticize` ordering). A nested
"table-recogniser inhibits chair-recogniser" is therefore not alien;
it is just absent as an explicit declaration.

---

## What the implementation does not yet take into account

### A — No signed scalar on a single edge

The plan splits influence into two non-negative maps (`excite` and
`inhibit`). The figure uses a single signed number per edge (*+3*,
*−15*). This is a representational choice; the two are not
equivalent when an agent appears in both sums or when one wants to
multiply them together later. There is no `weight: -15` shape.

### B — No weighted-sum aggregator anywhere

The pipeline's `criticize` / `censor` / `settle` chain aggregates by
*rule*: a blocking censor blocks; a hard-required critic is
respected; the rest is policy. Nowhere is the verdict computed as
`sum(weight_i * fired_i) ≥ threshold`. The perceptron arithmetic has
no host.

### C — Confounders are not declared

The figure shows *table* explicitly suppressing *chair*. The plan has
no `rivals:` field on a frame, a polyneme, or a critic. Rival
hypotheses must currently be handled either by humans reading the
manifests or by an LLM noticing the conflict at runtime.

### D — Magnitudes are not calibrated

`excite`/`inhibit` weights are floats in `[0,1]` but there is no
guidance on what a `0.3` means versus a `0.9`, and no documented
calibration step. The figure's *+3* and *−15* are explicit
trust-scores; the plan's `0.7` is not.

### E — No learning over weights

A perceptron is interesting because its weights can change with
experience. The plan's polyneme weights are hand-edited files under
git review. There is no learning loop that would adjust them — and
under
[`07-policies-and-safety.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/07-policies-and-safety.md)
and the `self-modification.frame.yml`, any such loop would itself
be subject to governance review, not silent adjustment.

This is a deliberate posture, not an oversight; the figure is
nonetheless a reminder that *if* weight adjustment is ever wanted,
its absence is structural, not accidental.

## Implication for the plan (no changes proposed here)

Figure 19-6 is the *minimum* numerical machinery for evidence-
weighing. The plan has the components (two-sided weights, separate
critics, ordered aggregation) but does not assemble them into a
weighted-sum recogniser. That is a deliberate consequence of using
governance and review in place of arithmetic — but it should be
named as a deliberate consequence rather than a coincidence.

Any move to add signed weights, a weighted-sum aggregator, a
`rivals:` field, or weight learning would touch the polyneme and
critic schemas in
[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md)
and
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md),
the settlement aggregator in
[`03-runtime-pipeline.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/03-runtime-pipeline.md),
and the safety posture in
[`07-policies-and-safety.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/07-policies-and-safety.md),
and so falls under the "stop and ask" rules in
[`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md) §12 and
[`CLAUDE.md`](../../../../../CLAUDE.md).
