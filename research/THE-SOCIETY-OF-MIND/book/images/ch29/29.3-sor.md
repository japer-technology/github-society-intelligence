# Figure 29-3 — Implementation analysis (SOR / FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION)

**Source figure:** [29-3.md](29-3.md) — *Give in the physical realm
(Trans-frame: Origin → Trajectory → Destination).*
**Analysis target:** [`FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/)
(plan-of-record for `.forgejo-society/` and
`.forgejo/workflows/forgejo-society.yaml`).

Figure 29-3 is the first of three parallel figures (with
[29-4](29-4.md) and [29-5](29-5.md)) that show *one phrase parsed
three times* through a fixed slot structure. This file inventories
the ideas it carries and checks them against the plan.

---

## The ideas Figure 29-3 actually carries

1. **A Trans-frame as a fixed slot triple.** Origin / Trajectory /
   Destination — three named terminals, in order.
2. **Slot fillers are realm-specific.** *Mary's hand*, *the kite*,
   *Jack's hand* — physical entities for the physical reading.
3. **Connecting bars carry the realm label.** "physical", "motion"
   — the bars name the *kind* of transit, not just the endpoints.
4. **A horizontal strip is enough.** No internal recursion is needed
   to express "give" in one realm; three slots and two bars suffice.
5. **One reading at a time, in this figure.** Figure 29-3 isolates
   the physical reading; the multi-realm picture is deferred to
   [29-6](29-6.md).
6. **The frame is reusable across verbs.** Any verb of transit can
   fill the same Origin / Trajectory / Destination slots.
7. **The frame is reusable across realms.** The shape will repeat in
   [29-4](29-4.md) (estates) and [29-5](29-5.md) (mental) without
   change.
8. **Parsing is structural, not lexical.** "Mary gives Jack the kite"
   does not survive as a word string inside the frame; it survives as
   slot bindings.

## What the implementation already absorbs

### 1 — Frames as a named primitive

[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md)
defines *frame* as a first-class concept and uses it as the substrate
of `activate` and `deliberate`. Figure 29-3's Trans-frame is one
instance of that primitive: a small bundle of named terminals that
agencies can attach to.

### 2 — Typed slots on signals and handoffs

[`09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md)
gives signals and handoffs a fixed set of typed fields. That is the
operational form of "Origin / Trajectory / Destination": a payload
whose shape is known in advance, with each slot identified by name
rather than by position in a word string.

### 3 — Reusability across producers

Multiple agencies can emit the same signal kind; multiple agencies
can consume it. Figure 29-3's point 6 (one frame, many verbs) is
honoured by the schema's separation between *signal kind* and
*producing agency*.

### 4 — Structural, not lexical, parsing

The runtime never reasons over a raw phrase; it reasons over signals
and frame bindings produced by `perceive` and `activate`
([`03-runtime-pipeline.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/03-runtime-pipeline.md)).
This matches point 8.

## What the implementation does not yet take into account

### A — *Trans-frame* is not a named frame kind

The plan has *frame* in the abstract but ships no catalogue of frame
shapes. There is no `frames/trans.frame.md`, no
`kind: frame.trans`, and no schema that says "a Trans-frame has
exactly three terminals named origin, trajectory, destination." Any
agency that wants to think in transit terms must invent its own
slot names locally.

### B — Slot identity is not stable across agencies

Without a shared frame catalogue, two agencies that both reason about
transit may use different field names (`source`/`target`,
`from`/`to`, `origin`/`destination`). The figure assumes a *single*
named slot structure that all readers can index into. The plan's
signal schemas in
[`09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md)
do not currently enforce a vocabulary of slot names.

### C — Connecting bars (realm + verb) are not modelled

Figure 29-3's bars are labelled "physical" and "motion" — they carry
*realm* and *predicate*. The plan has no field for either on a
signal. A `kind:` is closer to "motion" than to "physical", and there
is no `realm:` at all (see also [29-1](29.1-sor.md) gap A,
[29-2](29.2-sor.md) gap A).

### D — A frame is not addressable as a unit of memory

In Minsky's text, a *filled* Trans-frame is what gets stored, recalled,
and compared. The plan stores *signals* and *settlements*
([`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md))
and does not have a *frame instance* as a memorable object. K-line
retrieval keys off labels and embeddings, not off "find me the
Trans-frame whose Origin was Mary's hand."

### E — One-realm reading is not labelled as such

Figure 29-3 is explicitly *the physical reading*. The plan has no
way to mark a parse as "this is the realm-X reading of input Y," so
it cannot represent the deliberate *narrowing* the figure performs.
The chapter's eventual move — three parallel readings — needs that
labelling as a starting point.

### F — Honest scope

Figure 29-3 on its own is small. Most of its weight comes from the
*pattern* it establishes for [29-4](29-4.md), [29-5](29-5.md), and
[29-6](29-6.md). The plan's gap is not specifically about transit;
it is about the absence of a *named frame catalogue with stable slot
names*. That gap is recorded once here and will recur in the next
two analyses.

## Implication for the plan (no changes proposed here)

Figure 29-3 points at a *frame catalogue* the plan does not yet
maintain: named frame shapes with stable terminal names, each
addressable as both a parsing target and a memorable object.
Introducing such a catalogue would touch
[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md),
the signal schemas in
[`09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md),
and identity scopes in
`THE-SOCIETY-OF-REPO/02-protocols/01-identity.md`. Under
[`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md) §12 and
[`CLAUDE.md`](../../../../../CLAUDE.md), no change is proposed here.
