# Figure 29-2 — Implementation analysis (SOR / FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION)

**Source figure:** [29-2.md](29-2.md) — *Pages of an encyclopedia of
everything (PHYSICAL / PERSONAL / MENTAL, with internal levels of
detail).*
**Analysis target:** [`FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/)
(plan-of-record for `.forgejo-society/` and
`.forgejo/workflows/forgejo-society.yaml`).

Figure 29-2 turns the stance of Figure 29-1 into a picture of
organisation: knowledge as parallel *pages*, each page carrying its
own internal *levels of detail*. This file inventories the ideas it
carries and checks each against the plan.

---

## The ideas Figure 29-2 actually carries

1. **Knowledge is paged, not flat.** *Physical*, *Personal*, *Mental*
   are distinct pages of one encyclopedia.
2. **Each page has its own column of levels.** Structures / Objects /
   Atoms; Families / Persons / Traits; Cultures / Minds / Ideas.
3. **Adjacent levels within a page are bridgeable.** Bricks → walls
   → houses is the easy case.
4. **Distant pages are not bridgeable in small steps.** "Minds and
   bricks lie volumes apart."
5. **Comprehension within a realm is the easy case.** Most cognitive
   work stays on one page.
6. **Crossing between pages is the work the rest of the chapter
   describes.** Figure 29-2 names the problem the later figures
   solve.
7. **The set of pages is small and named.** Three, not three thousand.
8. **The order of levels within a page is also small and named.**
   Three rows, not arbitrary depth.

## What the implementation already absorbs

### 1 — Pages, in the form of families

[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md)
organises agencies into a small, named set of families (perception,
memory, code, safety, identity, integration, assembly, meta-admin).
Each family directory under `.forgejo-society/agencies/` plays the
role of a *page*: a column of related units that share vocabulary and
ask similar questions. The plan's family count is small and named, in
the spirit of points 7 and 8.

### 2 — Levels of detail, in the form of summary tiers

[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md)
and the `agencies/assembly/summary-tier-*` units in
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md)
implement levels of detail *for outputs*. A finding can be raised one
tier at a time — atoms → objects → structures, in the figure's terms
— and the assembly family is the place where that compression
happens. Point 3 ("adjacent levels are bridgeable") is honoured for
signals.

### 3 — Adjacent-level bridging as the easy case

The summary-tier chain is sequential and bounded: tier 1 → tier 2 →
tier 3, never tier 1 → tier 3 in a single jump. This matches the
figure's bricks → walls → houses staircase rather than treating
"raise a finding to executive level" as a single magical step.

### 4 — Distant-page work is at least segregated

The plan does not let `agency.code.*` write directly to memory or
let `agency.safety.*` produce code patches. Cross-family effects
travel through typed handoffs
([`09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md)).
This honours point 4's intuition that distant pages do not communicate
in one hop, even though the plan does not phrase it that way.

## What the implementation does not yet take into account

### A — *Page* (realm) is not modelled

Families are *organisational folders*; they are not *epistemic
pages*. The plan has no field that says "this family operates in the
physical realm" vs "the possessional realm" vs "the mental realm".
Two families could be in the same Minskyan realm (e.g. both physical)
without the schema being able to express it. The figure's first
distinction — three pages — has no representation.

### B — Levels of detail are output-only

Summary tiers compress *signals*, not *units*. Figure 29-2 puts
*Atoms / Objects / Structures* as the page's own internal axis: the
page contains entities at each level. The plan has no equivalent
axis on agencies themselves. There is no `level:` field, no notion
of "this agency operates at the *Objects* level of its family", and
nothing in
[`11-mapping-sor-to-implementation.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/11-mapping-sor-to-implementation.md)
that maps level to manifest.

### C — Cross-family bridging is implicit, not staged

Point 4 ("distant pages need work to bridge") is honoured by typed
handoffs but is not staged into named *intermediate levels*. There is
no "physical-to-personal bridge" agency, no
`agency.bridge.<from>-to-<to>.md`, and no folder
`.forgejo-society/bridges/`. The plan moves between families in one
hop via signals; the figure suggests there should be an intermediate
representation that both sides can read.

### D — "Easy case stays in the realm" is not policy

The plan does not encode the assumption that *most cognition stays
on one page*. There is no preference, budget, or routing hint that
says "prefer same-family deliberation; treat cross-family activation
as a stronger event." `excite:` and `inhibits:` in
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md)
do not distinguish same-family from cross-family edges.

### E — No "encyclopedia" surface

Figure 29-2 is a *picture of the whole index*. The plan has no
single document or runtime view that is "the index of all
realms × all levels". The closest analogue is
[`11-mapping-sor-to-implementation.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/11-mapping-sor-to-implementation.md),
which maps Society of Repo vocabulary to implementation artefacts —
useful, but a different axis from realms × levels.

### F — Number and identity of the pages

Minsky names three pages (Physical, Personal, Mental); the plan names
eight families. The two counts are not in conflict, but the plan does
not record *why* eight, nor whether some families belong to the same
page. The figure's quiet claim — *the set of pages is small enough
to enumerate* — has no analogue in the implementation's choice of
family count.

## Implication for the plan (no changes proposed here)

Figure 29-2 suggests two axes (realm, level) that the plan currently
collapses into one (family). Promoting realm and level to first-class
fields, adding a `bridges/` folder, or introducing a same-realm
preference would touch the manifest schema in
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md),
the activation protocol in
[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md),
the handoff schemas in
[`09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md),
and the identity scopes in
`THE-SOCIETY-OF-REPO/02-protocols/01-identity.md`. That places any
such change under the "stop and ask" rules in
[`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md) §12 and
[`CLAUDE.md`](../../../../../CLAUDE.md). Recorded as analysis only.
