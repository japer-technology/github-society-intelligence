# Figure 29-8 — Implementation analysis (SOR / FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION)

**Source figure:** [29-8.md](29-8.md) — *Epigraph from Robert Herrick
(a metaphor that anchors too tightly to the source realm).*
**Analysis target:** [`FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/)
(plan-of-record for `.forgejo-society/` and
`.forgejo/workflows/forgejo-society.yaml`).

Figure 29-8 is an epigraph in the *likenesses and analogies* section.
Its operational content is small but specific: an analogy *fails*
when its surface description holds the listener inside the source
realm. This file inventories what the figure carries and checks each
item against the plan.

---

## The ideas Figure 29-8 actually carries

1. **Analogy is a realm-crossing operation.** It only works when the
   listener can shift from source realm to target realm.
2. **Surface detail can prevent that shift.** Tendrils, leaves, dried
   wine — too vivid in the source realm.
3. **A failed analogy produces a wrong-realm parse.** "Fantasies of
   vegetables with hands and feet."
4. **Success and failure are mechanical, not aesthetic.** The
   problem is not bad taste; it is mis-activation of the wrong
   memories.
5. **The judgement is local to the listener's machinery.** Whether
   an analogy switches realms depends on which agencies the surface
   excites first.
6. **Epigraphs frame, they do not implement.** This figure is the
   section's case study, not a diagram of parts.

## What the implementation already absorbs

### 1 — Mis-activation is recognised as a category

[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md)
includes critics whose job is to catch wrong-target activation
(e.g. `critic.scope`, `critic.evidence`). The general shape — *some
activations are wrong, even when individual units behave correctly*
— is part of the plan.

### 2 — Inhibition exists

`inhibits:` lines in agency manifests let one unit suppress another
when context makes it inappropriate. This is the operational shape
of "stop the listener from following the surface description into the
wrong realm." Point 5 has at least a primitive to attach to.

### 3 — Censors as a last line

[`07-policies-and-safety.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/07-policies-and-safety.md)
gives censors the role of blocking outputs that, however well-formed,
should not be acted on. A wrong-realm parse that survives critics is
still subject to censorship.

## What the implementation does not yet take into account

### A — No realm-crossing operator

Without a realm axis (see [29-1](29.1-sor.md) gap A,
[29-2](29.2-sor.md) gap A, [29-6](29.6-sor.md) gap A), the plan
cannot represent *analogy as realm-crossing* at all. The figure's
core verb has no operational form.

### B — No notion of "surface description over-anchors"

Critics in the plan check for evidence, scope, safety, etc. None
checks for "this input excites a strong cluster in realm X when the
intended target is realm Y." There is no `critic.wrong-realm` and
no signal that flags excessive source-realm activation.

### C — No analogy-evaluation phase

The pipeline has `criticize` and `censor` but no step whose specific
job is to evaluate whether a proposed mapping between two
representations preserved the relevant relations. Analogy succeeds
or fails by accident of which agencies happen to activate, not by
deliberate evaluation.

### D — Wrong-realm outputs are not a typed failure

The plan can record that a settlement was *rejected* (by a censor)
or *withdrawn* (by a critic), but does not have a failure type
"settled in the wrong realm." A run that produced *fantasies of
vegetables with hands and feet* would either pass or fail on other
criteria — it has no native error category.

### E — No "switch realms cheaply" affordance

Point 1 presupposes the listener *can* switch when surface detail
permits. The plan has no cheap inter-realm jump. Cross-family
handoffs go through typed signals and `settle`; there is no
lightweight "consider this in the other realm" branch in
[`03-runtime-pipeline.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/03-runtime-pipeline.md).

### F — Epigraph honesty

Figure 29-8 is an epigraph, not a mechanism. Its real operational
content is small and largely a recap of the chapter's prior figures
applied to a literary example. Most of the gaps above are
restatements of gaps already named in [29-1](29.1-sor.md),
[29-2](29.2-sor.md), and [29-6](29.6-sor.md), specialised to the
case where realm-crossing *fails*. The unique contribution is gap
B — a wrong-realm critic — and gap D — wrong-realm as a typed
failure.

## Implication for the plan (no changes proposed here)

Figure 29-8 sharpens, but does not extend, the gaps already named
across Chapter 29. The two genuinely new items are a *wrong-realm
critic* and *wrong-realm* as a typed failure category. Either would
sit on top of a realm axis that the plan does not yet carry, and so
would touch the manifest schema in
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md),
the policy surface in
[`07-policies-and-safety.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/07-policies-and-safety.md),
and identity scopes in
`THE-SOCIETY-OF-REPO/02-protocols/01-identity.md`. Under
[`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md) §12 and
[`CLAUDE.md`](../../../../../CLAUDE.md), no change is proposed here.
