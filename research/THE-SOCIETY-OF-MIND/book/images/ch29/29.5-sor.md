# Figure 29-5 — Implementation analysis (SOR / FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION)

**Source figure:** [29-5.md](29-5.md) — *Give in the social or
psychological realm (Trans-frame; Origin and Destination both reside
in Mary; bars labelled mental / change).*
**Analysis target:** [`FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/)
(plan-of-record for `.forgejo-society/` and
`.forgejo/workflows/forgejo-society.yaml`).

Figure 29-5 is the *mental* reading of the same phrase
[29-3](29-3.md) and [29-4](29-4.md) parse physically and
possessionally. Its specific contribution is *internal change*: the
transfer is inside one agent. This file inventories the ideas it
carries and checks them against the plan.

---

## The ideas Figure 29-5 actually carries

1. **The Trans-frame shape survives again.** Same Origin /
   Trajectory / Destination slots.
2. **Origin and Destination can be inside the same actor.** The
   transit is intra-agent, not inter-agent.
3. **The "object" of the verb can be a disposition.** Affection,
   obligation, regard — not an artefact.
4. **The trajectory can be the actor herself.** *Mary* sits in the
   middle bar; the carrier is the giver.
5. **Mental change is a first-class kind of event.** Not derivative
   of physical or possessional change.
6. **Three readings run side by side without interfering.** The
   chapter's analogy: colour and shape both describe an apple at once.
7. **The realm has its own connective vocabulary.** "Mental",
   "change" — distinct from "physical/motion" and
   "possession/transfer".
8. **An internal state has identifiable parts.** *Mary's disposition*
   is named as a node, not as an opaque inner condition.

## What the implementation already absorbs

### 1 — Self-modification as a named primitive

[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md)
names a `self-modification` frame and an
`agency.meta-admin.differentiation-broker`, both of which describe
*intra-agent change*. Point 2 (Origin and Destination in the same
actor) has a partial analogue: the unit-of-change *can* be the same
unit being changed.

### 2 — Memory as a representation of internal state

[`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md)
keeps K-lines, settlements, and decisions. These are the plan's
representation of *what the system is disposed to do next*. Point 3
(a disposition can be the object of a verb) has a partial home:
K-lines *are* dispositions in the operational sense.

### 3 — Parallel readings can coexist

Multiple agencies activate on the same input
([`03-runtime-pipeline.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/03-runtime-pipeline.md)).
The plan does not force one agency's reading to override another's
before `settle`. Point 6 (parallel readings without interference) is
honoured at the activation layer.

### 4 — Distinct event kinds for distinct realms

The plan separates `event.document.*`, `event.policy.*`,
`event.settlement.*`, etc. (per
`THE-SOCIETY-OF-REPO/02-protocols/01-identity.md`). Different *kinds*
of change are not collapsed into one. Point 5's intuition — mental
change is its own kind — has at least an analogue in event taxonomy.

## What the implementation does not yet take into account

### A — *Mental change* has no event shape

The plan has `event.document.*`, `event.settlement.*`,
`event.policy.*`, and so on, but no
`event.disposition.changed.*` or comparable shape that captures
"agency X's stance toward Y has shifted." K-line updates happen, but
they are not surfaced as events that other agencies can subscribe
to. Point 5 is present in pieces but not as a typed signal.

### B — Internal trajectory (Mary as the bar) is not modelled

Figure 29-5 puts *Mary* in the middle bar — the giver is the path.
The plan has no place for "the unit performing the change is also
the medium of the change." Self-modification flows from
`agency.meta-admin.differentiation-broker` *acting on* another
agency; there is no shape for "this agency is both Origin,
Trajectory, and Destination of its own change."

### C — Dispositions are not addressable

A K-line is retrievable by label or embedding, but is not exposed as
*a named disposition belonging to a named agency*. A query like
"what does agency.code.patch-imaginer currently incline toward when
input matches X?" has no schema-level answer. The figure's claim —
that *Mary's disposition* is a node, not a fog — would require
disposition to be a first-class object.

### D — Side-by-side readings are not preserved past settle

Multiple agencies can read in parallel during `activate` and
`deliberate`, but `settle` collapses to a single settlement. The
chapter's "colour and shape both describe one apple" is broken at the
settlement boundary: only one reading survives into `act`. Nothing
in
[`03-runtime-pipeline.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/03-runtime-pipeline.md)
preserves the *triple* of physical / possessional / mental readings
as a structured artefact.

### E — Mental-realm vocabulary is missing

There is no "mental" label, no `realm: mental`, and no
`kind: change.disposition`. The bars of Figure 29-5 cannot be
expressed. This is the same missing axis flagged in
[29-1](29.1-sor.md) gap A, [29-2](29.2-sor.md) gap A, and
[29-4](29-4.md) — recurring here in its third specific form.

### F — Internal-state inspection is not a workflow phase

Figure 29-5 presupposes that one can *examine* Mary's disposition
before and after. The plan's runtime does not have a phase whose
job is to snapshot internal state of agencies. Inspection happens
indirectly, through settlement artefacts and `remember`. The figure's
implicit affordance — "you can look inside" — is not directly
provided.

### G — Honest scope

This is the third of three parallel figures. Several of the gaps
above (E, the missing realm vocabulary; "frame catalogue absent"
from 29-3) are restatements of structural gaps already named in
earlier analyses. The genuinely new gaps are A (disposition as event),
B (intra-agent trajectory), C (disposition as addressable object),
and D (parallel readings collapsing at settle).

## Implication for the plan (no changes proposed here)

Figure 29-5 surfaces three things the plan does not yet hold:
*disposition* as an addressable object, *mental change* as a typed
event, and *parallel realm readings* preserved past settlement. Each
would touch the identity scopes in
`THE-SOCIETY-OF-REPO/02-protocols/01-identity.md`, the runtime
pipeline in
[`03-runtime-pipeline.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/03-runtime-pipeline.md),
the signal schemas in
[`09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md),
and the memory model in
[`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md).
Under [`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md) §12 and
[`CLAUDE.md`](../../../../../CLAUDE.md), no change is proposed here.
