# Figure 29-4 — Implementation analysis (SOR / FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION)

**Source figure:** [29-4.md](29-4.md) — *Give in the realm of estates
(Trans-frame: Origin / Trajectory / Destination, bars labelled
possession / transfer).*
**Analysis target:** [`FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/)
(plan-of-record for `.forgejo-society/` and
`.forgejo/workflows/forgejo-society.yaml`).

Figure 29-4 is the *possessional* reading of the same phrase
[29-3](29-3.md) read physically. Its specific contribution is the
*estate* realm: ownership, rights, transfers. This file inventories
the ideas the figure carries and checks them against the plan.

---

## The ideas Figure 29-4 actually carries

1. **The Trans-frame shape is invariant.** Same three slots, same
   order, as [29-3](29-3.md).
2. **Slot fillers change to suit the realm.** *Mary's estate*,
   *the kite's ownership*, *Jack's estate*.
3. **A transfer can happen with no physical motion.** Ownership can
   change while the object stays still.
4. **The realm of estates sits between objects and ideas.** Plans
   depend on having the right to use materials, tools, concepts.
5. **"Right or might" precedes use.** Before an agent can act on
   something, possession or permission must be established.
6. **Possession is itself a slot value.** "The kite's ownership" is
   what travels, not the kite.
7. **The same verb crosses realms cleanly.** No new word is required
   to express *give* in the possessional realm.
8. **The realm has its own connective vocabulary.** "Possession",
   "transfer" — not "physical", "motion".

## What the implementation already absorbs

### 1 — Possession-like concepts appear in policy

[`07-policies-and-safety.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/07-policies-and-safety.md)
and the authority registry referenced from
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md)
already require that a unit *be authorised* before it can act. The
authority levels (`read`, `draft`, `propose`, `act`, `govern`,
`human`) are the plan's vocabulary for "right or might precedes
use." Point 5 has an operational analogue.

### 2 — Ownership of artefacts is tracked

`settle` produces a settlement, and the act phase produces a commit
under a named author identity
([`03-runtime-pipeline.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/03-runtime-pipeline.md)
and [`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md)).
Git's authorship model is, in Minskyan terms, the *possessional*
record of the run: who proposed, who approved, who acted.

### 3 — Transfer events are auditable

The whole plan rests on Git as the audit substrate
([`00-overview.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/00-overview.md),
[`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md)).
Every change of state is a transfer with named endpoints. Point 3
("transfer without physical motion") matches the plan's *commits and
labels* — pure metadata moves.

### 4 — Shared frame shape across uses

The same handoff schema in
[`09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md)
is used whether the payload is a code patch, a memory write, or an
authority grant. Point 1's invariance of shape is preserved.

## What the implementation does not yet take into account

### A — *Estate* is not a first-class object

The plan has authority *grants* per unit, but no *estate* — a
declared bundle of things (paths, tools, secrets, budgets) that a
unit owns and from which transfers can flow. There is no
`estates/<agency-id>.md`, no `estate:` field on a manifest, and
nothing the runtime can query as "show me agency X's current
holdings." Possession is read off scattered places (manifest
`authority:`, policy files, runner config) rather than presented as
one object.

### B — Transfers between units are not modelled

A grant changes; a tool becomes available; a secret rotates; a path
opens. The plan has no *transfer event* with explicit Origin and
Destination units, no `event.estate.transfer.*` shape, and nothing
in
[`09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md)
that captures "agency X granted agency Y the ability to write path
Z." Figure 29-4's central act — a transfer in the possessional realm
— is therefore unrepresented as a primary event type.

### C — Possession as a slot value (vs the thing possessed)

Figure 29-4 distinguishes *the kite* from *the kite's ownership*.
The plan treats artefacts and rights together: an authority line
references a path, but does not name the *right over the path* as a
separate object. There is no schema for "this is a right; it is
distinct from the artefact; it can move independently."

### D — The middle realm is not named

Minsky places estates *between* physical and mental. The plan has
no explicit "middle layer" between artefacts (files, commits) and
intent (settlements, K-lines). Authority sits there in practice, but
is not labelled as a distinct realm (see [29-1](29.1-sor.md) gap A
and [29-2](29.2-sor.md) gap A — the same missing field, viewed from
a different figure).

### E — "Right precedes use" is not enforced as a phase

The plan's pipeline is `… censor → settle → act …`
([`03-runtime-pipeline.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/03-runtime-pipeline.md)).
Censors check policy *during* deliberation, and acting is gated by
the workflow runner. There is no dedicated *acquire-rights* phase
between settle and act in which missing authorities are explicitly
requested, granted, or denied. Point 5 is satisfied by accident of
phase ordering, not by a step named for it.

### F — No vocabulary for "right or might"

The plan has authority levels and policies, but no distinction
between *granted right* (registered authority) and *practical might*
(secrets, network paths, runner permissions). Both confer the
ability to act; only one is currently visible to the manifest layer.
This is a real asymmetry the figure surfaces and the plan does not.

### G — Honest scope

This is the second of three parallel figures. Much of what is "missing"
here is the same missing piece called out in [29-3](29-3.md) — a
named frame catalogue with stable slot names — applied to a different
realm. The unique contribution of 29-4 is the *estate* concept itself
(gaps A, B, C), which is genuinely new.

## Implication for the plan (no changes proposed here)

Figure 29-4 surfaces a missing object — an *estate* — and a missing
event — a *rights transfer* — that together would give the plan a
clean home for possession. Introducing either would touch the
manifest schema in
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md),
the policy surface in
[`07-policies-and-safety.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/07-policies-and-safety.md),
the signal schemas in
[`09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md),
and the identity scopes in
`THE-SOCIETY-OF-REPO/02-protocols/01-identity.md`. Under
[`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md) §12 and
[`CLAUDE.md`](../../../../../CLAUDE.md), no change is proposed here.
