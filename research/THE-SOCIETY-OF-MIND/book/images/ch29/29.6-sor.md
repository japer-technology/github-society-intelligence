# Figure 29-6 — Implementation analysis (SOR / FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION)

**Source figure:** [29-6.md](29-6.md) — *Paranomes across stacked
realms (three Trans-frame sheets — PHYSICAL, POSSESSIONAL,
PSYCHOLOGICAL — wired by superpolynemes, superonomes, and
superpronomes).*
**Analysis target:** [`FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/)
(plan-of-record for `.forgejo-society/` and
`.forgejo/workflows/forgejo-society.yaml`).

Figure 29-6 is the architectural payoff of [29-3](29-3.md),
[29-4](29-4.md), and [29-5](29-5.md). It introduces *paranomes* —
the wiring that lets one slot signal address the same role across
three realms at once. This is the chapter's densest figure for the
plan to test itself against.

---

## The ideas Figure 29-6 actually carries

1. **Three realms are stacked, not merged.** Each sheet keeps its
   own slot grid; the realms remain distinct.
2. **The slot grid is shared.** *Actor / Instrument / Object*
   columns, *Origin / Destination* edges — identical across sheets.
3. **A paranome addresses *one role across all sheets at once*.**
   The same "Origin" signal binds Mary's hand, Mary's estate, and
   Mary's disposition simultaneously.
4. **Superpolynemes activate memories across the stack.** A single
   activation signal reaches into all three sheets.
5. **Superonomes control level-bands across the stack.** Level of
   detail is coordinated across realms, not chosen per realm.
6. **Superpronomes blink frame-terminals across the stack in
   unison.** Slot fillers can be re-bound in step.
7. **Each realm still thinks on its own.** Coordination does not
   collapse the realms into one.
8. **The language-agency keeps them in step.** A specific agency is
   responsible for the unison.
9. **Coordination is a separate apparatus.** Wiring (paranomes)
   sits outside the sheets, not inside them.

## What the implementation already absorbs

### 1 — A coordination apparatus exists

[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md)
already names *polynemes* and *K-lines* as the cross-cutting wiring
of the runtime. The plan therefore inherits the *idea* of a wiring
layer that sits outside individual units and coordinates them.

### 2 — Activation can target many units at once

Polyneme excitation, label-based routing, and `activates_on:` lists
in
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md)
let one signal reach many agencies. This is the operational seed of
point 4 (superpolyneme = one activation reaching all sheets).

### 3 — Levels of detail exist as summary tiers

The summary-tier chain (see [29-2](29.2-sor.md)) lets a finding move
between levels. Coordinated level-band changes — point 5 — have a
substrate to attach to, even if the coordination itself is missing.

### 4 — Hierarchy and summaries are documented

`THE-SOCIETY-OF-REPO/02-protocols/13-hierarchy-and-summaries.md`
maps to `agencies/assembly/*` and the summary tiers, providing the
*hierarchical* dimension the paranome picture sits on top of.

### 5 — A "language-agency" analogue exists

The assembly family (`agencies/assembly/*`) is the plan's nearest
analogue to point 8 — a family whose job is to keep parallel
findings in step at presentation time.

## What the implementation does not yet take into account

### A — Sheets (realms) are not modelled

The plan does not represent *realms* as sheets at all (see
[29-1](29.1-sor.md) gap A, [29-2](29.2-sor.md) gap A,
[29-4](29-4.md), [29-5](29-5.md)). With no sheets, there is no stack
to wire across. The whole figure presupposes the realm axis that the
plan currently lacks.

### B — Shared slot grid is not enforced

Point 2 requires that *Actor*, *Instrument*, *Object*, *Origin*,
*Destination* mean the same thing across realms. The plan's signal
schemas in
[`09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md)
do not enforce a shared slot vocabulary across signal kinds (see
[29-3](29.3-sor.md) gap B). Without that, there is no shared grid
for paranomes to pin to.

### C — Paranomes have no analogue

There is no "one signal addresses the same role on every sheet"
construct in the plan. Polynemes excite *units*; they do not address
*roles across realms*. The figure's central new object — the
paranome — is unrepresented.

### D — Superpolynemes / superonomes / superpronomes

These three named bundles are the figure's mechanism for *cross-realm*
activation, level control, and slot re-binding. None of them has a
plan equivalent. Activation cannot say "raise the level-band of
every sheet at once"; rebinding cannot say "the new filler is X on
every sheet." Each of the three is its own gap.

### E — Coordination cannot be assigned to a named agency

Point 8 ("the language-agency keeps them in step") is precise: a
*specific* unit owns the unison. The assembly family is the closest
analogue, but it operates over *outputs*, not over *active
deliberation across realms*. There is no `agency.meta-admin.unison`
or similar.

### F — Unison without collapse is not a guarantee

Even if the plan added cross-cutting wiring, nothing currently
enforces point 7 — that each realm continues to think on its own
after coordination. `settle` collapses to one settlement
([29-5](29.5-sor.md) gap D), so the figure's "in step but distinct"
property could not be preserved end-to-end as the plan stands.

### G — Visualisation surface for cross-realm state

Figure 29-6 *is* a visualisation. The plan has no run-time view that
would let an operator see three parallel sheets and the wires
between them. The closest analogue is settlement artefacts in
[`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md),
which are post-hoc and one-sheet.

### H — Honest scope

This is the chapter's heaviest figure for the plan. Six of the eight
gaps above are *new* (A is recurring; H is housekeeping). Closing
any of them would require coordinated changes across identity
scopes, manifest schema, signal schema, runtime pipeline, and
memory model. This analysis records the shape of the work; it does
not propose it.

## Implication for the plan (no changes proposed here)

Figure 29-6 is the densest implementation challenge in the chapter.
Honouring it would require a *realm* axis, a *shared slot grid*, a
*paranome* primitive, and three coordinated activation bundles
(super-polyneme, -onome, -pronome) — together with a way to
preserve parallel realm readings past settlement. Each of those
would touch identity in
`THE-SOCIETY-OF-REPO/02-protocols/01-identity.md`, the manifest
schema in
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md),
frames and polynemes in
[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md),
signal schemas in
[`09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md),
the runtime pipeline in
[`03-runtime-pipeline.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/03-runtime-pipeline.md),
the memory model in
[`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md),
and the SOR-to-implementation mapping in
[`11-mapping-sor-to-implementation.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/11-mapping-sor-to-implementation.md).
Under [`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md) §12 and
[`CLAUDE.md`](../../../../../CLAUDE.md), no change is proposed here.
