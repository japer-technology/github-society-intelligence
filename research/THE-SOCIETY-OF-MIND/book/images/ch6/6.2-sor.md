# Figure 6-2 — Implementation analysis (SOR / FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION)

**Source figure:** [6-2.md](6-2.md) — *Slanting lines of memory.*
**Analysis target:** [`FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/)
(plan-of-record for `.forgejo-society/` and
`.forgejo/workflows/forgejo-society.yaml`).

This file inventories the ideas carried by Figure 6-2 and checks each
one against the implementation plan. The aim is to mark, plainly, what
the plan already absorbs and what it does not yet name.

---

## The ideas Figure 6-2 actually carries

Pulled from the figure caption and Minsky's surrounding text in §6.6
*Momentary mental time*:

1. **Physical time and mental time are not the same.** Real time runs
   left-to-right; the experienced "now" at any one site does not.
2. **Different signals take different numbers of hops.** A message
   from `VOICES` reaches `PLACES` later than a message from `FACES`,
   because the first must pass through `NAMES`.
3. **The "now" of any agency is a slanted slice.** What an agency
   sees as simultaneous is in fact composed of inputs of different
   ages.
4. **Latency is structural, not noise.** The slant is fixed by
   wiring (which hops a signal must take), not by jitter.
5. **A back-loop can carry old material forward.** The arrow from
   `NAMES` back toward `PLACES` shows that a destination receives
   not just current inputs but echoes of earlier ones.
6. **The receiving agency does not know the slant.** `PLACES` is not
   drawn with a clock. It treats its inputs as a single moment even
   though they are not.
7. **Memory and perception are the same machinery.** The figure is
   in a chapter about memory but is drawn entirely as signal
   propagation: a memory in this view is just a signal that took
   more hops to arrive.
8. **The construction is unavoidable.** The slant cannot be removed
   by any local operation at `PLACES`; it can only be acknowledged
   by an outside observer who knows the wiring.

These eight items are what the rest of this file tests against the
implementation plan.

## What the implementation already absorbs

### 1 — Signals are records with a place in time

The signal schema in
[`09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md)
carries `stimulus_id` and `cycle`, and signals are stored in
`state/mind/issues/<n>/signals.jsonl` as an append-only line-oriented
log. This gives every signal a determinate position in the run's
sequence, which is the substrate any account of "slanted time" would
need.

### 2 — Per-run append-only state

[`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md)
makes `state/` per-run and append-only, and commits it at the end of
each workflow run. Order of arrival is preserved in Git. The figure's
left-to-right physical time is at least faithfully recorded, even if
nothing yet reasons about the slant.

### 3 — Multi-hop activation through polynemes and frames

[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md)
describes path-, label-, and phrase-polynemes that excite agencies
which in turn emit further signals. The plan already has a notion of
*messages that propagate by more than one hop* — the structural
prerequisite for Figure 6-2's slant.

### 4 — Back-loops via K-lines

K-lines, described in
[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md)
and `THE-SOCIETY-OF-REPO/06-memory/klines/`, reactivate prior
configurations when their `restore_when` matches. This is the plan's
operational form of Figure 6-2's back-arrow: an old pattern reaches
a current agency by a different route than the live percepts do.

### 5 — Memory and percept share a substrate

`agency.memory.kline-retriever`, `agency.memory.prior-decision-resonator`,
and `agency.perception.intake-bee` all emit the same `Signal`
record shape into the same `signals.jsonl` file. The plan's
machinery does not distinguish "this signal is a memory" from "this
signal is a percept" at the transport layer, which matches idea 7
above.

### 6 — A settlement is an explicit cross-section

The settlement record in
[`09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md)
freezes "what was activated, with what weight" at a single moment.
That is the closest the plan comes to standing at `PLACES` and
writing down what arrived. The settlement is therefore the natural
place where the slant *could* be made visible.

## What the implementation does not yet take into account

These are the parts of Figure 6-2 that the plan currently leaves
implicit, partial, or simply absent. None of them are urgent; all of
them are real.

### A — No per-signal hop count

The signal schema records `cycle` (the run's logical tick) but not
`hops` (how many agency-to-agency transitions produced this signal
from the originating stimulus). Without a hop count, the plan
cannot tell `FACES → PLACES` (one hop) from `VOICES → NAMES → PLACES`
(two hops). Idea 2 — the figure's load-bearing claim — has no
field in the schema.

### B — No notion of signal age at the point of consumption

When a critic or an integrator reads `signals.jsonl`, it sees the
records in arrival order but has no derived value for "this signal
is N cycles older than that one relative to the same stimulus." The
blackboard at
[`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md)
is flat in time. A receiver currently treats its inputs as a single
*now*, exactly as `PLACES` does in the figure — but unlike the
figure's external observer, the plan has no view from outside that
notices the slant.

### C — Back-loops are not labelled as back-loops

K-lines can reactivate a prior configuration, but the resulting
signal is not tagged as a *re-entry*. Nothing in
[`09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md)
distinguishes a forward signal from a return signal, or marks the
echo from `NAMES` back toward `PLACES` as structurally different
from the forward `FACES → PLACES`. Idea 5 has no schema field.

### D — Wiring is not modelled as latency

[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md)
describes which agencies excite which other agencies, but not how
long any such excitation takes to propagate. The plan has no
adjacency map of agencies and no "shortest signal path from
`agency.X` to `agency.Y`" derivable from manifests. Idea 4 —
structural, not jittered latency — cannot be computed today because
the structure itself is not enumerated as a graph.

### E — No reasoning about simultaneity

A critic that says "agencies X and Y agreed at the same cycle"
currently means "their handoffs were appended in the same `cycle`
field." It does not mean "the inputs each was responding to had the
same age relative to the stimulus." The plan has no `kind:
asynchrony` objection, no critic that flags "you are treating a
stale percept as if it were current," and no censor that suppresses
candidate actions built on inputs of mismatched age.

### F — No representation of mental time in memory

`memory/episodic/` and `memory/events/` in
[`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md)
record what happened in wall-clock order. There is no analogue of
"a moment of the past as seen from a particular agency" — no
agency-indexed timeline. The figure's slanted lines, if drawn from
the implementation, would have to be reconstructed from `signals.jsonl`
after the fact; they are nowhere stored.

### G — The settlement is not annotated with input ages

The settlement schema records which agencies were activated and
with what weight, but does not record "the inputs this agency
acted on were N cycles old." A reviewer reading a settlement cannot
see the slant of the `now` it captured. Idea 6 — the receiver does
not know the slant — is faithful to the plan, but for the wrong
reason: the plan also gives no one else a place to write the slant
down.

### H — No "outside observer" role

Figure 6-2's caption is spoken by a narrator who knows the wiring
and can therefore see the slant that `PLACES` cannot. The plan has
no first-ship agency in
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md)
whose job is to describe the temporal shape of a run from outside
the cycle — no `agency.meta-admin.temporal-shape` or equivalent.
The narrator's role in the figure has no operational counterpart.

## Implication for the plan (no changes proposed here)

Figure 6-2 is the figure that earns the word *now* by warning that
it is a construction. The implementation plan honours the *substrate*
(append-only signal log, multi-hop activation, K-line back-loops,
shared memory-and-perception transport) but does not yet honour the
*observation* (a representation of slant, a hop count, an asynchrony
critic, an outside narrator who can describe a run's temporal shape).

The gaps above are recorded here as analysis, not as a change
request. Any move to close them — for example, a `hops` field on
the signal schema, a `kind: re-entry` tag on K-line-sourced signals,
an agency-adjacency graph derived from manifests, an `asynchrony`
objection kind, or a `meta-admin.temporal-shape` agency — would
touch the schemas in
[`09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md),
the activation model in
[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md),
the state layout in
[`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md),
the catalogue in
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md),
and the identity scopes in `THE-SOCIETY-OF-REPO/02-protocols/01-identity.md`,
and so falls under the "stop and ask" rules in
[`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md) §12 and
[`CLAUDE.md`](../../../../../CLAUDE.md).
