# Figure 6-1 — Implementation analysis (SOR / FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION)

**Source figure:** [6-1.md](6-1.md) — *World, A-Brain, B-Brain.*
**Analysis target:** [`FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/)
(plan-of-record for `.forgejo-society/` and
`.forgejo/workflows/forgejo-society.yaml`).

This file inventories the ideas carried by Figure 6-1 and checks each
one against the implementation plan. The aim is to mark, plainly, what
the plan already absorbs and what it does not yet name.

---

## The ideas Figure 6-1 actually carries

Pulled from the figure caption and Minsky's surrounding text in §6.4
*B-Brains*:

1. **Two brains, one body.** Cognition is split across at least two
   distinct units that share no privileged channel beyond the wiring
   the figure draws.
2. **The A-brain is the only one wired to the world.** Stimuli enter
   the A-brain; actions leave the A-brain. The world has no direct
   port to the B-brain.
3. **The B-brain sees only the A-brain.** Its inputs are the A-brain's
   internal patterns — not percepts, not raw events.
4. **Pattern-level supervision.** Because the B-brain cannot read the
   world, it cannot judge whether a thought is *true*; it can only
   judge whether a thought *looks like a familiar bad shape*
   (looping, daydreaming, drifting toward a destructive path).
5. **Return channel as control.** The B-brain feeds signals back into
   the A-brain, which is its only means of intervention. It cannot
   act on the world directly.
6. **Bundles, not single wires.** Both the forward and return arrows
   are dense bundles. The relationship is many signals across many
   internal sites, not one supervisory verdict per cycle.
7. **Recursive wiring as a primitive.** The figure draws three units
   in the same shape, suggesting that a C-brain over the B-brain
   would be the same construction one layer up.
8. **Asymmetry of trust.** The B-brain is implicitly more conservative
   than the A-brain: it exists to catch the A-brain in failure modes
   the A-brain itself cannot notice.

These eight items are what the rest of this file tests against the
implementation plan.

## What the implementation already absorbs

### 1 — A second layer that watches the first

The critics and censors families in
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md)
are explicitly second-order: they consume what other agencies have
produced (`signals.jsonl`, `candidate-actions.jsonl`, handoffs) and
emit `objections` rather than acting on the world. The `criticize` and
`censor` phases in
[`03-runtime-pipeline.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/03-runtime-pipeline.md)
are the workflow's place for "watch the previous step." This is the
B-brain idea at the cycle scale.

### 2 — A meta-admin family that watches the society itself

The `meta-admin` family in
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md)
(`agency.meta-admin.differentiation-broker`, ecology review, the
`evolution/` hook in `report`) exists to read the society's own
records and propose adjustments. It never speaks to the world; it
only speaks back into the society. This is the closest first-ship
analogue to a B-brain.

### 3 — Pattern-level signals rather than world-level claims

The signal schema in
[`09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md)
gives critics a structured way to say "this looks like a familiar bad
shape" — `kind: scope`, `kind: overconfidence`, `kind: staleness`,
`kind: source-quality` — without requiring them to re-verify the
underlying world claim. That is exactly the B-brain's epistemic
position: judge the *shape* of the A-brain's output.

### 4 — A return channel that cannot reach into the world

Critics and censors have no `act` authority. Their only effect is
through `objections`, `inhibits:`, and (for censors) mutation of the
tool surface in `policy.ts`
([`07-policies-and-safety.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/07-policies-and-safety.md)).
They steer the A-side by signalling, never by acting. The asymmetry
the figure draws — "B can only affect A, not the world" — is honoured
by the authority registry.

### 5 — Dense bundles, not a single verdict

A single cycle can produce many objection records and many signals.
The `criticize` phase aggregates all of them; `settle` reads the full
bundle. Nothing in the plan reduces critic output to a single
pass/fail, which matches Figure 6-1's drawing of many arrows in each
direction.

### 6 — Asymmetric conservatism

[`07-policies-and-safety.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/07-policies-and-safety.md)
describes the runtime as *fail-closed*: censors fire before tool
grant, suppressors fire on candidate outputs, and any blocked
candidate produces a *blocked* settlement rather than silent
inaction. The watching layer is structurally more conservative than
the acting layer, which is the B-brain's posture in the figure.

## What the implementation does not yet take into account

These are the parts of Figure 6-1 that the plan currently leaves
implicit, partial, or simply absent. None of them are urgent; all of
them are real.

### A — No second *brain*, only second *agencies*

Figure 6-1 draws the B-brain as a *unit* — its own blob, its own
boundary, its own population of agents. In the plan, the B-side is a
scatter of critics and censors inside the same workflow run as the
A-side. There is no `.forgejo-society/b-brain/` tree, no separate
manifest for "the B-brain as a whole," no boundary that says "these
are the watcher's agencies and they are not the worker's." The
schema in
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md)
has `kind: critic` and `kind: censor` per file, but no aggregate
construct that *is* the B-brain.

### B — Critics see the world the A-side sees

In the figure, the B-brain is wired only to the A-brain. In the
plan, critics read from `state/mind/issues/<n>/` — the same
percepts, the same stimulus, the same blackboard the worker
agencies read. There is no enforced narrowing that says "critics may
read agency outputs but may not read raw percepts" or "censors may
read candidate actions but may not re-read the issue body." The
B-side currently has full A-side visibility, which is *more* than
the figure grants.

### C — No persistent B-brain across runs

The figure's B-brain is a brain — it has continuity. In the plan,
the critic/censor population is reconstituted per run from manifests;
there is no `b-brain/state/` tree analogous to
`state/mind/issues/<n>/` that records *what the watcher has been
noticing over time*. The `evolution/reinforcement-log.md` and
`evolution/ecology-review.md` in
[`03-runtime-pipeline.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/03-runtime-pipeline.md)
come closest, but they are owned by the `report` phase, not by a
standing supervisor.

### D — No recursion

Figure 6-1's three identical blobs imply a C-brain over the B-brain.
The plan has critics and censors over agencies; it has no critics
*of critics*. There is no `kind: meta-critic`, no run-level review
of the criticism layer, and no `b-brain.kline-retriever` that the
B-brain itself could consult. The recursive shape the figure asserts
as primitive stops at one level in the plan.

### E — No catalogue of "familiar bad shapes"

A B-brain's job is to recognise looping, daydreaming, redundancy,
fixation on the wrong sub-goal, and similar pattern-level failures.
The plan has objection `kind`s (`evidence`, `scope`, `cost`,
`privacy`, `risk`, `overconfidence`, `source-quality`, `staleness`,
`other` in
[`09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md)),
but no enumerated catalogue of *cognitive* failure modes — no
`looping`, no `unproductive-elaboration`, no `means-end-drift`. The
B-brain vocabulary is missing.

### F — No model of the A-brain's internal patterns

The B-brain reads patterns, not events. The plan's critics read
records (handoffs, signals, candidate actions) and the blackboard
prose, but there is no abstraction of "the A-brain's current shape"
— no compact representation of which agencies have been firing in
what order across the last N cycles. Without such a representation,
the watcher can only object to individual records, not to the
trajectory.

### G — No isolation of B-brain failure

In the figure, if the B-brain fails, the A-brain still has its
wiring to the world. In the plan, critics and censors run inside the
same workflow as the workers; an error in `criticize.ts` or
`censors.ts` will fail the whole job. The plan does not yet describe
a degraded mode in which the A-side proceeds without the watcher
(which would in any case need its own approval gate, since
fail-closed is the current discipline).

### H — Bundles are read, not interpreted as bundles

The figure's dense forward and return bundles imply that the
B-brain's signal *to* the A-brain is itself many-to-many: an
objection from one critic can excite or inhibit several agencies at
once. The plan supports `inhibits:` per manifest, but there is no
"the watcher's combined verdict" record — no equivalent of "the
B-brain has decided the A-brain is in a loop" that fans out to many
A-side agencies in a single broadcast.

## Implication for the plan (no changes proposed here)

Figure 6-1 is the figure that earns the word *supervisor* by drawing
one. The implementation plan honours the *function* of supervision
(critics, censors, fail-closed defaults, meta-admin reflection) at
the cycle scale, but does not yet honour the *anatomy* (a separate
brain with its own boundary, its own state, its own narrowed view,
and a layer of supervision over itself) at the runtime scale.

The gaps above are recorded here as analysis, not as a change
request. Any move to close them — for example, a `b-brain/` tree
parallel to `agencies/`, a `kind: meta-critic`, a constrained
B-brain read surface, a catalogue of cognitive failure modes, or a
"combined verdict" broadcast — would touch the manifest schema in
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md),
the pipeline in
[`03-runtime-pipeline.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/03-runtime-pipeline.md),
the policy layer in
[`07-policies-and-safety.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/07-policies-and-safety.md),
and the identity scopes in `THE-SOCIETY-OF-REPO/02-protocols/01-identity.md`,
and so falls under the "stop and ask" rules in
[`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md) §12 and
[`CLAUDE.md`](../../../../../CLAUDE.md).
