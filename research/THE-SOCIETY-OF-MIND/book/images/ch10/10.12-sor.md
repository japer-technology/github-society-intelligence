# Figure 10-12 — Implementation analysis (SOR / FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION)

**Source figure:** [10-12.md](10-12.md) — *The fully grown Society-of-More.*
**Analysis target:** [`FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/)
(plan-of-record for `.forgejo-society/` and
`.forgejo/workflows/forgejo-society.yaml`).

Figure 10-12 is the endpoint of the chapter's constructive argument:
the same four-level Society-of-More as [10-7](10-7.md), but now
*reached by repeated detour-building* rather than presented as a
finished design. This analysis records what the implementation can
say about reaching a steady state by accretion.

---

## The ideas Figure 10-12 actually carries

1. **A complex hierarchy is a finishable construction.** It is not
   built all at once and not built by editing — yet it does
   converge.
2. **The endpoint is identical regardless of construction order.**
   Figure 10-12 matches Figure 10-7 even though the path differs.
3. **Papert's principle is a *procedure*, not a *metaphor*.** The
   chapter's closing claim.
4. **Construction never halts the underlying society.** The system
   keeps working through every stage.
5. **The strata visible in the mature mind are *records* of past
   growth.** Each level is the fossil of a successful detour.
6. **Convergence to a *known* target is possible.** The figure
   matches a previously-published target.
7. **The end of construction is not the end of the system.** The
   society remains live, ready for the next round.

---

## What the implementation already absorbs

### 1 — Convergence to a target layout

The plan declares a target architecture
([`01-target-layout.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/01-target-layout.md))
and a sequenced path to reach it
([`10-bootstrap-checklist.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/10-bootstrap-checklist.md)).
This honours *"the endpoint is identical regardless of construction
order"* by fixing the endpoint in advance.

### 2 — Construction does not halt the society

The forge keeps serving issues, runners, PRs while the
implementation is bootstrapped. The plan's commitment to a
self-hosted Forgejo on owned hardware
([`WARNING.md`](../../../../../WARNING.md)) is the substrate guarantee
that *"the underlying society does not halt during construction."*

### 3 — Strata as records of past growth

Git history is the literal fossil record. Every commit that introduced
an agency is preserved. This delivers point 5 of the figure at the
version-control level.

### 4 — The system remains live at the end of construction

`forgejo-society` is described as ongoing rather than terminal in
[`00-overview.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/00-overview.md)
and
[`11-mapping-sor-to-implementation.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/11-mapping-sor-to-implementation.md).
The plan does not treat finishing the bootstrap as finishing the
society.

## What the implementation does not yet take into account

### A — Convergence by accretion is asserted, not measured

The plan describes a sequence
([`10-bootstrap-checklist.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/10-bootstrap-checklist.md))
but does not have a *convergence test* — no critic whose job is to
check *"does the live catalogue now match the target layout?"*
Reaching the figure's endpoint cannot be detected by the runtime.

### B — Strata as semantic records, not just commits

Git records *when* an agency was added; it does not record *what
detour it was part of, what older layer it superseded, what round of
growth it belongs to*. The figure's claim that each stratum is the
fossil of a *named* successful detour is preserved as prose at best,
never as a structured manifest relation.

### C — "The same endpoint regardless of construction order" is not enforced

Two repositories that both completed the bootstrap might in
principle arrive at different catalogues. The plan does not have a
*canonical reference catalogue* whose presence can be checked. The
figure's identity-of-endpoints claim has no enforcement mechanism.

### D — The post-construction round has no named successor

The figure leaves the system *ready for the next round*. The plan
ends at *"add the assembly tier when traffic justifies
summarisation"* and similar items in
[`10-bootstrap-checklist.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/10-bootstrap-checklist.md);
there is no document whose job is *"what is the next round of
growth after the catalogue stabilises?"*

### E — Recursive construction of the construction process

Each detour-build in 10-9/10-10/10-11 is itself a small society of
agents (the introducer, the exerciser, the promoter). The plan does
not have those *meta* agencies. `agency.meta-admin.*` is the closest
family in
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md),
but it does not yet contain *detour-introducer*, *shadow-exerciser*,
*promotion-recorder* members. Reaching figure 10-12 by the means
the chapter describes would require those members to exist.

## Implication for the plan (no changes proposed here)

Figure 10-12 is the figure that closes the chapter by saying *"the
endpoint is reachable, and you can see how."* The plan delivers the
endpoint-declaration and the sequenced path, runs on a substrate
that does not halt during construction, and treats the post-bootstrap
society as ongoing. What it does not yet deliver is a *convergence
test*, *semantic stratum records*, an enforced *identity of
endpoints*, a documented *next round*, or the *meta-agencies* that
would carry out detour-building as a first-class operation. Any
move to add a convergence critic, a `stratum:` relation on agency
manifests, a canonical reference catalogue, a *next-round* document
under
[`FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/),
or *detour-introducer / shadow-exerciser / promotion-recorder*
members of `agency.meta-admin.*` would touch
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md),
[`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md),
[`10-bootstrap-checklist.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/10-bootstrap-checklist.md),
and
[`11-mapping-sor-to-implementation.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/11-mapping-sor-to-implementation.md),
and so falls under the "stop and ask" rules in
[`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md) §12 and
[`CLAUDE.md`](../../../../../CLAUDE.md).
