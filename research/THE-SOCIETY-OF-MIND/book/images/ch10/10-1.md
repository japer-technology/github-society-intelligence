# Figure 10-1 — Piaget's eggs and cups, BEFORE

![figure](10-1.png)

**File:** `ch10/10-1.png`
**Appears in:** [../../som-10.1.md](../../som-10.1.md) — *Piaget's experiments*

## What the image shows

A captioned panel labelled **BEFORE**. Inside it, two parallel rows
sit one above the other: a row of five plain ovals (eggs) over a
matching row of five small goblet-shaped cups, each egg directly above
its cup. To the right, a caption reads, *"Most children, young or
old, say, 'They're the same.'"*

## What it illustrates

The starting condition of Piaget's conservation-of-number experiment.
With the eggs and cups in one-to-one correspondence, the equality is
obvious to every age. The figure establishes the baseline against
which the *after* panel ([10-2.md](10-2.md)) will reveal that
younger children abandon counting in favour of visual extent — the
opening puzzle that the rest of chapter 10 is built to explain.
