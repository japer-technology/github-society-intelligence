# Figure 10-6 — Implementation analysis (SOR / FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION)

**Source figure:** [10-6.md](10-6.md) — *A new inner layer: APPEARANCE and HISTORY.*
**Analysis target:** [`FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/)
(plan-of-record for `.forgejo-society/` and
`.forgejo/workflows/forgejo-society.yaml`).

Figure 10-6 is Papert's principle as a diagram: growth happens by
inserting a new administrative layer *between* the apex and the
existing leaves, not by adding more leaves. This analysis records
what the implementation does about middle-layer insertion.

---

## The ideas Figure 10-6 actually carries

1. **Growth by layer insertion.** Cognitive progress is a new layer,
   not new leaves.
2. **Administrative agents group existing skills by kind.**
   *APPEARANCE* and *HISTORY* are not new judges; they are *types*
   of judgement.
3. **The grouping itself is the substantive claim.** The choice of
   which leaves go under which administrator is the developmental
   step.
4. **The leaves remain unchanged.** *TALL*, *THIN*, *CONFINED* are
   identical in *Early Society* and *Later Society*.
5. **The apex remains unchanged.** *MORE* still sits at the top.
6. **A layer can be added without rewriting the system.** The figure
   shows the new layer simply inserted between two existing levels.
7. **The right grouping makes verdicts robust; the wrong grouping
   would not.** The figure's specific groupings are claims about
   what *kinds* of evidence cohere.

## What the implementation already absorbs

### 1 — An assembly tier exists

[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md)
catalogues `agency.assembly.summary-tier-1` and
`agency.assembly.summary-tier-2`, where tier-1 compresses raw
signals into per-family summaries and tier-2 compresses those into a
settlement-ready brief. This is a structurally similar
middle-layer-by-insertion pattern, applied to *outputs* rather than
to *agencies themselves*.

### 2 — Adding the layer is treated as a deferred step

[`10-bootstrap-checklist.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/10-bootstrap-checklist.md)
explicitly says *"add the assembly tier when traffic justifies
summarisation."* The plan recognises that middle layers are added,
not present from the start — matching the figure's *Early Society*
→ *Later Society* arc.

### 3 — Leaves and apex remain stable across the change

Adding the assembly tier does not require renaming or rewriting the
leaf agencies or the workflow's terminal phases. The figure's
"leaves and apex unchanged" property is preserved.

---

## What the implementation does not yet take into account

### A — No middle layer over *agencies* themselves

The assembly tier groups *signals*, not *agencies*. Figure 10-6
inserts *APPEARANCE* between *MORE* and the leaf judges — a layer
that groups *the judges*, not their outputs. The plan has no
`agency.appearance` that owns `tall`, `thin` and produces a single
*APPEARANCE-level verdict* by arbitrating between its children.

### B — "Group by kind of evidence" is not a catalogued axis

Families in
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md)
are organised by *function* (perception, memory, code, safety …),
not by *kind of evidence* (visual / historical, appearance / record).
The figure's groupings are along an *epistemic* axis the plan does
not currently use.

### C — Inserting a layer is not a documented operation

The plan describes a *target* layout; it does not document the
operation *"insert a new administrative agency between an existing
parent and its children, rewire activations, leave leaves
untouched"*. The closest piece is
`agency.meta-admin.differentiation-broker` named in template 1.2-sor
gap-F context, but that broker handles *splitting* (one → many), not
*inserting* (above existing).

### D — No record of which grouping was *rejected*

Papert's principle implies that the developmental progress is the
*choice* of grouping. A real cognitive history would record *"we
considered grouping by colour, we adopted grouping by extent."* The
plan's decisions memory under
`THE-SOCIETY-OF-REPO/06-memory/decisions/` records adopted
decisions, not the structural alternatives that were tried and
abandoned.

## Implication for the plan (no changes proposed here)

Figure 10-6 is the figure that the plan partially anticipates and
partially does not. The assembly tier is exactly *one* instance of
the right move (insert a layer that summarises outputs); the figure
asks for a *second* kind (insert a layer that arbitrates agencies).
Any move to add an agency-arbitrating middle layer, an epistemic
family axis, or a record of considered-but-rejected groupings would
touch
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md),
[`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md),
and
[`11-mapping-sor-to-implementation.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/11-mapping-sor-to-implementation.md),
and so falls under the "stop and ask" rules in
[`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md) §12 and
[`CLAUDE.md`](../../../../../CLAUDE.md).
