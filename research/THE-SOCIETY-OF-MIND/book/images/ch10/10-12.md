# Figure 10-12 — The fully grown Society-of-More

![figure](10-12.png)

**File:** `ch10/10-12.png`
**Appears in:** [../../som-10.9.md](../../som-10.9.md) — *Learning a hierarchy*

## What the image shows

The complete four-level hierarchy assembled by repeated detour
building. **MORE** at the apex; **APPEARANCE** and **HISTORY** below;
**SPATIAL**, **NUMERICAL**, **REVERSIBLE**, **CONFINED** below them;
and eight leaves — **EXTENT**, **DENSITY**, **COUNT**, **SPREAD**,
**MATCH**, **COMPRESS**, **ADD**, **REMOVE**.

## What it illustrates

The endpoint of the construction sequence that began with
[10-9.md](10-9.md). What started as a flat collection of conflicting
agents has, through successive insertion of administrative layers,
become the multi-level hierarchy first sketched in [10-7.md](10-7.md).
The figure closes the chapter's argument: Papert's principle is not a
metaphor but a constructive procedure that can be carried out by
adding strata one at a time without ever halting the underlying
society.
