# Figure 10-7 — Implementation analysis (SOR / FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION)

**Source figure:** [10-7.md](10-7.md) — *The Society-of-More for the eggs and cups.*
**Analysis target:** [`FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/)
(plan-of-record for `.forgejo-society/` and
`.forgejo/workflows/forgejo-society.yaml`).

Figure 10-7 is the first fully worked four-level hierarchy. It claims
that *"more"* — a single word in ordinary speech — is in fact the
top of a tree with two administrators, four mid-level coordinators,
and eight leaves. This analysis records what the implementation has
at each level.

---

## The ideas Figure 10-7 actually carries

1. **Four levels are required for one concept.** *MORE* needs three
   levels of structure beneath it to deliver a robust verdict.
2. **Branching factor varies by level.** *MORE* has two children,
   each of which has two children, each of which has two leaves.
3. **A coordinator withdraws when its children conflict.**
   *SPATIAL* hands over to *NUMERICAL* when *EXTENT* and *DENSITY*
   disagree.
4. **Two redundant paths exist for the same question.** Counting
   and history can each answer *more?* independently.
5. **Leaves are domain-specific; upper layers are reusable.** The
   leaves are about eggs; the administrators are about *kinds of
   evidence*.
6. **A correct verdict can emerge without a single
   knows-the-answer agent.** *MORE* itself does not know what
   *more* means; the structure decides.
7. **"Withdraw" is a verb at the coordinator level.** Failing to
   produce a verdict is itself a meaningful act.

---

## What the implementation already absorbs

### 1 — A multi-stage pipeline produces a single verdict

The phase chain in
[`02-workflow-design.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/02-workflow-design.md)
runs many agencies in many phases before `settle` produces one
outcome. The "no single agent knows the answer" property is honoured
at the workflow scale.

### 2 — Domain-specific leaves, reusable structure

[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md)
makes the leaf-vs-upper distinction explicit: families are
reusable, members are domain-pointed. This matches the figure's
*"leaves are about eggs; administrators are about kinds of
evidence"* shape.

### 3 — Abstain is a first-class verdict

A critic or agency may abstain rather than vote. This is the
operational form of the figure's *"withdraws when children
conflict"*.

---

## What the implementation does not yet take into account

### A — Four levels are not representable

This is the recurring gap from 1-2 (recursion) sharpened. The plan
supports two levels (family → member); the figure requires four
(apex → administrator → coordinator → leaf).
[`11-mapping-sor-to-implementation.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/11-mapping-sor-to-implementation.md)
maps SOR hierarchy to `agencies/assembly/*` but only for *output
summaries*, not for nested agency composition.

### B — Coordinator withdrawal does not cascade

In the figure, *SPATIAL* withdrawing transfers control to
*NUMERICAL*. The plan's `settle` step
([`03-runtime-pipeline.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/03-runtime-pipeline.md))
does not have a *handoff* rule of the form *"when this coordinator
abstains, activate that one."* Abstention is recorded; cascade is
not driven.

### C — Redundant paths are not declared

The figure presents two independent routes (NUMERICAL via counting,
HISTORY via add/remove) for answering the same question. The plan
has no `redundant_with:` or `alternative_path:` field that lets two
agency branches *declare* they cover the same evidential ground.

### D — Branching factor is not declared per level

Manifests do not say *"this agency expects exactly two children,
each producing a verdict."* The fan-out structure visible in 10-7
exists only as the count of files in a folder.

### E — A "kind of evidence" axis is not catalogued

*SPATIAL*, *NUMERICAL*, *REVERSIBLE*, *CONFINED* are evidence
categories. Families in
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md)
are functional categories. The vocabulary the figure depends on is
not present.

## Implication for the plan (no changes proposed here)

Figure 10-7 is the worked example that makes the gaps of 1-2 and 10-6
concrete on a single page. The plan delivers single-verdict outcomes
without a single all-knowing agent, but it cannot yet represent the
figure's four-level structure, its withdrawal-cascade, or its
declaration of redundant evidential paths. Any move to add nested
agency composition, a withdrawal-handoff rule, or a `redundant_with:`
relation would touch
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md),
[`03-runtime-pipeline.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/03-runtime-pipeline.md),
and
[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md),
and so falls under the "stop and ask" rules in
[`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md) §12 and
[`CLAUDE.md`](../../../../../CLAUDE.md).
