# Figure 10-8 — Implementation analysis (SOR / FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION)

**Source figure:** [10-8.md](10-8.md) — *The Society-of-More for the water jars.*
**Analysis target:** [`FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/)
(plan-of-record for `.forgejo-society/` and
`.forgejo/workflows/forgejo-society.yaml`).

Figure 10-8 is the companion of [10-7](10-7.md): the same upper
structure adapted to a different substrate by swapping the leaves.
Its central claim is that *administrative layers are portable across
domains*. This analysis records what the implementation does with
that claim.

---

## The ideas Figure 10-8 actually carries

1. **Upper layers are domain-independent.** *APPEARANCE*, *HISTORY*,
   *SPATIAL*, *QUANTITATIVE*, *REVERSIBLE*, *CONFINED* recur from
   10-7 unchanged.
2. **Leaves are domain-specific and replaceable.** *TALL*, *THIN*,
   *MEASURE*, *ESTIMATE*, *POUR*, *SPOON*, *LEAK*, *SPILL* are
   particular to liquids.
3. **A new domain is reached by adding leaves, not by re-architecting
   the upper layers.** Growth at the leaf level is cheap.
4. **The mid-level categories may shift slightly.** *NUMERICAL*
   becomes *QUANTITATIVE* — a small, principled rename to fit the
   continuous domain.
5. **Reversibility leaves remain stable across domains.** *POUR /
   SPOON / LEAK / SPILL* mirror *MATCH / COMPRESS / ADD / REMOVE*
   structurally even though the verbs differ.
6. **Architecture is a reusable asset.** The cognitive cost of
   handling a new domain is the cost of its leaves, not of its tree.

---

## What the implementation already absorbs

### 1 — Family structure is reusable

[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md)
fixes a small set of families (perception, memory, code, safety,
identity, integration, assembly, meta-admin) that recur across
deployments. Adding a new repository does not require new families,
only new members.

### 2 — Leaves are first-class and addable

The member-of-family pattern means new leaf agencies can be added
under an existing family without touching the family itself. This
honours *"growth at the leaf level is cheap."*

### 3 — Naming conventions support principled renames

The identifier protocol `agency.{family}.{name}` allows a member to
be renamed without breaking the family. The figure's
*NUMERICAL → QUANTITATIVE* shift is the kind of rename the
identifier scheme can express.

---

## What the implementation does not yet take into account

### A — Mid-level categories are not first-class

The figure has *SPATIAL / QUANTITATIVE / REVERSIBLE / CONFINED* as
mid-level coordinators. The plan does not have a level between
family and member where category-like coordinators live. Renaming
*NUMERICAL → QUANTITATIVE* in the plan would mean renaming nothing —
because there is no such node.

### B — Cross-domain isomorphism is not asserted

The plan can describe two member agencies, but it has no schema for
*"this member is the water-domain counterpart of that one in the
eggs domain."* The structural mirror between *MATCH/COMPRESS/ADD/
REMOVE* and *POUR/SPOON/LEAK/SPILL* is invisible to the
implementation.

### C — A "new domain" is not a unit of work

There is no `domain:` field on a manifest, no `domains/` directory
under `.forgejo-society/`, and no checklist item in
[`10-bootstrap-checklist.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/10-bootstrap-checklist.md)
of the form *"add leaves for a new domain while reusing the upper
tree."* The reuse claim of the figure is not operationalised as a
workflow.

### D — Continuous-quantity leaves still have no schema

This is gap C from [10-3](10.3-sor.md) reappearing. *MEASURE*,
*ESTIMATE*, *LEAK*, *SPILL* all carry magnitudes the signal
vocabulary in
[`09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md)
does not catalogue.

## Implication for the plan (no changes proposed here)

Figure 10-8 is the figure that says *the value of a society is the
architecture, not the leaves*. The plan delivers the leaf-cheapness
property at the family level, but does not yet have mid-level
coordinators, a cross-domain isomorphism schema, or a *"new domain"*
unit of work. Any move to add a mid-level coordinator layer, an
`isomorphic_to:` relation, or a domain-onboarding checklist would
touch
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md),
[`09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md),
and
[`10-bootstrap-checklist.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/10-bootstrap-checklist.md),
and so falls under the "stop and ask" rules in
[`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md) §12 and
[`CLAUDE.md`](../../../../../CLAUDE.md).
