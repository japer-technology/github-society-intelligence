# Figure 11-1 — Implementation analysis (SOR / FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION)

**Source figure:** [11-1.md](11-1.md) — *From sensors to the word "red".*
**Analysis target:** [`FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/)
(plan-of-record for `.forgejo-society/` and
`.forgejo/workflows/forgejo-society.yaml`).

This file inventories the ideas carried by Figure 11-1 and checks each
one against the implementation plan. The aim is to mark, plainly, what
the plan already absorbs and what it does not yet name.

---

## The ideas Figure 11-1 actually carries

Pulled from the figure caption and Minsky's surrounding text in §11.1
*Seeing red*:

1. **A minimal naming chain.** Sensors → detector → pronouncer →
   spoken word. Three steps suffice to *say* the name of a colour.
2. **Specialists per layer.** Hue sensors do not classify; the
   red-agent does not pronounce; the pronouncer does not look. Each
   step knows exactly one job.
3. **Symbol grounding by activation.** The sensor's output, not its
   reading, wakes the red-agent. Meaning is what gets turned on, not
   what is computed.
4. **A "pronouncing agent" as terminal voice.** Whatever the chain
   concludes leaves the head through a single named output unit.
5. **The chain is embedded in a wider society.** The red-agent sits
   in a cluster of mental agencies; its activation depends on context
   from many neighbours, not on the sensors alone.
6. **Naming is not seeing.** The figure exists to be denied: even
   when the chain works perfectly, nothing in it amounts to the
   experience of red.
7. **The trap of the thin chain.** A short input → label → output
   pipeline is *seductively* sufficient — it always produces an
   answer — and that is precisely why Minsky warns against it.
8. **Texture, form, memory, and meaning are missing.** The figure
   silently flags what a real percept would also have to recruit.

## What the implementation already absorbs

### 1 — A single terminal voice

[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md)
names `agency.integration.conscious-presenter` as the *sole* producer
of Spock's visible response, and `outputs: [final_response]` is
restricted to narrators. This is the pronouncing agent at workflow
scale: one unit owns the mouth.

### 2 — Specialists per layer

The first-ship perception family (`intake-bee`,
`issue-kind-detector`, `ambiguity-detector`, `urgency-detector`)
splits intake the way Figure 11-1 splits seeing: each unit does one
narrow job and hands on to the next.

### 3 — Activation rather than computation

[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md)
makes signals, polynemes, and K-lines the way one unit wakes another.
A signal *fires*; downstream agencies *light up*. This matches the
figure's "sensor turns on the red-agent" verb rather than a
"sensor computes red" verb.

### 4 — A pipeline that embeds the chain

The phase chain `normalize → perceive → activate → deliberate →
criticize → censor → settle → act → remember → report`
([`03-runtime-pipeline.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/03-runtime-pipeline.md))
is a longer version of Figure 11-1: input is normalised, named,
deliberated, and only then voiced.

### 5 — The wider society is structurally available

`activates_on.frames` and inhibitory signals
([`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md))
let any "detector-style" agency be gated by frame and context, not
by its input alone. The mechanism for embedding a narrow detector in
a wider cluster exists.

## What the implementation does not yet take into account

### A — No sensor primitives

Figure 11-1 starts with *hue sensors* — typed, raw, pre-symbolic
inputs. The plan starts at `normalize`, which already produces a
structured `stimulus.json`
([`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md)).
There is no class of unit below `agency.perception.*` that *only*
turns an external surface signal into an internal signal. The chain
begins one step too late to model the figure literally.

### B — No "agency that names X" pattern

There is `issue-kind-detector` and `ambiguity-detector`, but no
schema slot that says "this agency exists *solely* to attach the
name X when feature Y is present." Naming is fused into perception
agencies' prose bodies rather than declared.

### C — Pronouncer–detector decoupling is implicit only

The figure separates *deciding it is red* from *saying "red"*. The
plan has `conscious-presenter` as the speaker and other agencies as
the deciders, but nothing in the manifest schema *prevents* a
detector from also producing user-visible text. The discipline is
present in the catalogue, not in the validator.

### D — No explicit "thin chain" warning

Minsky's whole point in Figure 11-1 is that the chain looks
sufficient and is not. The plan does not carry a critic or censor
whose job is to object when a settlement was reached via a short
detect-and-pronounce path with no recruitment of memory, frame
slots, or contradiction-finders. The `code-change` frame's required
slots come closest, but there is no equivalent guard on a *naming*
or *classification* response.

### E — Texture, form, memory, meaning are not required

Figure 11-1's implicit claim — that a real percept also recruits
texture, form, memory, meaning — has no operational analogue. There
is no required-slot list on a perception frame that demands a
memory-agency activation before settlement. The presenter could, in
principle, voice a label without `prior-decision-resonator` or
`kline-retriever` ever having fired.

### F — The figure's wider cluster has no first-class name

The figure shows the red-agent inside a *cluster of mental
agencies*. The plan has families (`agencies/perception/`,
`agencies/memory/`, …) but no manifest for the cluster itself, and
no way to say "this signal must traverse at least two clusters
before reaching the presenter." This is the same family-as-folder
gap noted elsewhere, recurring here in a perceptual context.

## Implication for the plan (no changes proposed here)

Figure 11-1 honours the *shape* the plan already uses (narrow
specialists feeding a single voice) while warning against the very
sufficiency that shape produces. The plan absorbs the shape; it does
not yet absorb the warning. Closing gaps D and E — for example, by
adding a `critic.thin-chain` or by extending frames with required
memory-recruitment slots — would touch the manifest schema in
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md)
and the frame schema in
[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md),
and so falls under the "stop and ask" rules in
[`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md) §12 and
[`CLAUDE.md`](../../../../../CLAUDE.md).
