# Figure 11-4 — Implementation analysis (SOR / FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION)

**Source figure:** [11-4.md](11-4.md) — *Pairs of opposing words.*
**Analysis target:** [`FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/)
(plan-of-record for `.forgejo-society/` and
`.forgejo/workflows/forgejo-society.yaml`).

This file inventories the ideas carried by Figure 11-4 and checks each
one against the implementation plan. The aim is to mark, plainly, what
the plan already absorbs and what it does not yet name.

---

## The ideas Figure 11-4 actually carries

Pulled from the figure caption and Minsky's surrounding text in §11.9
*Dumbbell theories*:

1. **Two-pole carving is the default human move.** Good/Bad,
   Right/Wrong, Same/Different — the catalogue is large because the
   habit is universal.
2. **Oppositions are useful starting points.** They give a first cut
   when nothing else is available.
3. **Oppositions are dangerous as resting points.** Stopping at two
   poles hides the third (and fourth) alternative.
4. **The missing middle is the real subject.** Most interesting cases
   live between, beside, or orthogonal to the two named ends.
5. **The catalogue is itself an exhibit.** Showing the pairs side by
   side makes their structural sameness visible — and that sameness
   is the warning, not the insight.
6. **One opposition invites projection onto another.** Once *X / Y*
   feels familiar, *A / B* gets read as another instance of it.
7. **Some oppositions are real distinctions; some are conveniences.**
   The figure does not deny that any pair carves anything; it denies
   that all pairs carve well.
8. **Vocabulary commits us in advance.** Naming the poles *first*
   shapes the questions we then ask.

## What the implementation already absorbs

### 1 — Critic / agency duality, used carefully

The catalogue
([`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md))
uses pairs like `patch-imaginer` / `diff-skeptic` and `implementer`
/ `revert-path-finder` deliberately — proposer plus sceptic. This is
the "oppositions as starting points" use, kept honest by the fact
that both sides are named units rather than abstract poles.

### 2 — Authority is graded, not binary

Authority levels are `read`, `draft`, `propose`, `act`, `govern`,
`human` — six steps
([`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md),
[`07-policies-and-safety.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/07-policies-and-safety.md)).
The plan explicitly refuses to collapse capability into a
permitted/forbidden dumbbell.

### 3 — Settlement is multi-outcome

Settlement (`workspace/active-settlements/<id>.yml`,
[`03-runtime-pipeline.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/03-runtime-pipeline.md))
produces a structured outcome, not an accept/reject vote. The
runtime is shaped to record nuance rather than a single bit.

### 4 — Critic responsibilities are named, not coded as opposites

A critic is a named unit with a job
([`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md)),
not "the negative of agency X." This avoids the figure's chief
trap: defining a unit purely as the opposite of another.

## What the implementation does not yet take into account

### A — No critic against binary framing

There is no `critic.dumbbell` whose job is to flag candidate
responses (or settlements) that quietly carve the situation into
two camps when a third option exists. The catalogue can be honest
in its own structure without policing the *content* of what Spock
says.

### B — No "third alternative" required slot

Frames
([`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md))
have required slots (`relevant_files`, `proposed_patch`, `risks`,
…) but none called `alternatives_considered` or
`third_option_examined`. A response that proposes exactly one
option and rejects it can still settle.

### C — Settlement can still effectively collapse to accept/reject

Although a settlement record is structured, the *visible outcome* —
comment, PR, or no-op — has a small set of shapes. The richness in
the record is not always carried through to the response, which
makes the act surface look more dumbbell-shaped than the cognition
behind it.

### D — Vocabulary commitments are not audited

The plan inherits a vocabulary (agency / critic / censor;
propose / act; draft / govern) that itself contains useful
oppositions. Nothing checks whether new agencies, frames, or
critics are being named in ways that quietly create new dumbbells
(e.g. a `fast` agency implied as the opposite of a `careful` one).

### E — No catalogue-level reflection

Figure 11-4 works by *showing the pairs at once* so the reader sees
the shared template. The plan has no view, report, or scheduled
review that lists all named oppositions in the catalogue side by
side. A `report` step
([`03-runtime-pipeline.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/03-runtime-pipeline.md))
exists per run; an opposition audit across the whole catalogue does
not.

### F — Honest scope for "useful starting point"

The figure concedes that oppositions are useful early. The plan
does not distinguish *provisional* oppositions (admitted as
scaffolding, to be replaced) from *committed* ones (kept on
purpose). Every pairing currently reads as permanent.

## Implication for the plan (no changes proposed here)

Figure 11-4 catalogues a habit the plan partly resists (graded
authority, structured settlement) and partly inherits (a vocabulary
of paired units, an act surface with few shapes). The cognition can
be richer than the surface it settles to. Closing gaps A, B, and E
— for example, a `critic.dumbbell`, an `alternatives_considered`
slot on the more deliberative frames, or a periodic opposition
audit run by `meta-admin` — would touch the manifest schema in
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md),
the frame schema in
[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md),
and the policy surface in
[`07-policies-and-safety.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/07-policies-and-safety.md),
and so falls under the "stop and ask" rules in
[`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md) §12 and
[`CLAUDE.md`](../../../../../CLAUDE.md).
