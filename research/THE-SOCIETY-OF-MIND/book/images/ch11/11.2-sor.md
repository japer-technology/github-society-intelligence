# Figure 11-2 — Implementation analysis (SOR / FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION)

**Source figure:** [11-2.md](11-2.md) — *Parallel nerve bundles from skin
to brain.*
**Analysis target:** [`FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/)
(plan-of-record for `.forgejo-society/` and
`.forgejo/workflows/forgejo-society.yaml`).

This file inventories the ideas carried by Figure 11-2 and checks each
one against the implementation plan. The aim is to mark, plainly, what
the plan already absorbs and what it does not yet name.

---

## The ideas Figure 11-2 actually carries

Pulled from the figure caption and Minsky's surrounding text in §11.3
*Nearnesses*:

1. **Topology preservation.** Nearness on the skin tends to map to
   nearness in the brain.
2. **Many parallel wires.** The mapping is carried by a *bundle*, not
   a single channel; redundancy is part of the design.
3. **Approximate, not exact.** Most lines run straight; a few cross
   over. The mapping is faithful enough to be useful and irregular
   enough to be noticeable.
4. **Topology can be discovered by correlation.** A receiver agency
   can learn which inputs are "near" each other purely from the fact
   that they fire together, without any prewired map.
5. **Tidying is required.** Because the bundle is messy, some later
   agency must straighten out the irregularities before the topology
   is usable.
6. **The source surface has structure of its own.** The skin is not a
   flat list of inputs; it has a 2-D layout that the brain ends up
   *re-representing*.
7. **No central wiring diagram.** The brain does not consult a map of
   the skin; it grows one from the statistics of what arrives.
8. **The two surfaces are different media.** Skin spots and brain
   spots are not the same kind of thing, but the *relations between
   them* can be carried across.

## What the implementation already absorbs

### 1 — Many parallel wires

Signals, polynemes, and K-lines
([`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md))
are deliberately *many*: any agency may emit signals, and any other
may activate on them. The activation surface is a bundle, not a
single bus.

### 2 — Redundancy through families

The first-ship perception, memory, and code families
([`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md))
contain several agencies whose activation regions overlap. If one
detector misses, a neighbour often catches. This is the bundle
property in coarse form.

### 3 — Relations between media

Polynemes
([`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md))
exist precisely to carry a *relation* (a path, a label, a phrase)
across heterogeneous units. They are the implementation's answer to
"how does structure on one surface arrive on another."

### 4 — Co-activation is recorded

`state/mind/issues/<n>/activation.jsonl` and `signals.jsonl`
([`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md))
already capture which agencies fired together on which stimuli. The
raw material for correlation-based learning exists in episodic
state.

### 5 — K-lines as durable nearness

`memory/klines/**` records bundles of units that succeeded together.
A K-line is the closest operational analogue to "these inputs are
near each other because they fired together."

## What the implementation does not yet take into account

### A — Topology is not learnt from co-activation

Although `activation.jsonl` records co-firing, nothing in the
runtime distils it into a *map*. There is no module that promotes
"these two signals consistently arrive together" into a structural
fact about the signal space. K-lines record successful bundles,
not the topology of the input surface itself.

### B — Signals are declared, not discovered

A new signal name comes into being only when an agency manifest
mentions it. The figure's *bundle* metaphor — many wires, some of
which simply *exist* and reveal their structure later — has no
counterpart. The plan cannot answer "what signals showed up that we
never declared?"

### C — No tidying agency

Figure 11-2 needs a downstream unit that smooths irregularities in
the projection. The plan has critics that object to candidate
actions, but none whose job is to *clean up* the signal-to-agency
mapping over time. Drift in activation patterns is logged, not
straightened.

### D — The source surface has no first-class structure

The Forgejo "skin" — labels, paths, file trees, comment threads,
event types — is a structured surface. The plan reads it through
`stimulus.json`
([`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md))
but does not maintain a representation of *the surface itself*
(e.g. "which paths are neighbours," "which labels co-occur"). The
brain side of the diagram exists; the skin side is flattened.

### E — No approximate-projection vocabulary

The figure's "mostly straight, occasionally crossed" character has
no schema entry. An activation rule either fires or it does not;
there is no notion of a *typical* projection with declared
exceptions. The "tidying" gap (C) follows partly from this:
without an idea of typical-vs-exception, there is nothing for a
tidying unit to enforce.

### F — Correlation-based growth is not a sanctioned evolution path

`self-modification.frame.yml`
([`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md))
gates schema changes, but the *trigger* for proposing a new signal
or a new polyneme from observed correlations is not specified.
Evolution by activation statistics — the figure's main payoff — is
neither encouraged nor forbidden; it simply has no slot.

## Implication for the plan (no changes proposed here)

Figure 11-2 is about *learning a layout from traffic*. The plan
already records the traffic (`activation.jsonl`, `signals.jsonl`,
K-lines) and carries relations across media (polynemes). What it
does not do is treat the layout itself as a thing that can be
inferred, tidied, and stored. Closing gaps A, C, and F — for
example, a co-activation distiller, a tidying agency, or a
sanctioned "signal proposed by statistics" path — would touch the
manifest schema in
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md),
the activation protocol in
[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md),
and the memory layout in
[`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md),
and so falls under the "stop and ask" rules in
[`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md) §12 and
[`CLAUDE.md`](../../../../../CLAUDE.md).
