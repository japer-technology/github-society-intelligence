# Figure 11-5 — Implementation analysis (SOR / FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION)

**Source figure:** [11-5.md](11-5.md) — *The Thought / Feeling dumbbell.*
**Analysis target:** [`FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/)
(plan-of-record for `.forgejo-society/` and
`.forgejo/workflows/forgejo-society.yaml`).

This file inventories the ideas carried by Figure 11-5 and checks each
one against the implementation plan. The aim is to mark, plainly, what
the plan already absorbs and what it does not yet name.

---

## The ideas Figure 11-5 actually carries

Pulled from the figure caption and Minsky's surrounding text in §11.9
*Dumbbell theories*:

1. **One template, many oppositions.** Thought / Feeling, Reason /
   Emotion, Logical / Analogical, Mechanical / Vital, Cool / Warm,
   Head / Heart all align in the same direction.
2. **Apparent unity from shared template.** The columns *feel*
   profound because they corroborate each other, not because they
   discovered the same fact.
3. **Cross-contamination of analogies.** Once Thought / Feeling is
   accepted, any new pair gets slotted into it.
4. **The alignment is the trick.** Drawing the pairs in two columns
   makes the contamination visible; left ungraphed, it goes
   unnoticed.
5. **Cultural taxonomy disguised as discovery.** The pairs come from
   inherited vocabulary, not from analysis.
6. **The Thought / Feeling carving in particular is contagious.** It
   is the dumbbell most prone to invading other taxonomies.
7. **Resistance requires explicit register choice.** Without a stated
   stance on tone, the system will drift toward one column or the
   other.
8. **Refusing the dumbbell is not refusing the distinctions.** Some
   of the underlying pairs are real; only their *alignment* is the
   artefact.

## What the implementation already absorbs

### 1 — Explicit register, single voice

`agency.identity.tone-stabilizer` and
`agency.identity.spock-self-model`
([`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md))
hold the response to a single calm register defined in
[`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md). The visible voice does not
swing between *Cool* and *Warm* columns; it stays on a chosen line.

### 2 — Soul-file guardian against silent drift

`agency.identity.soul-file-guardian` vetoes edits to `AGENTS.md` and
`APPEND_SYSTEM.md` outside the `self-modification` frame
([`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md),
[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md)).
The vocabulary that defines the register is itself protected from
casual change.

### 3 — Conscious presenter as the sole speaker

A single narrator owns the user-visible output
([`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md)),
which limits how far any "Thought" or "Feeling"-flavoured agency
can colour the final text on its own.

### 4 — Honest scope guidance in the project voice

Style guidance — describe what exists, mark what is planned — is
inherited from [`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md) and
[`CLAUDE.md`](../../../../../CLAUDE.md). This is the operational
analogue of "refuse the contagious carving": describe parts, not
poles.

## What the implementation does not yet take into account

### A — No detector for Thought / Feeling slotting

Nothing in the catalogue flags a draft response that, e.g.,
implicitly treats `code` agencies as "logical / cool" and
`identity` agencies as "warm / vital." The tone guard polices
*register*; it does not police *taxonomy*.

### B — No critic for analogical contamination

The figure's chief warning — that an accepted opposition pulls
unrelated pairs into its template — has no `critic.contamination`.
A settlement can rest on an unstated analogy ("X is to Y as Thought
is to Feeling") without objection.

### C — The catalogue itself is not audited for template effects

There is no scheduled review that lays out current agency names,
critic names, and frame names side by side to check whether they
have quietly aligned into a single dumbbell. The opposition audit
flagged for Figure 11-4 (gap E there) would also serve this figure;
it does not exist yet.

### D — Register is held; column-projection is not refused

The tone guard keeps Spock on one register, but the *cognition*
behind the response can still be organised around a Thought /
Feeling split (e.g. "the logical side says X, the intuitive side
says Y") and then be smoothed into calm prose by the narrator.
Register stability can mask, rather than resolve, the underlying
slotting.

### E — Real distinctions vs. artefacts are not separated

The plan does not mark which of its own pairings (proposer /
sceptic, act / inhibit, draft / govern, perception / memory) it
considers *real distinctions* and which it considers *useful
scaffolding*. Without that mark, the figure's eighth point —
"refusing the dumbbell is not refusing the distinctions" — has no
schema home.

### F — No defence against inherited cultural pairs

New agencies, critics, and frames are named freely. Nothing
specifically resists imports of culturally loaded oppositions
(*hard / soft*, *machine / human*, *cold / warm*) into the
manifest namespace. The identifier protocol enforces shape, not
flavour.

## Implication for the plan (no changes proposed here)

Figure 11-5 sharpens Figure 11-4 into a specific warning: a Thought
/ Feeling carving is the dumbbell most likely to swallow the others.
The plan defends the *voice* against this carving (tone guard,
single presenter, protected soul files) but does not yet defend the
*cognition* or the *catalogue*. Closing gaps A, B, and E — for
example, a contamination critic, an explicit list of which pairings
the project considers real vs. scaffolding, or naming-flavour
guidance in the identifier protocol — would touch the manifest
schema in
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md),
the frame schema in
[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md),
and `THE-SOCIETY-OF-REPO/02-protocols/01-identity.md`, and so falls
under the "stop and ask" rules in
[`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md) §12 and
[`CLAUDE.md`](../../../../../CLAUDE.md).
