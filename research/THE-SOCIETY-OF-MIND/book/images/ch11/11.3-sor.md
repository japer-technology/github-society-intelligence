# Figure 11-3 — Implementation analysis (SOR / FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION)

**Source figure:** [11-3.md](11-3.md) — *Mirror-image hemispheres.*
**Analysis target:** [`FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/)
(plan-of-record for `.forgejo-society/` and
`.forgejo/workflows/forgejo-society.yaml`).

This file inventories the ideas carried by Figure 11-3 and checks each
one against the implementation plan. The aim is to mark, plainly, what
the plan already absorbs and what it does not yet name.

---

## The ideas Figure 11-3 actually carries

Pulled from the figure caption and Minsky's surrounding text in §11.8
*Half-brains*:

1. **Paired architecture.** Most agencies come in two, not one.
2. **Mirror duplication, not random pairing.** The two halves start
   as approximate copies of the same plan.
3. **Massive cross-connection.** A thick bundle of fibres joins the
   pairs; the pairing is functional only because the channel is wide.
4. **Symmetry as redundancy.** Either half can carry the work if the
   other fails; the architecture tolerates loss.
5. **Symmetry as a substrate for differentiation.** Twin copies are
   what make later specialisation cheap — one side can drift without
   destroying the other.
6. **Refusal of the left-brain / right-brain myth.** Symmetry does
   not license a clean reason-vs-feeling carving.
7. **The midline is a structural commitment.** *Where* the split
   runs, and *how wide* the cross-channel is, are first-class
   architectural choices, not incidental.
8. **Pairs can diverge but stay coupled.** The point of the band of
   fibres is that the two specialised halves keep talking.

## What the implementation already absorbs

### 1 — Differentiation as a first-class move

[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md)
routes splits through `agency.meta-admin.differentiation-broker` and
the `self-modification` frame
([`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md)).
The mechanism for *one becomes two* exists; it is just not seeded by
mirror pairing.

### 2 — Critic / agency duality at the catalogue level

Several agencies have shadow critics (`patch-imaginer` vs
`diff-skeptic`, `implementer` vs `revert-path-finder`,
`blast-radius-fear` vs `permission-minimizer`). This is a soft form
of pairing — a unit and its sceptical counterpart — even though it
is not a mirror in Minsky's sense.

### 3 — A wide cross-channel exists

The signal/polyneme bus
([`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md))
gives every agency a way to reach every other. If two paired
agencies needed to stay coupled, the bandwidth is available.

### 4 — Tone and identity guards refuse simple dualism

`agency.identity.tone-stabilizer` and
`agency.identity.spock-self-model`
([`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md))
enforce a single calm register. This structurally resists the
left-brain / right-brain temptation at the *voice* layer, even
without paired agencies.

## What the implementation does not yet take into account

### A — No pairing primitive

There is no manifest field that says "this agency has a paired
sibling." A pair is at best two manifests in the same folder; the
*pairing* itself is invisible to the runtime. Nothing can ask
"who is X's mirror?"

### B — No mirror-duplication path

The differentiation-broker can produce a child, but there is no
sanctioned move that *seeds a new agency as an exact copy of an
existing one and lets the two drift*. Splits start narrow; they do
not start as twins.

### C — No high-bandwidth pair channel

Although the signal bus is wide, there is no notion of a *private*
or *prioritised* channel between two paired agencies. Whatever they
say to each other is broadcast on the same bus as everything else,
which is the opposite of the figure's dedicated commissural bundle.

### D — Redundancy / fail-over is not declared

The plan has budgets (`budget.max_tool_calls`,
`budget.max_wall_clock_s`,
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md))
and inhibition, but no rule of the form "if agency X is silent or
exhausted, its mirror Y takes the role." Loss-tolerance through
pairing is absent.

### E — No critic against the half-brain myth

Figure 11-3's polemical point — *do not collapse pairs into clean
reason-vs-feeling labels* — has no enforcement. Nothing prevents a
later author from describing, say, `code` agencies as "the logical
half" and `identity` agencies as "the feeling half." The tone guard
keeps the *voice* even; it does not police the *taxonomy*.

### F — The midline is not modelled

Where pairs would split, and how wide the bridge would be, are
architectural questions the plan does not ask. The current
architecture has a single bus and many units; it has no concept of
a *split with a bridge*. Introducing pairing without first naming
the midline would reproduce the same single-bus shape with twice as
many agencies.

## Implication for the plan (no changes proposed here)

Figure 11-3 argues that pairing-with-a-bridge is a *primitive*, not a
late refinement. The plan today has units and a bus; it has neither
pairs nor bridges, and it has no rule against the half-brain myth
the figure was drawn to refute. Closing gaps A, C, and E — for
example, a `paired_with:` manifest field, a dedicated cross-channel
between pairs, or a critic that flags reason-vs-feeling carvings of
the catalogue — would touch the manifest schema in
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md),
the activation protocol in
[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md),
and the identity scopes in
`THE-SOCIETY-OF-REPO/02-protocols/01-identity.md`, and so falls
under the "stop and ask" rules in
[`AGENTS.md`](../../../../../AGENTS.md) §12 and
[`CLAUDE.md`](../../../../../CLAUDE.md).
