# Section 19.8 — Implementation analysis (SOR / FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION)

**Source section:** [som-19.8.md](som-19.8.md) — *Generalizing*
(Minsky, §19.8).
**Analysis target:** [`FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/`](../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/)
(plan-of-record for `.forgejo-society/` and
`.forgejo/workflows/forgejo-society.yaml`).

§19.8 is the chapter's most epistemically humble section: there is no
foolproof generalization scheme. Minds *accumulate societies of
learning-schemes* that differ in quality and kind. The section relates
generalization to representation — what we already know shapes what
plausibly generalises — and quotes Hofstadter: variation works
because concepts are divisible into significant subconceptual
elements.

---

## The ideas Section 19.8 actually carries

1. **There is no foolproof generalization.** Whatever is learned may
   turn out wrong.
2. **Minds use *many* learning schemes.** Not one rule but a society
   of them.
3. **Generalization depends on representation.** The same evidence
   suggests different generalizations under different
   representations.
4. **Uniframes** — descriptions that suppress insignificant
   details — are one route.
5. **Level-bands** are another.
6. **Polynemes themselves generalize** by combining expectations
   over independent properties.
7. **Weight perturbation produces useful variants.** Changing a
   recognizer's weights yields nearby recognizers; *if* they are
   composed from already-useful subconceptual elements.
8. **Variation is not magic.** It is the consequence of the
   divisibility of concepts.

---

## What the implementation already absorbs

### Many schemes, not one (idea 2)

The plan composes generalization out of several mechanisms:
frame `default_actions` reuse expectations; K-lines reuse prior
configurations; polynemes route shared symbols; analogies fall back
when nothing else matches
([`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md)).
No single mechanism is the "general intelligence layer."

### Polynemes as generalization by composition (idea 6)

A polyneme's `excite:` map is exactly the composition idea: the
symbol arouses many *independent* property axes (the families noted
in §19.4-sor), and a candidate response emerges from their joint
state. The reuse of `directive.*` phrases across stimuli is a
working generalization scheme.

### Variation by recombination of subconceptual elements (idea 8)

Frame slots, polyneme weights, and K-line cues are themselves
reusable subconceptual elements. Imagination branches
(`society/<stimulus_id>/candidate-<n>` in
[`03-runtime-pipeline.md`](../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/03-runtime-pipeline.md))
let `agency.code.patch-imaginer` propose variants whose pieces have
already proved useful. Hofstadter's claim is honoured at the *code*
level.

### Generalization may be wrong (idea 1)

The plan's whole posture — `propose` and `act` separated from
`govern`, critics that object, censors that veto, the human approval
gate — assumes that any retrieved generalization may be wrong.
[`07-policies-and-safety.md`](../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/07-policies-and-safety.md)
codifies the conservatism.

## What the implementation does not yet take into account

### A — No uniframes

Idea 4. The plan has frames (`*.frame.yml`) and analogies
(`memory/analogies/`), but no *uniframe* — no agent or schema that
produces a description by *suppressing* details deemed insignificant.
Frames are inclusive (slot lists); the inverse move (drop everything
but what matters) is not represented.

### B — No level-bands

Idea 5. The plan has `agency.assembly.summary-tier-1` and
`agency.assembly.summary-tier-2`
([`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md)),
which are *summarisation* tiers, not *level-band* descriptions. A
level-band is a description appropriate to a *scale* of phenomenon
(e.g. "this is a routine documentation change" vs "this is a
governance-touching change"). The plan has frames for the latter
distinction but not graded-level descriptions of the former.

### C — No mechanism for proposing recognizer variants

Idea 7 expects new recognizers to be generated by perturbing the
weights of existing ones. The plan has no pass that says "take the
`code-change` frame, vary one slot's `filled_by`, and propose the
variant for trial." `agency.meta-admin.differentiation-broker`
proposes splitting an over-broad agency, which is close — but it
requires the meta-admin to author the split, not to generate
variants automatically.

### D — Representation choice is not a first-class question

Idea 3 says generalization depends on representation. The plan's
representations (manifest, frame, polyneme, K-line) are fixed by the
schemas. No agency or critic is tasked with asking *is this the right
representation for this stimulus?* The representation-discipline
protocol
([`THE-SOCIETY-OF-REPO/02-protocols/09-representation.md`](../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-INTRODUCTION/THE-SOCIETY-OF-REPO/02-protocols/09-representation.md))
constrains *where* representations live; it does not ask *which*.

### E — Wrongness is detected late, not modelled early

Idea 1's "what we learn may turn out wrong" is handled in the plan by
*outcome* mechanisms — settlement, critics, the `outcome.json`
record. There is no early *expected-wrongness* annotation on a
generalization: a K-line's `reuse_count` records how often it was
applied, not its hit rate, and there is no field for "this
generalisation has been wrong N times."

## Implication for the plan (no changes proposed here)

§19.8 is the section the plan honours most strongly in posture
(humility about generalization) and most weakly in mechanism
(uniframes, level-bands, weight perturbation, and a representation
critic are all absent). The most useful unforced opportunity is gap
E — a `hit_rate` field on K-lines, computed from
`evolution/reinforcement-log.md`, so that wrongness is visible *before*
the next reactivation. Any such addition would touch the K-line
schema in
[`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md),
the credit-assignment runtime in
[`03-runtime-pipeline.md`](../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/03-runtime-pipeline.md),
the credit-assignment protocol in
[`THE-SOCIETY-OF-REPO/02-protocols/10-credit-assignment.md`](../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-INTRODUCTION/THE-SOCIETY-OF-REPO/02-protocols/10-credit-assignment.md),
and the evolution surfaces in
[`THE-SOCIETY-OF-REPO/10-evolution/`](../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-INTRODUCTION/THE-SOCIETY-OF-REPO/10-evolution/),
and so falls under the "stop and ask" rules in
[AGENTS.md](../../../AGENTS.md) §12 and [CLAUDE.md](../../../CLAUDE.md).
