# Section 18.9 — Implementation analysis (SOR / FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION)

**Source section:** [som-18.9.md](som-18.9.md) — *Robustness and recovery*
**Analysis target:** [`FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/`](../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/)
(plan-of-record for `.forgejo-society/` and
`.forgejo/workflows/forgejo-society.yaml`).

§18.9 closes the chapter with a structural inventory of *how* a mind
keeps working while it is being changed, injured, or partially lost.
Minsky names four mechanisms: duplication of function across many
agents, self-repair of damaged parts, distribution of each function
across many places, and accumulation of alternative sub-agents
under each supervisor. The last is the most psychologically
interesting: ordinary learning, by accumulation, is also the most
generous source of robustness.

---

## The ideas Section 18.9 actually carries

1. **Minds must keep functioning while changing.** A mind cannot
   "close for renovations". Updates must happen in flight.
2. **Duplication.** A function embodied in several agents survives
   the loss of some; many brain regions have duplicates.
3. **Self-repair.** Many organs regenerate; brains generally do
   not, because the value of brain cells is in their *learned
   connections*, which cannot be regenerated by replacing the cells.
4. **Distributed processes.** A function spread over many places
   degrades gracefully rather than disappearing entirely when one
   place is harmed.
5. **Accumulation.** A supervisor that has accumulated several
   sub-agents (each accomplishing the goal in a different way) is
   robust without any special redundancy mechanism: the simplest
   form of learning *is* the robustness.
6. **Versatility and robustness coincide.** A center of diversity
   keeps working until reserves are exhausted; impairment may be
   invisible at first.

## What the implementation already absorbs

### Keep working while changing (idea 1)

`self-modification` frame
([`06-frames-polynemes-klines.md`](../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/06-frames-polynemes-klines.md))
and the candidate-future branch protocol
([`03-runtime-pipeline.md`](../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/03-runtime-pipeline.md),
[`07-policies-and-safety.md`](../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/07-policies-and-safety.md))
mean that the society does its changing on a branch while `main` —
"accepted reality" — continues to serve incoming stimuli. The
runtime never has to halt while it re-authors itself; the kill
switch is the only path that pauses operation, and it is reserved
for governance, not maintenance.

### Distributed processes (idea 4)

A cognitive cycle is distributed across multiple agencies in the
deliberate phase
([`02-workflow-design.md`](../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/02-workflow-design.md)):
perception, memory, code, safety, identity, integration, assembly,
meta-admin. No single agency carries any cognitive function alone;
the conscious-presenter
([`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md):
`agency.integration.conscious-presenter`) is the *sole producer* of
visible response, but it assembles from many upstream contributors.
A perception agency that goes silent still leaves the cycle running
on the remaining signals.

### Accumulation under a supervisor (idea 5)

The agency families in
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md)
each have multiple workers. The *perception* family alone has
`intake-bee`, `issue-kind-detector`, `ambiguity-detector`,
`urgency-detector`; the *memory* family has four; the *code*
family has six. Each family is the §18.9 supervisor; each member is
a sub-agent that handles the family's goal in a different way. If
`urgency-detector` is muted, the family continues to perceive.

### Settlement-level redundancy (idea 5)

Several agencies can independently propose the same candidate
action; the settlement
([`09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md`](../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/09-handoff-and-signal-schemas.md))
records each proposer separately. When one agency is removed via the
`retirement-broker`, other proposers can still surface the same
candidate via different reasoning paths. This is accumulation
operating at the proposal layer.

### The kill switch is *fail-quiet, not fail-loud* (idea 1)

`forgejo-society-ENABLED.md` and `policies/kill-switch.yml`
([`07-policies-and-safety.md`](../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/07-policies-and-safety.md))
let the operator stop the society without modifying any cognitive
record. The mind is parked, not erased; restoration is a single
governance action. This protects the §18.9 invariant that minds
should not collapse when they pause.

## What the implementation does not yet take into account

### A — No explicit *duplication* policy

Idea 2 (duplication) is unrepresented. There is one
`agency.perception.intake-bee`, one `agency.memory.kline-retriever`,
one `agency.code.implementer`. If any one is removed by a
self-modification settlement, that function vanishes until the
catalogue is restored. The plan currently relies on *accumulation*
within a family (gap-mitigating, idea 5) but not on *duplicated
copies of the same agency*. There is no document recommending or
forbidding duplication.

### B — No self-repair mechanism for agencies

Idea 3 (self-repair) is mostly absent and mostly correct. Minsky
notes that brains do not self-repair cells, because the value is in
the connections. The plan's analogue: an agency manifest can be
restored from git history, but its *learned connections* — its
reinforcement weights, its credit-assignment history, its accrued
K-line associations — depend on `evolution/reinforcement-log.md`
([`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md)),
and the plan has no procedure for *replaying* that log against a
restored agency. A retired-and-restored agency starts cold.

### C — Distributed processes are not measured for degradation

Idea 4 promises *graceful degradation*: a partly impaired
distributed function still works, only worse. The plan distributes
function across agencies but provides no metric for how much of a
family's function is present at a given time. If three of the four
perception workers fall silent, the cycle runs; nothing measures
that perception has lost three-quarters of its normal coverage.

### D — Accumulation is bounded by the bootstrap catalogue

Idea 5's robustness scales with the *number* of sub-agents under
each supervisor. The first-ship catalogue
([`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md))
fixes the initial number; growth happens only via the
`differentiation-broker` and human-approved settlements
([`07-policies-and-safety.md`](../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/07-policies-and-safety.md):
approval gate). Accumulation as a *natural* by-product of learning
is not the plan's mode; addition is governed. This is correct for
safety and is the §18.9 gap simultaneously.

### E — Reserve-exhaustion invisibility (idea 6) has no monitor

§18.9 closes with the observation that a *center of diversity*
may appear to function normally until reserves are nearly exhausted.
The plan has no monitor that watches reserve depth — no record of
"three of four redundant supports for this proposal type are now
retired, the fourth is the last one standing". The ecology review
([`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md):
`agency.meta-admin.ecology-monitor`) is the natural place for it;
the precise check is not specified.

## Implication for the plan (no changes proposed here)

§18.9 closes the chapter with a defensive geometry: minds keep
working because they are *redundant in several distinct ways*.
The implementation absorbs two of the four mechanisms cleanly —
distributed processes across the agency families and accumulation
within each family — and absorbs the in-flight-modification
discipline via the `self-modification` frame and candidate-future
branch protocol.

The unrepresented half is *quantitative*: no count of duplicates,
no metric of family coverage, no replay of learned weights when an
agency is restored, no reserve-depth alarm. The plan trades some of
§18.9's natural robustness (spontaneous accumulation) for governed
evolution; the trade is correct for safety and is the closing gap
of the chapter.

These are recorded here as analysis, not as a change request. Any
move to close them would touch the agency catalogue in
[`05-agencies-critics-censors.md`](../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/05-agencies-critics-censors.md),
the memory and evolution sections of
[`08-state-and-memory.md`](../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/08-state-and-memory.md),
the credit-assignment protocol in
[`THE-SOCIETY-OF-REPO/02-protocols/10-credit-assignment.md`](../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-INTRODUCTION/THE-SOCIETY-OF-REPO/02-protocols/10-credit-assignment.md),
and the ecology-review hook noted in
[`03-runtime-pipeline.md`](../../../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/03-runtime-pipeline.md),
and so falls under the "stop and ask" rules in
[AGENTS.md](../../../AGENTS.md) §12 and [CLAUDE.md](../../../CLAUDE.md).
